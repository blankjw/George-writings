THE FACES OF ANGER

By George Gondron

THE FACES OF ANGER The most popular anger is self referential anger Next, other-centered anger And then, disguises of one for the other The first is a reaction of the ego, the mind The second a movement of the heart Ego anger is quick in eruption, an outburst Unexamined, it remains just anger But, underlying the anger are our true emotions Something always precedes the anger Frustration, loss, anxiety, fear, unhappiness Helplessness, defeat, abandonment, neglect Those are just some of the real emotions behind anger Those are our true teachers, our helpers For if I never identify the source of my ego anger I'm doomed to repeat it over and over again It's like fishing, I have to go below the surface To find the connection, to make the catch Then there's other-centered anger Or, what some would call righteous anger Anger that arises from the heart When we witness abuse, of any kind Especially of the defenseless, the helpless, the child The disenfranchised, the marginalized, the weak Not just of humans, but of all life It is the anger that brings tears to the eyes Sorrow to the heart, an ache to the soul Usually, the worse the abuse, the stronger the anger An anger that motivates to defend the defenseless Seek understand your anger, else it controls you Find the hidden feeling, else it repeats itself It's called awareness, become aware

George G
