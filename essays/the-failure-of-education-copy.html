THE FAILURE OF EDUCATION - Copy

By George Gondron

THE FAILURE OF EDUCATION The failure of education doesn’t rest on the shoulders of the teachers It rests on the shoulders of the system itself Teachers are ‘hamstrung’ forced to follow the dictates of a system more concerned about test scores than the innate gifts of the child It takes a special person to choose to become a teacher In a system where they are underpaid and undervalued It’s shocking to realize an elected politician is paid three to four times that of a teacher Part of the problem lies at the feet of the family as well As long as little Suzy or Johnny graduates, all is well And then there’s the topic of discipline I remember if you were a disruption in school you were sent to the principal’s office along with a note home to your parents – no longer Now the student has more rights than the adults that teach them Here’s some recent statistics – 54% of adults, 130 million people have a literacy level of 6th grade – this should shock you One out of five adults are illiterate, cannot read or write And then there’s a matter of a college degree The choice to pursue a degree in higher education is for most a choice to burden themselves with debt for years The average college graduate reads one book a year, mostly fiction The value of education cannot be overstated In conjunction with how that value is accomplished With the explosion of robotics and artificial intelligence The necessity of proper education is even more glaringly important Art, literature, all the humanities have been the bastions of history throughout history – they are vanishing When what is offered hinges on a price tag- all suffer To be part of the solution our individual education need not cease Without critical thinking and deep reflection, we merely consume Add a meditate/contemplative practice to your daily agenda Spend a few minutes in silence, in stillness, be present to Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit We are not innocent bystanders; we have a role to play

George G
