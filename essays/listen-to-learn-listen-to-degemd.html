LISTEN TO LEARN, LISTEN TO DEGEMD

By George Gondron

LISTEN TO LEARN, LISTEN TO DEFEND If only, if only we’d listen learn The most important characteristic in primary and secondary relationships is healthy communication Active versus passive listening skills applicable in all interactions between another person or persons Few there are that are proficient in listening to learn Many there are that listen to defend Most are only concerned about their own agenda or agendas Our immediate teachers are our five senses Our ‘on the job training’ so to speak Taste, touch, smell, hear , and see even though immediate can be filtered by our own learned prejudices and acquired biases For example, our ability to hear can be filtered through a closed mind resulting in a listening to defend versus a listening to learn, or a listening to react versus a listening to respond Those who wish to learn and become more aware and conscious do so with open minds, not closed minds It seems most people are attracted to the flamboyant, ‘gaslighting’, propaganda, and alternative facts than the truth Influenced more through their emotions than their critical thinking All human resistance to change has one source, the ego Back in the ‘1970’s’ there was a popular TV sitcom called ‘All in the Family’, the main character was Archie Bunker, a perfect example of a closed minded individual unwilling to change And most of us, unwilling to see our own shadow, contain parts of the Archie Bunker personality, we don’t need to change, he/she/they do If we are open to seeing our own closed mindedness, our own shortcomings, our own bias, prejudice, discrimination and inability to learn but to only defend w are blessed for most do not Individuation and self-discovery are not easy tasks but the necessary tasks toward wholeness and a healthy humanity An adoption of a meditative/contemplative practice is so important A merging of inner and outer resources for becoming fully human A few minutes daily for silence, stillness, and Presence To focus on the breath or a short mantra, when distracted rinse and repeat over and over again Listen for the whispers of your inner guides heart, soul, and spirit

George G
