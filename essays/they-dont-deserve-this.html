THEY DON'T DESERVE THIS

By George Gondron

THEY DON’T DESERVE THIS Please, don’t turn away from scenes of starving children From children with missing limbs as a result of Self-righteous men bent on revenge Violence only begets more violence It never, ever solves the real problem Wherever conflict reigns, the innocent suffer And there’s always a benefactor waiting in the wings Be it a single individual, a group, or a nation Without a conscience they celebrate their assumed victory But in reality, no one wins, ever Unfortunately, war in some form or fashion, is perpetual And the reasons are almost always a result of fear, not love Don’t confuse rebellion with war, they are not the same Rebellions are more about injustice and suppression of rights War is more about greed, you have what I want Sorta like killing 80 to 100 million native Americans for their lands After all, it was in the name of ‘progress’ Or enslaving black people, they are only 3/5 human And we’ll use their ‘free labor’ for whatever our desires Or, more recently, lets separate children from their families Put them in cages, they are just ‘animals’ poisoning the blood Of our ‘Christian’ nation – there’s an oxymoron for you It’s so interesting how we are able to justify our actions Whatever they may be to the detriment of other human beings Men, women, and children and of course they are not ‘white’ Here’s what’s deserving, that our true history be taught To our children that they might not repeat the ‘sins of the fathers’ That they might truly know what equal freedom really means Regardless of skin color, sexual orientation, or religious preference Our children and grandchildren deserve to know the truth Revealed in the clear light of day, not hidden in a closet We have a lot to answer for, the ‘bad guys’ are not just out there Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life Choose a few minutes of silence, stillness, and Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your hear, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your answers, they patiently wait

George G
