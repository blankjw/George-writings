DON'T RUN

By George Gondron

DON’T RUN There are some experiences that call for running Running away for our own safety But there are many, many more that necessitates That we remain firm and steadfast for our own Psychological wellbeing, our own mental health However, far too many hold on to their emotional pain Through denial, rationalization, justification, or any other Of the many psychological defense mechanisms Which are in themselves subjugation and suppression Mechanisms If those don’t work, we may choose medication A pill for anxiety, depression, or some other medical diagnosis That only masks the real problem and becomes Just a crutch, or worse we turn to some other chemical addiction That we ingest as alcohol, ‘street drugs’, or food Or we act out through indiscriminate sexual behavior Regardless our choice, in the end, none of the above work They actually do more harm, physically, emotionally, and spirituality If we have the strength and courage to examine the real problem If we are brave enough to face the real issue It is us, we are the ones responsible for our behavior No one else, ever The reasons we use to justify our behavior have no merit For in the end, those reasons don’t matter What matters is our health and wellbeing A personal problem is never solved through replacement It is only solved through sincere self-discovery Perhaps through the help of a trusted professional But more importantly through a change in lifestyle Looking at the false self in the face, in the mirror And committing to discover the ‘true self’, inside waiting We are spiritual beings living in a non-spiritual environment We can through a meditative/contemplative practice Discover our own spiritual nature, our true essence Spend a few minutes each day in silence, in stillness, Empty the mind and listen to the whispers of your inner self Listen to the inner Source that sustains each one of us, always

George G
