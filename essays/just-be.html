JUST BE

By George Gondron

JUST BE The Western world is all about performing, achieving, doing The ability to just be has faded away, perhaps never known We can more correctly be identified as ‘human doings’ Rather than human beings Our waking hours filled with activity physically and cognitively Even when there’s no need to focus, the inner noise continues We identify with what we do and where we’re from Rather than who we are, but maybe that’s not important Since our behavior indicates who we are, or, are not It seems once we’ve become ‘settled’, or set in our ways We resist change, disregard new information And be very careful, we don’t like to be challenged We may not be right, but we’re never wrong We fall into the trap of becoming nothing more than consumers Just a means to and end, someone else’s end We have joined the ‘rat race’ Even our dreams are filled with messages, important messages Which we fail to comprehend We live in prisons, all self-made, and we are our own jailers Maybe the freedom we seek is connected in learning How to be, and not just do, we knew that as children Children have an ability to trust and be spontaneous Play comes natural for them as does sharing and loving A small child has no identity of a separate self They are connected to others until the almighty ‘I’ emerges As Descartes once said, ‘cogito, erg sum’ -I think therefore I am But unfortunately, in the child’s thinking they are influenced To see difference before sameness, unalike rather than alike Maybe as adults we need to awaken our potential to be And not just do, we have that innate inner ability, it’s a choice Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your being A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence A few minutes to re-energize our ability to be, not just do Focus on a brief mantra or just your breath Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
