GANDHI^J A MAN OF PEACE

By George Gondron

GANDHI, A MAN OF PEACE The renown Hindu Indian statesman, Mahatma Gandhi, freed the country of Indian from British Christian Imperial Rule through a policy of nonviolence A highly educated yet humble person, he is quoted as saying, ‘I love your Christ, but not your Christians, they are nothing like your Christ’ And therein lies the rub, for centuries the Christian faith has committed and excused atrocities perpetrated against human beings they considered to ‘be in error’, being judge, jury, and executioner A reality that is juxtaposed to the message taught by the one they say they follow, Jesus the Christ In a way, modern day, and even historical Christianity reveals itself as a belief of convenience One could say it’s a ‘cafeteria’ or ‘fast food’ religion where one can pick and choose what they want as they move through the live or off the menu and ignore the rest, how appropriate I know some very good people who actually live what Jesus taught, most of whom are in religious orders and profess a vow of poverty The Buddha taught four noble truths, the world is full of suffering, the cause of suffering is desire and attachment, the relief of suffering is the cessation of desire and attachment, and the last the Eight Fold path: Right intention, right action, right speech, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right meditation Consequently, in the Catholic faith, a vow of poverty makes perfect sense, there is nothing to be attached to Sadly, most people are born into the faith they follow, if they follow When asked today if I am Christian, I no longer answer in the positive I simply say I’m a follower of Jesus, to the best of my ability The hypocrisy of all major organized religions is astounding, including Christianity, they all insist on making the simple difficult It’s easy to see why so many don’t believe in a Mystery called God But then again, that Mystery is not intellectual, it is experiential It is discovered from within using the appropriate context One of which is a meditative/contemplative practice A daily emptying of our ‘monkey minds’ that we might be present Present to our inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit A time to connect inner and outer, to discern To discover our true self, to find our answers, to know

George G
