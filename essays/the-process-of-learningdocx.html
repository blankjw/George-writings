THE PROCESS OF LEARNING

By George Gondron

THE PROCESS OF LEARNING Learning, an increase in awareness and consciousness Is not just about adding to, but also about a letting go We can’t ‘unlearn’ what we already know But the mark of intelligence is the ability to change That which no longer fits, is no longer beneficial For that which is better in thought, word, and deed Our opportunities for learning have never been greater As well our possibilities to be deceived, to play the fool If we have the courage to step back and widen our point of view Open our lens a little broader That we might see the forest and the trees We might notice ‘the carrot on the stick’ Perhaps our perceptions might become clearer And our responses more accurate The simple definition of ignorance is the lack of knowledge Accurate, fact related, scientific, proven information Ignorance is more easily influenced through the emotions Than through the intellect Our intellect supports our ability to create and construct To maintain and sustain But it is our direct experience that underlies all our values Our ability to discern, distinguish, to intuit, to see beyond Beyond the smoke and mirrors, the emotional jargon The hype, the naysayers, the propagandists, the BS-ers When the repeated narrative is negative in content It is almost always a half-truth, a lie, or the new ‘alternative fact’ The conspiracy theorists and deep state-ers love the negative Educating ourselves is not a simple matter of ‘flipping’ channels It requires critical thinking, research, deep reflection, and doubt Beyond direct personal experience truth is elusive As the saying goes, ‘the truth will set you free, but first it will make You miserable’ – learning is not meant to be easy Add a mediative/contemplative practice to your valuable life Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, be present Focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
