FOCUS

By George Gondron

FOCUS Where we place our energy Through our thoughts, feelings, and actions Our usual normal focus Demonstrates who we are much more than our words In essence, words are nothing more than labels That distracts rather than confirms who we are We know about who a person is or is not Through objective unobserved examination But since we’re not the ‘fly on the wall’ It’s much more difficult for us to discern or to know This simple phrase, ‘With whom am I having the pleasure?’ We have all lied at some point Either the ‘little white lie’ or the whopper And the lie, big or small, is simply a cover-up And the question follows, a cover-up for what? And what we normally cover up is a thought, feeling, Or action that we already know is flawed or incongruent But the truth is always better than the lie And it is only fear that prevents the truth And through the lie, we harm first ourselves and then the other Living with the lie is nothing short of being self-destructive A nagging itch that won’t go away Lingering just around the corner, unless of course We’re an egotistical, narcissistic, pathological liar Or a sociopath that cares only for themselves and no one else To those, the lie is just a normal way of life Like ‘water off a duck’s back’, the lie is their truth To fully understand, comprehend, and discern Requires more than just the mind, the intellect, the ego It must necessarily include our inner core best awakened Through a committed meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day spent in silence, with stillness In the presence of Presence, one’s image of God, of Isness A balanced focus, inner and outer, a letting-go The mind clings to, the heart frees from Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, they have your answers, they wait

George G
