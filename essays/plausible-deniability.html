PLAUSIBLE DENIABILITY

By George Gondron

PLAUSIBLE DENIABILITY The ability of people, especially in positions of power, To deny knowledge or responsibility for any Damnable actions committed by others That is plausible deniability in a nut-shell Regardless of the truth, loyalty is more important That is the picture of our current political environment The one important fact, above all others Including the Constitution and the survival of the Republic What do I need to do to be re-elected, period the end Truth doesn’t matter, facts don’t matter, Conscience doesn’t exist Another argument, ‘I must represent my constituents’ Translated I must bow to the lobbyists from whom I receive money, money, money, and more money You and I do not matter, the constituents Let me repeat that – you and I do not matter This dilemma will never change until three things happen Term limits to get rid of professional politicians Campaign reform, the elimination of huge unknown donors And lastly, no more lobbyists All three end up being only ‘pipe dreams’ They will never happen Even though they would solve the problems The honest politicians that walk the halls of Congress Are too few, it is too evident Most are in the back pockets of Corporate interests Not the interests of those they supposedly represent And even our highest court in the land The Supreme Court has become more and more politicized I wonder what the Founding Fathers would have to say Plausible deniability keeps us on the ‘merry-go-round’ Of blame and finger-pointing Those that have the power to call out corruption That is so rampant, don’t have the courage to do so I respect and honor the few, on both sides of the aisle That do, but they are not enough I fear for the future, but I will cling to hope

George G
