LOOK OVER THERE

By George Gondron

LOOK OVER THERE Distraction is a reality that’s ‘baked’ into our culture We avoid ‘drilling’ down on facts and truth Much preferring to be distracted in order that we Might be ‘saved’ from taking a hard look at what is As it is, for if we do, we just might have to change And one of the ego’s most important tasks Is to resist change, at all costs, on every level If we reflect on the idea of change at all It goes like this What needs to change is not me, it’s you or them Or it, anything but me Those that work in the field of human behavior and Mental processes dealing with emotions and relationships Reveal that 70 to 80 % of those who seek counseling Do so to change someone else, not themselves Let me offer this observation The only person we have the power to change On any significant level, is ourselves, us And if we’re honest, if we’re willing to remove our ‘blinders’ We know the truth of that statement But if the name of the game is blame Is to deny and choose distraction instead Alas, we cling to the familiar even if the familiar is painful Part of the fear behind our resistance to change is Whom would I be if I changed? what would I have to give up That’s what fear does, focuses on subtraction, not addition But love, on the other hand, whispers ‘imagine what you would gain’ Fear does not comprehend the meaning of freedom Only love does Fear seeks the distractions, seeks anything that prevents Self-examination, self-criticism, self-reflection Love embraces self-awareness with the knowledge We can only change what we’re willing to clearly see A guaranteed path to self-knowledge is a contemplative practice Just a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, with Presence A time to empty our ‘monkey minds’ and listen to the whispers Of our own heart, soul, and spirit, our inner Truth

George G
