UNIMAGINABLE

By George Gondron | Source: UNIMAGINABLE.docx

UNIMAGINABLE When we stop long enough When we let go of our desires and attachments Just for a few moments, we take the focus off of ourselves Surely it’s unimaginable, overwhelming, and incomprehensible To know in the clearest manner possible The utter lack of morality that exists in this world And even more difficult and impossible to understand Is the fact that too much of the breakdown of morals Is because of religion, all organized hierarchical belief The hypocrisy of fundamentalist religion is appalling Every crime imaginable, every horrible act against humanity Can be attributed to some justifiable religious edict Witness the conflict in the Middle East at this very moment Both believing in the same Creator One is called Yahweh, the other is called Allah Both are the same God, and this God weeps Weeps for what is being done in its name And the Jews and Muslims both follow the words of men Who claim they speak for their God As Meister Eckhart said, ‘God save me from God’ Only an inflated selfish ego can slaughter in the name Of the God, they claim they serve If we have an ounce of compassion, of kindness, of love We know that cannot be true All current organized religion has no clue about teaching The importance of a relationship with the Divine Regardless of the name, the necessity of spirituality Not worship, what a puny God that demands our worship We operate from an egoic justification for our behavior Totally disconnected from the knowing of the heart Unaware of the yearning of our own soul and spirit Let me suggest a meditative/contemplative practice A process of connecting inner and outer A few minutes each day in silence, be still, embrace Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They will teach you discernment, they hold your true self They have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
