PETTINESS & PRIVILEGE

By George Gondron

PETTINESS & PRIVILEGE 'Mountains out of mole hills' The elixir of the petty 'Much ado about nothing' The nectar of the privileged The vast majority of the world, not privileged The privileged can't 'see the forest for the trees' The privileged 'cry over spilt milk' The masses have no milk to cry over The privileged live in their bubbles Void of reality No understanding of the struggles of most With blinders and turned backs They ignore the obvious They shut out the apparent, the observable Does privilege belie responsibility? Does pettiness excuse obligation? To look up from the bottom one sees the truth But from the top to the bottom, ignore the truth When there is abundance, how is their suffering? When there is a cure, why the disease? Who determines the importance of life? Who suffers and who doesn't? Who lives and who dies? The petty and the privileged seem not to care Secure in their beliefs Protected in their safeholds Who does care? Those who love, they care Love cannot, not care Love is not petty, nor privileged Love always, always cares

George G
