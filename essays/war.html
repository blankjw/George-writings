WAR

By George Gondron

WAR It's too late for me Too late to see, to witness the end of war All war I often wonder if there will ever be a generation That will know a life without military conflict Is it possible for those who are 'hawks' To ever lose their grip, their false sense of security As they surround themselves with weapons of mass destruction How far away are we from a 'robotic' war When we find another justification for the taking of human life Just send out the robots, they can kill for us And of course the unseen enemy will do the same There are no winners, only losers We all will lose in the end And when the planet is burned out and lifeless Perhaps life in some other form will emerge from the chaos Perhaps I know the stupidity and selfishness of the human psyche As well I know its ability to be kind and compassionate Not unless, not until we see the sacredness of life All life Will we cease to be the reason for our own demise We refuse to see the divinity we carry within We reject the very Love that creates all of existence And yet we are forever held within that very same Love One day I will ask God to share His/Her pain with me The pain of watching the destruction of perfection Of rendering asunder the beautiful and resplendent But then again I don't need to wait, I see it now In the empty eyes of the orphaned child The broken bodies of the wounded warriors The twisted minds filled with nightmares of those who survived I know that in the end, Love will succeed, will win But no one knows what that winning will look like I still hope, I still pray, that we will wake up That we will see we are the same enemy we fight against And until we do, nothing will change, I hope I am not alone

George G
