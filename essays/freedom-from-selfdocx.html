FREEDOM FROM SELF

By George Gondron

FREEDOM FROM SELF There is a common theme within all belief systems It is freedom But not freedom of self, but rather freedom from self From the self that is conditioned, programmed, indoctrinated The self that is controlled and driven by the ego The self which competes, compares, and controls It is in a true sense, the death of who we think we are In order to become who we truly are In psychological terms it is the awakening , consciously To the egotistical, narcissistic self that we mostly are That transformation or change, when genuine, feels like a death It usually happens as a result of emotional pain Pain that can no longer be avoided through distractions Our constant 'doing', thinking or feeling Or, put in another way When we've attempted to assuage the yearnings within With our own preconceived ideas, and nothing works There is an adage, 'the only way out is through' That's what we attempt to avoid, the 'through' It doesn't mean that in order to accomplish this change We no longer have a self, anything but We now have an authentic self, not one that's ego driven One that embraces heart, soul, and spirit knowing As well as the mind One that is first accepting of its personal weaknesses And second of its connection to all that is There is an expansion of awareness and consciousness An understanding that it is part of the greater whole Not a separate entity unto itself Once the door to the above has been opened It can never be closed, in fact, there's no longer door For some this process never happens Able to withstand the heart's passion, the spirit's whisper Through the small self's insistence, of remaining in control There many who think they are free, yet remain empty inside They are the 'great pretenders', clinging to their false security In the silence and stillness, in the Presence, we do find freedom

George G
