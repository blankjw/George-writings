DECEPTION

By George Gondron

DECEPTION Of all creation, humans alone are the great deceivers The great imposters Pretenders of what we aren't With the gift of consciousness comes the power to deceive Life without consciousness depends on instinct for survival Instinct and deception, very different in motivation Deception is about being selfish Deception is about being in control Regardless of the pretense, the reason, the justification The underlying hypocrisy of deception is betrayal Misleading behavior, regardless of appearance, is ultimately about self-satisfaction Consciousness is our greatest gift and our greatest burden Used by a few for the benefit of others Used by many for the benefit of themselves In the end, when the dust settles, the smoke clears The lonely are the deceivers, the imposters Left with an emptiness within, the pretenders A hollowness, echoing their cries of defense Defense for behavior that can only be seen as self-serving Deception, the carrot on the stick Dressed up in righteousness, adorned in devotion Hiding motives that serve the self I hope I know my deceptions They hide in the shadows of my mind Ever eager to have their way My awareness keeps them at bay My connection to the All holds them in restraint Ever watchful, ever mindful, ever vigilant Ever awake

George G
