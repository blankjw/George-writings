FORGIVENESS, THE GIFT OF FREEDOM

By George Gondron

FORGIVENESS, THE GIFT OF FREEDOM To forgive others for past or current actions Does free us from clinging to that which Is of no benefit, and has no useful purpose For lack of a better image It’s akin to dragging around a ball and chain Or being pulled down by an anchor Through forgiveness We free ourselves so that we might live healthier lives More meaningful and purposeful lives For, after all, it’s our inability to let go That really keeps us stuck When we can’t forgive We literally imprison ourselves And our freedom only happens with forgiveness We hold the key to our release The need to forgive is always a result Of some interaction between another or others Emotional or physical abuse Broken trust, mistreatment on the job Destroying the character of another person Through gossip and false information However, here’s the most difficult process In the realm of forgiveness that must happen We must forgive ourselves We must reflect, examine, recall, and remember Those instances, those events, those occasions Where we were hurtful to others With intent or unintentionally By accident or on purpose As the saying goes ‘The unexamined life is not worth living’ In our humanity, we will hurt others And if we are aware, we hurt ourselves as well Freedom requires forgiveness Forgiveness leads to freedom Forgive others, forgive yourself Be free

George G
