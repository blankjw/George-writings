QUESTIONS 2

By George Gondron

QUESTIONS Who, what, where, when, how Precursors of change Initiators of transformation Without the questions, nothing changes, all remains the same It is not from the answers that new possibility arises It is from the questions, from our reflection, out of our discernment Existence remains static, fixed and firm without inquiry But wait, we forgot the question 'why' Our most beloved question The one dearest to a child's heart, perpetuated by adults In children it's normal, in adults it promotes a push-back A 'why' question elicits a defensive posture A stance from which I no longer hear you, too busy defending my position Sadly, what we're really asking with our 'why' is how did this happen, rather than 'why' We can hear 'how' much easier than 'why' How is explainable, why is not Why is an attack, how is a welcome for more information There is never an answer to why It is only a self-serving question If used at all, it is best used to reflect on one's own behavior Ask questions, be curious, it leads to new awareness But, please, avoid the 'why'

George G
