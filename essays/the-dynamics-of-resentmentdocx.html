THE DYNAMICS OF RESENTMENT

By George Gondron

THE DYNAMICS OF RESENTMENT At one time or another We have each been resentful, if we're honest But then honesty is another topic all together Back to resentment The shared characteristic with most resentment It's not verbalized and lies just beneath the surface It's manifested in passive aggressive behavior Resentment thrives in every arena imaginable The workplace, the church, the school, in sports Between friends, in families, among siblings Anywhere there's human interactions You can be assured there will be resentment So what's the common denominator? The underlying dynamic that encourages, fosters, And perpetuates resentment? There are a number of them And they all begin with an internal dialogue 'I deserve, I don't deserve, why me, they don't understand' And even if what we are resentful about When examined objectively is not valid The ego is still capable of holding onto resentment The almighty 'I' is seldom satisfied You will frequently find resentment with a person Who refuses to look in the mirror, the finger points elsewhere The common characteristic with the resentful person Is blame or some other psychological defense mechanism Resentful people are mostly dualistic thinkers Their approach is never both/and, but always either/or And here is the paradox The one who holds the resentment is the one who suffers And not that which is resented, be it person or otherwise Regardless the circumstances we humans are very adept At creating our own misery To let go of resentment is to heal one's self first In the silence and stillness you will find healing Only if you choose, It is always available, waiting But like all personal change, it is a choice

George G
