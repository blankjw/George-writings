GUN VIOLENCE

By George Gondron

GUN VIOLENCE – A DIFFERENT VIEW The go-to excuse for the increasing number of gun violent deaths, especially mass shootings, is mental instability. It’s the favorite argument by Republican politicians and many gun enthusiasts. Here’s a little scientific information about our brain’s development. The most important brain area to become fully ‘wired-up’ in adulthood is the prefrontal cortex – the front portion of the frontal lobe. This area handles many of our higher-level cognitive abilities such as cognitive control – the ability to suppress impulses in favor of more appropriate actions. The adult brain is better wired for cognitive control compared to the adolescent brain, which is more influenced by emotions, rewards, and social acceptance when it comes to making decisions. ` Intelligence also peaks during early to middle adulthood, roughly ages 25 to 60. Consequently, how does it make sense to allow an 18-year-old person the option to buy an assault weapon, i.e. an AR-15? Insurance companies have known for years about the risks of insuring male drivers below the age of 25. The rates are extremely high, dropping substantially after age 24. The last two mass shootings in our nation, ten days apart, were by 18-year-old males using assault-type military weapons. Obviously, our Republican lawmakers along with the NRA could care less. When is enough, enough? Maybe if the images of the slaughtered children were shown to those same lawmakers, perhaps there would be change. Sad to say that for the first time in our history the main cause of childhood death is now guns. I pray for common-sense gun legislation, but I doubt any common sense exists among Republican politicians.

George G
