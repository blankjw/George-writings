LIES, FALSEHOODS, UNTRUUTHS

By George Gondron | Source: LIES, FALSEHOODS, UNTRUUTHS.docx

LIES, FALSEHOODS, UNTRUTHS Here is a true statement, liars cannot be trusted, ever Never before, in the history of our nation, have we elected a leader that constantly distorts the truth Consequently, we have elected, willingly, a consummate liar Distortion, fabrication, and exaggeration are his ‘go to’ preferences for communicating, And obviously, his methods of governing belie the negative effects imposed on all our citizens, except of course, the wealthy Who gets hurt matters not to him, young, old, handicapped, child, gender, poor, immigrant and disenfranchised His very existence is nothing more than a history of lies, consistently We all know how to lie, and I’m sure we all have, but for your life io be nothing more than a pattern of lies, is a serious psychological problem, it is the nature of a psychopath and a sociopath Not the kind of personality one would want to run a government Not a reliable preference for a Commander-in-Chief Nothing is sacrosanct to him except the insistence of undying loyalty to his every desire, regardless of the outcome He is a ‘Nero’ in disguise, willing to burn down the nation as he fiddles As long as he gets his way, as long as you ‘bend the knee’ His new, DOGE, department of government efficiency by all appearances is nothing more than a means to punish those he dislikes, which includes anyone that speaks against him Anyone who wounds his fragile ego, receives narcissistic retribution Historically, what is happening now is nothing more than a repeat of what happened in Germany in the 1930’s, only the names and places are different, but unlike the Germans we can’t say we didn’t know, because of social media, all the world knows It begs the question, how long before he controls the media? There is a terrible price to pay for ‘chosen’ ignorance, for blindness Please, take time for deep reflection, critical thinking, even prayer We are in tenuous and precarious times; we need to wake up Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, in the presence of your image if God Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out When the ego resists, the mind wanders, rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit

George G
