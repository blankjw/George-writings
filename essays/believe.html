BELIEVE

By George Gondron

BELIEVE What a dualistic mind believes about itself Is at best only half true, if that From being inflated on one end of the continuum To being worthless on the other end, both exaggerations From an ego whose only reference point is external Comparing and contrasting itself with others Cannot possibly be honest and clear-sighted about itself Our strongest emotions are projections toward others Put-downs of ourselves, or denial of our weaknesses All of which are inaccurate Never understanding that the finger we point at others Is the same finger that points back at us If we haven't acknowledged our devils and demons If we haven't looked in the mirror and seen The worse that we can be As well as seeing our angels and saints then We're living under a false pretense We are living without humility, the flip side of love If what we say we believe is not what we know Factually, experientially, personally Then, what proof of our knowing? If what we believe about ourselves is not inclusive Our strengths, our weaknesses, our failings, and successes Then we actually live with an illusion, strangers to ourselves The easiest and most common journey of never knowing Knowing who we truly are is called distraction Busyness, living in the past or worrying about the future Having the same repetitive thoughts Day after day, after day, after day And of course the most important of all Is the avoidance of an internal life Living a life called the 'externals', the human 'doings' Our 'being-ness' is lost, our spirituality languishes The belief of who we are can't be known through the mind It can only be known in silence, stillness, and Presence Known in honest relationships, with others, with God Any other way is not knowing at all, it's all smoke and mirrors

George G
