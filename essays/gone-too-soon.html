GONE TOO SOON

By George Gondron

GONE TOO SOON Is there ever a right time for death? It's okay, they lived a long life How tragic, they were so young Our greatest fear, death Ours, not someone else's The mourning of another's dying, is a chance A chance to reflect on our own mortality Oh, how we avoid the idea of death, images of death Stuffed in the far corners of our mind An enemy to be feared, the Grim Reaper Perhaps the thoughts of death can also be our teacher A reminder of the sacredness of life, all of life From the unborn to the aged From the healthy to the infirm No difference in the eyes of the Creator Always a focus on the created It is good, all good, a manifestation of Goodness We see difference through our labels and separateness We miss the connections, the sameness Death, a common kinship with all life A reciprocity that's inescapable Maybe Death can be our teacher A teacher of life as opposed to existence A teacher of compassion as opposed to apathy A reminder to be present to each day To ourselves, to one another, to creation Perhaps Death can teach us to really live To celebrate life Perhaps
