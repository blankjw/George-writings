THE BLESING OF BROKENENSS

By George Gondron

THE BLESSING OF BROKENNESS Brokenness from being human falls into several dimensions Physical, psychological, intellectual, emotional, egotistical Just to mention a few The breaking of the ego, of the all-powerful ‘I’ Of the psychological defense mechanisms Of the emotional barriers we build and maintain These are the most resistant and the most resilient They will cling to the familiar even in discomfort Even in unimaginable pain for fear of the unknown If you’re lucky, you will experience this brokenness Maybe at least once in your lifetime Spiritual writers call it ‘the dark night of the soul’ And the blessing will happen when and if you can Part the curtain of your brokenness And perceive with clarity the possibilities Of a different here and now, a new now moment But if you’re like most in this culture, this society You will simply commiserate with your brokenness And miss the blessing entirely Embracing your self-righteous pain even more tightly Clinging to a closemindedness refusing to learn Unwilling to let go, and let be They say a mended broken bone is even stronger Than before the break A mended broken ego, persona, facade, pretense Is as well, open to new possibilities, new learning A new focus on the meaning of your life, your creativity If you’ve been broken, choose a new path A path that leads to balance, congruence, discernment A path that leads to wholeness, wellness, and truth A path that leads to stronger relationships A meditative/contemplative practice for a few minutes each day Is that important path A time for emptying the mind of its incessant repetitive thoughts A time for silence, stillness, being in the presence of Presence A time to listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, your true self, they patiently await

George G
