KNOWING

By George Gondron

KNOWING How do we truly, genuinely, know? Anything Those who seem so sure, so confident So convinced of their position So certain of their beliefs Ask them One simple query, how do you know? Almost with no exception, if they're honest You will receive these replies 'I heard it from a reliable source' 'It was written by a noted journalist' 'Isn't that what everyone believes?' 'Well, so and so said' 'This politician, evangelist, advertiser, marketer Said it on TV, the web, Face-Book, or U-tube' And the best response, 'I just know it's true' Which is second to the famous reply to What is it about her/him you don't like? 'I just don't like them' There is one way, and one way only, to know Experience, personal experience Hands-on involvement, direct observation Contact, exposure, struggle, and encounter There is no other way to know experientially Intellectual knowledge, is knowing without experience How much destruction, death, turmoil, and pain Has been loosed upon the world, upon people With no experiential knowledge and manipulation So, when you feel firm about anything Ask yourself, how do you know? Hopefully your answer is, through experience

George G
