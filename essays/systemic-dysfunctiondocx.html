SYSTEMIC DYSFUNCTION

By George Gondron

SYSTEMIC DYSFUNCTION Systems that can be labeled as dysfunctional Are those who are primarily concerned about control They survive by promoting ignorance They much prefer an uneducated citizenry Fur the uneducated can be easily manipulated All dysfunctional systems maintain their control through illusion Fabrications that claim their way is the only and correct way But if you take the time to examine the dynamics of the system You will discover it’s almost always about control That enables a few to remain in power And reap the economic benefits of their particular system I’m sure you’ve noticed those at the top are never without They lack for nothing No system is exempt from dysfunction A thief by any other name is still a thief A perfect example is Christian hypocrisy TV evangelists making millions of dollars with no shame Fortunately, not all Christian leaders are the same And then there’s politics There is a saying, show me an honest politician And I’ll show you a politician out of office Would that were true about corporate CEOs Profiting off the backs of their ‘indentured servants’ Too few know too little about politics Too few know too little about the importance of education Too few know too little about themselves, about relationships The system convinces us the road to happiness is consumption Spend more of what we don’t have on things we don’t need What is life, life is relationship, not ownership In our culture we are a means to an end, that end is consumption We are humans first, last, and always Meant to be connected, meant for compassion, empathy, kindness Meant for love, not for power, manipulation, and control Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, embrace Presence Empty your mind, listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your truth, your answers, they wait Grow in awareness, consciousness, and discernment, we need it

George G
