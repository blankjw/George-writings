DEADLY SILENCE

By George Gondron

DEADLY SILENCE All around us the loud exclamations of prejudice Shouts of bigotry and discrimination Of religious fundamentalism Of political conservatism and liberalism And then there's the deadly silence No shouts for kindness, compassion, charity No call for collaboration, cooperation, discussion Only the empty rhetoric, empty words of what's different And the mind whispers, the ego proclaims How can what's not like me Be considered worthy of inclusion We seem to care to the extent that what we want Is what's important, me and mine What has value is what we value Where are the cries for equality, justice, mercy, and love Who speaks for the homeless, the disenfranchised Who speaks for those living in poverty For the single parent working two jobs, or maybe three Just to make ends meet I do feel anger when I hear the judgmental voices Hurling names against those receiving welfare With no personal information at all about their plight Yes, the silence of compassion, of kindness, of charity Is deafening There is a great abyss between the obvious divide The divide of who cares and who doesn't Lip service is useless, lip service puts no food on the table
