NATURAL, RHYTHMS

By George Gondron | Source: NATURAL, RHYTHMS.docx

NATURAL RHYTHMS Nature, and all things in nature, have their natural rhythms They manifest those rhythms through their behavior and growth All things great and small have a natural pattern All except the human species Unlike all other life forms, humans can go against Their own existence when they become splintered Experiencing a disconnect between their minds, their ego And the innate knowing of their heart, soul, and spirit And ironically it seems the more advanced their thinking The greater the divide Witness the indigenous peoples still today have that connection They know that all of life, all of nature, is related Depending one on the other for their mutual existence We humans have become accustomed to being ‘fractured’ Acculturated to a new reality that what we think Is all that matters We have lost touch with an internal reference, an inner core Negative thoughts and negative feelings rule the day We, it seems, are unconcerned about humanity as a whole We defend our immediate space and all else is secondary Even our religion, if we practice one, is very limited Maybe practice is not the appropriate word Rather than orthopraxy, for most it’s orthodoxy A form of meritocracy that has no basis whatsoever Ah, but I regress, back to the natural rhythms Here’s a suggestion To reconnect to our better nature, our whole self Choose a meditative/contemplative daily practice For just a few minutes each day Be silent, be still, empty your mind of its incessant thoughts Thoughts that for the most part are repetitive Thoughts that go nowhere and accomplish nothing Be in the presence of the One you may call God Not for conversation, just for relationship Words are an obstruction, the greatest knowing comes from Not knowing – listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They have your answers, they hold the truth, and they wait

George G
