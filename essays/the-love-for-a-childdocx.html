THE LOVE FOR A CHILD

By George Gondron

THE LOVE FOR A CHILD The love for a child is a special kind of love Maybe it's your own child Or perhaps a grandchild, or if we're lucky A great grandchild, an adopted child, any child If we have a humble heart, a compassionate nature To witness the abuse of a child is a painful experience Like no other pain you will ever endure 'It would be better for you if a millstone were hung around your neck and you were thrown into the sea than for you to cause one of these little ones to stumble' Some see abuse only as something physical But it is so much more than physical, as terrible as that is It is also emotional, and yes, spiritual as well The physical scars can heal, the others may never heal If you're blessed to have a child in your life You will feel your own pain, emotional pain The pain of watching them face their own particular battles Their struggles to understand their own relationships With their peers, with their teachers, with you, with themselves There are moments when you will feel unwanted, but not unneeded Moments when their other outside relationships Will appear to be more important than the relationship with you Moments when you feel and believe you are nothing more Than a means to an end, a necessary burden to be tolerated A relationship that seems to be an occasional acknowledgement With a string attached, a monetary string To be and remain objective in the 'growing up years' of an adolescent Is nothing short of miraculous, it requires a special faith Remembering our own struggles as a youth is a help But, not a cure, remain clear about your own value and worth Demonstrate that you will not allow yourself to be abused Hold hope in your heart that what you once witnessed in the child Will return in the maturing young adult The bonds of love created in the adult, child relationship Are never broken, they may be weak at times, but last forever Remember we all have strayed from the One we may call God No matter the reason, but that Love that bond remains, always

George G
