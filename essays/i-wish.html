I WISH

By George Gondron

I WISH If wishes were dollars, we'd all be rich How many 'I wish' we've said to ourselves And those wishes, so few realized So many abandoned I've had a few myself I have learned many things about wishes That all I've wished for is mostly useless Mostly, unimportant in the end And sadly, the 'grass is not always greener' 'All that glitters is not gold' And 'patience does not always pay off' Being the 'captain of one's ship' is not easy The mind most often chooses the course The heart is left in the wake of the rudder To be dragged along at the ego's will But, I do know this I wish you enough pain to encourage you to change Enough love to become more aware Enough silence to hear your truth Enough stillness to feel the Presence Just enough I wish

George G
