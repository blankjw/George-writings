THE POWER OF WORDS

By George Gondron

THE POWER OF WORDS Words have the power to unite but also to divide Maybe if we didn’t have the ability to speak The world, all humanity, would be a better place Words are nothing more than symbols linked together To express certain thoughts, feelings, and ideas Some beneficial and helpful, others controlling and destructive Words have the power to deceive and manipulate But behavior on the other hand What you see is what you get The three words, ‘I love you’, need never be said For if it’s real, it’s shown in behavior, not words We trust words far too much, from every source And the more we trust only the words The more we’re likely to be conditioned, controlled, and Propagandized ‘You gonna believe me, or them ole lying eyes?’ There is a famous quote from Edgar Allan Poe ‘Believe nothing you hear, and only one half that you see.’ The easy path to new knowledge is to be ‘spoon fed’ Just click on your favorite media source The one that fits your character and personality And just sit back and drink the ‘Kool Aid’ The same with your preferred religion Requires no critical thinking, no doubting, no questions Just be a sponge and soak it all up But guess what, that’s not learning, that’s conditioning That’s allowing some external source to subtlety Form who you are without any personal discernment Without any critical thinking or reflection If we lost the sense of hearing, the other four senses Are more felt experiences, more able to be trusted Choose to spend a few minutes each day with a contemplative Practice – in silence, with stillness, in the presence of the Source Learn to be present to the essence of your own heart, soul, And spirit – they are your innate inner guides Automatically part and parcel of this gift called life Your aides in discernment and intuition – trust them

George G
