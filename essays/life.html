LIFE

By George Gondron

LIFE How would you define life? And what would life include, what would be its description? Would it just be humans, perhaps the animal kingdom? The insects, all flora and fauna? If life is described as that which has energy Then everything is alive, for science tell us That everything is in motion, even though it can't be seen Everything has a certain resonance, a vibration, a dynamism It is not just inert, dormant, inactive, and lifeless Everything we can describe is made up of atoms Each atom carries various configurations Of electrons, protons, and neutrons Spinning constantly around the nucleus of the atom If you were to remove all the empty space in the atoms That make up the Empire State building You would have an object no bigger than a grain of sand Which is another way of saying we are more nothing than something But life is contained in everything, energy is in everything The next big question becomes, do we value life? Do we value all that exists? Or do some things matter and others don't? Or some things sacred and others not? Do we reflect on the gratuity of life, all life, its future promise? It's obvious some don't, some consider life to not be sacred As evidenced through their behavior Taking another's life easily, be it a man, woman, or child Pillaging and poisoning the earth, polluting the air and water Caring little or nothing about the future But caring about the bottom line, their own selfish desires All life doesn't matter, unless it's theirs There is a cancer that pervades the world If we don't see it, we are blind If we don't hear it, we are deaf For most, all of life is not sacred They do not grasp the meaning of original blessing The reality is, life is not about us We are about life, life depends on us

George G
