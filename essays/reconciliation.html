RECONCILIATION

By George Gondron

RECONCILIATION A lack of understanding that life and death are inseparable Is the realization that as we approach death, the end of our journey If we are fortunate, we begin to reflect on unreconciled Differences we are still clinging to involving past and current Relationships – or, if you prefer, items of unfinished business Put another way, our inability to let go of that which has, Through our own choices, been our own fault, no one else’s We truly are our own worst enemy Building our self made prisons to which we hold the key There is a saying that those who fight and scream against death Do so for one reason, they realized too late, they’ve never lived One of the constants in life, there are no ‘do-overs’ Do it now, fix it now, change now, tomorrow may be too late Admittedly, it is a hard task to be reconciled within one’s self But it is the necessary journey of individuation, of self-actualization Of becoming fully human, and fully alive And few there are that make the journey We waste untold amounts of time and energy Attempting to change others, but not ourselves, which is The only person we can change, we live by ‘if only’ We cannot know what we do not know, but once we know We then become responsible for the necessary changes in our life No one else – blaming and pointing fingers no longer matters In the end he, she, it, or they are not responsible for my choices I alone must take ownership for my life, there’s no gun to my head At the time, whatever my choices, may have seemed correct I have the power to change, I can change, I must change If I am to become who I was meant to be My creativity, my gifts, my uniqueness, my connections Too often we become trapped in the negativity of our own culture Human producers and consumers, a means to another’s end We lack balance, inner and outer Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for well-being A few minutes each day be silent, be still, embrace Presence Your image of Universal Consciousness, of Isness, of God Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, they hold your true self

George G
