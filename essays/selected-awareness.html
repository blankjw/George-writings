SELECTED AWARENESS

By George Gondron

SELECTIVE AWARENESS Ah, don't read any further, you may be disappointed You may find discomfort in a few little words To live in the richest nation in the world To live in 'the home of the brave, land of the free' Tell me, what is wealth? What is bravery? What is freedom? Do they also foster and nourish a sense of responsibility A sense of compassion and caring for all If so, help me understand how poverty grows Give me reasons for increased violence Help me grasp why enough is never enough Could it be that I'd rather live in my own little 'bubble' My safe and secure 'status quo' while 'Rome burns' It seems I chose to be aware of only that which directly effects me Only that which disturbs my security and comfort Only that which threatens my little 'kingdom' It's easier to cross over to the other side of the road Avoid becoming involved, keep my distance I think I remember reading once, 'there are none so blind as those who will not see, none so deaf as those who will not hear'. A good definition of selective awareness Comfort in the moment, disappointment in the end The finger of selective awareness points only in one direction It points to me

George G
