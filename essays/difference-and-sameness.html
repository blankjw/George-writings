DIFFERENCE AND SAMENESS

By George Gondron

DIFFERENCE AND SAMENESS To reject out of hand that which is assumed to be different Is akin to refusing to unwrap a gift freely given For in truth, everything in life is a gift Offered for our understanding, our growth, our humanity Learning through personal experience is the only genuine Way of preventing bias, prejudice, bigotry, and unfounded Judgements – the only way to truly know beyond mere belief To refuse to understand difference is to embrace fear It is to assume without knowing Ask yourself this question – what are the underlying reasons For eliminating historical truths from our educational Curriculums – the refusal to teach our whole history and not Just parts of our history, especially our ‘darker’ history A leader who sees sameness before difference is a uniter One who does believe in freedom, equality, and opportunity For all – recognizing difference as strength, not a weakness One who does not attempt to coverup our past discretions But sees them as events from which we can learn and Become better human beings, a better democracy for all A leader who sees difference before sameness is a divider Using difference to create fear and separation Us against them, and they do this for personal gain only Their hyperbole and ‘gaslighting’ has only one goal To defeat, eliminate, or ostracize that which they deem different Kindness, empathy, mercy, compassion, and love are not in Their repertoire, only negativity, blame, and condemnation Abraham Lincoln once said, ‘A house divided against itself cannot stand’ – At this point in our history we are divided The contrasts for our future could not be more pronounced Our choices could not be more significant We must use critical thinking, educate ourselves, research To be led by our emotions and not by our intellect is a very Dangerous path to follow, it’s a dead end Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Discern, listen to your heart, soul, and spirit Awaken your inner core, your internal references They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
