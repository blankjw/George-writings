CONTEXT DOES MATTER

By George Gondron

CONTEXT DOES MATTER All learning happens in a context Whether that’s formal educational learning in a classroom Like the ‘three R’s’, or experiential hands-on learning The mechanic, the plumber, the electrician, all craftsmen We limit our abilities to learn when we limit our context Part of the change process, if we even recognize our need to change Is to either create or embrace a new context That is geared toward aiding or enhancing the change we seek And believe me, we all need to change, how do I know? Our first clue is the ego’s resistance to change The ego prefers the familiar to the strange Even when that familiar is painful or unpleasant I’m sure you know the feeling We may not be right, but we’re never wrong OK, back to context If we sincerely seek change, if we drop our defenses And allow ourselves to be vulnerable and take a risk That’s how change happens you know We must also find the context for that change to happen Maybe we decide to seek professional help, a good start But once a week is not the magic elixir that will bring change It’s what we do between those weekly visits that matters Let’s say you are interested in exploring your own spirituality Attending an occasional church service or reading a few Bible verses is not the answer, not even the icing on the cake There is a saying, anything worth having requires concerted effort In other words, you have to put in the work, the commitment To experience, to know, your own spiritual dimension Your own relationship with the Mystery of creation, your God Requires a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence One of the pillars of all belief systems, religious or otherwise Which includes Buddhism is the necessity of quiet reflective time A time to empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts To focus on the breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
