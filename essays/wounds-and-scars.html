WOUNDS AND SCARS

By George Gondron

WOUNDS AND SCARS If you're reading this, you've been wounded We all have our scars Most heal, some never do Some are received through the actions of another Others are self-inflicted Most, the result of a shared dispute The external scars are the obvious But, the hidden ones inside are the deepest The physical pain is eventually forgotten But the emotional pain may never leave No one really wins in the end Regardless of perceived rightness or wrongness Resulting always in some form of loss Not just for one, but both, and for all Whatever the source of the wounds and the scars Something is lost in what once was The question becomes, can it be mended? It can but rarely ever is At best it becomes a shared indifference A quiet tolerance tainted with mistrust True mending requires a recognition A recognition by each and all concerned For openness in self-examination Willingness to be self-critical Ownership for my own dysfunction My contribution to the wounds and the scars And if necessary, forgiveness, by each, by all If we learn in the process, we become more aware We become more human We become more We become, if we learn
