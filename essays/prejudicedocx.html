PREJUDICE

By George Gondron

PREJUDICE Who among us is without prejudice? - none Are there any without bias? - no It is not a matter of denying our intolerance Refusing to see our preconceptions and unfairness It is a matter of discovering what they are The small ways in which we prejudge The insignificant partiality or tunnel vision view The refusal to accept other possibilities And then there's the subtle discretion By those who call themselves Christians Please, I don't mean all Christians It happens in all religions But those who believe God only loves them A very comfortable position, for those It gives perfect justification for prejudice and bias What a small and petty God that must be To create and tolerate something unlovable It's the human small-mindedness that's the issue Not the Creator of this universe It's easy to become aware of our obvious prejudice It's more difficult for the obscure Change can only happen with acceptance first And then determined effort, tireless endeavor To recognize and erase our prejudice is, To heal ourselves, heal our world It is to see our connections, our basic sameness The gifts that we are, one to another Acknowledge, search, change your prejudice To do so is our liberation To do so is our life To do so is Love

George G
