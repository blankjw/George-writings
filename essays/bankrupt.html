ESSAY

BANKRUPT

By George Gondron

BANKRUPT Lacking in some quality, destitute - bankrupt Most consider bankruptcy in financial terms But there are many ways to become bankrupt As a nation And as espoused by many, a Christian nation How are we bankrupt? At least according to 'Christian' values and precepts I can think of two; morally and spiritually There's no need for anyone that's not a Christian To assess or infer how a Christian follows Or does not follow their own beliefs Their actions reveal how they follow what they believe And their words are their own judge and jury As they express their sympathy, their sorrow for others Sympathy doesn't put food on the table of a hungry child Sympathy doesn't provide a living wage Sympathy doesn't provide health care Sympathy doesn't provide for higher or fair education Sympathy doesn't provide equal pay for equal work In fact, as the saying goes, one finds sympathy Between 'shit and syphilis' in the dictionary Gandhi once said, 'I love your Christ, but your Christians Are nothing like your Christ' And then there's morality The posture of 'righteous indignation, finger-pointing By those who hold hidden secrets themselves Refusing to see their own 'shadow' The rhetoric 'We the people' is a lie The proclamation of 'family values' is a lie The narrative of 'justice for all' is a lie Unless of course, you're one of the fortunate few Poverty is a creation, it's not a given It happens for one reason only, selfishness Yes, bankruptcy is much greater than just financial It is the compromise of integrity, ethics, and truth as well And not until we recognize our own participation Our own bankruptcy, will there be significant change

George G
