IF YOU LOVE ME

By George Gondron

IF YOU LOVE ME The years grow shorter Time seems to move swifter The seasons become blurred, running together My bones grow weary, more aches than before But, the mind is still clear Clear enough to spend a part of each day, dreaming A part of each day just sitting, being still, in the silence With the One who gives me life and the ones that I love Yet holding the pain of the world within my heart In touch with the burning in my spirit for the many who suffer Suffer needlessly from hands which are no different than mine Still willing to do what the body no longer can Hoping my few words will touch some wounded soul And give them pause to not lose faith If you love me, remember how important your are How special you are to this world, how very special How much you are, and will always be loved Remember connections are never, ever lost Only made different, lasting forever And when my friend death comes to call To begin a new journey Start a fresh chapter and write a new story If you love me, don't cry Celebrate my living as well as my dying As I dwell in your heart until we next meet I live on in your dreams I am there in your laughter, as well as your tears I will be in your pain, in the moments of darkness Holding you close and giving you strength If you love me Dig my grave, carry my coffin, sing some songs Laughing out loud Dance, don't mourn, for my life has been full Don't weep at the grave, it's not a time for sadness If you love me, love yourself, love the One you call God Love others, with all your heart, your soul, and your strength For after all, there is only Love

George G
