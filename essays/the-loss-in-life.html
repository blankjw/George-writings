THE LOSS IN LIFE

By George Gondron

THE LOSS IN LIFE Immersed in the fabric of life is unimaginable ecstasy, undeniable agony A leave-taking from our origins, the beginning of loss Life, not a vacuum if experienced to the full Not just a symphony, but also a cacophony The pain of a single parent child, of the orphan The loneliness of the widow or widower The weeping over the death of 'Lazarus' Nailed to the crossroads of experience, the struggle with opposing forces There is loss in life, beginning with birth How we assimilate the losses brings despair for some, hope for others The strength we need is part of our essence It is found in our relationships It dwells in our souls Being present to who we are, not who we pretend to be Listening to the whispers of our heart, not the promises of the mind We detect, become aware of the nature of loss We bring to light the lesson to be learned Be it large or small, unbearable or tolerable We evolve, we overcome, we survive We have found our inner Guide We cling to our Beginnings We find Solace within We become We

George G
