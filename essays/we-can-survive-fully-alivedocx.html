WE CAN SURVIVE, FULLY ALIVE

By George Gondron

WE CAN SURVIVE, FULLY ALIVE Broken, empty, helpless, lost, and desperate Clinging, clutching, grasping for the last thread of hope You, I, all of us, we've been there We know what it's like, how it feels And we've survived, one way or another Whether it's in a broken relationship A dead-end job, a financial burden Some are able to live in that pain through distraction More than a few, perhaps most But in so doing it's not life at all, it's merely existence A pretend life, a life of self-deception, an empty life Those painful experiences can be a blessing in disguise They are the events that scream to us, 'wake up' But the ego holds on to the anchor that pulls us deeper At the risk of death, a living death So survival becomes a matter of 'how' The how becomes a matter of choice, of 'letting go' There's no need to ask, 'let go of what?' For we already know, that part has become very familiar Without being honest with ourselves first, always We will never let go We will continue to allow the ego to have all the power And not listen to our hearts, wherein the truth lies The 'letting go' will require risk, vulnerability It will require a re-examination of our life An honesty that in the past has been missing Well, not really missing, just ignored With repeated internal self-talk of blaming everyone Blaming everything, except of course ourselves The success of a life lived to the full, a fully human life Is always about 'letting go' We fool ourselves into believing our attachments are not a problem But they are, whether things, people, thoughts or feelings What we're attached to always has the power We discover life by letting go, by letting be, by listening to our heart Learning to live with the Giver of life and the Love we contain In learning this, we will more than survive, we will be fully alive

George G
