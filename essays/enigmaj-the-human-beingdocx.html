ENIGMA^J THE HUMAN BEING

By George Gondron

ENIGMA, THE HUMAN BEING Something that baffles understanding, fraught with contradictions This species we call the human being, homo sapiens Capable of the most selfless as well as the most selfish acts More destructive than any other species, and more gratuitous Willing to sacrifice one’s life for the good of the community As well, willing to kill others for the protection of the community So, what is it that separates selfishness from selflessness? It resides in the gift we call consciousness, in free will, in choice The mind, the ego, the desire to have as one’s own opposed to giving So that others may have as well, regardless their station in life The true definition of love is sacrifice, doing for others Even to one’s own detriment, a rare quality indeed in this culture The human being that seeks positions of power and control That seeks wealth and material goods over meaningful relationships That demands fealty and loyalty above mercy, compassion and justice By definition is a selfish person We all have that capability and without exception we all at one time Or another, have been selfish, put ourselves before another If you’ve been fortunate enough to have been transformed by guilt You are lucky, more bury their guilt and carry on or never feel The guilt at all, we witness it today if we are not totally blind It has a common refrain, ‘don’t do as I do, do as I say do’ And no one has the courage to proclaim, ‘the emperor has no clothes’ Truth never needs defending, but the lie always does And our normal narrative today is very defensive Which is the result of not wanting to know the truth To live in our own little ‘bubble’, and embrace our fears endlessly Yes, we are a mystery, a conundrum, our own worst enemy We it seems have lost the ability to be critical thinkers To discern, reflect, question, and doubt, we just accept We have but one task, to ceaselessly change ourselves, become better Adopt a meditative/contemplative daily practice awaken your insides Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on a short mantra, breathe in wellness to yourself, breathe Out wellness to others, the will mind wander, rinse and repeat Listen to, the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
