THE SPAN OF A LIFETIME

By George Gondron

THE SPAN OF A LIFETIME How does one recount the span of a lifetime? What to remember, what to forget The seemingly important events for some Apparently insignificant to others What is of value on the scales of importance Is there some measurement which we can employ That will determine definitively all the differences? I think not To begin to approach the breadth of being human A whole, healthy, inclusive human One must include and never forget the failures Not just the successes The pain, not just the joy The broken relationships, not just the faithful We disparage what is omitted, good or bad And what we omit makes us less than what we are Our scars and our wounds, of the body and the soul All of them matter They are as much a part of our story, our history As the moments and memories we fondly recall Nothing can be excluded, left out, or discarded Every experience large, small, or inconsequential Cannot be rejected, eliminated, nor ruled out They all bring us to this very moment This present breath with each and every heartbeat Every human life we encountered along the journey Had meaning, touched us in some particular way And those we have yet to meet, will as well Take time to reflect, to remember and recall The span of your lifetime, whether long or still short It's never too late for unfulfilled dreams Never too late to reach out and include Include what may have once been rejected And when the last great experience of death comes to call Let us be glad for the span of our lifetime Celebrate and rejoice for another transition now awaits A new beginning that will last forever

George G
