ESSAY

MEANING

By George Gondron

MEANING Aware or unaware we make meaning of thousands of experiences Each and every day, even when we sleep Through our senses, taste, touch, smell, hear, and see Through our thoughts and feelings We cannot, not make meaning, but here’s the problem/problems In most cases we either are unaware or we assume We may cling to an old meaning for a present experience He/she were rude to me the last time we met, I avoid them Could it be their rudeness had nothing to do with you? If we’re not aware, we assume much more than we know In fact, most of what we see and hear on social media Which we may believe if we trust the source, another issue entirely Could be classified as an assumption for we lack the experience Of really knowing the fact or fiction of their presentation Since after all, surely they’re not biased or prejudiced Again, we make our own meaning aware or unaware Interestingly enough, we gravitate toward emotional content Before factual content, especially if it’s negative versus positive Perhaps that’s because we’d rather put others down As opposed to lifting them up, just a guess of course Blame is a game we all play, helps us avoid our responsibilities Helps us to deny our own power to critically think, reflect, question, Doubt, and do our own research, our own ‘fact checking’ Are we so ignorant that even the obvious lies we swallow whole? When we continually ignore the obvious, we eventually suffer the Consequences, and then it’s too late To become more aware, to search for meaning, is to be present In the now, not resenting the past or fearing the future, be here now To lack awareness, to make assumptions, is to have a closed mind We don’t have to agree, but we do need open unbiased discussions Which currently seems to be the exception and not the rule Seek to know the truth, avoid the opinions, it is possible Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for balance in your life Connect inner and outer, spend a few minutes each day in Silence, stillness, with Presence, Isness, Mystery, your name for God Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, your true self

George G
