II WAS YOUNG AND STUPID ONVE

By George Gondron

I WAS YOUNG AND STUPID ONCE Not sure I’m that much smarter now, maybe a little I do know for certain that what matters now in my eighth decade Is vastly different than my early years, time is a teacher One of the realities about being young, when we’re wrong We don’t think we’re wrong, but just maybe if we are able to Gain some new awareness we will begin to recognize when we Are wrong and cease to lie to ourselfves I’ve disappointed a few people I my life and been betrayed by A few as well, but hopefully I learned some valuable lessons I’ve learned emotional pain is a gift, a teacher It teaches me about myself and my defenses against my own Weaknesses like blame and denial to mention just a few It has taught me that most of my pain was the result of desire or Attachment, an unwillingness to ‘let go’ but continue to cling For what we are unwilling to ‘let go of’ has a way of controlling us I’ve learned that bias, prejudice, exclusion and discrimination Are like poisons we drink that harm ourselves and others I’ve learned that what’s important in life are people, not things But that’s not the message of our culture, our society It’s more along the lines of ‘I’ve got mine; you get yours’ I’ve learned that belief is not enough, but we can know And that experiential knowing means faith is never blind I’ve learned that the individual is never greater than the community That we are all connected regardless of race, color, creed, or gender I’ve learned that the enemy is a closed mind unwilling to learn A mind that is stirred by emotions and not by truth I’ve learned that increased awareness and consciousness Lead to greater clarity and perception I’ve learned that for every door that closes there is another To be opened, there is no end for our potential to learn And most of all, I’ve learned that love is much more than a feeling Love is an action, a sacrifice, to do for others who are in need, Even an unspoken perceived need, what is necessary in their lives We always have a choice to be selfless or selfish, choose selfless Perfection does not exist, but to strive to become better does Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Listen to your better angels, heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
