IF YOU BECOME AWARE

By George Gondron

IF YOU BECOME AWARE There are moments in our lives, several in fact When, for lack of a better explanation Our ego is shaken just enough to allow a new awareness To enter our consciousness uninvited but much-needed Unfortunately, most of us will not stop long enough To pay attention or reflect on the experience As if something is attempting to get our attention We simply continue our frenzied and distracted lives as usual Never giving it a second thought These moments are usually connected to times of Deep emotional pain, or a feeling of unexplained loneliness An unexplained yearning and emptiness deep within Or if we’re lucky, a moment of feeling unconditional love St. John of the Cross writes about these types of experiences In The Dark Night of The Soul, a powerful spiritual writing Discussed and written about by many spiritual teachers over The centuries and still studied today As individuals, to have a numinous experience, a breakthrough Is an experience that is too self-evident to be denied And as well, too deep and profound to be described Moments that can only be known through experience And those experiences are for each of us to know When and if we’re open, when we seek a connection to, our soul In our culture, we avoid two things that aid in cracking The shell of our egoic false self, our illusory self Those are silence and time spent alone The name of the game in our culture is distraction To fill every waking moment with thoughts, feelings, and actions Those are only barriers and roadblocks to greater awareness The Western mind resists change, refuses to let go, to become more Scientific research reveals the importance and positive effects Of some form of meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Time set aside to empty the mind, focus on the breath Freeing ourselves of incessant and unnecessary repetitive thoughts That we might hear the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit Discover our true self, our inner truth, they patiently await

George G
