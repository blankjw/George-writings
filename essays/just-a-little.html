JUST A LITTLE

By George Gondron

JUST A LITTLE If we only realized Just how little it takes To lift the spirits of another We would become more and more aware Of just how important that is Of just how much we are all connected And what a better place the world would become Yet because of our own individual issues Caught up in our own dilemmas, mostly emotional We don’t realize, we don’t even imagine I have a small request Each morning before you brush your teeth Before coffee or your drink of preference Smile at yourself in the mirror Let that smile be a gift to others Family, friends, co-workers, strangers It will lift the spirits of another A nod of the head, a touch on the shoulder Squeezing a hand, and if allowed a great hug We don’t realize the importance of touch It means we exist, we matter, we are alive We are human beings and not just objects We are subjects, we have a history and a story And those stories are important We are more alike than unalike Our hopes, dreams, wishes, and desires Are very similar A little kindness A small amount of compassion A few minutes of listening Goes a very long way Please, don’t use the excuse I don’t have time You do, maybe you don’t manage your time Good morning, have a good day, good afternoon And if you know the other person An ‘I love you’ could make their day Be a gift to others as well as yourself – we need it

George G
