RELATIONSHIP

By George Gondron

RELATIONSHIP What is the purpose of a life, if not for relationship There can be no other possible reason One to another, one to many, one to all Relationship That which brings forth life, sustains life, continues life At times, the most fragile of bonds In moments, the weakest of bonds At its best, the strongest of bonds Some, relationships that are painful, hurtful, demanding More ownership than partnership More an object, than a subject Something to be survived, to be endured Then there are those that are little more than mere tolerance Existing in proximity only, with no real substance Based only on need and not one of choice Held together out of guilt alone, not out of caring Continued merely out of familiarity, yet are lifeless The path of relationship is not an easy one There are many secrets to those that eventually flourish That blossom into the strongest of ties, despite the differences Able to withstand the worst of times Capable of healing the wounds arising from our humanness Gradually learning that most hurts are not intentional Committed from lack of awareness, lack of consciousness Relegated to our individual defenses, separate weaknesses The biggest secret to relational success, is really very simple It's called self-understanding, individuation, personal growth By each person, for themselves and for their partner We are each responsible in our relationships Responsible individually for our personal changes, our process Responsible to each other to support those changes For love to survive, to thrive, to empower It must begin with self first, becoming a joint effort One to the other, non-clinging, not grasping Healthy relationships are not pain free, never will be They survive through self-knowledge and committed love Just as our lives exist, through Unconditional Love

George G
