WHO DO WE CHALLENGE

By George Gondron

WHO DO WE CHALLENGE? To challenge is not to accuse, they are very different Challenge is not blaming, it is more of a question Not of why, but rather of how Especially as it relates to ourselves as individuals How do we, you and I, challenge ourselves? Particularly in those areas, we take for granted Our values, our ethics, our core beliefs, our inclusiveness If I were to ask, how are you different today In your thoughts, feelings, and actions than you were One, two, or three decades ago especially toward others And you cannot readily answer with a substantive reply Have you really changed? The real challenge in our lives is how do we challenge ourselves Not others, be it he, she, or they, only ourselves For honestly, the power to change others is an illusion We can only choose to change ourselves, that’s what’s needed For when we change, everything around us changes as well Especially our relationships, for the better There is an old saying, ‘Physician heal thyself’ But what we attempt to do, consistently, is change others But not ourselves, it does not and will never work Our path to wholeness, to peace, to understanding, and joy Is, and will always be an inside job, we alone are responsible So here’s the challenge for me and for you – ready? We must continually challenge ourselves Question our thoughts, feelings, and actions Be reflective, respond versus react, and be an active listener What we project onto others, see in ourselves as well As the saying goes, ‘We must carry our own water’ Or in psychological terms see our own shadow A practical way to maintain a necessary balance in our lives Is to choose a meditative/contemplative daily practice Each day, for a few minutes, be silent, be still, embrace Isness The voice that says, ‘I am who am’, Universal Consciousness Breathe in, breathe out a short mantra, or focus on your breath Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, your truth, they wait

George G
