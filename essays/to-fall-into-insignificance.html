TO FALL INTO INSIGNIFICANCE

By George Gondron

8TO FALL INTO INSIGNIFICANCE Without some connection to Mystery, to Isness We fall into the trap of measuring our value based on Some arbitrary nonsense determined by ‘others’ And eventually, rather than pursuing our own creativity And potential, we fall into the grip of the three C’s Comparing, contrasting, and competing with others Consciously or unconsciously while neglecting our own Uniqueness, our own individuality, our own essence We fall into insignificance becoming part of the crowd Every human life has value, but when that value is Prescribed by ego as opposed to heart, difference becomes The measure by which people are defined rather than Sameness – when in fact we are more alike than unalike As the fox tells the Little Prince, ‘It is only with the heart That one sees rightly’ When the influence of social media is objectively studied It becomes obvious that it’s all about the ‘externals’ Selling us something that will improve our personal dilemma Promoting ideas, people, and things that will solve the Problems in our individual lives Economically, politically, religiously, financially, etc., etc. What about relationally, psychologically, or spiritually? Social media is more about manipulation and indoctrination Than it is about objective education, significant learning How about replacing the three C’s mentioned earlier with Three others – care, compassion, and cooperation A significant life is a balanced life – inner and outer Mind, body, heart, soul, and spirit, all together not separate The whole is always greater than the sum of its parts A few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Attains balance, equanimity, connectedness, and wholeness Listen to your inner guides, they hold your true self They have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
