DOES TRUTH STILL MATTER

By George Gondron

DOES TRUTH STILL MATTER? It seems that the lie has become the norm But it's called the truth And yet, when the lie is revealed There are those that still trust the liar Are we just fools motivated by the 'slick tongues' The 'fast talkers', the 'used car salesmen' Whose words when analyzed make no sense at all Is it our need to voice our own prejudice Our own 'righteousness' but in a secondary manner Through the voice of a propagandist or politician Through the mind of a person who cannot answer Answer a simple question that requires a 'yes' or a 'no' Unwilling to acknowledge our own 'shadow' Silently agreeing with the venom spewed from another Believing an obvious lie is very telling For it reveals more about us than it does the liar It speaks to where our false truth lies, not the liar's We carry the responsibility of whatever the results Of trusting an obvious lie Has truth 'died' and is it buried forever? Is there none left to speak truth to power, to deceit Are do we sit silently by, for to do otherwise Would mean being irresponsible for our own humanity For that which is an innate gift of life Compassion, kindness, love, peace, and justice Not just for ourselves, but for all human beings Maybe we have been so conditioned by the lies That the truth really does no longer matter Maybe the lie is too convenient, too comfortable Perhaps we need the lie to hide our own shortcomings Our own self-effacing nature For truth to survive we need listen to our hearts Take heed of our souls and spirits If we listen, listen in the silence and stillness We can hear them, away from the noise and distractions Don't let truth die Don't let the liars win, or we may all die

George G
