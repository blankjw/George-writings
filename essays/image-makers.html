IMAGE MAKERS

By George Gondron

IMAGE MAKERS What we are attracted to, the focus of our interest Whether positive or negative Arises from our own thoughts, feelings, and actions We create the images that dwell in the recesses of our minds They become our familiar Our experiences color our perceptions which are seldom Images of objective reality, they remain subjective bias And the answers we proclaim we seek hide behind the curtain Of our own illusions, our own prejudices The task of becoming fully human Necessarily must include the integration of our own shadow Which for the dualistic mind is a Sisyphean task The path of wholeness can never be accomplished With an either/or mentality, black-and-white thinking It requires a both/and approach to our life’s journey It is inclusive versus exclusive, open versus closed A life forgiving of its own foibles as well as those of others The images we cling to reinforce our polarized thinking As well, that same thinking maintains those dysfunctional images Without critical thinking and deep self=reflection Our journey remains focused on those created images That effect our daily lives and current relationships And unfortunately without the necessary connections Between mind, heart, soul, and spirit Those effects are almost always negative in nature There is no magic pill or secret elixir that bypasses The individuation process of becoming fully human There is only the desire to become more aware and conscious We have a tendency to insulate ourselves from that which Could lead us to positive change by choosing distractions Rather than realizing our angst and discomfort are friends A meditative/contemplative daily practice is a potential solution Spend a few minutes each day in silence, in stillness, with Presence Presence being your image of God, of Isness, Universal Consciousness Empty your mind of its incessant thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your truth, your healing

George G
