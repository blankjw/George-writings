OUR MANY CHOICES

By George Gondron

OUR MANY CHOICES One of our most difficult lessons to learn as we become more aware and conscious: we choose what we think, feel, and do or do not do No one is holding a gun to our heads forcing our choices The sooner we learn we are responsible for our decisions the healthier we become Blame, finger pointing, accusations, incriminations, will no longer work when we are faced with the reality of our own choices It’s hard to escape what we do or do not do as it relates to our choices, even though we may try It’s much easier to deny what we think and feel, especially if we struggle to even know ourselves If we happen to be fortunate enough to consciously choose a path of personal growth, intellectually, emotionally, and spiritually we will be required to add new contexts to our lives Many choices we make will require a committed internship, a training program, ‘OJT’ on the job training, all new contexts Once our patterns of thinking, feeling, and doing or not doing become our ‘familiars’, they are unlikely to change substantially We definitely are creatures of habit, even painful habits Ironically, the most difficult path in the beginning to embrace is the spiritual path, its requirements are so few Most people in our culture choose religion over spirituality, even If it’s just going through the motions A true spirituality necessarily includes silence, stillness, and Presence A theme in all belief systems The Western mind abhors silence and stillness and primarily only believes in an intellectual God not a relational God, sadly Please adopt a meditative/contemplative practiced, a new context Begin with just a few minutes of silence, stillness, and Presence Eventually increase 20 t0 30 minutes Focus on your breath or a short mantra, I.e., breathe in ‘I love you’ from God, breathe out ‘I love you’ to God Your mind will wander, simply rinse and repeat, consistently Listen for the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit They hold your ‘true self’, they have your answers, they wait This brings needed balance to your unique and sacred journey

George G
