OUR WEAKNESS, UNABLE TO LET GO

By George Gondron

OUR WEAKNESS, UNABLE TO LET GO It is the torment of the human mind The inability to let go And most unfortunately, unable to let go Of that which has never been experienced But only imagined and through the imagination Creating that which can’t be released Seemingly forever Thoughts, feelings, beliefs, and even actions Beliefs in particular are held as if by proxy With no personal experience at all to support the belief There are many reasons we have such a difficult time Letting go Especially of consistent patterns and habits But the most relentless reason is fear Afraid of what may happen if we let go Other words are used as excuses Doubt, anxiety, uncertainty, mistrust, disbelief All fall under the influence of fear What becomes our familiar Even though it’s dysfunctional Is difficult to let go of It’s very understandable I can relate to the familiar Even those familiars that are painful But I can’t relate to what may happen if I let go It’s always the unknown That becomes our nemesis And it’s so sad for change is the one inevitable Whether we resist or welcome Run from or embrace, hide or welcome Change will happen, sooner or later This is the grace of letting go Especially of those things that hold us back Once we do, the resultant benefits become enormous We then are much less likely to resist further changes And much more likely to receive with excitement New possibilities, be the change you seek

George G
