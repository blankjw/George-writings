EXPERIENCE ONLY

By George Gondron

EXPERIENCE ONLY The most important singular events of our life Can only be known through direct experience They can’t be known academically or intellectually In the psychology of Carl Jung, they are numinous experiences The ones that are so self-evident, they can’t be denied So deep and profound, they can’t be explained Stimulated through the individuation process Or experiences described in the poem ‘Dark Night of the Soul’ By John of the Cross, experiences of spiritual transformation Or the words of the human Jesus, we must die unto self There are many paths that can lead to an inner transformation All of which are of a spiritual nature, an inner awakening Vision quests, silent retreats, contemplative practices The practice of Buddhism, which is not a religion But a study of the mind, with a strong emphasis on meditation Any practice that gets us out of our egos, our minds As Virginia Satir’s ‘family reconstruction’ or ‘parts party’ Acting out through role-play the four communication stances of Blame, placating, super reasonableness, and irrelevancy The eight universal aspects of all human beings Intellectual, physical, contextual, sensual, spiritual, nutritional, Interactional, and emotional That which gets us out of our analytical minds Has the potential to provide an awakening to our inner core The failure of all organized religions is the same Regardless of the belief system or philosophy Their focus is on the externals Rites, rituals, dogma, doctrine, smells, and bells Little or nothing about spiritual transformation Or any form of contemplative practice We are built for relationship, we are connected It is only our minds that separate us Pay attention to your inner yearnings, your intuition Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, and With Presence – your image of a personal creator God Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your inner truth, we are all stardust

George G
