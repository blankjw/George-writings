CURIOSITY

By George Gondron

CURIOSITY Curiosity is a mainstay when it comes to new learning Without it, we simply take in through our five senses What is presented to us and either accept or reject the premise With little doubt or second thought especially If it appeals to or is denied by an already-formed ego Set in its ways willing to be spoon-fed without any effort Or forethought to establish the truth of what is being received Ignoring the advice of ‘trust but verify’ It seems far too many of us are willing to trust Without any verification whatsoever Curiosity is a major step toward verification, as well as doubt Remember, ‘don’t believe what you hear and half of what You see’ One of the paradoxes of the mind is that it’s willing to Accept without evidence what it hears and sees If it stirs the emotions, especially if those emotions Are connected to its desires and attachments If your desires and attachments supersede your relationships Regardless of your words, you are easily manipulated Defined as being closeminded and very polarized Always being right and never wrong in your thinking It’s always the ‘other’ or ‘others’ that have a problem To take the risk and ask ourselves, ‘what attracts me to The thoughts and feelings I cling to?’ What attracts you to the people who influence you The people you listen to, for psychologically To a greater or lesser degree, like attracts like The only person, we can change is ourselves, no one else Imagine the wasted time and energy trying to ‘fix’ others For, if it’s you in my mind that needs to be fixed It’s also me as well, so how do I ‘fix’ me Without an internal reference, an inner core you can’t For your true self, your truth, your healing Is an inside job Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Shut out the noise and incessant thoughts and be present To the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit – they wait

George G
