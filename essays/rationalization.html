RATIONALIZATION

By George Gondron | Source: RATIONALIZATION.docx

RATIONALIZATION Of all nature, humans are the only species that use rationalization Justification for why we do what we do Which honestly, if what we do is not harmful in any way To nature in all its many forms, especially other humans There is no need to rationalize or justify Of course, rationalization is just one of the many Psychological defense mechanisms we humans all use easily We all possess internally, a truth teller That part of us that knows ‘rightness and wrongness’ or situational They are not absolutes but depend upon circumstances Depend upon the situation, but rather than listen to our Truth teller, our internal guide, we much rather prefer to defend Any and all behavior that might be interpreted as unhealthy, Dysfunctional, selfish, irrational, untruthful, especially The ‘third party’ information we get from the internet The gift of free will, our ability to choose, has its dark side And currently in our society it is alive and doing very well If what we do in our individual lives is caring, sharing, including, Empathizing, and loving others there is no need to defend It all begins and ends with our own wellbeing Our own ability to critique ourselves To examine our own thoughts, feelings, and behavior first Before we pass judgment on others There is saying, ‘the truth will set you free, but first it will Make you miserable’, not many want to be free All the answers to our problems, real or imagined, can be Found within ourselves, but defending is so much easier And defending will never bring peace, only more of the same I humbly suggest this, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Bring balance into your life, discover the wonder of yourself Warts and all, the beauty and the beast, the shadow and the light Healthy self-love has no need to defend, ever Without belief in a creative Source, we have a hole in our soul Maybe we deny it, but it’s there, in the recesses of our mind A few minutes each day be silent, be still, empty your mind Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
