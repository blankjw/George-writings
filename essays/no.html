ESSAY

NO

By George Gondron

NO Two letters One syllable Enormous power Combined with one other word Also one syllable but just one letter, I I and no together carry imagined meanings If I can get what I want without asking I can avoid the dreaded no Either as the one asking, or the one being asked Somewhere, sometime, we learned what no means It means for so many, I'm flawed, not fit, undeserving When we hear 'no' to our question Especially a question of relationship, of being with There is a touch, a hint, of not being significant Of not really mattering We are caught in a personal reaction to 'no' When there are many other possibilities, not personal The inflated ego hears no, and discounts the other The narcissistic ego hears no, and puts down the other The infantile ego hears no, and becomes hysteric The egoless self hears no, and responds congruently The true-self, in response to a personal invitation When it is 'no thank you' Simply says, 'perhaps another time' We are not reduced by another's behavior When we know who we are And neither are they any less than their true-self It's okay to ask, and okay to hear 'no' It's okay to say, 'this doesn't fit for me'

George G
