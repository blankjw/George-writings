MISSING PIECES

By George Gondron

MISSING PIECES The experiences in life one will never know The missing pieces, the absent involvement Some positive and nurturing Others negative and rejecting One parent families, an only child Kindred spread far and wide No extended family Close knit communities Growing up, growing old together The defenseless children, bullied by peers Birth of a child, immeasurable joy Death of child, inconceivable grief The hungry, the homeless, the abused The intellects, the educated, the mind police The yea-sayers, the nay-sayers The poor, the wealthy, the empty, the full All , all have missing pieces We cannot say, 'I know your struggle' We cannot pretend we have the answers We do not know, and pretense is not an answer What is the common thread? What keeps us connected? What gives us cause for hope, for communion? We do, you know, we do hope We are not sufficient unto ourselves We rely not just on our beliefs, our images of God The Creator loves all, we rely on each other How can the Creator not love what the Creator formed? How can I not love what the Creator loves? We are pieces of the puzzle Our missing pieces are filled by others In the end, there are no missing pieces In the end, we are part of the whole In the end, we are just beginning

George G
