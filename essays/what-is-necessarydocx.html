WHAT IS NECESSARY

By George Gondron

WHAT IS NECESSARY? How is it possible to believe in a creative source, a God Created by a male ego? A God that requires we jump through hoops to be worthy We follow the Ten Commandments, all the Levitical laws Subscribe to The Doctrine of Faith, attend RCIA classes, Know the Baltimore Catechism, be baptized and confirmed Adhere to the doctrine and dogma of our chosen belief system These male ego lists are endless, and renewable When in fact God is not a belief God, like love , is an experience And while we’re so busy following beliefs, we miss the experience Let’s take an honest look at one belief system, Christianity The man Jesus, who was not a Chrisitan, hung on a cross One hanging next to him recognized he was innocent Recognized his own personal guilt but risked asking Jesus This question, ‘when you enter your kingdom, remember me’ There is no evidence Jesus asked him anything in return But simply said, ‘you will be with me in Paradise this day’ He didn’t ask anything about his beliefs or the rules he did Or did not follow, and then this man Jesus Toward the end of his life made this statement regarding Rules, laws, and beliefs He said, ‘only two things are necessary, love the Lord your God With all your heart, soul, mind, and strength, and your neighbor As yourself’, That’s it, period the end Would that we could do the same, regardless of our belief Indigenous peoples, Buddhists, and many agnostics Are more loving, more spiritual, more kind, more accepting Than the followers of all organized religion Our greatest enemy is and always has been ourselves, not God If you haven’t already, I wish you a loving experience of God Choose a meditative/contemplative practice, an experience Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, in the Presence of your image of a loving God, let belief fall away Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your true self, your inner core, they wait

George G
