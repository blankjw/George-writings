GOD, A BELIEF

By George Gondron

GOD, A BELIEF For most, at least those that believe in a creative Source Something that is responsible for this amazing universe Regardless of their religious belief That something is an objective reality Not a subjective relationship Interestingly enough, life is best defined as relationship And the most loving act we can do for those relationships In our own lives, is simply to be present And yet the very Source of our existence is seldom included In that equation, in the act of being present to For most, God is an intellectual concept Not an experiential reality, a living relationship Our ideas of God and our subsequent behaviors Are mostly cognitive about concepts and actions Meant to adhere to our particular religion’s Rules, regulations, rites, rituals, dogma, and doctrines There is very little offered to enhance a personal relationship With the one we call our God It all remains very dualistic and intellectual Focusing on meritocracy, membership, and sadly money Attentive to right/wrong, good/bad behavior That we might gain acceptance from our particular God With the gift of life, there is one most important gift That reveals we are loved unconditionally, always And that gift is free will It’s difficult to imagine loving anyone more Than to give them free will and the risk is They are not required to love back To understand the nature and power of free will do this Choose a daily meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes to empty your mind in order to be present In the silence, in the stillness, present to your image of God With all the distractions available to us seldom do we Develop, nurture, and maintain an internal reference An inner core represented by our heart, soul, and spirit That connection holds our true self, our inner truth That connection whispers to us the yearnings we hold within

George G
