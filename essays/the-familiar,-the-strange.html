THE FAMILIAR, THE STRANGE

By George Gondron | Source: THE FAMILIAR, THE STRANGE.docx

THE FAMILIAR, THE STRANGE What is it within human nature that prefers the familiar As opposed to the strange, all the while knowing The familiar is uncomfortable, perhaps even agonizing There can be only one reason, the power of attachment We cling to the known and avoid the unknown We dance with the 'devil' we know To avoid the one we don't know How can there be security in suffering and mistreatment Ask the abused child who wishes to return home Or the sheltered battered wife who misses her husband The employee stuck in a dead-end job Enduring verbal insults and sexual harassment The sexually abused child at the hands of a 'holy' person The sway of human connections, even the unhealthy ones The fear of insecurity, unable to predict tomorrow Conditioned and programmed, we avoid the strange We trust the 'system', knowing it's corrupt, sacred or secular Yes, the power of the unknown is much more frightening Than the fear of the known, the familiar And those in control remind us of that fear In order to maintain their control The more rigid the 'system', the more the fear Dependency stifles initiative, yields to the familiar Change has no chance Words of compliance are nothing more than 'quicksand' To maintain the status quo, the existing state of dysfunction The mind keeps us trapped, the heart sets us free If we but listen to our hearts It is the Spirit within, the small soft whisper In moments of solitude, of stillness, of calm Love is not found in the mind, in power or control Love is found in the heart, the soul, and the spirit Love is willing to risk the strange, trust the unknown When the familiar destroys and takes away life Destruction disguised as promise, death by another name Choose the silence, the stillness, the whisper Choose Life, choose Love, always choose

George G
