OUR HAPPINESS

By George Gondron

OUR HAPPINESS If you were to ask one hundred people what makes them happy More than likely, you’d get one hundred different answers And none would be correct For what we may believe makes us happy, once removed Leaves us with a feeling of anything but happiness True happiness is not found True happiness is uncaused, it is spontaneous It is when we are caught unawares, surprised, not expecting To search for happiness means we have some preconceived Idea or image of what makes us happy The flip side of that coin is disappointment when either That idea or image is not found and/or doesn’t last We lack genuine happiness for lack of spontaneity We have forgotten, or have never known what it means To be surprised by joy, to be amazed by the moment True happiness is more about being distracted, caught off guard An example – a young man’s girlfriend breaks up with him He is overwhelmed with sadness, anxious, and depressed His friends finally succeed in dragging him to a funny movie And before you know it, he’s laughing and having a good time He feels happiness with his friends The movie ends, they leave the theater, and he sees a young Couple holding hands and suddenly his happiness is gone Did the young couple cause that to happen, of course not When we are unwilling, not unable, to let go of whatever It is that allows us to cling to negativity, then we are As Pogo said, ‘I have met the enemy, and it is me’ It is OK to feel our feelings, they are teachers, not enemies They are there to be embraced, processed, and released And by not releasing pain appropriately we are only building A self-made prison to which we hold the key The key to our own freedom and uncaused future happiness Choose a path of balance, add a meditative/contemplative Practice to your daily life – silence, stillness, Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Become spontaneous, listen to your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
