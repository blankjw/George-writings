GUILT THROUGH SILENCE

By George Gondron

GUILT THROUGH SILENCE No action, no reflection Being voiceless In the obvious face of destruction In the face of dehumanization In the torture and murder of innocent human beings Is either a clear indication of not being able To process the atrocities witnessed Or a mindset of out of sight, out of mind These images need to be burned into our memories Held within our awareness and consciousness Less we forget, less we dull our compassion And become stoic in the face of reality We turn the channel because of fear An unspoken fear that these atrocities Have the potential to happen here, now And to some degree they have happened In our own dark history The history we don’t teach The history we deny The history that screams to be known Just one historical truth we keep hidden Eighty to one hundred million native Americans Were killed by us, documented There are many others we keep out of our History books Formulated, perpetrated, and acted out By rich and powerful men Who did so because of their own fear Fear of losing their own power and wealth It’s always about power, wealth, and control When there is no connection Between the ego, the mind, and the heart The result is always detrimental and damaging Always Search your own heart, speak out when abuse appears Don’t remain silent, don’t be voiceless It could happen here, to us, to our children

George G
