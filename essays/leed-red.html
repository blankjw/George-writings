By George Gondron | Source: leed red.docx

THE FAILURE OF ORGANIZED RELIGION Any system of religious belief that does not stress the transformation of human behavior into that of compassion, kindness, inclusion, and love for all beings, for all life, is nothing more than an organization of membership formulated through a system of ‘dos and don’ts’ supported financially in order to feel good about themselves I do realize that within each belief system there are exceptions, those whose hearts, minds and behavior reflect their transformation However, the vast majority give ‘lip service’ at best to their belief It’s not recognizable at all in their thinking, feeling, or action Simply follow the rites, rituals, dogma, and doctrine prescribed by the powers that be and all will be well It’s also interesting to note that those who challenge the status quo within the system are either ostracized, or worse put to death History is filled with examples of the same, Jesus the Christ for one And the church hierarchy to this day does what it can to exclude the female gender from positions of authority, what are they afraid of? Unfortunately, our human weakness is such that we focus first on our differences rather than our sameness, especially in religion When in truth, we are all more alike than unalike We all bleed red, we all have hopes and wishes I know that one of the universal aspects of being human is that we all have a ‘spiritual’ piece, not religious but spiritual That which connects us to the greater Whole and to all of creation But most never discover their spiritual piece, too busy living in their heads, never aware of their heart, soul, and spirit Indigenous cultures are spiritual by nature, unlike mainstream organized religions which have been judging, condemning, and killing one another for centuries to this very day Our innate spiritual nature seeks recognition, yearns for connection All ‘knowing’ is contextual If we’re curious about something we don’t know we seek the context that provides us the opportunity to ‘know’, seek and you will find Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Each day spend a few minutes is silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath, rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
