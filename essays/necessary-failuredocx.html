NECESSARY FAILURE

By George Gondron

NECESSARY FAILURE From the soil of brokenness, sprouts compassion and humility It is only our necessary failures, not our successes That eventually bring us to our meaningful existence The purpose of life isn't found in 'degrees' or 'pedigrees' Not in wealth or riches, what we grasp with our hands It is found within, between, and among other human beings In relationships, in connections, in human bonds Life, left to the ego alone becomes very self-centered All about 'me and mine' and sometimes 'ours' If the 'ours' doesn't cramp my style Failure is a great teacher, probably our best instructor Without it, humility is non-existent, only feigned humility It is much easier for those not born into privilege To understand the meaning of compassion and loving kindness Failure has a way of helping us understand that our successes Are not just about us They are also about those who helped us to become successful Failure also aids us in seeing what success really is If it is a result of 'walking' on the backs of others Is that really success, is that a valuable accomplishment? Tell me how you define success I'll tell you where your values lie The broken person, the failed person, I would trust Before the one who has never failed The one who has never failed lacks understanding They have never 'walked a mile in the shoes' of failure Those who have failed are more fully human Than those who have not And in reality, we have all failed at something Some are able to admit their failure and become better The rest deny, and maintain their projections As if failure were some sort of stigma And they are right, it is, it means we're all human Cherish and learn from your teachers, your failures If you do you will become more human, a better person Your heart will sprout in the soil of brokenness And discover your humility and compassion

George G
