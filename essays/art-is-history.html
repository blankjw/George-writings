ART IS HISTORY

By George Gondron

ART IS HISTORY Without art we would have little history We often think of art as a beautiful painting A powerful sculpture chiseled out of marble But art it is so much more, so very much more The great authors of the past give us images in words That are at times more powerful than the reality Parietal art, paintings on cave walls and ceilings From 40,000 years ago tell us an amazing story Ancient cities found throughout our planet Revealing architecture that is stunning in size and detail Pottery from lost civilizations, fired with beautiful images That tell stories unable to be narrated in no other way The bushmen of Africa The aborigine from Australia Their art is in the telling of their tales How the universe was formed, how life began And how it continues Artifacts buried with the deceased of all cultures Hold evidence of their artistic abilities The very nature of art is grounded in the soul's need to create To express itself, to participate in creation In words, in paint, in clay, in brick and mortar In dance, in song, in rite and ritual There are those who listen to their internal creativity Their particular mode of artistic ability Every life contains an inner artist But few listen, for little in our culture encourages the artist Supports the artist, champions the artist Or, as in earlier centuries is a patron to the artist In our modern times, the humanities are losing support A loss that will affect us much more than we realize Imagine the loss of all religious art forms from all belief systems Art has the power to touch the soul unlike no other medium Maybe that's what's happening, we're becoming soul-less We must care, we must support, we must encourage That which engages the artistic creativity energy of us all Or we face becoming sterile, antiseptic, and barren

George G
