WHEN THE TEARS COME

By George Gondron

WHEN THE TEARS COME For the child it's really very easy When the knee is skinned, the toe is stubbed The tears will follow, they don't hold back But as we grow, little by little Through the many influences in our lives We are taught directly or indirectly To stuff our hurts, ignore our emotions That sympathy is just a word found in the Dictionary Between 'shit' and 'syphilis' And in so doing, in believing the lie We do a great injustice to the human psyche We deny a great part of what it means to be human And as a result the world suffers the consequences It is always appropriate to feel what we feel To take ownership for our own feelings Notice, I said ownership - not blame others And reject the voices that say 'You shouldn't feel that way' Or, 'I know exactly how you must feel' No, you don't In fact most of us have bought into the narrative Of not sharing our deepest feelings, our human emotions So much that we have difficulty in naming them at all You can ask someone how they feel about a situation And inevitably they will respond with a thought Not a feeling Feelings are visceral, not cognitive So, our tears dry up Dammed behind walls of our own making Only because we listen to the externals And not our own inner truth, our inner wisdom Please, break the dam Learn to weep, to sob, to cry It's good for the soul, washes away the pain And if nothing else, cleans the tear ducts I know you can relate to all that's been written above And I also know, only you change the results

George G
