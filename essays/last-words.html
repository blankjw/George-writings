LAST WORDS

By George Gondron

LAST WORDS At some moment, we really never know when Maybe many moments from now, or our very next breath We will utter our last words Soft or loud, strong or weak, or perhaps silently Sometimes I wonder what those words will be Or who they may be spoken to A spouse, a child or grandchild Brother or sister, relative or friend Maybe a complete stranger, or maybe to no one Probably not something we think of, last words Not in our usual everyday thoughts Yet it's strange, how many times I've heard someone say After the death of someone they knew, 'I wish I would have' I know I've had that thought, wishing I had said something It's sad enough that often we don't consider that something Until it's too late, and yet even sadder Is when it crosses our minds, while they're still alive And we still don't take the opportunity To express what remains unspoken, that dwells in our hearts Whether it's just an appreciation for their presence in our lives Or perhaps an expression of love and caring We can't find the time, we'll do it tomorrow, or Maybe the next visit, or our next get-together And the 'wheel turns' and suddenly there's no next time That's one of the quirks regarding death The 'grim reaper' doesn't announce His coming There's no schedule that says, 'I'll be here next week' We never know the exact moment, the appointed time So maybe, rather than have those regrets That thought of, 'I wish I would have' We could share what remains unspoken, now Not wait until it's too late Loving kindness can't be used up, there's no expiration date It only requires a little effort, takes a very few moments Simple words that mean so much Speak those last words routinely, say them often to others Don't let your last words be, 'I wish I would have'

George G
