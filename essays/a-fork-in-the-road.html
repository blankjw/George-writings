A FORK IN THE ROAD

By George Gondron

A FORK IN THE ROAD If you are one of the more observant people in this society Who is more interested in the current dysfunction of our nation Than the next ‘shiny’ object or conspiracy theory Then you realize we truly are at a crossroads And the path we choose has a significant impact On the future, perhaps our very survival itself The attack on our Capital January 6, 2021, is a clear indication Of the fragility of our democracy and our Constitution Hundreds of years ago Benjamin Franklin was asked What had been accomplished by the formers of this union And his reply was, ‘a Republic if you can keep it’ If we are willing to have new eyes to see New ears to hear, then we know we are perilously close Too losing what Benjamin Franklin feared could happen A citizenry who is unable to see beyond the smoke and mirrors Or the rhetoric of deceptive politicians and the ultra-rich Say to themselves, ‘That could never happen here’ Are simply living under the illusion that the Germans had During the 1930’s under the influence of Hitler But alas it seems we’d rather be emotionally charged Through the numerous conspiracy theories The narrative of the ‘deep state’ trumpeters Who are motivated by fear as opposed to love Who would rather ‘drink the Kool-Aid’ than critically think Than question, doubt, reflect, and search for the truth Who abandon their intellect at the behest of their emotions We truly are at a fork in the road The choices we make significantly impacts our future Choose reason over lies, love over fear, it matters Remember ‘all that glitters is not gold’ And the one with the megaphone is often the problem Bring balance into your life, choose a meditative/contemplative Practice – spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, Be in the presence of Presence, your image of a loving God Empty your mind, focus on your breath, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
