THE VOID

By George Gondron

THE VOID Throughout our lives, there will be voids Voids represented by people who were once close Some, are those that we eventually accept They are natural, as unplanned accidents Or unexpected deaths Those we eventually process, make peace with But then there are voids created because of conscious choice When someone significant and important, walks away When trust and loyalty are thrown aside With no explanation and seemingly no remorse Those are the voids that linger forever and hurt the most A hurt formed from an experience of deep sadness Filled with unanswered questions And lingering thoughts of ‘What have I done?’ These voids are all about what life is in the end Human relationships, family and friends Remember the good times, resist the bitterness Resist the thoughts of, ‘Was it ever really real’ It was for you and that’s what matters Never contaminate fond memories with self-doubt The past is gone, there is only now To balance outer with inner is a path to healing To let go of what binds us is as well Thinking alone never heals, it picks at old wounds We most assuredly lack balance in our Western culture We have little or no clue at all about our innate nature About our internal resources, our inner core Our existence is lived through our egos, our minds To sit in silence, with stillness, and an empty mind Is asking the ego to let go, and it won’t, not willingly For your wholeness and well-being make a new choice Begin a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, be in the presence Of Presence, your image of Isness, Universal Consciousness, of God To stay focused, choose a brief mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They have your answers, they hold your true self, they wait

George G
