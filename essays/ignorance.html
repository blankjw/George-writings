IGNORANCE

By George Gondron

IGNORANCE The greatest enemy of equality Of peace, mercy, love, and justice Is ignorance, which seems to be a very harsh condemnation But it's not Ignorance is nothing more than unawareness Inexperience, disregard, confusion, insensitivity Lack of education and knowledge And throughout the world, and in this nation We are drowning in 'docta ignorantia' A Latin phrase translated 'learned ignorance' And it is so-called since we refuse to educate ourselves We'd much rather listen to false narratives and half-truths We'd much rather deny factual evidence We'd much rather be literalists and fundamentalists We are the blind being led by the blind Our egos refuse to believe that perhaps We may not have all the answers We refuse to acknowledge that there's so more That we don't know than what we do know We polarize our lives Clinging to the right or the left Missing the great middle where life can flourish Polarization only leads to death and destruction And there are many ways to die other than physical A mind that does not incorporate anything That is not its belief, even unfounded belief That does not acknowledge the yearnings, the whispers Of the heart, soul, and spirit of our humanness Is a mind that is uncompromising, a black and white existence An either/or, yes/no, good/bad, always and never life And, ironically in its quieter moments It knows, the mind knows, something is missing But fearful that, that something will take its imagined power Its imagined control over its rigid idea of life If you don't believe me Practice a little silence, stillness, and Presence each day But beware, it will bring about change, if you are willing

George G
