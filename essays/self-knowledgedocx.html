SELF-KNOWLEDGE

By George Gondron

SELF-KNOWLEDGE The most difficult process of life, genuine self-understanding But only if it's a sincere quest, a longed for search Individuation is not an easy task, even with conscious effort The ego resists the discovery of the true-self Preferring instead the controllable false-self The self of mind only, exclusive of heart, soul, and spirit Few know who they truly are, the gift that they are They know only what the ego wishes for them to know The mind alone defends, justifies, and excuses its behavior At the cost of others, as well as itself 'Weakness is strength', makes no sense to the ego How is it possible that 'everything emerges from nothing?' And 'silence' has all the answers My 'shadow' is darkest in the denial of these concepts The 'shadow' makes a weapon of what's not understood Dualistic thinking never doubts or questions It has all the answers and they're always correct But in truth There is no thought I cannot have No feeling I cannot experience No act I cannot commit It is in that 'knowing' I'm less likely to harm It is in that 'knowing' that I begin to discover Who I am, who I belong to, and the meaning of that belonging When I realize, I'm only a part of the greater whole I am connected to all that is, a relational connection A piece of 'divinity' in this divine universe Then my understanding of self is deepened No longer separate, but a part of No longer alone, but in communion with There is no 'you' nor 'I', there is only 'us' A union in our diversity within our humanity Secured by grace, held by unconditional love Not a belief, but an experience A knowing that can't be unknown As sure as the night follows the day We are gifts from the One, to each another, returning to the Source

George G
