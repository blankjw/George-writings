INSTITUTIONS

By George Gondron

INSTITUTIONS All institutions have one thing in common, control Political, religious, financial, economic, educational, etc., etc., etc. The more polarized and dualistic their mandates, the less flexible our opportunities for increased awareness and consciousness We become nothing more than a number expected to fall in lockstep with their rules, regulations, and requirements at the expense of our own unique gifts and strengths Simply becoming one among the many without an identity Following the old saying, ‘don’t do as I do, do as I say do’ Under those constraints there is little possibility of discovering our own uniqueness, our own special talents and gifts Many they are that follow the precepts, rules, and regulations of the institutions and deny our own intuition and inner guides Probably the greatest weakness we have as human beings is to ignore our own potential, our undiscovered possibilities We cling to the known, even if the known is harmful We refuse to risk, be vulnerable, and take a chance Nothing ventured, nothing gained mentality The importance of an interior life is nonexistent relying entirely on external resources for our opinions and decisions Without some form of spiritual practice, we rely on a religious belief that is grounded in dogma, doctrine, rites, and rituals Bereft in relationship with Mystery but clings to the eternal actions of membership and its requirements Genuine spirituality is unique to the individual, it is relational Just as love is not about emotions, it is about action The fact that we have free will is enough to believe and more importantly to know that we carry the Divine spark of life Experientially we become aware of relational spirituality through a chosen context called meditation and/or contemplation A chosen few minutes each day of silence, stillness, and Presence Time to empty our ‘monkey minds’ of their incessant and repetitive thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out The ego will resist, the mind will wander, simply rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
