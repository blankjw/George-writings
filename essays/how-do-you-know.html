HOW DO YOU KNOW

By George Gondron

HOW DO YOU KNOW Or better yet, how do you know what you say you know It’s a fair question, especially in our culture A culture in which we burn the candle at both ends Hardly able to remember what we did yesterday Much less a week or a month ago Thoughts fill our minds with mostly meaningless information Repetitive thoughts that focus mostly on other, other being People, places, and things, hardly ever about ourselves Well maybe on ourselves, as it relates to other people Relational thoughts of, ‘well they should, ought to, always, Never, could if they wanted to’, you know the patterns Who needs to be fixed? Have you noticed it is always other, not ourselves We are filled with complaints about what needs to be fixed What needs to change And it seems, our ill-formed solutions are not about ourselves But almost always about other Unfortunately, few people are concerned about their own Personal growth, their own continued increase in awareness Yep, I’m just fine, it’s the other guy, the other group The other religion, the other party, the other nation, always You want to be courageous, do a little self-examination Be honest, be self-critical, be objective There’s always a reason, a purpose, a belief behind Every thought, feeling, and action we choose And if our analysis is sincere you will find it’s mostly Selfish, not selfless for one primary cause We, our ego, does not like change, we like the familiar Even when the familiar is uncomfortable For you see, I can always blame my discomfort on others Here’s a tool to aid you in your examination, if you choose Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness; In the presence of your image of a creative Source, of Isness Empty your mind of its incessant thoughts, choose a brief mantra For example, breathe in ‘I love you from God’, breathe out I love you back to your image of God, rinse and repeat As the mind quietens listen to your heart, soul, and spirit

George G
