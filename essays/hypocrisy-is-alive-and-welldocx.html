HYPOCRISY IS ALIVE AND WELL

By George Gondron

HYPOCRISY IS ALIVE AND WELL The mental construct of our society, hypocrisy And we all participate, to one degree or another And the worst among us, those who deny the loudest Can be found in every profession Every level of education, every political office, And especially in every religion A few descriptors for hypocrisy are Pietism, lip service, pharisaism, bigotry, deception, dishonesty Underlying this phenomenon of hypocrisy is one simple fact We all want to look better than we really are Hypocrisy is grounded in the 'yes but' narrative Primarily to defend a position not grounded in experience But rather grounded in selfishness, disguised as innocence How else, for example, can someone call themselves Christian And then justify their lifestyle without being hypocritical The same applies for any other belief system One would think that the lessons taught by Jesus Taught by the Buddha, taught be even indigenous peoples About community, inclusion, and interdependence About love, kindness, and compassion Would be readily embraced, but they're not And one would at least think as the Dalai Lama often has stated 'If you can, help others; if you cannot do that, At least do not harm them' And we all harm in direct and indirect ways Some sooth their souls through anonymous charity (and some not so anonymous) But the hypocrisy lying within their thoughts has not changed All hypocrisy is supported by fear, not love, not compassion Fear that maybe we too, if we're Christian, will be crucified Not literally, but in ways that would deflate our fragile egos That would remove the masks of who we really are We all pretend, we all defend what we are not deep inside To be true to our existence we must be people of heart Of soul, and spirit and not just our egos Our actions, thoughts, and feelings must be grounded in truth And not in hypocrisy if we are to survive, together

George G
