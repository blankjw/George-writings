VULNERABLE TO ,ISINFORMATION

By George Gondron

VULNERABLE TO MISINFORMATION When, as adults, are we not responsible for our own behavior? If a person commits a crime using the excuse, they relied on Information from another source to support their actions If those actions were unlawful, then a crime has been committed It is nothing more than an attempt to defend the indefensible Something we witness daily in the arena of politics And, as a result of the previous administration has become More and more blatant, more pronounced It seems any excuse will do to support the obvious corruption The obvious lies, and half-truths Which have become so numerous they are now our familiar Oh well, the lies are business as usual, nothing new here And it follows, where do we go from here Unless there is a course correction The only place we will go is too frightening to imagine When we elect people with low morals or no morals at all Should we expect anything different? Not really, we get what we asked for when casting our vote We do live in a nation that is morally and spiritually bankrupt But we pretend it’s not It is always the ’other’ that is to blame, never ourselves The other party, the other gender, the other group, The other religion, the other ethnicity, always It begs the question Why are we so willing to embrace misinformation? We totally are unmindful of the saying Don’t believe what you hear and half of what you see We are very gullible it seems Often willing to accept what even a child would reject Accepting the unreasonable is a very narrow path And never has a good end, usually a dead end Using the excuse, I was vulnerable to misinformation Is just that, an excuse, and a very poor excuse There is a truth speaker within each of us Found in the moments of a meditative/contemplative practice In silence, with stillness, in the Presence Listen to the whispers of your own heart, soul, and spirit

George G
