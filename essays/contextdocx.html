CONTEXT

By George Gondron

CONTEXT Our familiars, our patterns of thinking and feeling Arise from the contexts of our daily lives, chosen ore unchosen It follows, in order to change our familiars We must also change our context either by adding something new To our environment or changing our environment entirely Not an easy process but very necessary for our well-being The saying, we prefer the familiar to the strange Even if the familiar is painful is very true, for significant change Is a matter of risk and vulnerability and it’s the human condition To stick to what we think we know than what we don’t know But it’s also true, no risk, no reward The old warning, GIGO, ‘garbage in, garbage out’ is more valid Today with the explosion of social media than ever before For if social media, in most instances, is your source for learning You will remain ignorant, influenced through your emotions Not your intellect It is the exception to the rule to witness two people ungagged In a challenging tropic with an open mind and not be overpowered By their emotions – a rare observation indeed Objectivity is never accomplished emotionally but only through Information carefully critiqued and analyzed intellectually The phrase, ‘alternative facts’ is only something other than factual Facts meant to deflect from what is true, you know the saying ‘You gone believe me or those ole lying eyes?’ Truth can withstand the clear light of day, a lie cannot For most of us, if we really want to know the truth We must change our source, our context, we must learn It takes no effort to turn on our favorite social media source To just become a ‘sponge’ and soak in the flavor of the day Without criticism, without reflection, without critical thinking To seek to know requires effort and dedication, it ‘ain’t’ easy But the ego much prefers easy, ‘so=and-so said’ has much more credence Yes but-isms, and what about-isms, and ten years ago are more important Than actual facts, truth, and the reality of here and now Make a meditative/contemplative practice part of your context Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Gradually increase your sit from a few minutes to 20 or 30 Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breath out Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answer, they patiently wait

George G
