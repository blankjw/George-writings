THE MORE WE KNOW THE MORE WE UNDERSTAND

By George Gondron | Source: THE MORE WE KNOW THE MORE WE UNDERSTAND.docx

THE MORE WE KNOW THE MORE WE UNDERSTAND There is no terminus, boundary, or limit to learning Learning is a perpetual activity or process that only enhances Our understanding as it increases our awareness and consciousness No moment when we can say, ‘OK I’ve learned enough’ In fact, feelings of frustration, anxiety, tension, etc. are Simply precursors urging us to further examine the meaning Behind those unpleasant experiences that only lead to our Geater potential and growth on all levels in all areas of Our humanity, physical, intellectual, emotional, and spiritual As we grow older we have a tendency to ‘settle’ for what is As it is – like living in a large house with many unexplored Rooms whose doors remain shut to our curiosity never to be Opened to discover what may be found inside Children by nature are very curious, full of questions But it seems as we become adults somewhere in our journey We cease to question, we have arranged our own little world We have all the answers that we need, yet in our quieter Moments there are whispers in the recesses of our minds That tell us there is more, we are more It’s interesting to note that most human beings have two Areas in their lives which they only partially or never explore Their own psychology, their ‘dark night of the soul’ journey And their rejection or disbelief ofu a Universal Consciousness Or God if you prefer, compromised through an intellectual belief But absent a relational spiritual experience Or put another way, we have a resistance to opening ourselves To our own ‘shadow’ and our own ‘divinity’ An atheist or agnostic will deny that God exists while At the same time refusing to engage experiences that allow for The possibility they may be wrong – God is not a thought God is an experience, a relational experience If you dare adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Your ego will resist, do it anyway Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, be present Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, your inner core You will find your true self, your answers, they wait

George G
