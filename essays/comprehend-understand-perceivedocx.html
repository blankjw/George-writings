COMPREHEND UNDERSTAND PERCEIVE

By George Gondron

COMPREHEND UNDERSTAND PERCEIVE Our level of awareness and consciousness Is directly connected to our level of understanding Tied to our level of comprehension and perception as well When we are confronted with information that’s unfamiliar We are at a crossroads, a juncture of choice Either we have a tendency to be open, to learn Or, we are closeminded and reject the possible What we’re not willing to explore further, the unfamiliar Has unrealized consequences formed from unrecognized fear The process is as follows when we are closed What we don’t understand we learn to fear Fear, unexamined, eventually leads to hate Where there is hate, there is no love Where there is no love Kindness and compassion are not operative What is operative is bias, prejudice, and judgment All the result of a dualistic mindset As opposed to an exploratory both/and mindset All the aforementioned is tied to one’s curiosity to learn The word ignorant is about learning Or better stated, the refusal to learn Already knowing the answer before the question is asked Having a position, an opinion, or even a strong belief That has no experiential basis, no connection to reality How we know what we know does matter A contradictory nature is a life lived without personal experience Lived through ‘he said, she said, they said’ A fully human life is one of connection Connection between mind, heart, soul, and spirit Connection to the ‘true self’ as opposed to the egoic ‘false self’ Choose a daily meditative/contemplative practice Discover your ‘true self’ – for a few minutes each day Be silent, be still, be in the presence of Presence Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts That you might hear the whispers and yearnings That dwells within your own heart, your soul, your spirit The innate knowing given at your birth, they patiently await

George G
