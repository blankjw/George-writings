THE CIRCLE OF A LIFE

By George Gondron

THE CIRCLE OF A LIFE Imagine a spiral staircase Envision a circle on the ground The staircase is a metaphor for change Each step an increase in awareness A higher level of consciousness The circle on the ground, goes nowhere With each step it becomes more of a rut If the journey is one only of destination And not of immersion, participation We miss the trip entirely The rut only becomes deeper Leading to a death, while still alive Ah, but I'm successful, I've stuck to the path I've maintained my circle Really? Successful at what, gone where, lived how? When examined, my success is only my doing It's not my being It's easier to do, but harder to be I cannot hug my doing, caress my success Doing only, is a temporary satisfaction Leading to emptiness until I do more Learning to be, awakens one's life Bringing awareness beyond just the doing Seeing and hearing what I missed before Slowing down to enjoy the journey Content with what I have Take the spiral staircase, become more aware Get out of the circle that leads to nowhere Don't wait, there's still time There's always enough time

George G
