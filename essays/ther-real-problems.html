THER REAL PROBLEMS

By George Gondron

THE REAL PROBLEMS The failure of formal education is not the fault of teachers It is the fault of the system, the ‘paper pushers’ One out of five adults are illiterate, 20%, 54% of adults have a Literacy level of sixth grade or lower, that is shocking Our president-elect repeatedly said, ‘I love the poorly educated’ Poorly educated people are easily manipulated and controlled Undocumented immigrants are a drain on our democracy Undocumented immigrants contribute $31 billion annually to Social Security and Medicare from which they receive no benefit In addition, they also contribute another $65 billion to state and Local taxes, but they contribute nothing to the economy, right? Undocumented immigrants are murders and rapists Three women a day are murdered by current and former romantic Partners in this nation who are not undocumented immigrants Without a doubt, most of us get our information from social media Is a sad replacement for valid factual data, based on ‘gaslighting’, Hyperbole, alternative facts, conspiracy theories, and lies It’s so much easier than seeking the truth, that would require effort Critical thinking, reflection, research, questions, and doubt The problems we experience in this Nation are too obvious to Ignore; problems of conscience, of decency, integrity, human values, Lack of kindness, racism, bigotry, prejudice, discrimination, exclusion Another problem of the social media ‘addiction’, loss of social skills Advancements in technology are both a blessing and a curse Human interaction is necessary for mature human development But as long as we can communicate with a ‘hand-held screen’ We, all of us, are the real problem, no need for personal interaction We misidentify, misdiagnose, and refuse to take ownership for our Own dysfunction, our own lack of awareness and understanding The common refrain, ‘it’s always someone else’s fault, not mine’ Without a connection to our own internal resources, our inner core That will never change; adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out The ego will resist, the mind will wander, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Your inner guides hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
