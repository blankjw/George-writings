THE BALANCE OF A LIFE

By George Gondron

THE BALANCE OF A LIFE To know ourselves, to know others, to know life to the full Is to experience every emotion, every moment, fully To be present in such a way that we take ownership For what is as it is without blame in full awareness I promise you this To the degree and depth that you love, you also will suffer To the degree and depth of your joy, you will also know pain The tears you shed, the heartache you feel The empathy and compassion you experience You will never know as burdens only as gifts When you have faced the ‘dark night of your own soul’ They only connect us to one another, to all humanity Maybe that’s what love really is, how we connect How when it’s time for us to give, we are there and ready When it’s time for us to receive, we are open and welcoming We are able to see our sameness before our differentness We are all spokes in the wheel of life None greater or lesser than the other All pieces of stardust from the beginning of time No emotion, no feeling is ever the enemy It is always the teacher, the experience that pushes us Further into our full humanity, into our connectedness To navigate the waters of our individual lives, to be truth seekers Is never an easy journey, the waters are seldom smooth The distractions are many and seem to be unending But we are always stronger together than apart Unity within diversity, all genders, all ethnicities, all beliefs To accomplish balance within each one of us is a personal choice To maintain that balance requires a consistent practice Some form of meditative/contemplative practice Whatever fits for you, a time for emptiness, an alone time The basic practice is to spend a few minutes each day in silence, In stillness, in the presence of your image of a creative Source The Consciousness of the Universe has many names, God is but one Empty your mind of incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or a short mantra, listen for the whispers Your heart, soul, and spirit hold your truth, your answers, they wait

George G
