SOCIALLY DISADVANTAGED

By George Gondron

SOCIALLY DISADVANTAGED Take a guess Those who are socially disadvantaged Exist day after day with what predominate emotion? Fear With what repetitive internal question? Who am I, what am I? All wrapped in the narrative of our Declaration of Independence All men are created equal With certain unalienable rights Life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness Of course, we first must be able to recognize To acknowledge, that yes, there are those Who are socially disadvantaged But if you’ve had a good education Have a good-paying job with benefits Come from inherited wealth Maintain an adequate standard of living Living inside your safe and secure bubble Maybe you don’t see the socially disadvantaged But their legions are endless Women, single moms, the poor, the homeless, The orphans, the elderly, and families living below poverty levels The LGBTQ+ community, the handicapped, etc., etc., etc. When our context never changes Our interactions remain the same We live with our heads in the sand, with blinders on It is the safe way, so we can feel good about ourselves The result of a culture that is dead from the neck down No connection between the head and the heart Everything is about the externals, about the material About power and control in the hands of the few And those few, if you’ve ever noticed, are mostly white males Singular or plural, CEOs or Board rooms Engage your mind with your heart Recognize the socially disadvantaged, take off the blinders Be kind and compassionate, and give of your excess Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit

George G
