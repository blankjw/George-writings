A NECESSARY CHALLENGE

By George Gondron

A NECESSARY CHALLENGE The most important challenge of our individual lives Is always the challenge of our own understanding Especially of and about ourselves For if we don’t first understand ourselves Especially the origins of our own defenses How can we ever hope to understand the other or others? All change always begins and ends with self Not with others, not with he, she, it, or them But in truth, it is the ego, the ‘I’ that resists change So, we end up in a culture where everyone And everybody expects all others to change But not themselves Few people seek counseling to change themselves Few have the courage to look in the mirror and say I am the problem, I need to change, I need help We are a nation of ‘finger pointers’ Unaware and unwilling to take ownership of our own Dysfunction, our own addictive nature For we are a nation of addiction For addiction is much more than what we ingest It includes most everything we can’t release Or cease to engage in, including thoughts and feelings There’s an old saying, ‘what you can’t let go of Has power over you’, no matter the source, and it does I offer to you, a challenge Each day look in the mirror and ask, what do I need to change About my perceptions, my beliefs, my ideas of others If you find nothing, look again, you are deceiving yourself Be brave enough to point the finger back at yourself Take that first necessary step toward change, let go Let go of what holds you back, that stagnates your growth In addition to that process, please do this Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of your image of a creator God, Source, Isness Empty your mind of its addictions, its incessant and often Repetitive thoughts that you might hear the whispers Of your own heart, soul, and spirit – they patiently wait

George G
