WHO ARE WE^J EACH OF US

By George Gondron

WHO ARE WE, EACH OF US When our lives come to an end, sooner or later How will we be remembered, what will our mark be? Will it be the words we spoke or the actions of our lives Will we be known as a good listener with an open mind? Easy to be around, welcoming of all others Regardless of their race, color, or creed? Courageous enough to challenge what may be wrong Without a doubt, without question, regardless the consequences Will we be known for our kindness, compassion, and empathy Defending the disenfranchised, the underprivileged, the poor Supporting the elderly, the orphan, the homeless Will we be known for our love of others demonstrated Through our actions, our sacrifices, not just our words Will others remember us as selfish or selfless More concerned about others than ourselves More concerned about unity than division, inclusion versus exclusion More interested in solving problems than creating them More willing to be peace than fight for peace You may say, all the above is too much, too much for me But together it’s not too much, if we each do a part Or like most people we can pretend, out of sight, out of mind Or perhaps the better word, we can be hypocrites Saying one thing, doing another, playing a part Or like the saying, talking the talk but not walking the walk Offering our ‘thoughts and prayers’ but little else There’s little evidence ‘thoughts and prayers’ solve the problems They’re really nothing more than self-appeasement for nonaction I hope our lives reflect the former choices, not the latter Our words are useless without action, always To know, to discern our better actions we need balance Incorporating our mind, heart, soul, and spirit each day Change requires both addition and subtraction Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, be in the Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
