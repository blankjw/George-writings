DO YOU WONDER

By George Gondron | Source: DO YOU WONDER.docx

DO YOU WONDER? When the noise stops and the bright lights dim I often wonder About the mystery of this astounding universe Beginning with this planet we call our home In the predawn hours as the sky just begins to lighten Or the evening sunset when the stars begin to emerge Listening to a soft rain pattering on a tin roof Feeling the warm sun on my upturned face All of this is cause to pause and just to be, to experience Life, all life truly is a miracle That mostly we take for granted, unappreciated And for us humans, we absolutely are a marvel The workings of our physical bodies Everything we become encoded in a single cell More complex and more self-regulating than anything man-made And our wondrous minds More sophisticated than any computer And let us not forget our consciousness and awareness Our souls and spirits carry within each of us a special gift How can each human life past, present, and future Not be so very important, so very unique Yet also drawn together in our sameness I wonder at times what am I about? And before the thought is finished, I know the answer I know in my heart and soul we are created from Love A Love that can not love, that fills the universe That sustains and breathes all that exists My life, your life, all life created from this Love is relational We are all connected whether aware or unaware With each other and with the One that creates That One asks only one thing of us, a very small thing Simply to be in a relationship, nothing more It is possible, I know, I have experienced that relationship Through intention, silence, stillness, and through wonder It is possible, for all, with no exception Take time to wonder, don't miss the beauty, know yourself You are special, you have a gift, share the unusual you

George G
