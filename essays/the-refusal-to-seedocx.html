THE REFUSAL TO SEE

By George Gondron

THE REFUSAL TO SEE There is a mode of behavior that permeates our culture Seemingly innocent and unassuming, but it is deadly It is the refusal to see, to turn a blind eye Bury our heads in the sand, and wear blinders Also among us are the particular Particular about what we see, what we hear Particular in our beliefs, our thoughts, our feelings It is the disease of close-mindedness Of don't confuse me with the facts Or put another way, don't disturb me with the truth The truth that is apparent as the sun will rise tomorrow The mentality of 'if you're not for me, you're against me' The hidden danger of this phenomenon is plain to see For those willing to look It goes by the simple phrase called 'right thinking' What I think is right, what you think is wrong Trapped in the middle are innocent human lives Of all ages, all ethnicities, all beliefs, all genders This reality leaves no room for inclusion, for unity What separates and divides cannot bring peace and harmony It is a mindset that cannot see diversity in unity Cannot comprehend difference in sameness It only sees what is not like 'me' This blindness is fueled and motivated by fear Given strength and potency through indifference The refusal to employ critical thinking To never question and doubt, simply follow, has a definition It is called ignorance, lack of awareness, unconsciousness Cured and remedied only through intentional communication Not being talked to and manipulated through propaganda But being talked with, in an open dialogue Without self-reflection, without critical informed thinking Without educating ourselves, we know the enemy The finger points back to us, not to the other If we want change, it is ourselves that must change first If we want to survive, along with future generations The only choice is change

George G
