THE PROSE OF KNOWING

By George Gondron

THE PROSE OF KNOWING Personal knowing, at its deepest level, is an impossible Experience to adequately share, no words are descriptive Enough to make known or communicate that deep knowing Our common everyday language does not suffice, it rings hollow Perhaps It’s easier to say, it’s for the knower alone The authors of history repeatedly tell us that the most Awe inspiring and numinous experiences are singular They are personal for the individual, they are a relational Experience itself with what is only described as Other The essence of our being, at its most fundamental nature Is to this day still a mystery to science, but maybe that’s changing For quantum physics is hypothesizing that the universe is conscious But how we relate to that hypothesis is personal And even further, if our context never changes so does the Possibility that each of us will never scratch the surface of That deep personal knowing we all contain inherently In our everyday familiar we manage to stifle the whispers Of our inner yearnings to ‘know’, to connect to Mystery Or we get caught in the game of, ‘what I believe is right And what you believe is wrong’, or we don’t believe at all The interesting thing about belief, love is not afraid to know But fear is – uncertainty remains uncertain without a desire To know, to experience, to risk and be vulnerable One of the promises of life is that letting go leads us to knowing But clinging to unfounded belief is to remain stuck It’s relatively easy to understand our resistance to even know That which is Other, Isness, Source, Creator, Mystery, or God Our minds are filled with nonsensical information, thoughts That dwell on yesterday, tomorrow, and seldom on the now We resist any form of meditative/contemplative practice To be quiet, still, and empty our minds is impossible for even A few minutes much less half an hour, context matters if change Is to be experienced, but the ego doesn’t like change I hope you experience quiet time, be present to Presence, daily I hope you awaken your inner core, your internal references And listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they are your guides, they wait

George G
