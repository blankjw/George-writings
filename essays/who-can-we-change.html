WHO CAN WE CHANGE

By George Gondron

WHO CAN WE CHANGE? No one, well, there is one, it’s ourselves Yet we spend our time and energy attempting To change others, family, friends, even strangers If all that time and energy were to be focused On ourselves, the world would definitely be a better place Those in the helping professions tell us Seventy percent of their clients seek counseling Not for themselves, but to change others Consequently, the only behavior they defend is their own And there are lots of ways to defend our behavior And of course none of which are beneficial They accomplish only one thing, we remain ‘stuck’ Our way is the right way, all others are wrong Sound familiar? If you want to be a gift to others, to the world Change yourself first, examine yourself first In most cases the finger we point at another Points back to us, guilt is never singular Obviously, in our society today, change is definitely needed But all meaningful change begins with self, always Truth is never found in changing others It is found in changing ourselves first Those who listen to defend are the strongest resistors To any and all change, the ones who are never wrong The ones who don’t have the courage to consider They may not be correct, they may even be mistaken Those who cannot say, ‘I apologize, I am sorry’ Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of Presence – it will teach you to let go Empty your mind that you might hear the whispers Of your heart, soul, and spirit – they hold your truth They reveal your true self, they patiently wait

George G
