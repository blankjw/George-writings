ESSAY

CHOICE

By George Gondron

CHOICE Every waking moment of our lives What we do or do not do in each of those moments Is a matter of personal choice Not what is done to us, but what we do Including not only our actions but our thoughts and feelings as well The blinders that we wear, our closemindedness Also, are ultimately our own choice And as well our own responsibility Blame, denial, contrast, and comparison afford us the opportunity To avoid taking responsibility for our choices It’s so much easier to point the finger elsewhere And never at ourselves It provides one with a sense of self-righteousness Don’t look at me, look over there That’s where the fault lies If we all took responsibility for our own choices The world would change overnight Beginning with our personal interactions with all the Relationships in our lives, near and far The alternative to not being responsible for our choices Is nothing more than ignorance, taking the easy way Relying on alternative facts, believing the unbelievable We open the ‘tap’ of our chosen social media platform And swallow hook, line, and sinker the ensuing narrative Even when we suspect the whole truth is lacking We are attracted to sources of ‘opinions’, not facts For opinions are so much easier to agree with For they stir our emotions, not our intellect Seek balance, awaken your inner core, your internal reference Your true self is a manifestation of the knowing of Your own heart, soul, and spirit, not the false self of the ego Become aware of your choices, own them, change them Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of Presence, your belief in a creator Source Commit to a meditative/contemplative practice daily You will hear the whispers of the truth you innately possess Given you at birth, connected to the Isness of the universe

George G
