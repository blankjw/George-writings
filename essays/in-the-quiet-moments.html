IN THE QUIET MOMENTS

By George Gondron

IN THE QUIET MOMENTS Life has a way of becoming real in those quiet moments The in between times, when the mind shuts down Those rare instances when we're caught off guard Undefended, unprotected, alone When we're no longer able to push away the obvious tugging In the recesses of our resistant minds Resistant to become more than just what we do To recognize the unique value of our life The gift we are to this world In this intricate web we call our personal existence Touched by grace with every breath Each inhale, each exhale Every beat of our hearts Held together by the Consciousness of this universe When we are no longer gods unto ourselves And know we are not our own creation A moment of true humility, received as well through grace From the Source of all good Because of this Source I am, for this I love, for this I have life For this I have relationship, connection, belonging An unbreakable bond freely given for all eternity There is no price to pay, no recompense demanded It is gift, pure and simple All that I can do is be grateful, be thankful All I can do is be kind to others, be present to their lives Listen to their stories, hold their cares in my heart Hold their hands between mine, enfold them in my arms Life is surely a gift, meant to be shared To be in relationship with all that is To be in relationship with not just what I see But also with that which I don't see, the Source of all that is In those quiet moments In the stillness and silence I now chose to sit with the Source, called by many names There are no words, there are no images, just intention The intention to be with, a shared relational intention It is the least that I can do, and the most that I can do, it is

George G
