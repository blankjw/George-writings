THERE ARE NO WINNERS

By George Gondron

THERE ARE NO WINNERS In our many arguments, disagreements, and hostile interactions There are no winners, only losers We revolve in an endless circle, a merry-go-round Of competition, comparison, contrast, and self-righteousness A polarized existence of opposites Right/wrong, good/bad, mine/yours, us/them When all the emotions subside Even the supposed winners are left with a hollow feeling At best only a temporary solution has been imagined And until the next ‘bump-in-the-road’ There’s an uneasy truce, an unfulfilled yearning Which is really about me, and not the other or others Life’s journey is about each of us becoming fully human Body, mind, soul, and spirit Being responsible for our individual lives And yet seventy percent of those who seek counseling Do so to ‘fix’ someone else, not themselves Point fingers, blame, and deny is the cultural norm And we are no better off today than yesterday Or even a hundred years ago, perhaps we are worse It seems to complain is preferable to self-actualization To take ownership of our own thoughts. feelings. actions Not to mention our non-actions Maybe the better path is one of cooperation, compromise Conciliation, congeniality, compatibility, and compassion All positive change is always and must be about self Not about them, the others, but about me For your potential, your wellbeing Add a new path to your daily life A meditative/contemplative path, time to be alone path For a few minutes each day be silent, be still, be present Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Thoughts about yesterday and tomorrow that go nowhere And do nothing, all the while missing the now Be present to Presence, your image of a creative Source Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your truth, your healing, they wait

George G
