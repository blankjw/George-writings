TOO OFTEN, TOO LATE

By George Gondron

TOO OFTEN, TOO LATE In the briefness of time In our so limited human existence There will be at least one thought, one feeling, one person That entered our awareness, our consciousness, our lives Which was stronger than any other And which We failed to pursue, explore, or measure That experience lingers in the recesses of our mind A mind that always has a reason, a justification, an excuse For an unexamined possibility, now gone by That thought of, 'I could have, but I didn't' 'I wonder, what if?' Those important decisions, made only with the mind Are almost always, significant missed opportunities It is only with the heart, the soul, and the spirit Along with the mind That those opportunities are recognized for what they are Some may be recaptured Incorporated into our present lives But, most cannot It's too late Most, if not all, If given the chance to revisit those moments In retrospect, would choose a different response A different course of action But, we too often become aware, too late Give notice to those moments that struggle to be heard Whether feelings, thoughts, people, or actions They all have merit, purpose, and value They can become recognized for what they are, gifts In the form of hidden talents, new careers, exciting surprises A new relationship, a lasting friendship, a cherished love What goes unnoticed always has worth Don't let it slip by Don't let it be Too often, too late

George G
