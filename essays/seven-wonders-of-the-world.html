SEVEN WONDERS OF THE WORLD

By George Gondron

SEVEN WONDERS OF THE WORLD We each, you and I, all human beings Possess the seven wonders of the world They are always with us every moment of our lives Taste, touch, smell, hear, see, think, and feel And as well, most of us take these wonders for granted Gifts beyond measure, priceless in their value, irreplaceable They are part and parcel of this thing we call our body A housing supported by skin and bones Filled with various internal organs Connected by nerves and blood vessels Ligaments and muscles – we are walking miracles Engineered to perfection in purpose and goal And our greatest developed gift is also our fatal flaw Free will, our ability to choose We don’t choose our seven wonders, they are innate with our being But at some point, our ego realizes that it is a choice maker For good or bad, for right or wrong, selfish or unselfish We all choose Some choose with an informed mind, others not al all At least not with reliable and trustworthy information Beginning with reliable personal experience The ‘knee-jerkers’, not the responders In the evolutionary chain of history, we truly are amazing Creative beings without comparison, capable of being Both kind and cruel without measure, a matter of choice So, what is it that leads one to be more subjective or objective More motivated by fear than by love Perhaps it’s a disconnect between inner and outer Our failure to recognize we are more alike than unalike Have we lost touch with the innate gifts that come with birth The knowing held within our hearts. souls, and spirits Built in software that has become drowned out by ego Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, reconnect Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, embrace Presence Honor your seven wonders, renew your choices Empty your ‘monkey minds’, focus on your breath Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
