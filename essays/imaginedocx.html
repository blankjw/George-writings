IMAGINE

By George Gondron

IMAGINE Imagine if you will How would you change life for others If you could, I mean all life Not just your little circle For actually What we do to and for others We do as well for ourselves For better or for worse How would you help others Discover their unique gifts Waiting to be shared with humanity How would you embrace their pain And celebrate their joy How would you help them see What they yet can’t see in themselves Imagine if we recognized our sameness first Rather than our differences If we understood that what separates us Is not the truth But our conditioning, our programming, our illusions Before we can understand and help another We must first understand ourselves We must realize that what we think, feel, do Is a matter of choice Just as it is for all others Which then makes judgment very difficult We all need to experience forgiveness Especially of ourselves To recognize that you can’t know What you don’t know But also, once you do know You, me, all of us become responsible for our actions For our beliefs, for our values, for our lives Yes, imagine If all life was about caring and sharing About compassion, kindness, love, and connection Just imagine

George G
