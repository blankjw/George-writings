DISCONNECTED

By George Gondron

DISCONNECTION On the highway called life, our personal journey We will encounter ‘speed bumps’ periodically Unexpected events that throw us off balance That disrupts our daily activities and agendas Most of which we can handle and deal with But there are those that have a significant impact On our psyches, our emotional intelligence Our self-image, and leave us with a feeling of disconnection As if we’re on an island all alone Dealing with specific feelings which Almost always involve relationships Where do we fit in, how do we fit in, do we fit in? The pattern goes as follows The less the interaction with those we consider Significant others, family, friends, etc. The greater the tendency to take on an imagined belief That there may be something wrong with us, ourselves Especially when we are young Our need to be included, accepted, and engaged Is very important and necessary for our wellbeing Without those experiences at a young age Of being accepted, loved, included, and nurtured Especially in our family systems We are vulnerable to outside forces that are not In the end beneficial Forces that are willing to manipulate and condition us For their benefit, their purpose, their goals Convincing us that they have all the right answers And we need only listen to and follow them They seek the vulnerable, the needy, the easily conditioned The ‘snake oil salesmen’, the ‘false prophets’, the ‘naysayers’ The ‘self-righteous’, and the ‘propagandist’, prey on the weak If you experience any of the above, know of someone that does I recommend a daily practice of a few minutes of silence, Stillness, with Presence, a meditative/contemplative practice If consistently committed to, you will find your own inner Strength, your own inner truth, your own inner healing

George G
