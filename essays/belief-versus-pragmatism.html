BELIEF VERSUS PRAGMATISM

By George Gondron

BELIEF VERSUS PRAGMATISM Pragmatism is the doctrine that the content of a concept For example, a belief Consists only in its application, or its practice If the foundation of our democracy is our right to vote If you don’t exercise that right, what support do we have To say we believe in democracy? The same could be said about anything we call a belief That belief is only valid in its practice, not in ‘lip service’ It is, in fact, demonstrable action, what we actually do In 1782 the United States Congress adopted the motto ‘E Pluribus Unum’, out of many, one A belief embraced by the founding fathers, a nation undivided Would that be true today, our divisions run deep The belief has little merit The same can be said of any significant belief we hold dear Phrases like ‘liberty and justice for all’ Or, ‘all men/women are created equal’ Or, ‘no person is above the law’ Beliefs that when examined under the magnifying glass of history As well as present-day reality Are nothing more than empty platitudes, they are a lie And finally, the really great paradox, that of a religious belief One that states there is only one true, correct, authoritative Source for religious belief whose pragmatism seems to be Fulfilled through the ritual of attending a brick and mortar Building once a week, warming a pew, and paying fire insurance All the while as the One they profess to believe in weeps as He Witnesses the failure of one of His main teachings ‘Love one another as I have loved you’ All of His teachings were about action, about relationships About gratitude and thanksgiving, not about worship Discover those aspects of that belief within yourself They are all there, the God you believe in doesn’t need worship Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of the Isness of the universe, the I Am Listen to the whispers of your own heart, soul, and spirit There you will find your truth, action beyond belief

George G
