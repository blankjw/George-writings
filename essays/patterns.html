PATTERNS

By George Gondron

PATTERNS Repeated behavior in thought, feeling, and action Eventually becomes a pattern in our daily lives Those patterns are our ‘familiars’ acted out, acted upon Mostly unawares, like physical muscle memory But include your intellectual and psychological muscles And if our minds are dwelling in the past or the future In our present-day moments we will more than likely Become ‘reactors’ versus ‘responders’ Only because our pattern has become a divided mind Yesterday, now, and tomorrow and a divided mind Cannot be fully present to the now Few people are fully present to now during waking hours Which is another way of saying we miss much of our life As the saying goes, because ‘we’re not here now’ All our patterns are developed in a context In a particular environment, our home, our work, our relationships Our patterns, formed in different contexts, illicit memories Those memories which result from our previous patterns Will be recalled when we enter the context where The pattern developed, like our old neighborhood or school Or previous employment, perhaps reconnection to an old friend The point of all this is, is one important fact We have the power to choose our patterns and our context We can take ownership for the unique person that we are Rather than allowing external forces to direct our lives We can learn to filter what we take in, through discernment Accepting that which fits and rejecting that which doesn’t Inclusive of thoughts, feelings, people, ideas, and actions We alone are responsible for our choices, no one else I humbly suggest a new pattern, a new context, for balance Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice For a few minutes each day find a place, a context To experience silence, stillness, and Presence consistently Until it becomes a pattern, a necessary part of your life Empty your mind, be present to the now, focus on your breath Listen for the whispers of your heart, spu;, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait
