HUGS

By George Gondron

HUGS Several hugs a day, keep the doctor away Much better than apples Hugs, so natural for some So awkward for others The air hug The bear hug The neck clinging hug The do that again hug The male-to-male homophobic hug Not a hug at all, but a manly handshake The woman-to-woman hug A lean forward, hands gently on the shoulders hug Ah, then there's the child's hug A hang on for dear life hug, the best kind At least until they find the adolescent off switch The universal response to great loss, great joy The hug Through the gentle hug, the soft holding, we share We share in the deep sorrow of another We share in the jubilant rejoicing of another To experience the hug, the touch of another, I exist With that existence comes a history With that history comes a story, your story, my story Let us listen to each other's stories Let us hug, sensing our common humanity Let us heal

George G
