FAILURE IS PART OF THE JOURNEY

By George Gondron

FAILURE IS PART OF THE JOURNEY Behind every success, there has first been failure And in most cases failure many times over Two other ingredients included in the eventual success Are sacrifice and courage Success in every area of life is not the result of luck But rather another four-letter word called dedicated work The most important part of our life’s journey Is in the realm of human relationships Everything we do is connected in one form or another To people, relationships, both intimate and cordial The failure of those relationships, or their tolerance Exceeds the percentage of successes The divorce rate alone is more than fifty percent Even those marriages that do not end in divorce Many survive in a form of quiet tolerance When society’s focus is on ‘things’ versus people Relationships on every level suffer Relationships in all circumstances are in essence Partnerships, not ownership, even between employer and employee In any relationship where one or both treat each other as objects As opposed to subjects, there will always be failure, not success All change begins with the single individual We can’t change others, only ourselves The necessary changes for each of us are always from the inside out Bu a person driven by ego alone only perceives the externals There is no connection to an inner core or internal reference Success is manifested through balance, becoming fully human A path that leads to balance is a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, with Presence Being present to Presence, your image of God is relational Life defined in its most important state is relationship We cannot not be in relationship In that daily committed practice, we learn to empty Our ever-busy ‘monkey minds’ of their incessant repetitive thoughts We wait for the innate truth held within We listen for the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit We become whole, we succeed, we find our true self

George G
