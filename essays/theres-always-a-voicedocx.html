THERE'S ALWAYS A VOICE

By George Gondron

THERE’S ALWAYS A VOICE Inside each of us, there abides a voice, a quiet voice A discerning voice that guides us in our journey Informing us of choices, right or wrong, good or bad But even more importantly gives us the awareness That seldom is something always right or always wrong Seldom is life black or white but mostly gray Life is mostly situational, circumstances and context matters A dangerous mind is an either/or mind, a judgmental mind A mind that is polarized and closed, having all the answers A mind that never listens to that inner voice, totally unaware A mind that clings to the status quo, unwilling to let go Refuses to risk or be vulnerable, a fearful mind Our three greatest struggles Heal our own pain, accept unconditional love, learn to be We cannot think our way through any of the three But we can make different choices that lead to new experiences As the saying goes, ‘no risk, no reward’ Resist first is always the ‘go-to’ position of a dualistic mind A mind that refuses to listen to the inner voice of discernment A mind that lives through distraction, the next shiny object That follows only what satisfies their ego and the beliefs that Have no experiential basis whatsoever, he said/she said/they said The Kool Aid drinkers and conspiracy theorists motivated by fear Absent of love A mind that is filled with incessant and repetitive thoughts Is like a glass of muddy water never able to clear, never still That’s the real problem for ‘human doers’, unable to be We are blind to our own shadow, our demons As well we are blind to our own divinity, our angels Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, a practice of being Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, and Presence Empty your every busy ‘monkey mind’, discover your inner voice Most of what’s important in life is only known through experience Not through cognition, to know is to do, not to think is to do Wisdom is not the result of age but of a clear mind, like a child In your contemplative moments focus on your breath, a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
