WHERE DO WE BEGIN? If we’re active listeners, which unfortunately most are not Perhaps we can make it a point to pay attention To conversations of everyday life within our families With our acquaintances, on social media, just about anywhere And you will discover that most of what you hear Will be about someone else and how they are _________ You fill in the blank If we’re honest, It’s almost always something negative Something we can complain about Something they need to change, fix, or become If there’s something we’re very good at It’s ‘pointing fingers’ and blame In psychology, it’s called projection The unwillingness to see in myself what I project onto others Here is a universal truth, if we want change It begins and ends with ourselves first, always We do not have the power to change others, not really Fear is a great motivator, but it doesn’t have the power To change a person’s internal beliefs Unless they have a low level of awareness and consciousness Unless they are ‘sheep’ and are prone to be followers Versus critical thinkers, suspicious of the propagandists And the ‘snake oil’ salesmen selling them ‘Kool Aid’ It’s interesting to note that most people who seek professional Help from a therapist, do so because of someone else That someone else needs to be ‘fixed’ and then all will be well Here’s where we begin, first, last, and always We begin with ourselves, it’s called choice No one is holding a gun to our heads, only ourselves A good beginning place is a meditative/contemplative practice But be warned, your ego will resist adamantly, relentlessly But also, I promise it will change you for the better A few minutes each day of silence, stillness, with Presence Those few minutes to empty your ever-busy ‘monkey mind’ Of its incessant and often repetitive thoughts, going nowhere A time to listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit That always hold the truth, that wait patiently

George G
