UNWILLING

By George Gondron

UNWILLING When a person is unwilling to have an open discussion On any individual topic The problem is not the topic, it’s the person It is a closed-minded attitude Not because of its controversy But because of a narrowmindedness A self-righteous attitude bolstered by an ego That is unable to accept that it might be wrong Under those circumstances, it is virtually impossible To ever utilize cooperation, conciliation, or compromise It is a communication style that follows the analogy ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ With that mindset, there is no hope for progress There is only an anti-attitude that if a change is to occur It will not be because of an agreement that is equitable If we are not willing to sit down and listen Not willing to hear one another with respect I fear for our future For our children and grandchildren’s future They will inherit our dysfunction They will pay for our stubbornness and prejudice As we now pay for the prejudice, bigotry, and racism Of those who came before us It is impossible to prevent change from eventually happening But it’s very possible to create pain through its resistance The problem lies within each of us as individuals For in truth the only person we can change is ourselves If our energy was devoted to that, and not changing others We would make the journey so much easier Easier to pursue our own creativity, our own gifts To be a help to all humanity and not a hindrance Choose to engage in a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts A mind focused on the past or the future, missing the now Rest in the whispers of the heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, your healing

George G
