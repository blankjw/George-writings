WHEN WORDS DON'T MATTER

By George Gondron

WHEN WORDS DON'T MATTER There is a special connection From one person to another And hopefully many others When words don't matter To say, 'I love you', isn't necessary In those special relationships, love is known Without speaking, without the words With those unique relationships, it is seen It is when we part or say 'goodbye' over the phone And we add in the parting the words, 'I love you' It's more of a reminder that when we're separate I still love you, you are in my thoughts I still feel your presence, my love doesn't change If one needs to be reassured of another's love Then it's not love at all, but only attachment If love is not seen in the behavior Between you and I Then why trust the words? It is the same with all words, which are mostly labels And labels have different meanings for different people If the word has one meaning But the actions or non-actions another What do you trust, what do you believe? In fact, if there was trust before But now the words don't match the behavior Trust is lost, and difficult to earn back Perhaps in certain areas of life Like politics, advertising, marketing, and even religion The words don't match the truth, the behavior And we either turn a blind eye, or swallow the lie Or, perhaps we're brave enough to point out the obvious That what we hear is not what we see But then again if our need to be loved is too great Then we don't speak out, don't rock the boat So if you love another be sure your behavior speaks loudly For words are not enough, in action lies the truth For the other, as well as for ourselves

George G
