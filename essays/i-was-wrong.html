ESSAY

I WAS WRONG

By George Gondron

I WAS WRONG Probably the three most important words A single human being can utter When they honestly recognize an error In their thinking, feeling, and or action Is to simply say, ‘I was wrong’ But it seems that in our current environment It is more the exception than the rule If you have a person or persons in your life that can Utter those words when necessary Hold on to them, cherish them, they are gems And more often than not, they can be trusted The importance of being able to say those words Is first for self-honesty For the ability to acknowledge we’re not always right To look in the mirror and say to ourselves We can do better; right is not always might It is not a weakness to admit when one is wrong It is a strength The inability to say, ‘I was wrong’ is a weakness First a lie to ourselves, and then to others Unable to say, ‘I was wrong’, when appropriate Is probably one of the greatest reasons Relationships eventually dissolve It’s like there’s an elephant in the room But no one wants to talk about it We build walls instead of bridges Becoming closed rather than open When there’s a fight between right and wrong No one wins, both lose It is the heart that can say ‘I’m sorry, I was wrong’ But the ego clings to ‘rightness’ It’s ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ Everything changes, nothing lasts forever It may be necessary to let go Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness With Presence, reflect on your own ability to say I was wrong, I can do better, I will do better

George G
