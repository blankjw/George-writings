OFTEN ENOUGH

By George Gondron

OFTEN ENOUGH I wish I had smiled, written, called, hugged More often Some things are never often enough Missed opportunities don't repeat themselves Here and gone, between breaths In the blink of an eye, the frequent distraction The subsequent, 'I wish I had' Often three small words, I love you, I miss you Lie in the heart, and remain unspoken, unuttered Next time, I tell myself Tomorrow I won't forget Funny thing about time, it's not repeatable There is no rewind Slipping through our hands like water or sand Lost moments of connection, of consideration Just small words, little actions that say 'I value you, you are important to me' It takes very little, to make another's day It sends untold messages 'I've thought of you, I wish you the best, I share your burden, I celebrate your joy' The circumstance doesn't matter It is the recognition that matters In the end There really is no 'often enough' In the end it's simply awareness Awareness of the other and our connection Through awareness, what we do is Always enough

George G
