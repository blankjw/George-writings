ONE DAY

By George Gondron

ONE DAY Imagine for a moment If for just one day All humanity could express kindness for each other Could be other-focused rather than self-focused Just imagine The Dalai Lama once said if we would teach all eight-year-olds How to meditate In one generation the world would be changed Changed for the better, for all humanity It seems we will never have peace for all Until there is peace within When we cease to defend and choose to own Our own dysfunction, our own ego needs, and desires We live in a culture that has become very fragmented Very polarized as a result of dualistic and tribal thinking Everything is either/or, right/wrong, good/bad Which leaves no room for critical thinking In fact, hardly any thinking at all Merely follow your preferred bias Never question, never doubt, just accept Whoever or whatever you believe and whatever they’re selling The phrase ‘trust but verify’ has no meaning We seem to simply trust blindly when our emotions are triggered Without balance in our lives, outer and inner There is little hope of peace within, peace between, peace among To an outside observer, everything about our lives Is external with little evidence of an internal reference at all Even our posturing of any religious belief is external Rites and rituals, smells and bells, doctrine and dogma Choose a new path Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your ever-busy mind of its repetitive thoughts And listen to the whispers of your own heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, your true self They carry the possibility of peace and kindness for all Beginning with yourself Silence speaks, learn to listen

George G
