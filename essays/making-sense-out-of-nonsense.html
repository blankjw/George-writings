MAKING SENSE OUT OF NONSENSE

By George Gondron | Source: MAKING SENSE OUT OF NONSENSE.docx

MAKING SENSE OUT OF NONSENSE Three ‘myths’ of our present time ‘Justice is blind’, ‘No one is above the law’, Equal justice for all’ All three fall far short of their original intent and purpose The image of a blindfolded Lady Justice comes from the Greek Goddess Themis and the Roman goddess Justitia Both known for their good judgement and righteousness Our justice system is supposed to protect the rights of all people Without preference or influence from outside pressures And unless we have our heads buried in the sand or wear blinders We know that isn’t true, injustice is alive and well today Justice is not equal, and far too many are above the law In general terms what is true for one person is not true for another And if you have to ask, what’s the difference, then you are the fool But what’s even more disturbing is the choice to support those Who break the law, who think they’re above the law and do so With no fear of retribution at all, they are untouchable Power corrupts and absolute power corrupts absolutely Those who seek power, wealth, and control have one agenda And that agenda does not include you except as an obedient follower You are nothing more than a means to an end, their end The reliability of a meaningful law is the fact it is obeyed But if it’s obeyed by some and not by all, it has no meaning The purpose of all law at its root is protection Protection against, protection of, and protection for So, when the law is broken, so are protections It seems the words of Benjamin Franklin are beginning to ring hollow On Monday, September 17, 1787, Franklin was asked, “Well, Doctor, what have we got a republic or a monarchy?” Franklin replied, “A republic, if we can keep it.” When our laws begin to fail, I wonder if our Republic will follow As we refuse to educate ourselves, be critical thinkers and respond Only through our emotions we become blind to reality, to truth Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, awaken your center Choose a few minutes of silence, stillness, with Presence each day Make sense out of nonsense, seek understanding and awareness Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
