THE PROCESS OF KNOWING

By George Gondron

,THE PROCESS OF KNOWING How do we know ? We only truly know to the extent that we experience Our five senses, taste, touch, smell, hear, and see Direct personal experiences of knowing How we interpret those experiences further Through thinking and feeling is an entirely different matter For you and I can have the same experiences But have very different interpretations It greatly depends on our individual understanding of learning Do we simply accept what is as it is, being nothing more than a sponge Or as the Buddha once said, ‘my words should be accepted by ‘the wise, not out of regard for me, but after due investigation, ‘just as gold is accepted as true only after heating, cutting, and ‘rubbing’ Another way of saying, regardless of who says it We are responsible for the interpretation, the facts, the truth We have all the tools necessary beyond mere acceptance To discover the accuracy and validity of what we hear, see, and read The ‘buck’ always stops with each of us as individuals Trust relies on truth and once the truth is revealed as a lie But we continue to trust, we are the fools When a person shows you who they are through their behavior Not through their words, friend or stranger, believe them In our process of knowing three of the most difficult lessons To learn are when to say no, when to let go, when to embrace change Education is a perpetual task, from birth to death The only way to become fully human, fully alive A lie can be told many different ways in the guise of truth But genuine truth stands alone and needs no disguise We don’t increase our awareness and consciousness Through ‘unlearning’, but through new learning and experience A meditative/contemplative practice brings new positive learning A few minutes each day choose silence, be still, embrace Presence Your experience or image of Isness, Mystery of the universe Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, and patiently wait

George G
