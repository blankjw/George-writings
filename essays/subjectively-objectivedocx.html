SUBJECTIVELY OBJECTIVE

By George Gondron

SUBJECTIVELY OBJECTIVE The only way to see clearly, to perceive accurately Is to be delivered from our subjectivity For we are all, with no exception, addicted to our own thinking We all view the world from our own set of 'rose-colored glasses' So we are in fact, subjectively objective To see the right thing in the wrong way, is to not see at all It is seeing 'through a glass darkly' We can argue ad nauseam about theories and philosophies About political, religious, economic, cultural ideas and ideals But when the discussion involves life, all life And the dialogue slips into objectification, we are on a slippery slope For life is subjective, not an inanimate object It is not an object, as some would have us believe Life is our common connection to and with one another None better than the other, or more important than the other Equally significant, equally meaningful, all with a gift No one's rights are above another's rights No one's needs for the necessities of life are greater than another's There should be no restrictions on the human potential Regardless of race, color, or creed We are brought together in our sameness We grow together in our differences There is unity in our diversity, but not mandated uniformity A closed mind treats almost everything, everyone as an object A closed mind is a self made prison, locked from the inside An open mind treats others as subjects, reflections of themselves With common hopes, wishes, and dreams The meaning of life is a singular important individual question Each of us needs to reflect on that query For it guides what we do and how we do it It fashions our lifestyle, selfish or selfless, or a bit of both If people are objects, if all sentient beings are objects If all of life is objective only, we have lost our meaning We have bought into the illusion that it is 'things' that matter Not people, not tears, not smiles, not hugs, not laughter or sorrow Things Ask the question; what matters to you and how you achieve it

George G
