ESSAY

WITHOUT

By George Gondron

WITHOUT What we are without, more than what we have Is our struggle to become fully human, fully alive When we are without understanding, when our minds are closed When we defend and make excuses, rather than being responsible When we exclude, discriminate, disenfranchise, and judge When we lack compassion, empathy, and common decency When we lack critical thinking and merely accept without question When we choose to remain ignorant rather than to actually learn When our idea of love extends no further than our small group When we confuse believing with knowing When we don’t see our sameness and only our differences We are without, we are the problem, we build our prisons What we lack is what we need, what is important So, the question becomes, what in our culture encourages us To change on any sufficient scale that would leads us too no Longer being ‘without’? Answer, nothing In fact, there is more of a ‘don’t do this’ mentality when it comes To personal growth and change than a please, ‘do this’ Through distraction, medication, drugs, and alcohol We give ourselves permission to resist change and the ego loves it Once our ‘familiars’ have been established we are doomed Even if those familiars are painful, like the saying, ‘better to dance With the devil we know that the one we don’t know’
