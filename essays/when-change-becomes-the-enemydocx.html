WHEN CHANGE BECOMES THE ENEMY

By George Gondron

WHEN CHANGE BECOMES THE ENEMY In a closed system, a very narrow-minded system When one of its members changes, it shatters the reality Of all the other members, the group prefers the status quo By nature, the system resists change When we review history, it becomes obvious that all positive Progress was accomplished only after many failures and struggles Isn’t it interesting, we fail to teach in our educational systems About the struggles, about what came before the successes About our actual history, let’s just eliminate those unmentionable Events from our history books, and consequently history repeats itself The concept of ‘shadow’ in psychology, the parts of ourselves we Choose to ignore, reject, and deny, applies to the group as well as The individual, history is filled with evidence of the same Burning witches at the stake, the crusades, all wars, genocide, The Inquisition, Andrew Jackson’s attempted eradication of Native American Indians, apartheid, the Holocaust, Hiroshima, etc., etc. And now in our current time, the ‘shadow’ of unending conspiracy Theories and ‘deep state’ fearmongering by those in power or Those who wish to be in power, remember, power corrupts The only change that those of extreme wealth and power desire Is change that benefits themselves, not all others Others are merely pawns, a means to an end, their end And sadly, that power is not limited to the secular but is alive And well in the sacred as well Those in power survive by creating an illusion that we need them Change is a threat to them, change only happens when it’s to Their benefit, it’s easy to distinguish a character that is selfless As opposed to those that are selfish Those that insist on your ‘loyalty’ as opposed to expressing your own Creativity, potential, freedom, and equality, the differences are clear We need to return to our own ‘center’, connect inner and outer Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on a short mantra or on your breath, breathe in, breathe out The mind will wander, the ego will resist, rinse and repeat Listen to the discerning whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they patiently await

George G
