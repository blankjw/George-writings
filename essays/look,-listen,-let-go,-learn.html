LOOK, LISTEN, LET GO, LEARN

By George Gondron | Source: LOOK, LISTEN, LET GO, LEARN.docx

LOOK, LISTEN, LET GO, LEARN Unintentionally, unaware over the years We become fixed in our nature Fixed in our beliefs, our values, our way of thinking Our approach to life becomes very polarized and dualistic We know all the problems, we have all the answers, just ask us We become so stuck in our familiar, so mired in our patterns That what we think, feel, and do becomes an impediment A self-made prison to which we hold the key We can’t see the forest for the trees, we wear blinders We see and hear only what we wish to see and hear Our minds are ‘made up’, our minds are closed It’s a kind of righteousness with no valid proof Grounded in irrational emotions lacking proven facts When asked, ‘How do you know?’, reply, ‘I just know’ And then we are willing to suffer the consequences of ‘I just know’, whatever they may be To be able to learn, continually, is a blessing To refuse to learn is a curse And learning is not choosing one’s favorite social media Ignoring all others, we fail to recognize the importance of choice To judge without evidentiary proof is a fool's game Regardless of the topic, person, place, or thing Psychological defense mechanisms are for those who Refuse to learn, that cling to the familiar, and resist change They listen to defend, not to learn Be curious, question, doubt, research, critically think Look with fresh eyes, hear with open ears, let go Defense is nothing short of perpetual anxiety, it is useless Defensiveness has only one outcome Not to be right, but to remain stuck, to be closed Add this gift to your life, a daily meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes of intentional silence, with relaxed stillness In the presence of Presence, wordless relational interaction Empty your mind of all incessant and repetitive thought Be open, be defenseless, and listen to the whispers of Your true self, of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, they have your answers, they wait

George G
