OUR BROKENNESS

By George Gondron

OUR BROKENNESS Our most difficult journey is the journey within And the path to our wholeness is through our brokenness Most avoid the journey and few take the path We live in a culture where thinking is paramount but only as it’s activated by our emotions, especially negative emotions The reconciliation of opposites within ourselves is the place of tension, of anxiety, and to maintain and process that tension is to embrace the journey within that leads to self-actualization That leads to individuation, an increased evolving of awareness, where unconscious content becomes conscious Where shadow and light merge following a spiral toward balance, toward a genuine connection between inner and outer If we consciously choose this journey within there will be experiences of what Jung called a numinous nature, that are too self-evident to be denied where the idea of a Creator God is not to be proved or even believed, but a psychological fact of immediate experience Being open to the inward journey means being open to a context which allows for us to be present to and experience what does not happen in the course of our normal day-to-day activities In other words, we choose what is not a part of our familiar The choice of a context to further deepen our understanding and potential for new experiences along the way will vary For this writing I suggest a meditative/contemplative practice Something that is foreign to most in our culture, but throughout history has been practiced by all the major religions and belief systems including indigenous peoples as well In the religious orders of the Catholic faith, it has been a mainstay in their daily lives, as well in Buddhism and Hinduism Silence and stillness are important teachers, the ego resists and fears both, we have ‘monkey minds’ that are endlessly busy And as a result, in our Western culture we are very polarized and dualistic, either/or versus both/and thinkers, witness our society Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence You will struggle at first for the ego will resist but stay committed Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
