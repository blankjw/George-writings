THE SUFFERING OF GOD

By George Gondron

THE SUFFERING OF GOD A God who cannot suffer, cannot love, cannot relate One would think that Christians would know this since the One they profess to follow, Jesus, was according to Paul like us in every way except in the form of transgressions Medical doctors have written about the unbearable pain of crucifixion, God knows pain very well Perhaps the real problem lies in our concept of God Any conceptual talk of God according to the mystics is not God-talk but ego-ego, our projection of an ideal God Meister Eckhart, a famous Dominican priest and theologian, knew that the experience of God cannot to equated with the idea of God, he is quoted as saying, ‘I pray God rid me of God’ In our modern world we elevate God to a position of omnipotence, of omniscience, but seldom do we embrace the fact that God, in the person of Jesus, was fully human, fully relatable And there are those who believe through a scientific argument that God does not exist, or if He did, what kind of God allows for the suffering of children, war, famine, all manner of crime, etc., etc., etc. Ironically, quantum physics is postulating that the universe is conscious, is purposeful, evolving, and benevolent Is quantum physics more spiritual now than religion, if religion ever was, at least as a membership organization? What most people fail to understand or recognize in modern culture is that many of life’s greatest understandings are not cognitive but experiential, arising from allowing ourselves to embrace a context different from our familiars, our normal, our patterns Experience is the best teacher Maybe the argument against a Creator, lies in the behavior of humans – Jesus told his disciples you will do greater things than me But when religion is a matter of the mind and not the heart, a matter of worship and not practice, we witness clearly the results Please, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Each day spend a few minutes in silence, stillness, with Isness Connecting inner and outer, bring balance to your life Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out When the ego resists, the mind wanders, simply rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit

George G
