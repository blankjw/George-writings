CONFIRMED BIAS

By George Gondron | Source: CONFIRMED BIAS.docx

CONFIRMED BIAS Bias, an irrational preference or prejudice In most cases, those preferences have no factual basis Formed from a mindset that operates primarily From an emotional rather than an intellectual influence And those influences are almost always negative An anti, judgmental, critical, subjective, point of view A narrowness of mind with preconceived opinions And when challenged use third-party information To defend and support their bias A closed mind will always seek that which supports Their particular bias, their preferences, their prejudice And with the numerous social media sources available That support can easily be discovered A few keywords in a search engine and there it is And the proponent of the bias can say ‘See, I told you so’ When we refuse to use critical thinking When we don’t question or doubt or do our own research We fall into the trap of simply accepting what is offered With no filters whatsoever, garbage in, garbage out We attempt to influence others with our own unfounded bias To seek the truth, even at the expense of standing alone Is better than believing the lie, always Great changes throughout history have always been From those who stood alone, who challenged the lie Who refused to accept the status quo Who weren't afraid to ‘go it alone’, were willing to say no It requires strength and courage to question one’s own bias A process that can’t be achieved with the mind, the ego alone I encourage you, do this Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice Connect with your inner core, your internal reference Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, empty your mind Be in the presence of Presence, your image of God Breathe in, breathe out a short mantra to stay focused Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
