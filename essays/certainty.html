CERTAINTY

By George Gondron

CERTAINTY Certainty is the enemy of tolerance Certainty believes it is never wrong And certainty has a closed mind The inflexibility of certainty births the dysfunction of Exclusion, negativity, separation, and partisanship An ego of certainty sees reality from a very narrow lens It lacks flexibility, spontaneity, and understanding With a mindset of ‘I might not be right, but I’m never wrong’ Uncertainty is a sign of weakness, when in fact, uncertainty Is an indication of strength, a willingness to be introspective To objectively examine one’s own beliefs, critically Those things about which we are certain, are few Like attempting to hold on to a fistful of water We can’t even be certain of our next breath or heartbeat Certainty is the enemy of personal growth, new awareness, And greater understanding, it limits our potential Certainty is evidence we truly are our own worst enemy To be uncertain, to question and doubt, to critically think Is evidence of an open mind, a mind willing to learn Underlying the basic emotions of a closed mind is fear A mind that lives in the past out of anger and resentment Or fears the future because of uncertainty and insecurity All the while missing the most important moment, now Love on the other hand, sees potential and possibility Dealing with what is and not what was or may be Our greatest problems are our resistance to learn and our Insistent on depending on our egos alone for our decisions Neglecting our own innate internal resources, our inner core Those gifts that make us fully human, connecting to our capabilities To be kind, compassionate, inclusive, and nonjudgmental In our Western culture we lack balance, inner and outer Our focus is on the externals, motivated to be consumers, ‘doers’ Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for necessary balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Your better angels, they hold your true self and have your answers

George G
