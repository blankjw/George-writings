UNLOVE

By George Gondron

UNLOVE How do you unlove someone, forget what once was Erase the memories of the happier moments, you can't No matter the reason for the parting, the bond lasts Not as an attachment, not a clinging, not even a regret Simply the recognition of the value of what once was Granted it was a perceived value based on many things Most of which, in looking back, were selfish Believing that the emotional attachment was love But love is not an attachment at all Real love is totally unselfish, is freedom itself Time has a way of cleansing our perception As we become more aware, more conscious Regardless of the lives that have touched ours Whether few or many, something was exchanged There was a bond, a connection, an experience We keep what helped us learn, to become more Every life we touch has something to teach us Maybe those experiences have been instrumental In teaching us not what to see or hear But rather, how to see and hear Pain has a way of helping us deny that a bond once existed That something brought us together Whether, acquaintance, friend, lover, or spouse No matter the circumstance, the occasion, the reason That broke the bond emotionally and physically We can't 'unremember', 'unlove' the happier moments There is a lesson in every encounter, if we are present The saying, 'love is blind' is as far from the truth As the sun orbits the earth Love is never blind, attraction is blind Love sees clearly and responds accurately One doesn't 'fall in love', real love chooses We fall in 'attachment', we fall in 'desire', not love Love does not fail, our ideas about love fail Love is our greatest teacher, many miss the lesson What once we truly loved, cannot be unloved Just as what we learn cannot be unlearned, only added to

George G
