KNOWLEDGE VERSUS KNOWING

By George Gondron | Source: KNOWLEDGE VERSUS KNOWING.docx

KNOWLEDGE VERSUS KNOWING One can have a great deal of knowledge With very little knowing For knowing is experiential, knowledge is intellectual The ability to learn accumulates knowledge The willingness to experience creates knowing There are far too many who have knowledge But far too few with experience but yet believe they know Far too many who ‘don’t believe what they see’ And only ‘half of what they hear’ The self-reflective critical thinkers are in the minority The American showman P T Barnum once said ‘There’s a sucker born every minute’ Translated, people are easily manipulated and controlled What is witnessed on a TV screen or a computer monitor A laptop or telephone is only knowledge, not knowing But without critical thinking, without questions Without doubt, and without the research and fact-checking It is only knowledge, it is not knowing through experience It is as the saying goes, ‘drinking the Kool-Aid’ We currently live in a culture that is based on consumption Consumption depends on belief The belief that you need what you don’t need Whether you can afford it or not We are programmed, conditioned, and manipulated Through advertising, media, and ‘snake-oil salesman’ To believe what we hear and see without any evidence It is applicable to every area of our lives without exception Both secular and sacred We each have a ‘holder of the truth’ inside of us But without a practice that nurtures and develops Our internal reference our inner core We never separate fact from fiction Spend a few minutes each day in a contemplative practice Experience silence, with stillness, and Presence daily Empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts That go nowhere and accomplish nothing Listen to the whispers of your own heart, soul, and spirit

George G
