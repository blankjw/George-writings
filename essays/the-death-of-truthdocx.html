THE DEATH OF TRUTH

By George Gondron

THE DEATH OF TRUTH

The sacrifice of values, ethics, and morals The death of truth Is never a sudden event It's like a cancer that slowly kills its host Like water dripping on a stone The stone will eventually erode and be worn away And then one day, without question The lie becomes the truth, truth no longer matters Or maybe we can replace the word truth with fact Maybe facts no longer matter But I think it's probably both What is it in the human mind that brings it To accept the lie and deny the facts? Maybe morality and ethics in our leadership don't matter Or perhaps we have become so accustomed To immoral and unethical behavior, we no longer care I think maybe kindness and compassion are dying If kindness and compassion are only for the ones like us 'Us' being those like ourselves, excluding all others Then the better description is selfishness Not kindness and compassion, sympathy not empathy Our individual truth is limited to our individual experience What about the larger truth, that touches all humanity? To each individual that we halfheartedly proclaim Liberty, justice, and equality for all people The truth that history's great mystics and spiritual teachers Have all taught, all been in agreement with That life is love, love is relationship, relationship with all Even if we consider there is a God, it's our God And then we kill each other over who has the right God While God weeps We kill for only one reason, selfishness, not for God We would rather compete than cooperate Exclude rather than include, deny rather than accept Be a part of the bigger Truth, be love Don't let truth die

George G
