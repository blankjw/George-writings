THE GREATEST FALLACY

By George Gondron | Source: THE GREATEST FALLACY.docx

THE GREATEST FALLACY To believe without evidence is the greatest deception Even genuine faith is not blind, it is experiential A felt sense that requires no scientific evidence All that’s required is clarity of perception Would that were true about millions who believe What they see and hear on social media, without question The old saying, ‘fool me once shame on you, fool me twice Shame on me’, has no relevance for those who blindly follow Abandoning their ability to think critically, rationally Most success is preceded by failure first That failure included some form of effort to some degree Blindly following or accepting requires no effort at all And in the end It always leads to failure, emotional attachment Probably the greatest fallacy is when we lie to ourselves Those little thoughts, feelings, and actions That don’t quiet measure up, not only personally But perhaps in our relationships as well Maybe not intentionally, maybe we just cling to hope That something will change, but here’s a secret We only have the power to change one person, ourselves Everything we think, feel, do or do not do is a matter of choice The only ‘if only’ that enters our mind need be about Ourself, not he. she, it, or they Dualistic thinking is not rational or logical If my choices are only two, either A or B, I really have no choice For if I reject either one, I have to choose the other We must have more than two options for a both/and approach To life as opposed to an either/or approach And when 130 million adults in our society have a literacy level Of sixth grade, we have a lot of dualistic thinkers, frightening Educate yourself, don’t be deceived, reject the many fallacies For balance add a meditative/contemplative practice to your life Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, embrace Presence Empty your mind of incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath, in and out, or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, and patiently wait

George G
