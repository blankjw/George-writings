THE PRACTICE OF CONTEMPLATION

By George Gondron

THE PRACTICE OF CONTEMPLATION Contemplation: observation, being present to, without thinking To empty the mind of all thoughts is anathema to the ego It will resist with every trick imaginable A battle between the essence of one’s core and their own mind But it is a battle, that if accomplished, has many rewards Honest contemplation has no filters, no preconceived agenda, No conversation, no thoughts, no defenses, no judgments The mind, on the other hand, always has an agenda The mind fears emptiness for it only identifies with its thoughts It does not know or cannot experience the nature of just being And as a result will never benefit form the gifts of a consistent Contemplative practice, afraid to be silent, still, and present If we are only our thoughts, especially our repetitive thoughts Almost everything is self-referential, about my preferences My past, my future, my feelings, my actions, etc., etc., etc. We observe from our own bias and prejudice We have answers to questions that haven’t been asked Consequently, what’s true, what’s real, cannot get through In our Western culture, the ability to simply observe Without our internal commentaries is virtually nonexistent We have an opinion about everything and under examination Most of those opinions have no validity, they are only preferences They fit our current beliefs, not our current experiences Contemplation is a pathway to experiencing and knowing Isness, Universal Consciousness, Mystery, Presence, Creator, God, whatever name you prefer for the origin and Continued existence of this amazing universe, this is no accident Even quantum physics hypothesizes that the universe is conscious Is purposeful, evolving, and benevolent A practice of contemplation can only add to who we each are Increase our awareness, our understanding, our consciousness Almost all good things for our personal growth are not ego ideals But heart yearnings, ‘it is only with the heart that one sees rightly’ Take a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out The ego will resist, the mind will wander, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
