HOW AND WHY

By George Gondron

HOW AND WHY It’s interesting the way we use words How and why are both used to seek information One elicits a defensive response and the other an objective query One is more about an interrogation, the other about information When we are asked ‘why’, the mind goes into a defensive mode And when we’re defending, we feel guarded and vulnerable We’re not open and as well our ability to listen with clarity is lessened The scope of our response becomes very narrow as we struggle to Defend our action or our inaction, it’s a no-win situation But if we are asked ‘how’ something happened we are more open And feel less defensive and guarded, we’re not being attacked And consequently, are more able to explain with a reasonable reply The ‘how’ question is not interpreted as accusatory but sincere A ‘why’ question contains a subtle meaning that somehow we are Flawed and are guilty before proven innocent The ‘how’ question seeks more information surrounding the Action or behavior in question and we are more at ease in our Response if we’re not having to defend It can easily be stated, small minds ask ‘why’ Open minds ask ‘how’ ‘How’ seeks clarity and understanding ‘Why” wants, needs, demands and an explanation More importantly, if we begin by asking ourselves, what is Our own process about our own actions and inaction Our own thoughts and feelings, our own perceptions Do we ask the ‘why’ of those things or the ‘how’ And if we can, that will be our greatest teacher, self-understanding That really is our greatest task, to understand ourselves To become more aware, more conscious, more fully human To reach that place where we welcome change and not resist it To let-go and not cling to old patterns, routines, and habits We need balance, inner and outer, focus on how, resist why Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
