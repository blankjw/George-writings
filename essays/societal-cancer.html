SOCIETAL CANCER

By George Gondron

SOCIETAL CANCER A dualistic approach to reality A closemindedness toward seeking the truth An attachment to distractions To be guided by one’s emotions rather than intelligence A propensity to follow blindly the ‘soothsayer’ To cling to the past and/or fear the future The inability to have an open discussion Knowing the answer before hearing the problem What we think, feel, and do is right What you/they think, feel, and do is wrong What we fail to develop and mature within ourselves Our negative versus positive thinking Our defensive and argumentative nature Our refusal to be reflective critical thinkers To react rather than respond To judge rather than seek to understand All of the above, a societal cancer Slowly destroying itself from the inside out Unable to ‘see the forest for the trees’ Unwilling to ‘walk in another’s shoes’ Unwilling to compromise, conciliate, cooperate, coexist We are the perpetrators of our own demise, totally unaware We lack balance between inner and outer Our lives are all about the externals, the material What have or do not have Our persona, our facade, our masks, our ego We lack a connection to our innate inner core An internal reference connected to the Mystery of life Not a religious attachment but a spiritual reality Which joins all of existence universally without exception Choose a meditative/contemplative daily practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of Presence, detach from distraction Listen to the whispers and yearnings held within Within your heart, soul, and spirit They hold all the answers, the Truth, your true self They patiently wait, let go, and become

George G
