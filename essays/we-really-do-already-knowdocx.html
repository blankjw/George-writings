WE REALLY DO ALREADY KNOW

By George Gondron

WE REALLY DO ALREADY KNOW All the great mystics, spiritual teachers, philosophers Tell us in one way or another, we have within us the answers To all our important questions, all the necessary resources They come with this gift we call our life And with the proper nurturing and healthy role models They will blossom into the potential and creativity that is Unique to our existence, our personhood, our character Unfortunately, we teach that learning, through formal Education is the pathway to knowing, it’s not It’s the pathway to believing Personal knowing is only found through experience If what we take in is never put into practice, it’s only belief So, if we never venture outside our limited knowledge And never explore through direct experience, how do we know? And therein lies the root cause of all the chaos and turmoil We witness in our nation today We rely on outside sources for our beliefs, not our experiences He, she, they said, or it was on social media, and we swallow it One of the most dangerous results of this kind of maturity Or should I say lack thereof, is formal organized religion In all its many forms, it’s intellectual with a few rites and rituals Thrown in for flavor, supported by dogma and doctrine Let me challenge you with this one question if you believe In God, where in your religious beliefs or education is there A teaching or concept about having a personal relationship With your image of God? Throughout history there are numerous Authors that write about that personal experience We each, in our own way, stand in the way of that possibility We fear or resist the requirements necessary for that experience A few minutes each day of silence, stillness, with Presence Are all that’s required, if we’re willing to set aside our inflated egos To empty our minds of their incessant and repetitive thoughts Which seems so simple, is very hard for the ego, the ‘monkey mind’ It is in those moments that we listen for the whispers of our heart, Soul, and spirit, the yearnings of our inner core, that relationship They are our true self, have our answers, and they wait Remember, nothing ventured, nothing gained

George G
