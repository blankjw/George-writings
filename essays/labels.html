LABELS

By George Gondron

LABELS Through our labels we risk not knowing, not understanding But we think we do Labels are only words, poor descriptors at best Never the thing in itself Never the essence of what is labeled People in particular are never their labels Their persona is only a thin veneer of who they truly are The greatest labels begin with 'I' I am important, successful, mature, and valued I am sad, angry, happy, joyful But, you know, I've never seen an 'I' Who is this 'I'? As I sit here writing this reflection, I can see the writer Who is doing the 'seeing'? I can even see the writer, seeing the writer Still more perplexing, this 'I' The best that can be said, is that 'I' experiences Yet, it is not what it experiences It is undefined, limitless, boundless 'I' is beyond description I can't hold 'I' in my grasp, or think it out of existence It just is, it can't be labeled But, I use many labels each day to define who I am And, unfortunately for most, including myself We begin to be attached to our labels Thinking we are our labels, and we're not And sadly, the more we're attached the more we miss The more 'stuck' we become in our labels We experience a multitude of feelings, thoughts, actions We have many roles But, we're not any of those 'things' They are only experiences, they are not 'me' or 'you' We are so much more Drop the labels, trust the phrase, 'I' am experiencing In this moment 'I' am experiencing, but 'I' am not the experience We are so much more We are life, emerging from Love, we are each other

George G
