WHAT'S RELATIVE

By George Gondron

WHAT'S RELATIVE All of nature follows its innate contingent course And that is to reflect the richness of its Creator All that is, except one, the human being And what is so different in the human? Perhaps it's consciousness But according to some, all of creation has a form of consciousness So let's not confuse consciousness with free will Obviously what's relative between all of creation and humans Is that little 'thing' called choice It seems all of nature does what it's designed to do Through lack of choice, which really makes one wonder What was the Creator thinking Giving human beings the ability to choose? Giving human beings free will? I suspect what's important in asking that question Is perhaps how one views relationships I suppose that if I had to love you As opposed to making a choice to love you The relationship would not be very fulfilling In that regard, free choice is a big risk But that's what love is, a risk, not a guarantee Well, it is a guarantee from the side of the Giver For Love cannot not love Would that humans were the same But we, as the receivers don't have to accept that love Which is quiet evident based on how we treat one another However, there was a man, some 2,000 years ago I believe Jesus was his name That taught what was relative to life is love It's really amazing as we recognize all that humanity Has accomplished, but yet we have failed Failed on that one major lesson of loving 'Love one another as I have loved you' In the end there are only two requirements for humans Love your image of God, love your neighbor as yourself If it could not be done It would not have been made manifest

George G
