MEMORY

By George Gondron | Source: MEMORY.docx

MEMORY Both a blessing and a curse, memory A perceptive instructor, at times Teaching us what's important to retain The history that's worth recalling Valuable lessons, hard won at a cost Fond reflections of loved ones Memories of the heart Nonetheless, there remains the curse Unwilling, unable, powerless to forget A form of self-punishment, holding on can be Holding on to yesterdays pain and loss Clinging to hurtful moments, to no avail Of what possible use? Whatever the reason, they have passed They contaminate the present, the now They cloud today and strangle awareness The mind does not like to forgive The ego does not like to forget They both, like a plague, destroy themselves Destroy us The heart holds the answer The spirit within is our Healer The silence is our Teacher The stillness is our Renewal The presence is our Companion Yesterday has passed, hold on to the good Tomorrow is not yet, let go of the fear The moment is now, cherish the moment

George G
