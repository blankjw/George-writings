MIND TRAPS

By George Gondron

MIND TRAPS The ways in which we paint ourselves into corners In which we seem to have the same thoughts, feelings, issues Day after day, moment by moment Our own personal ‘mind traps’, our familiars that we cling to All of which keep us stuck and limit our potential, our creativity No one is exempt, some worse than others, But it’s an inclusive group, no one is left out, and all belong The ‘traps’ are legion, abiding in our thoughts especially We call the psychological defense mechanisms, defenses For the simple reason, they are the various accommodations We use to defend our own behaviors Of which two of the primary ones are denial and blame Both can be defined in different ways depending on the circumstances If ‘Utopia’ ever arrives it will be when there’s nothing else to defend Within ourselves, between ourselves, and among ourselves The process of self-criticism, the journey of discovering the self Includes examining the ‘mind traps’ we engage in, personally How do we make excuses for our own behavior Rather than taking ownership of our own behavior? It’s not easy, it’s not the way of the mind, the ego If we’re honest, we don’t like to look at ourselves For if we do, we will discover, sooner or later, our ‘mind traps’ When there is balance in our lives, both/and as opposed to Either/or thinking, feeling, and actions We begin to eliminate the ‘mind traps’ Remove the ‘rose-colored glasses’, broth bring clarity of perception The only thing that allows us to respond accurately A choice to spend a few minutes each day in a Meditative/contemplative practice is a wonderful beginning point For the development of your own interior life, inner reference Even in our moments of relaxation, our minds are forever active Filled with incessant and often repetitive thoughts, that are useless Merely unnecessary distractions, keeping us stuck Relax, sit quietly, empty the mind, focus on a short manta Breathe in, breathe out, wellness to yourself, wellness to others The mind will resist, the ego will reject, but stick with it Begin with just five minutes, hear the whispers of your heart

George G
