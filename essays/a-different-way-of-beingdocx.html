A DIFFERENT WAY OF BEING

By George Gondron

A DIFFERENT WAY OF BEING There is no doubt that if each of us would spend a Few moments reflecting on how we could be better, Kinder, more compassionate, more Inclusive, less Judgmental and bias our nation would be a better place But we don’t for the fault is never mine or yours Rather it’s always them, they are the problem Actually, we can discover any number of reasons Instead of pointing our fingers elsewhere We like the bubble we live in, our comfort zone Our small little world protected by our own misgivings Our sense of self-righteousness, our small, closed minds For after all we have the right answers, the right solutions Could it be we lack understanding, could it? Or could it be we just don’t want to know We really don’t want to educate ourselves For if we did, we might have to admit we could be In error about a few things, we might be wrong Ah, but it’s so much easier just to accept the ‘gaslighting’ Which when we look in the mirror, a reflection of Ignorance peers back at us, the unwillingness to know I do know this, it’s very important even necessary That we occasionally examine our own beliefs Our own implacable positions, our constant familiar Revealing that who we where yesterday, who we are today, And who we will be tomorrow is unchanging It is constant, it is stagnant, it is not open, it is closed To aid toward allowing ourselves to have a new understanding Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, choose balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Your ego will resist, your mind will wander, come back, focus Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
