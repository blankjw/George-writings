THE POWER OF LOVE

By George Gondron

THE POWER OF LOVE Let’s begin with the sad truth we have diminished the power of love The Greeks had a very clear understanding that love has many faces Many words that describe love in different circumstances Eros: romantic, passionate love Philia: intimate, authentic friendship Storge: unconditional, familial love Agape: empathetic, universal love Philautia: compassionate self-love In every instance the love was a result of some action, some doing Not just some emotion or feeling Not only have we limited our understanding of what love Really is, an action, but we have lessened its importance In our day-to-day vocabulary I loved that meal, that book, that movie, that trip, etc., etc., etc. When the actual feeling connected to those things are not love They are pleasure, excitement, joy, wonder, something we Experience through our senses, taste, touch, smell, hear, see We are so conditioned through our thinking that most of us Have difficulty identifying our feelings ‘I feel like you should’ – that’s a thought, not a feeling Never forget, love is an action, something we do for the benefit Of another or others, including ourselves, even a sacrifice Genuine love has no expectations, no string attached No ‘tit-for-tat’, no ‘quid pro quo’, only fulfilling an obvious need Attempting to relieve a small amount suffering in a world Filled with ‘takers’, blamers, deniers, the greedy and the selfish It is not in the receiving that we become rich, it’s in the giving It’s in our loving of others, the disenfranchised, those not like us The planet has more than enough resources to alleviate all Suffering, what is lacking is love, compassion, empathy, understanding The beginning of genuine love for others is loving ourselves The good and the bad, the light and the dark, strength and weakness We truly are, all more alike than unalike Choose a meditative/contemplative practice Spend time in silence, stillness, with Isness, your image of God Empty your mind, listen to your inner guides They hold your true self, your loving self, they wait

George G
