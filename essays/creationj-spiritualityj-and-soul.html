CREATION^J SPIRITUALITY^J AND SOUL

By George Gondron

CREATION, SPIRITUALITY, AND SOUL All current major beliefs systems, including indigenous beliefs Use language or narrative that speaks to an original creation And various tales about spirituality and soul One of the major thoughts goes something like this How can what is originally created not be infused with the Essence of That which creates? Put another way, everything that was, is, or will be Is nothing more than what was in the beginning Only in another form Energy is neither created nor destroyed, it transforms So, in a very real way, we are all stardust The atoms that make up our physical being have been here Since the beginning and for lack of a better description The DNA of That which created is also within each of us We are made in the image and likeness of – I read that somewhere It’s really not a reasonable premise to separate, divide, and exclude For in a real way, we, everything is/are all one And yes, I know it strains the brain to think in this way But the challenge is worth the effort And then there’s the connection between spirituality and soul Religion doesn’t feed the soul, spirituality does Religion feeds the mind’s need for understanding The soul already understands innately The souls need is relationship with Mystery, with Isness Developed through the practice of one’s own spiritual development Not through worship, but through experiential connection Spirituality is more about being empty than full More about nothing than something In our attempts to become more spiritual, our mind is the enemy You can’t think your way into becoming spiritual, it won’t work Not thinking at all, learning to be, adopting a contemplative practice Is one experiential path to one’s own spirituality A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence An emptying of the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Thoughts that defeat the possibility of a spiritual experience A time to listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit For they hold your true self, have your answers, and they wait

George G
