TEARS OF SILENCE

By George Gondron

TEARS OF SILENCE If we live long enough in this culture of disconnection Simply one of a number, recognized or not If we are fortunate enough to have a circle of friends Friends in the truest sense of the word We may eventually come to a place of knowing Knowing how we are more alike than unalike A place where ego is no longer important We will have a choice, a chance A chance to become compassionate people As we recognize our own brokenness Our own need to be a part of the whole Part of the whole of humanity And not just a singular isolated individual Recognizing the importance of inclusion No longer a mentality of 'me and mine' But now 'ours', together not separate When we can see man's inhumanity to man And not turn away, but face the obvious Then we will experience tears of silence Our hearts will weep, our souls will cry out Our spirits yearning for peace, mercy, and justice That yearning dwells within each of us, no exception It only needs nurturing, only asks to be heard And if we shut out that inner whisper If we are too blinded to see our connections Then we also will never be aware of our true self We will forever live in the false self, asleep, unconscious Lost in a world of illusion believing we are alive Going through the motions and missing the mystery And our hearts shed silent tears We are one, you and I, we are unimagined wonders Blinded by unending desires, walking down a path That leads nowhere Our joys live on the surface of our existence But our souls ache for the divine, in each of us We are the gatekeepers of our own bliss But we've lost the key, we've bought into the lie

George G
