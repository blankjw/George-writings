LISTENING IS LOVING

By George Gondron

LISTENING IS LOVING If you really want to show your love To someone special, a family member, a friend And even the stranger, learn how to listen Most of us listen to respond not to learn We already have an agenda in our minds A narrative that is under the influence Of our own thoughts, feelings, and opinions We listen passively, not actively As a result, we miss most of what is being said Here’s a test to see if you’re a good listener Sit quietly, relax, and breathe in the number one Breathe out the number one, attempt to reach ten But if your mind wanders you must return to one I’ll make you wager, it you’re honest You’ll never get beyond one Learning to be an active listener takes practice Lots of practice, it is a skill like any other Most of what we hear is already conditioned By our unconscious preferences, influenced by our beliefs By our own programming, biases, and prejudices Remember, clarity of perception leads to accuracy of response So, if the hearing is not accurate, neither is our response Even in our own reflective thoughts We’re only hearing what we already believe or know The ego doesn’t like change The important choice of beginning a meditative/contemplative Practice is also a choice of learning to love ourselves Learning to empty our ever-busy ‘monkey minds’ Of their incessant and repetitive thoughts A daily routine of just a few minutes, 20 to 30 To be silent, to be still, to be in the Presence of our image of God We learn to listen to the gentle whispers Of our own hearts, souls, and spirits To connect to our own inner yearnings To seek a deeper relationship with the Consciousness Of the universe, to Isness To awaken our inner core to what patiently awaits

George G
