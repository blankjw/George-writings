EXPERIENTIAL KNOWING

By George Gondron

EXPERIENTIAL KNOWING To believe and to know very different experiences We can believe without knowing We can’t know without believing Most people fall within the former category They say they believe without experience to back it up Nothing good comes from convincing the masses To believe something without their knowing But as history reveals, it’s not difficult at all Simply appeal to their emotions, find a scapegoat Separate and divide, us against them, always works People will claim they want to know, but they really don't They want to be fed, they want to be swayed They want to know without the effort of knowing Knowing only changes through a new experience Belief can change through a new narrative Or a new ‘soothsayer’, or ‘snake-oil salesman’ And by the time we realize we’ve been sold a ’bill of goods’ It is usually too late, the damage has been done And if the damage is not too severe, we may recover Education is so very important because it provides a doorway To discover the truth, experience the truth Rather than merely soaking up what’s offered as factual The definition of ignorance is the unwillingness to learn To take what is offered and believe it with no facts, just words It’s interesting to note that if you ask someone how they know What they adamantly believe, most often the reply is ‘Well so-and-so said’, and that’s who runs the nation, so-and-so While we dance to the beat of another drummer I offer this challenge, how much do you believe That you have never experienced? Held within yourself is a wealth of knowledge untapped Add a few minutes of silence, and stillness, with Presence each day Empty your mind and learn to listen to your internal reference Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Let the noise of your incessant and repetitive thoughts wan Discover your true self, not the egoic false self Discern your inner truth, it patiently awaits

George G
