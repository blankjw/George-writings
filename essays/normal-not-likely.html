NORMAL - NOT LIKELY

By George Gondron

NORMAL Normal, a normally 'loaded' word The 'normal' word, launches unending arguments Breakup of relationships, misunderstandings, even war All over a six letter word with a million definitions Let's begin Who's normal, mine or yours, his or hers, ours or theirs? For what's normal to you is not necessarily what's normal to me In fact there's no perfect definition for normal We can say however, nature is normal The seasons show up every year, that's normal The sun rises every morning, sets every evening It's normal for water to be wet and fire hot Almost all sentient beings follow their normal patterns All that is, except humans It seems there are as many normals for human beings As there are human beings What I repeat consistently, as well as you Becomes our normal As well as what we think and feel, do or don't do So for me to accuse you of not being normal Is probably because I'm making that decision based on my normal Not yours When our normal acts as a hindrance, an obstacle, an impediment Then it's abnormal, it holds us back And yet, most of us will continue to hang on Hang on, because it's still normal, still familiar, right? Interestingly enough, most animals, finding after repetition That something will not work, abandon the continued effort Not us humans, we never say never Even when there's clear evidence to the contrary Would that we could all find a way to be non-dualistic To include our hearts, souls, and spirits in every moment To make loving kindness, mercy and peace our normal Along with compassion, altruism, and charity To each have a relational spiritual connection A connection with our individual experiences of God If those were our normals, imagine the results

George G
