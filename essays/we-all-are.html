WE ALL ARE

By George Gondron

WE ALL ARE Our beginnings are all the same A mystery of conception In one magical moment, it is not yet known What or who we will become Not yet gender-specific, not yet gender-oriented Within that first cell is our entire being Our basic nature given in a microscopic dot Invisible to the naked eye Containing body, mind, soul, and spirit We are, first, a human being Creations by what is ineffable, unknowable Called by some; I Am, Isness, Love, or simply God As human beings we are different, no two alike Our numbers as heterosexuals are in the majority Translated as 'straight', as if that's better If there was no fear of being different Different according to only the 'straight' majority Indeed, 15 to 20 percent of all humans could say I'm not 'straight', but I am a human being Being not 'straight', is not a choice Just as being 'straight', is not a choice God makes no mistakes; we are all human beings first The LGBT community suffers at the hands of fear Fear of difference by those called 'straight' I am 'straight', but I am a human being first I do not fear what God has made perfect What I do fear is ignorance, bias, unconsciousness I do fear prejudice, bigotry, discrimination, and hatred We all are a mystery of conception, all human beings We all are a creation of Love We all are

George G
