UNTRUTH

By George Gondron

UNTRUTH An untruth, just a softer way of removing the sharper edges Of what otherwise is called a lie Something which we all have done, told a lie Admittedly some lies are necessary, most are not If we must lie to protect a life, ours or another's A life that would otherwise suffer, the lie is necessary Beyond that it's never necessary to lie When we misrepresent the truth, it's a lie As we defend dysfunctional behavior with a smile on our face That also is an untruth, perhaps the worst For it's the lie we tell ourselves When we spread rumors, unfounded rumors, it's a lie Distortion, deceit, pretense, evasion All are untruths, told either intentionally or unawares They all color, twist, misrepresent, or slant the truth And when a language of untruth becomes our norm The little 'white lies' become our standard Then we become stimulated and aroused by false rhetoric We believe the BS, the propaganda, the third party garbage Spread to stir up distrust, doubt, and disbelief Spread by throwing more fuel onto an already raging discontent The only way of winning is through truth, not the untruths But in a culture swimming in untruths Inundated with false propaganda, political pandering The fundamentalists, the literalists, of a patriarchal religion Shouting not the teaching of the human Jesus Nor the loving kindness of a compassionate Buddha The peace of a Martin Luther King, a Gandhi, a Mother Teresa Preaching only what's necessary to maintain their membership Nothing more, nothing less How do we free ourselves from these untruths? How do we wean ourselves from the ego's need to resist change? Transform and change ourselves into what we already know To what resides within each of us, our own individual truth Given in this gift we call life, through unconditional Love In stillness and silence, being present to Presence, in the whisper We will discover the ultimate Truth, the eternal Truth, called Love

George G
