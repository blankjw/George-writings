IN THE MIDST OF LIFE

By George Gondron

IN THE MIDST OF LIFE Whatever we may have experienced Is not what determines who we become It is what we have learned from those experiences That's what fashions our nature, makes us who we are Failure is as much a gift, as success, sometimes greater A teacher of what not to repeat in our next attempt There is an undeniable truth that supports all risk Whatever is worth doing Is worth doing wrong the first time The hardest lessons have the most rewarding results What it took to get us to the many plateaus in our life Is what we remember the most What is necessary for us to learn In this mystery of being human If not embraced, if held at bay or pushed away Will never let us go The yearnings may be a silent whisper But they will persist, no matter how hard we resist In the midst of our lives There is no destination, no goal There is only the journey, which most will miss Their lives filled with busyness at the expense of being Every moment of our existence, possibility surrounds us Every breath we take there is something new to be learned The boundary of the achievable doesn't exist The limits of the conceivable are restricted by false reasoning Our minds, our egos are all that hold us back In the midst of our lives if we never know compassion Not sympathy or pity, but true compassion The giving of ourselves with no expectation of return For the benefit of the suffering, the needy, the different If we haven't experienced that We are lesser for not knowing There remains a hole in our soul It is not what we gather that matters In the midst of our life It's what we give away, of ourselves

George G
