ESSAY

OTHERNESS

By George Gondron

OTHERNESS For a very long time now There continues to be too much ‘me’ And far too little ‘other’ Too much judgment, too little understanding Too much polarization and opposition Too little inclusion and cooperation When ‘I’ am more important than ‘you’ When ‘we’ are more valuable than ‘them’ Nothing changes, it only worsens It only perpetuates itself, creating a deeper divide The mind, the ego embraces one of three stances Denial, blame, or ignorance, blind to reality Who needs to change? Well, of course, they do, not me, not us Our personal problems are never fixed changing others If we can’t change ourselves, change our minds Nothing will change When we cling to concepts, ideas, beliefs That were necessary hundreds of years ago That were appropriate for the time But are now antiquated and outdated Then we choose ignorance over evidence We bury our heads in the sand, ignoring facts And continue to live by ‘what-about-isms’ Live through, ‘yes but’ and fear tactics Maybe we need to listen to the truth of ‘otherness’ Consider the benefits of clinging to the outdated The dysfunctional, of ‘I might not be right but I’m never wrong’ thinking If you have hope if you believe in change Then first change yourself, learn the truth

George G
