THE FRUITS OF FEAR

By George Gondron

THE FRUITS OF FEAR There's many forms of fear, more than we can count Fear based on the lack of basic human needs Food, shelter, clothing, medical care, companionship Fear from immediate danger, the need for safety Fear of abandonment, being left behind, left alone The fear of old age and then death And then there's motivation through fear And it begins at an early age Usually wrapped in the language of, if you don't And the if you don't's are many and varied And underlying them all is a fear of what will happen If we don't In religion it's the fear of God, if we don't In school it's the fear of failure, if we don't And in behavior it's the fear of shame, if we don't But the most unsavory use of fear is this Intentional motivation through fear for self-serving reasons The motivational fear that separates and divides That paints a dark picture of doom and gloom That points the finger of blame where it doesn't belong Fear whose ultimate goal is for personal gratification Personal gain wrapped in deceptive words For control and power, status and ego Fear that springs from lack of understanding That attempts to accentuate difference rather than sameness What we don't understand we learn to fear So those in power convince us we don't understand Fear then leads to hatred Where there is hatred, there is no love Where there is no love, there is no God So, the fruits of fear never lead to peace The fruits of fear are meant to divide and conquer Control and alienate, ostracize and demean Don't listen to the 'fear mongers' Less you become their next victim Listen rather to your heart, your soul, and spirit Therein lies your truth, not in, nor ever in fear

George G
