LIVING

By George Gondron

LIVING Question Are you living to die, or dying to live I must admit it's not a fair question Because if you're doing either You're not present to now Your thoughts, your focus, your attention For the most part are in the past or the future Even if that past was an hour ago Or that future is in the next hour It may sound ridiculous But not until we learn to be present to now Will we ever really live Only because our minds are about what happened Before now, or what may happen If you don't believe me, challenge yourself Observe your own thoughts And be honest with what you find And whatever your excuse for not being in the now That's all it is, just an excuse, a mind game Played by the ego in order to cling to the familiar And avoid what's present, now Behind every thought, there's an ego purpose Behind every feeling there's an untold story A story about ourselves Which for the most part we don't want to face For if we did face that story with honesty We'd discover that our story, our life Is a result of the choices we make Whether we point the finger elsewhere That finger of blame will change nothing And you may say, 'I didn't chose this' But you did, we always do By living in the present moment It's difficult to escape the choices we make For living in the present moment requires awareness Awareness is a result of being an authentic human Utilizing our minds, hearts, souls, and spirits, being inclusive

George G
