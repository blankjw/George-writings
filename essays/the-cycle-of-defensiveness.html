THE CYCLE OF DEFENSIVENESS

By George Gondron

THE CYCLE OF DEFENSIVENESS Defensiveness is nothing other than making excuses for our own Emotional weaknesses, our own misguided thoughts, words, and Actions, so we defend in order to protect our fragile egos We defend to resist the possibility of genuinely awakening to a New understanding and awareness about ourselves We struggle for years, even to the end of our life, to change others Never accepting that what needed to change is us, not them The ego doesn’t like to ‘let go’, wiling to endure emotional pain Which has become its ‘familiar’ rather than risk being vulnerable What’s the saying?, ‘No risk, no reward’, ‘No pain, no gain’ And what underlies, what is the foundation of all defensiveness? Fear, fear I may be wrong, and they are right Every refusal to risk, to be vulnerable, to ‘let go’ is fear based Something horrible, self-revealing, and frightening will happen So, we cling, we build our self-made prisons, choosing the familiar Admittedly, change is scary, but it beats the alternative If one wants to become fully human, fully alive, a creative being To embrace the energy of love, beginning with the love of oneself Is to reject the energy of fear and live in defensiveness Those really are our two main choices, love or fear Love is the characteristic of an open mind, eager to learn Fear is the preference of a closed mind, unwilling to learn Love has no expectations, is not attached, is free in its actions Fear has many expectations, attachments, and desires Whatever we are attached to becomes self-referential And when it doesn’t meet our expectations, defensiveness kicks in Love never treats others as objects, fear does Genuine love is selfless, fear on the other hand is selfish As well, unconditional love will never allow itself to be a door matt It can easily say, ‘you can go this far, and no further’ In our Western culture we lack balance, inner and outer I encourage you to adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Seek an understanding of your own defensiveness and resistances Focus on a short mantra, or your breath to clear your mind Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
