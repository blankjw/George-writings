MISTAKE OR POSSIBILITY

By George Gondron

MISTAKE OR POSSIBILITY How wonderful, our mistakes An opportunity to learn, maybe, maybe not The ones that result in harm To ourselves or another If a lesson is learned, well and good If not, they repeat themselves, double jeopardy Some mistakes, just unintentional blunders A slip of the tongue, the choice of a word Easily enough forgiven and forgotten Little failures, often our greatest teachers They offer the chance to learn humility Not too much in vogue currently, is humility Like, 'I may not be right but I'm never wrong' The most difficult of phrases, 'I was wrong' When the child never hears that phrase How can we fault them for their attitudes? They only learn what their role models teach I wish I had said I was wrong more often The only regret we should have of our mistakes Is, not learning the obvious lesson Not asking, what has this taught me? Will my pride allow it to happen again? Unlike words once spoken Some mistakes can be mended Words, however, cannot be unsaid Let your mistakes lead to new discovery Let them not be chains of shame Let them be your guide, not your jailer Embrace the learning, the possibility, the change

George G
