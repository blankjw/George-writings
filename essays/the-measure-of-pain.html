THE MEASURE OF PAIN

By George Gondron

THE MEASURE OF PAIN Pain has many, and varied, and subtle forms The pain of a physical nature with diverse degrees of gradation Some caused accidentally, some intentionally Some as a result of a physical goal to be reached From childhood sports to the professional athlete Endured without relief by some, medicated by others Then there's psychological pain, emotional stress Which in great part is created by the sufferer However, few ever learn they are the enemy That they are the perpetrator of their own suffering And it also is medicated in multiple forms Stiff upper lip by some, Band-Aid therapy by others Medicated through distraction and diversion Alcohol, drugs - both legal and illegal - by others What about spiritual pain, the hidden grace What some would call the 'dark night of the soul' The pain of the absence of what one might call God That when ended, cannot be explained but is real nonetheless And also, when ended, never forgotten A pain avoided by most, only known by a few And the deepest pain of all, that of a pure heart Not for itself alone, but known only for others The heart that suffers for those who are helpless The heart that suffers for the abuse of the many The heart that suffers for the unheard cries The voiceless, the hungry, the abandoned, the lost It is the pure heart that suffers for man's inhumanity to man And it is in that suffering that empathy is born The pure heart that carries the compassion for the world The pure heart that sees what most overlook The heart created through its own struggles and failures The heart that's been broken through its own weakness Burned pure in the crucible of its own humanness The heart that has found what gives it its life The Love of the universe that heals all the pain The Ground of All Being that connects us to all The Alpha and Omega within every soul

George G
