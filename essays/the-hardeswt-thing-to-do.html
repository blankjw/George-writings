THE HARDESWT THING TO DO

By George Gondron

THE HARDEST THING TO DO You guess What’s the hardest thing to do? Answer - It’s always the right thing to do, always It may not be the safest, the most welcomed, the most accepted To confront the obvious lie, to speak out against discrimination, To point out an injustice, to defend the defenseless, To walk away from an abusive relationship To admit we might be wrong, to ask for forgiveness, To simply say, I’m sorry – are not the easy things They are the hard things For an inflated self-righteous ego, for a closed mind For a dualistic thinker, an either/or thinker, a black/white thinker They are virtually impossible, beyond the realm of possibility They are a position of low self esteem, of even shame They are bluntly, a disconnect from conscience A position of a character more prone to follow fear than love A personality more prone to be anti than pro More ready to exclude than include, divide rather than unite More ready to follow than to question or doubt Led by their emotions more than critical thinking Yes, the right think to do is the hard thing to do To stand against the bully, to question bigotry To speak out against the callous behavior of another In word and deed, regardless their status or position Genuine love is hard for it demands sacrifice Fear is easy for it neglects, defends, and ignores Fear points a finger, love has open arms An informed mind will do the hard thing, a fearful mind won’t Choose a path less traveled, a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day experience silence, stillness, and Presence Connect inner and outer, choose balance a both/and existence Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing Breathe in , breathe out a short mantra, for example, Breathe in wellness to yourself, breathe out wellness to the universe Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
