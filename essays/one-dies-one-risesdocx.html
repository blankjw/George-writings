ONE DIES, ONE RISES

By George Gondron

ONE DIES, ONE RISES We still have two main political parties in our nation But one has died and another has taken its place The party of Lincoln, the Republican party no longer exists It has been supplanted by a new party, the Party of Trump And if you haven’t noticed, it’s vastly different from Lincoln’s party And if it wins in November, we will have a new manifesto The doctrine of Project 2025, it’s quite lengthy, with 900 pages I encourage you to read it for if its goals are accomplished We, and future generations, will be negatively impacted In essence it will be the beginning of the end Your freedoms will be changed forever and not in a positive way We may remain a republic, a democracy, in name only Under the auspices of an autocracy And of course, you remember the old saying ‘Fool be once, shame on you. Fool me twice, shame on me.’ Somehow, I believe we’ve reached the position of twice We are captivated and spellbound with conspiracy theories With a new phenomenon called ‘alternative facts’, the deep state We are led by our emotions and not our intellect ‘Full speed ahead, damn the torpedoes’ Or ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ Nothing quiet so arrogant as an inflated ego In Trump’s acceptance speech he told over twenty lies But none of his ‘Trumpet Sheep’ would dare challenge him Uninformed minds are the most dangerous minds, they are closed Closed minds are polarized minds, they never open One can only appeal to the integrity, ethics, and values of those Who have closed minds Encouraging them to reflect, question, challenge, and learn Beyond mere acceptance on an emotional level Our children’s and grandchildren’s futures are at risk Let us not fail them, let us become more aware and conscious Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your mind and let your heart, soul, and spirit teach you A meditative/contemplative practice is necessary for balance Especially in a culture filled with distractions and constant noise Value yourself, value others, value the Mystery of creation

George G
