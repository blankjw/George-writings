FRIENDS

By George Gondron

FRIENDS In these times It is more the exception than the rule To have a genuine, sincere, lasting, friend Maybe it's because we've lost the meaning The significance of what a real friend is 'Greater love has no man/woman than to lay down his/her life For a friend' Or perhaps we need several definitions for friend As the Greeks have for the word love Eros, sexual love Philia, love between parents and their children Ludus, playful love Agape, love for everyone Pragma, longstanding love Philautia, love of self, narcissism, or self-compassion Obviously, we use the word friend loosely We meet a stranger today and tomorrow they're a friend And sadly, we are not even friends to ourselves If that's true, how can we possibly be friends to others? It is not until we can befriend our own personal darkness As well as our light That we can be an authentic friend to another When expectations or conditions enter into a relationship There can be no friendship Expectations and conditions are nothing more than qualifiers Setting boundaries, limits, and conditions on the relationship True friendship has no boundaries, limits, or conditions To be a friend, begin with self 'To thine own self be true' Embrace and learn from your own shadow It's not your enemy, it only wants to be recognized Brought into consciousness and awareness It is nothing more than your own internal teacher It is the voice that says, 'I too can be a villain' 'I too can do what I project onto others' Learn to listen to that voice if you want friendship If you want agape

George G
