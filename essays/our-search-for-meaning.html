OUR SEARCH FOR MEANING

By George Gondron | Source: OUR SEARCH FOR MEANING.docx

OUR SEARCH FOR MEANING Throughout history there has been a repeated struggle By all individuals, our search for meaning, at least it used to be But now, in this culture, this society, it has become the exception To the rule, like the song by Peggy Lee, ‘Is That All There Is?’ Because of our many distractions, our psychological defenses, Our unfettered emotions and lack of critical thinking Life seems to pass us by and suddenly we’re at the end When we were busy about many things that had little substance, Little value, and meaningless importance We made ‘mountains out of molehills’, and to what end? The soul has a deep yearning to know Mystery, to touch the Source But we’re much too busy about nonsense, to become sensible Occasionally we must get off the ‘merry-go-round’, stop chasing Rainbows and take a little time to be reflective, to center ourselves To connect with our own internal core, our inner resources In a very real way, we are strangers to ourselves Afraid to even ask the question, ‘who am I’, what is my purpose? And with little effort we will discover that within this society We are only ‘consumers’, nothing but a means to an end Not an end of our own making, but of someone else’s Which puts us in a posture of being objects, not subjects We grease the wheels of those that have wealth and power That they might become more powerful and wealthy They depend on us to constantly consume, and they live by the Slogan, ‘more is better, faster is better, bigger is better’ And we listen, and we forget, and we lose ourselves Our purpose, our meaning extends far beyond consumption Our deeper meaning is, and always will be, about relationship We don’t live in a vacuum; we are not islands unto ourselves Love is the energy that fuels the universe, but fear has become Our preference and the two are diametrically opposed One unites and includes the other separates and excludes To embrace a meditative/contemplative practice is a path to meaning An inward journey that leads to discernment and balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, they patiently await

George G
