MASK

By George Gondron

MASK Who lives behind the mask, the persona, the facade? Without them who do we see in the mirror? And why is the disguise so necessary? It doesn't require a great deal of analysis for the answer It's all because of a set-up, fabricated lies A false narrative that shackles us to the phenomenon Of compare, contrast, and compete Who set the standard for human physical beauty? Physical perfection, both female and male It extends far beyond skin color Effecting all races; red, yellow, black, and white It's almost as if the more evolved we have become The more outrageous the disguise Which it seems, that evolution has not effectively Been through greater awareness and consciousness At least on a psychological level A self aware level, vanity it seems is more powerful The cosmetics industry grossed 62 billion dollars in 2017 Cosmetic plastic surgery over 16 billion in 2016 And both are employed by each gender, male and female Both these industries would vanish if only If only human beings felt okay about who they are Just as they are, without alterations If the mask is worn too long, one becomes the mask And then at some point one no longer knows who they are Without the disguise, the persona, the facade As long as what matters are the externals We will never know who we truly are We remain pretenders, hence the narcissist Gazing at their false beauty in the reflecting pond The egotistical personality writ large The only escape is an inward journey A confrontation against the external lies A rejection of the false self, a search for the true self The awareness of who we are is found internally In the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit Discover yourself, you are part of the Divine, of Love

George G
