IS THERE A MORAL ORDER

By George Gondron

IS THERE A MORAL ORDER If today, all organized religion were to disappear Would individual humans be inclined to be moral Evidence would suggest that morality based on belief alone Is far from being sufficient to prevent all that is immoral Witness the millions that have died over the centuries Who professed one belief system or another All of which have their own moral codes Codes which, not grounded in personal experience Become like most all religious beliefs, we pick and choose Those which fit and ignore the rest, the least objective After all we’re only human, certainly not perfect The whole purpose of this reflection is to delineate Between belief and experience Experience is much more likely to promote change than belief Considering experience always happens in a context Not in a vacuum, how do we learn to become moral Some say morals are an acquirement, others say they are innate In either case, in order to awaken, develop, or acquire Our individual moral code, a code we refer to whenever We encounter or witness and must challenge immoral behavior We must have experience, to immerse ourselves in the Suffering of others, hands on kindness and compassion To listen to the stories, the pain, the loss of others Not simply writing a check, putting money in a plate Or placing our butts on a pew occasionally In fact, we need never enter a church to have a moral code But in addition, I do know that if we have a sense of awe, Of wonder, of reverence for this amazing universe And the original cause of the same, Mystery, Creator, Isness The name does not matter, God doesn’t have an inflated ego And we choose to have an experience of that reality Our innate moral code will awaken, will manifest, always A meditative/contemplative practice is one way To develop and maintain that spiritual relationship, experientially Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your inner guides, they hold your true self

George G
