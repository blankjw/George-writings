EMOTIONAL FREEDOM

By George Gondron

EMOTIONAL FREEDOM Most of the emotions we experience, through choice Seem to arise from a negative influence As well as our thoughts and actions We live in a culture that’s more against than for And even what we say we’re for is more a reaction To what we’re against A negative outlook is a detriment to our health on every level Physically, intellectually, emotionally, and spiritually The ability to recognize that the emotions we feel Are nothing more than teachers about ourselves Is lost on a mindset that ‘cannot see the forest for the trees’ We can just as easily choose positive emotions As we do negative ones And the outcomes are much more beneficial for ourselves And for all those with which we interact Perhaps it’s the ego need to perceive life as dualistic Life writ large in a polarized dichotomy of either/or Never an option to perceive reality as both/and How do we allow ourselves to be trapped in such a condition? Is it a lack of education or critical thinking? Or the unfettered reliance on social media? Or are we afraid to doubt, question, do our own research? We obviously more often choose fear over love It’s so much easier to blame, deny, exclude, and discriminate Much easier to fan the flames of fear than love It seems a conspiracy theory lurks around every corner There is saying, ‘a rose by any other name is still a rose’ Perhaps we need recognize, a lie by any other name is still a lie Part of the problem in our Western culture We have lost or forgotten the importance of balance Our connection to our own internal resources, our inner reference Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life Just a few minutes each day of silence, stillness, and Presence Empty your mind, focus on a short mantra, perhaps your breath Experience the freedom of love over the slavery of fear Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your answers, your truth, they wait

George G
