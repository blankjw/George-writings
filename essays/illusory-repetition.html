ILLUSORY REPETITION

By George Gondron

ILLUSORY REPETITION Repetition as a path to knowing is an illusion When that repetition is only repeated words For truth doesn’t lie in words, it is a manifestation of actions Of what a person does or does not do, including ourselves Just as love has no meaning without action, without sacrifice Or, the words, ‘I don’t believe in God, a creative Source’ How does one believe or not believe in something they don’t know The more rational statement would be, ‘I don’t believe in A God that allows abuse, that doesn’t interfere in life threatening Illness, that doesn’t prevent natural disasters, etc., etc., etc. And I might ask, how does free will enter into all the above? How much of what one might call unacceptable is the result Of the free will of human beings? The ability to choose is both a blessing and a curse Back to repetition – when someone, person, group, or ‘ism’ Wants you to believe something they will use whatever Repetitive narrative over and over until it is accepted As listeners, our job is to do whatever steps are necessary To prove or disprove the narrative, not to blindly accept Ignorance loves ‘third party’ information, just drink the Kool Aid And guess who originates and supports social media The sellers of illusory repetition, we are the consumers The enemy of ignorance is learning, continuing education Not just soaking in what’s offered that stirs our emotions Which reveals our intellectual and emotional IQ are both lacking Our culture benefits from those who are critical thinkers Those who reflect, question, doubt, examine all sides Our culture suffers from those who accept illusory repetition Those who believe the next conspiracy theory We all have the ability to learn, we lack the desire To be a reflective person requires balance – inner and outer Embrace a meditative/contemplative daily practice For just a few minutes choose silence, stillness, be present Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Breathe in, breathe out a short mantra, or focus on your breath Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
