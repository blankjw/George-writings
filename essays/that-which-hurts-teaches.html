THAT WHICH HURTS TEACHES

By George Gondron

THAT WHICH HURTS TEACHES Maybe, but not always Some need to repeat the lesson over and over And still may never learn You remember ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ I bet you know a few like that, I certainly do In truth, there is something to be learned In every experience we may have Sometimes it may require that we swallow our pride Become humble, and simply state, ‘I was wrong’ As the saying goes It is in our weakness, we find our strength To learn from life’s experiences Is to become more aware and conscious And the more aware and conscious we are The more fully human we become More accepting, more inclusive, more connected More willing to give than receive More concerned about others, and not ourselves Seems like some of our leaders need to learn that lesson Leaders in all areas of our life There is absolutely no reason that any person In this nation, in this world, should suffer Should go without medical care or nutritious food Or sufficient housing, clothing, or education These problems only exist because of inequity Because of the human ego When selfishness is more the rule than selflessness The sense of sight is more influential than we know It is in the witnessing of human behavior That we are most affected If we’re unwilling to ‘turn a blind eye’ The compassion we feel, the empathy that arises Is a matter of the heart, not the mind Touching something deep inside, connecting our sameness There but for the grace of God, go I Learn from your hurts and share that learning

George G
