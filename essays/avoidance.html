AVOIDANCE

By George Gondron

AVOIDANCE The goal of the Western mind is to avoid Avoid that which is a personal challenge That which is a cause for self-criticism or reflection To avoid literally anything that is an opportunity To face our individual shadow Our dark side and self-righteous nature Regardless our belief system We accomplish this task in various ways Our too many words Our constant busyness Our avoidance of silence Even when we’re not in dialogue Our monkey minds are forever thinking Forever manipulating and controlling our environment Whether that’s as a single individual Or in relationships Silence is a scary proposition for the Western mind For silence is more about being Than doing More about an inward journey Than an external productive existence More about mystery Than assured convenience More about questions than answers More about energized growth and development Than stagnation and the constant familiar And the greatest avoidance of all – excuses We can’t be still, silent, self-reflective, Or critical of our own behavior We have too many excuses The greatest of which is being too busy Not enough time, we love to lie to ourselves Find a way to challenge yourself everyday The world desperately needs self-reflective people People of kindness and compassion Who are more willing to give than to receive We need you, more and more everyday

George G
