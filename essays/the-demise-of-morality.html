THE DEMISE OF MORALITY

By George Gondron | Source: THE DEMISE OF MORALITY.docx

THE DEMISE OF MORALITY Is there a standard for morality? And if so, how is it measured? Is it the same for everyone, everywhere, all the time? It begins and ends with one's definition of right and wrong Too often, because of fundamental religion Morality is relegated to the arena of sexuality And if examined closely, that's a distraction, a ruse Not that sexuality doesn't have a moral consequence Particularly when it's not subjective but objective No, significant morality is much deeper than sexuality It is glimpsed in the words of the world's great teachers It has more to do with decency, honesty, virtue With how we treat or do not treat all sentient beings Every action, when examined, has a moral consequence. Every word, once spoken, has a moral impact Our actions, our words, do matter, morally They automatically contain a degree of wellbeing As well as potential ill will Is it moral for human beings to suffer without health care? Is it moral that children are dying of starvation? Is it moral that millions live in poverty Surrounded by unimaginable wealth? Is gender inequality, racism, xenophobia, and homophobia Moral? It would appear that every great teacher of morality Throughout recorded history has been idolized But seldom followed, better to worship Has been quoted, but not practiced It is the mind's way, a way that says, 'yes but' It is not the way of the heart, the soul, the spirit A life lived with awareness, begins with a death A recognition of one's own shadow, a discernment The battle to become an authentic human being Is always a battle with the ego, the false self It is the most important battle of our lives The winners become people of compassion, loving kindness And the losers cling to their desires and attachments

George G
