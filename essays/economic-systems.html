ECONOMIC SYSTEMS

By George Gondron | Source: ECONOMIC SYSTEMS.docx

ECONOMIC SYSTEMS All economic systems have varying focuses But one primary purpose When that primary purpose is profit alone To the detriment of the common good And the community needs Morality has no importance whatsoever It, for all intent and purposes, has no significance Beyond mere lip service We currently live in an economic system in which Consumption is the primary focus The focus touches every area of our lives Food, clothing, shelter, health, education, services Whatever is developed, designed, or improved Beyond recovering production costs related to the same We discover the underlying profit motive A profit motive that could care less who can or cannot Afford the resulting product or service Witnessed by the exorbitant cost of health care Drug prices, higher education costs, and yes Even food, clothing, and shelter As the middle class slowly disappears in our society It begs the question, who are the beneficiaries of our economy? Currently, as the profits of Fortune 500 corporations soar As CEO benefits accelerate and expand When more and more millionaires and billionaires Are created, the gap between the ‘haves and have-nots’ Grows ever wider and pronounced The American dream is more and more difficult to reach The virtues of kindness, compassion, and concern for all Disappear, lost in a mindset of enough is never enough Greed cannot relate to the phrase, ‘I am my brother’s keeper’ I urge you to develop a meditative/contemplative daily practice Empty your mind for a few minutes each day, focus on your breath Listen to the quiet whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Awaken your inner core, given at birth That holds your true self, who you really are The Divine waits

George G
