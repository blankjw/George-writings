MANY MEANINGS

By George Gondron

MANY MEANINGS Life has many meanings, but only one definition, relationship Every individual life is filled with different experiences Each experience has a specific meaning for the individual And no two lives can have the exact same experiences They can be similar, but the resultant meanings will be different But as well, all lives are relational It is not possible to not be in relationship, ever Throw into the mix our ability to think and feel And further complicate the matter with our individual Capacity to comprehend fully, perceive clearly, and Be totally objective Now, approach any controversial issue with a closed mind Either your own or others And you have the recipe for disaster At best we are like the committee tasked with Designing a horse and ending up with a camel We only see and hear What we’re willing to see and hear, nothing more or less Meaningful interaction is meant to be informative Whose purpose is to learn Expand our awareness and consciousness So, maybe the real question is Do you, do I, enter into discussions with others To be informed, to learn, to grow, to become more aware Or do we do so to be right, to say, ‘well what about’ Sadly, I believe it’s the latter Our preconceived beliefs and ideas become self-made prisons And we are the warden and the jailer, only we hold the key To our release, to our capacity for new learning Honestly, without a balance in our lives, between inner and outer I believe we are more closed-minded than open-minded Listening only to ourselves and ignoring a challenge to be more That necessary balance can begin with a few minutes of silence, With stillness, in the presence of your image of a creator God A few moments to empty your ever-busy ‘monkey mind’ With its incessant and repetitive thoughts, you might hear The whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they patiently wait

George G
