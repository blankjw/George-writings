COURAGE1

By George Gondron

COURAGE Resoluteness, not an intellectual endeavor, but one of the heart The firmness to trust one's own inner experience At the risk of being misunderstood, being excluded or rejected That is courage, not to be confused with bravery Bravery in a just cause requires courage Bravery in an unjust cause is selfishness, or stupidity Courage is formed in the crucible of experience When the pain to not act is greater than the pain to remain silent It takes little courage to accept the status quo, the familiar It takes a great deal of courage to reject the status quo and risk A conscience that is awake, that refuses to accept false propaganda From any source, any venue, any 'ism' That cannot turn a blind eye or a deaf ear to obvious injustice Is a conscience with courage Regardless of the culture in which we are raised From the moment of our birth We are conditioned, manipulated, controlled, and influenced One could as easily say, we are given a drug and become addicted Addicted to our culture's way of thinking, acting, believing The great 'changers' of history, the prophets of all beliefs Were people who used their conscience and courage To challenge the dysfunction of their time and place We still have prophets today, people of change But, few listen, most just ignore and remain 'drugged' Easier to blame, complain, or not 'rock the boat' Than have courage True courage is always about helping the underprivileged The marginalized, the disenfranchised, the poor and abandoned It is about inclusion, not exclusion About unity, connection, and relatedness, not division It is not and will never be about condemnation It is easy to condemn, that only requires words Courage is more difficult, for it requires action Where do we find courage, where is it hidden It rests not in the mind but in the heart, soul, and spirit And if we choose silence, stillness, and Presence We will find our courage, we will find our Strength, we will

George G
