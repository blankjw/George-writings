THE GIFT

By George Gondron

THE GIFT We all arrive, we all exist as a matter of a gift The gift we call our life A gift that arises from a Mystery A Mystery as yet not fully understood Only glimpsed at, beyond our cognitive abilities Our origins truly are found in the stardust In the very beginning of what we call this universe Science, for all its accomplishments Still struggles to this day with the understanding Of the essence of life, of its purpose, its motives We understand the biology of birth But little else regarding the spirit of life Singularly we celebrate this gift once a year The day of our particular arrival, the day of our birth Truly this gift is worthy of celebration everyday A celebration of our connection to the Mystery Aware or unaware, we are connected to all of existence Simply through the stardust that we share We are undeniably, a part of the Mystery We have great value, we are the 'pearl of great price' We are the very thoughts of the Mystery There are no accidents, we have purpose We are a part of the whole, an important part One of the greatest significances of this gift Is our free will We don't have to acknowledge the Mystery There is no requirement for gratitude But our behavior is an indication of the value The value of our gift For what we bring to life Is our gift back to the Mystery Our gift to one another How we reflect on our existence, truly matters Just as how we treat one another Do yourself a favor, spend a little time each day With the Mystery, called by your particular name The name doesn't matter, the reflection does

George G
