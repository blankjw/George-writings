OUR SHADOW

By George Gondron

OUR SHADOW We actually have two shadows One we cast on the ground on a sunny day The other lives in our unconscious The one we run from in our dreams The one that’s black and shadowy in a white man’s dreams Or white and scary in a black man’s dreams The one we can’t seem to be able to elude We can’t run fast enough, our feet or like lead And just as we fear we’re going to be caught We awaken with a start, and we never get the message So, the scenario continues night after night In different contexts, different interactions The shadow is number one, not our enemy The shadow is our teacher, our friend Attempting to teach us something about ourselves Which we don’t care to see, we don’t want to know That reveals our own prejudice, our bias, our judgmental mind That part of us that is critical of others Dreams are the only means of bringing the unconscious Into consciousness, that’s called growth, individuation Ninety-five percent of the dream content is subjective It’s about me, the dreamer, in the guise of others Showing me that what I imagine in their behavior Is also in me, I have those same traits One major part of personal growth Is recognition of one’s own shadow Not until that happens will personal condemnation Personal prejudice, bias, and judgment of others Ever cease – that’s a guarantee Carl Jung, a famous Swiss psychoanalyst is quoted as saying The goal of a human life is to make the unconscious, conscious I am now 82 and I can attest to that with experience You will never love unconditionally without it You will never love the Lord your God with all your heart, Soul, mind, and strength, and your neighbor as your self Without it – choose a meditative daily practice A few minutes of silence, stillness, with Presence, to listen

George G
