WHO'S SHOES

By George Gondron | Source: WHO'S SHOES.docx

WHO'S SHOES Who's shoes do you walk in? The shoes of a woman, a man A husband, a wife, a mother, a father A sister, a brother, a gay, a straight Something we seldom if ever consider What it's like to walk in the shoes of another We, it seems, don't do very well Considering what that may be like But, we're quick to say who's shoes don't fit us Those that we don't want to walk in The single mom raising three children Working two jobs just for the bare necessities The new college graduate burdened with student debt Unable to find a job The father with a debilitating disease No longer able to afford health insurance The deaf person, the blind person, the handicapped Yes, we don't want to walk in their shoes How about the shoes of a young man sent to fight Fight in a far away country for a cause he doesn't understand Which when closely examined Has nothing to do with our nation's security But it does have something to do with money What's it like to live in his shoes after witnessing The death of so many, his comrades, the innocent Those that return with the loss of limbs, crippled forever What's it like to serve your country, to risk your very life Only to return and be tossed aside and ignored What's it like to be a minority, a person of color What's it like to be someone of a different faith The list is endless of those who's shoes We don't care to walk in Perhaps we should, perhaps every now and then We need follow behind someone who's shoes We don't want to walk in, maybe if we did Our attitudes might change, we might become more tolerant The world might become a better place, perhaps

George G
