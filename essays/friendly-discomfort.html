FRIENDLY DISCOMFORT

By George Gondron | Source: FRIENDLY DISCOMFORT.docx

FRIENDLY DISCOMFORT Few of us ever change once we settle into our familiars The way we think, feel, act, or do not act becomes routine All our emotional discomfort, anxiety, worry, frustration Not to mention the greatest of all, fear Become the ‘linchpins’, the essential elements controlling Our lives, day in and day out, we become puppets Undoable to cut the strings manipulated by our own egos Trapped in a self-made prison to which we hold the key Probably the greatest ‘awakening’, the most important awareness We can each achieve is the conscious understanding that We alone choose what we think, feel, and do without exception No one is holding a gun to our heads, at least not yet Can you imagine, if we all assumed responsibility for our own Thoughts, feelings, and action or nonaction Rather than blaming he, she, it, or they, what significant Changes could and would occur in our individual lives Not to mention our culture, our society, our politics, our religion Now that’s an interesting thought, become responsible for ourselves One of the interesting facts about us humans, especially in the West We want what we want, but few seek to go through the process Of accomplishing those desires in a responsible manner But we are sure quick to point fingers at those who ‘scam’ the system Without knowing their personal stories or conditions We are unwilling to, as the song goes, ‘Walk a Mile in My Shoes’ We simply rely on ‘third party’ information for our beliefs Everyone has a story and none of us has the right to pass judgment Without knowing that personal story, not one of us So maybe we can look at the emotional discomfort of our own lives As friendly reminders that we alone choose those discomforting Thoughts, feelings, and actions and we alone can ‘let go’ That’s always the first step for change, ‘letting go’ Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, some help to ‘let go’ Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Mystery Your image of the Isness of the universe, the name doesn’t matter Focus on your breath or a brief mantra, breathe in, breathe out The ego will resist, your mind will wander, simply rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
