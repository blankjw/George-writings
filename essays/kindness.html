ESSAY

KINDNESS

By George Gondron

KINDNESS The beginning of sincere kindness starts with self Not the ego ideal of kindness, an inflated goodness The 'let me tell you how good I am' kindness Rather the tenderness toward my broken and wounded self Those hidden parts that I'm not so proud of That have hurt others and thereby hurt myself as well If I can't be kind to myself in my own brokenness Unable to recognize my own shortcomings My daily imperfections held in doubt and uncertainty How can my kindness to others be genuine? And not just for show, just a way to feel good about myself A false kindness is anything but thoughtful or good In truth there's always a hidden agenda Either to 'pat myself on the back' and 'puff-up' my ego Or hope that others are aware of my kindness Honest kindness, natural thoughtfulness, is spontaneous There is no 'second thought', or is this the appropriate moment? No analytical determination of if, or when to be kind If there is an expectant outcome for your act of kindness Then it's not kindness at all It has a string attached, in one form or another And if there's an unspoken thought of What's this going to cost me? Then for sure, there's no heartfelt intention to be kind Like sincere love, forthright kindness is instinctual It is as natural as breathing in and out It's as simple as smiling at the stranger Calling the checkout person by the name on their badge Getting down on one's knees to speak to the child Not measuring the possible inconvenience of being kind When the chance for kindness is present, it's taken It's not deliberated or analyzed It flows from the heart, not the mind Be kind to yourself through self-forgiveness In order that your kindness to others may be sincere As the Giver of life is kind to us through infinite grace A grace called love given to all, withheld from none

George G
