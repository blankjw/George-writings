WHAT DID WE MISS

By George Gondron

WHAT DID WE MISS? In the frenzied activity of our everyday lives In the daily distractions In our actions when examined later that seem meaningless What did we miss? In our obsessions to satisfy some selfish desire That in itself had no value at all In our refusal to forgive, knowing we're at fault What did we miss? The meaning of a gentle touch never given The kind word never spoken The listening ear that we never gave What did we miss? How we see and hear, how we connect How we show kindness and compassion Matters, matters greatly The funny thing about time is this We can't get it back And yet we live as if time is forever We live life unconsciously as if the hand we've been dealt Is our burden to bear, it's our fate, but it's not We can reshuffle the deck, we can choose to change We can let go of the past and not fear the future We can be present to now There's nothing to miss when we're present to now It's all right here, right now The meaning of life is relationship There is no other reason for our existence An existence that has no other purpose than to love Than to be connected and interdependent What we miss in our human relationships Is always about personal choice Always about some 'condition' we apply to the relationship But genuine love has no time for blame, for conditions Love is the fruit of sacrifice Wealth is the fruit of generosity Don't wait until some tragedy to ask 'what did I miss'? Do it now, be here now, love now

George G
