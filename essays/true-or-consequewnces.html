TRUE OR CONSEQUEWNCES

By George Gondron

TRUTH OR CONSEQUENCES To insist that what we believe is true Means, that we are willing to accept the consequences if it’s not Therein lies the problem Seldom, if ever, do we see beyond our own noses We are so against anything other than our truth Which in most cases is not based on personal experience But rather, based on third-party information You know the kind, ‘he said, she said’, kind And if our expectations are not fulfilled, are not met Because of this third-party truth We have plenty of excuses for why we believed it at all Excuses that have no validity whatsoever Like the argument against common sense gun legislation ‘Will they’re gonna come and take our guns’ I dare you to provide any facts that support that lie The argument is usually, ‘Well they might’ Now that’s an argument for anything with common sense ‘Well they might’ Normally you suffer the consequences of an action Of non-compliance Like If you don’t pay your property taxes You’ll lose your real estate Or if you don’t have a valid driver’s license You may lose your right to drive And in both cases, there are requirements that need To be met in order to have that real estate and drive the vehicle Please, look beyond what you may believe And ask if it makes sense, or is it based on emotions We believe far too much, with far too little evidence Not to mention far too little personal experience Engage a meditative/contemplative practice Balance outer and inner, becoming a discerning person Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of your image of a Creator, your God Awaken your inner core, your internal resources They hold your true self, they have your answers What you hold inside is much greater than the ego

George G
