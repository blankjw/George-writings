POSSIBILITY IS NOT PROBABILITY

By George Gondron | Source: POSSIBILITY IS NOT PROBABILITY.docx

POSSIBILITY IS NOT PROBABILITY In the 'face of injustice', to remain silent is participation Neutrality has no defense when it comes to inequality There is little probability that a closed mind Sees the many possibilities of an open mind An egotistical dualistic mind is a closed mind A wealthy person is usually close-minded A person accustomed to power and control, close-minded The fundamentalist that clings to the externals of religion For the most part, is also close-minded It is not what we see and hear But rather, how we see and hear that matters How we connect to life, relate to the stranger Even how we relate to a loved one Selflessly or selfishly, open or closed-minded Where there is fear, hatred, condemnation, and greed There is a closed mind You only need to listen to the conversations From all forms of social media Including our personal conversations To understand the close-minded nature of most people With the close-minded, probability is limited With open-minded, possibilities are limitless Those with closed minds are the 'yes but' people Those who are unable to simply observe They critique with little or no factual information Unable to observe without comment until That which is observed reveals itself Reveals itself through its actions, not its rhetoric Its behavior, not its words Compassion, kindness, inclusion, and harmony Are seen in behavior, not in the 'quagmire' of words Truth is ninety nine percent personal experience To 'shout out' a truth that is not experiential, is a lie To become fully human, we need engage our humanity To become spiritual, we need pursue our spirituality To be aware of the probabilities We must be open to the possibilities

George G
