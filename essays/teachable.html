TEACHABLE

By George Gondron

TEACHABLE The success of learning is based on the ability to be taught The ability to be taught is based on an open mind For a closed mind is unteachable Of course, admittedly we can learn in many ways Beginning with our five senses, taste, touch, smell, hear, and see But further education of the mind, of the intellect Demands an openness, a willingness to learn To become more and better informed To expand one’s awareness and consciousness To not be so easily fooled by false information So readily acceptable by our infantile emotions Held even as adults, or better stated, regrettably as adults Notice how emotional people become over obviously False information, alternative facts, half-truths And out-right lies and unfounded narratives All the aforementioned are driven by a fear-based mentality Not a mentality of openness, kindness, compassion, and love We it seems are more prone to be ‘anti’ than ‘pro’ More prone to reacting than responding We cling to our ‘familiars’ in our thoughts, feelings, and actions Unable it seems to ‘let-go’ of whatever it is that keeps us closed We prefer the easy path, the known path, the common path Even when those paths lead to or keep us dysfunctional Keep us separated and segregated, disconnected Our ability to learn is a matter of choosing As well as our ability to defend and remain stuck, both choices A path few take in our modern-day culture Is a daily few minutes of silence, with stillness, and with Presence A time to empty our ever-busy ‘monkey minds’ Of their incessant and repetitive thoughts that go nowhere To stop and listen to the quiet whispers Of our own hearts, souls, and spirits To be open to teachable moments that we so desperately need To become more fully human, more whole not a half To become our ‘true selves’ as opposed to a ‘false self’ A disconnected self, a façade and persona unrecognizable Even as we gaze in the mirror at our reflection – please

George G
