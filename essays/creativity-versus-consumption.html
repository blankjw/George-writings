CREATIVITY VERSUS CONSUMPTION

By George Gondron

CREATIVITY VERSUS CONSUMPTION If a stranger was tasked with observing our Western culture Would they observe a people of creativity or consumption? Almost everything we hear and see is focused on consumption Buy this, purchase that, invest in this product, just consume We really don’t care about your potential creativity We want you to spend more than you have Especially on what you don’t really need There is a disconnect between our morality and reality Virtue is about inclusion, gratuity, and sacrifice But we exist in a ‘me culture’, individually and corporately Selfish versus selfless, is the general modus operandi More is better, bigger is better, faster is better, keep consuming I often wonder if we didn’t grease the wheels of capitalism With continuous consumption But focused on kindness, compassion, altruism, and love What would our society look like If our creative energy was about inclusion versus separation Harmony versus discord, unity versus division And justice, equality, and freedom were available for all If we recognized how alike we are rather than unalike See our sameness first and honor our diversity The very nature of our psychological defense mechanisms Is a clear indication of the disconnect between head and heart As a culture, our focus is on the externals, the material Organized religion supplants relational spirituality Ego desire and attachment replaces moral behavior To be fully human is to be connected to our inner core To an internal reference, DNA innate to our essence But buried beneath the mind’s, the ego’s need to control We have become separated from our better ‘self’ A meditative/contemplative practice is a path to wholeness To spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness In the presence of your image of the Creator of this universe We are not accidents, we are intentional, we are creative Gifts to one another, gifts to the world, not just consumers Empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
