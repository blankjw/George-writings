DESPAIR

By George Gondron

BEYOND DESPAIR Whatever the tragedy, imagined or real Nurtured in the dark dry soil of fear Is this feeling called despair Surviving only as a result of our attachments Attachments to what is impermanent and fleeting This despair hinders our ability to be present Present to this particular breath that excludes the past That is not focused on the future Experienced in the precious moments of what is now That is where we find true meaning What is it that is so very important? So necessary beyond the human relationships of our lives That like a magnet draws us to this despair Where do our values lie? Places, things, careers, our very selves Yes, they all have their value But it is in the bonds created in our human relationships That sustains our very existence That gives meaning to those present moments In the end, all else is lifeless and empty What we deeply enjoy, what touches our soul Is experienced in relationship In our immediate presence Or hidden within our intimate thoughts It is through our personal relationships That we push through and beyond our despair Where we maintain the goodness and essence of life Drawn together in our sameness And supporting our uniqueness Knowing that we are one, we are not separate We survive because of each other, not alone Despair is simply an emotion that has no remedy If left to the mind alone, it is an endless morass But in relationship hope is renewed, despair is defeated We support, heal, comfort, and love one another Life is relationship There's no other reason for our existence Love one another Love the One that loves you, unconditionally

George G
