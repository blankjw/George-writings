HYPOCRISY - Copy

By George Gondron

HYPOCRISY There is nothing more reprehensible than a hypocrite The degradation of the moral fiber of a person or people Who places value on any and everything above The value of life in all its forms We can debate the many reasons for this degradation That like a cancer eventually destroys itself But the most obvious reason is selfishness Me and us first You and them second No matter the venue, secular or sacred There was a time in our past when the obvious Was less obvious, maybe it’s always existed Or maybe the explosion of social media Has made the hypocrisy more obvious and evident Brought into the clear light of day There are three or four areas that stand out In the realm of the hypocrite Religion, politics, economics, and gender Each of which could easily be expanded to their own subsets The hypocrisy of the professed Christian is clear to see The politician whose morals are not only suspect but so apparent The wealthy who exploit the labor of their own workers And the gender inequality that persists today Hypocrisy is very much alive and well in all the above Through our own complicity overtly and covertly We are all guilty of some hypocrisy Do yourself a favor and examine your own bias and complicity Choose a contemplative practice of just a few minutes each day For silence, stillness, with Presence A time to empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Of all the distractions that prevent your own objectivity Your own clarity of perception Listen to the whispers of the truth held within your inner being Held quietly by your heart, soul, and spirit – they wait For life to have meaning and purpose, to be whole There must be a balance between inner and outer The mind alone is not enough, and never has been

George G
