THE OTHER ADDICTIONS

By George Gondron

THE OTHER ADDICTIONS Let’s talk about the addictions that aren’t ingested Those that we don’t put into our bodies But which can be and often are much more damaging At the top of the list is polarized thinking Or what can be described as black and white thinking We gravitate toward the camp of either anti or pro For or against on almost any and every topic If you want our opinion, just ask us, we’ll tell you We can also be addicted to certain feelings At the top of that list is worry–translated fear There’s nothing that can be excluded from worry The side effects Anxiety, tension, apprehension, distress, nervousness A nagging sense of unease, constantly All of the above are the result of our adaptability Our capability of being focused on the negative Rather than the positive A mindset that has little if any awareness Of how one creates their own reality A reality based on the choices we make as individuals We choose what we think, we choose what we feel No one is holding a gun to our heads We build the prison in which we live As well, we hold the key to that same prison To escape the pattern of black/white thinking To escape the feeling of worry We can choose to change, become a nondual thinker Become love-based rather than fear-based It doesn’t require a prescription or a therapist Just a committed persistent daily practice A meditative/contemplative few minutes each day Moments of silence, stillness, with Presence Presence being your belief in a creator God A time to empty your ‘monkey mind’ of its Incessant and repetitive thoughts A time to listen to the whispers of your heart A time to heal and discover your own truth, please

George G
