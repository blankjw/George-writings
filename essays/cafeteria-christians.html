CAFETERIA CHRISTIANS

By George Gondron

CAFETERIA CHRISTIANS A term I read somewhere which basically means Those Christians that pick and choose what they believe And conveniently ignore the rest, you know the hard parts Mercy, justice, and generosity toward the poor, the outsider, The marginalized, the foreigner, the orphan and widow You know the ones, the ‘prosperity’ evangelicals taking money From widows living on social security while amassing personal Fortunes, living in palatial estates, owning jet planes and driving Fancy cars, wearing Rolex watches and silk suits If you look up the word hypocrite in the dictionary You’ll see their pictures, or perhaps predator is a more apt word It’s interesting to note that the one they say they follow Wait, perhaps follow is the wrong word for normally if you are Following someone you’re attempting to emulate their behavior They seemed to have forgotten Jesus never had a home Didn’t ride around in a fancy chariot and he criticized the leaders Of his own faith, the Jewish establishment He discriminated against no one; race, gender, color or creed I have a sneaky suspicion that if Jesus returned today He would be crucified again, but this time by Christians For honestly, who wants to personally feed the hungry, give Drink to the thirsty, clothe the naked, visit the prisoner, Take care of the widow and orphan – you, me, anyone? Honestly, most of us, Christian, Jew, Muslim, etc., etc., etc. Are hypocrites We are ‘don’t do as I do’, but are ‘do as I say do’ people All belief systems have a definition or an understanding of the Phrase, ‘poverty of spirit’ – yet most follow a path of least resistance A path of ‘let me live in my delusion’ not in reality As M Scott Peck wrote, let me ignore ‘the road less traveled’ We each carry the divine DNA of the Mystery that creates ceaselessly The name doesn’t matter, the relationship does Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice, choose balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Mystery Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen for the whispers of your inner guides, your true self Your heart, soul, and spirit have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
