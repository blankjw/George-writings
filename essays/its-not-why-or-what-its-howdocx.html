IT'S NOT WHY OR WHAT, IT'S HOW

By George Gondron

IT'S NOT WHY OR WHAT, IT'S HOW Change happens as a result of becoming objective If that's at all possible for us humans For, if you didn't already know, we are very subjective It is only when our subjectivity becomes objective That we experience transformation Until then, as usual, the ego remains in total control Put very simply It's not what or why we see or hear It's rather, how we see or hear that really matters Even for the transformed person It's difficult to remain totally objective all the time But for the unaware person, unconscious person It is impossible to be objective at all They see with colored lenses, hear with muted ears The subjective mind only sees and hears what it wishes The only truth it knows, is its subjective truth Which one could say is a certain truth, in a way But, not a truth that is cohesive, whole, or inclusive For the most part, it is merely a selfish truth A comfortable, contented, satisfying truth To see and hear objectively is to have one's reality shaken To see life as it really is, in all its many dimensions Not as it's seen in my own little small 'bubble' Not as it's seen through my critical eyes With primarily 'hear say' to support my criticism I once heard there was a saying that goes something like this 'Don't believe what you hear, and only half of what you see' Which unfortunately, is not the 'hallmark' of a subjective person But, then that's the result of living through the ego only The objective person knows they don't know They have learned that true 'knowing' is experiential And, that experience changes Changes with the inclusion of the heart ,soul, and spirit Along with the mind, a dynamic process, not static The subjective person always knows, just ask them As a matter of fact, now that this reflection is ending Do you know, and if so, how do you know?

George G
