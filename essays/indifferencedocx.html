INDIFFERENCE

By George Gondron

INDIFFERENCE Lack of care, interest, or concern Having little or no importance, insignificant, mediocre How does a human mind reach a state of indifference Concerned only about their immediate relationships But having no concern for all of humanity, all creation The answer is simple We become human ‘doings’ rather than human beings Our lives are filled every waking moment with activity Either physical or mental or both, we seldom stop When we become physically tired, we rest Our minds, except for our sleeping hours, never rest If we never pause, never reflect, never just be Then it’s easy to become indifferent To that which lies outside our immediate circle Indifferent to poverty, abuse, injustice, inequality Prejudice, bigotry, the list is endless Like water dripping into a sponge indifference is subtle Until the sponge becomes saturated And the result, our indifference is now our familiar Unaware and unconscious, others suffer And we take little notice The ‘bag lady’ and her cart, the beggar on the street corner The millions living in poverty in our very midst We barely notice we are indifferent, numb to their pain We are more concerned about holding on to our own Gaining more and giving less Living in our false consolation, ignoring our true desolation How do we change, can we change Are our attachments and desires to strong to let go? We lack balance, inner and outer, we lack connection There is a solution, a daily meditative/contemplative practice Just a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence A daily emptying of the mind to touch the ‘true self’ To let go of our incessant and repetitive thoughts Just for a few moments, to engage our inner core That we might hear the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit They wait, patiently, they hold our truth, our healing `

George G
