POSTURING

By George Gondron

POSTURING No, not the way we stand, or sit, or walk But more of an 'air' of notice me, that posturing The message, I'm important, I know more than you And if you study me enough, Listen to me carefully, Believe in me always, maybe just maybe You'll approach my excellence What the posturer pretends to know, is irrelevant It is more about performance than essence More about form than substance No area of life is immune from this behavior Because no area of life is free from ego Butcher, baker, candlestick maker Politician, prophet, priest, or professor All are subject to posturing To question their performance is to risk their ire Even the condescending smile hides the message How dare you dim my shine, doubt my methods Like the Cheshire cat, the grin conceals an unknown An intentional unknown, to leave you guessing Never shrink from the questions Don't be fooled, there are no perfect teachers Let not the posturing deter your search for truth The only truths that truly matter Are the ones you discover for yourself The ones that you hold within Given as a gift in the life called you Never doubt your ability to discover your truths In the silence and stillness you will find them In the presence of the One that gives them In the presence of Love, there lie your truths

George G
