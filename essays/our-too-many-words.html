OUR TOO MANY WORDS

By George Gondron

OUR TOO MANY WORDS Twenty six Latin symbols The origin of our current alphabet Used to formulate words, eliciting images in the mind That call forth emotions of action, and of passivity Jumbles of letters creating a morass of distortion Most of which are half truths, and many are lies And the most proficient at the use of these words The ministers of propaganda, advertising, and marketing The 'silver tongued devils', power brokers, and politicians The 'bull-shiters' and 'fear mongering' self deceptive orators Selling 'ice to the Eskimo', and 'glasses to a blind man' And we all, are the consumers of these 'half-baked' truths How are we so easily manipulated, fooled By these too many words? We trust what is untrustworthy, only the words And don't trust the obvious, what can be seen We are conditioned to be inspired by words alone Especially words that arouse the emotions We trust social media, in all its forms To bring us the truth, but they, like us Believe the misrepresentations and support the farce Biased and opinionated with little objectivity The voice of the 'truth-sayer' is only a whisper Unheard over the din of the familiar but uncomfortable And the common response to the feared question of 'How do you know what you pass on is true?' 'Well it must be true, because so-and-so said it was' What saves us from this 'morass' of too many words? What is our lifeline through this fog and confusion? It is our experience, nothing more, nothing less Experience gained through our own critical thinking Our willingness to search where the truth lies, not just the words To not seek a solution, is to be part of the problem Blame doesn't excuse our gullibility, our 'easy' beliefs If we don't make the effort to perceive with clarity How can we ever respond with accuracy? Don't be fooled by the too many words, trust the behavior

George G
