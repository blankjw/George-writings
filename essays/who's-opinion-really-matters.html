WHO'S OPINION REALLY MATTERS

By George Gondron | Source: WHO'S OPINION REALLY MATTERS.docx

WHOSE OPINION REALLY MATTERS Throughout our lives we listen to many opinions Some we accept, others we reject And as an afterthought, if truth be known A few of the ones we accepted we would change And a few of the ones we rejected, we may now accept History has one primary purpose To teach us what not to do again, which has seldom worked And to remind us of what we failed to do in the past To live a life based on the opinions of others And to assume they are true or false Is not only foolish, but thoughtless and irresponsible If our values are formed by 'ism's', by group ideology We risk the chance and probability of living a lie Thinking all the while, it's the truth Hence, the meaning of the adage, 'the blind leading the blind' A life lived through 'third party' information Is not a meaningful life at all, it is a pretend life Knowledge has one primary purpose To give us information, even if it's about a belief or religion

That points us in the direction that will lead us To our own personal experience Progress, advancement, and development in all arenas Happens as a result of trial and error, that's the only way To live a life without our own trials and errors Without risk and vulnerability, without question and doubt Is to live an illusion, either of complacency and apathy Or false security surrounded by what in the end Is meaningless and empty Our mistakes, our failures, teach us much more Than our successes, they give meaning to our accomplishments They reinforce faith, hope, and trust The achievement of personal experience, is personal truth Not the opinions of others, as well intentioned as they may be And always remember, that in the end Everyone has two things, their opinions, the other you know Trust your experience, trust your inner voice, your opinion

George G
