WHAT DO WE SEE

By George Gondron

WHAT DO WE SEE? WHAT DO WE HEAR? How distorted is our reality? Twisted by our own desires and wishes Fashioned by the trends of the day Nothing more than passing gratification that never really fulfills Existing in our encapsulated small worlds with our limited views and narrow opinions Are we concerned only for our own security, blinding us to the needs of others Do we really see? Do we give notice to the strain and strife of the less fortunate? The struggle of the single parent or the elderly who live alone Do we really hear? Who among us listens to them, holds them, comforts them - looks into their eyes? The voice of despair, the hopeless sob, the loss of dignity To say we care with our minds and not our hearts, is not caring, it's to ease the conscience To say we see and hear, but not with our souls, is only meaningless words The finger points back to me, I am not blameless, we are not blameless We are asked to be healing balm for others Joined in their discomfort, misfortune, and need Compassionate in their struggle, understanding in their search for meaning What do we see? What do we hear? Hopefully, ourselves, in our less fortunate brothers and sisters A common humanity, searching for a common good, for all

George G
