HUNGRY GHOSTS

By George Gondron

HUNGRY GHOSTS In our modern day with its never ending distractions The hungry ghosts abound, within each of us Looking constantly for external gratification We think too much, but not critically We hardly question, seldom consider We reflect on what has no value and soon is useless Thinking happiness lies in the externals, but it doesn't Seeking satisfaction in the esoteric, another diversion Our hungry ghosts, never satiated, never having enough Our doing, more important than our being We are easily distracted, living in an illusion Filling our days with 'busyness', with 'noise' A ghost has no substance, just as our desires Many of our achievements, most of our possessions Lie covered in dust, many unwrapped, never opened Perhaps used a few times, then shoved into a corner Until our hungry ghosts get filled again, hastily Where is the value, what is the essence Of what only briefly gratifies our hungry ghosts This type of need, this form of hunger A 'merry-go-round' of endless searching Will never fill our hungry ghosts Like pouring water through a sieve Or watching smoke rise in the sky, disappearing There is no satisfying this hunger What satisfies is not tangible What soothes the empty soul cannot be bought What heals the aching heart Calms the inner yearning Is found not in things, but in people, in relationships In connection with what is, yet unseen, and yet known In harmony with one's image of a Creator In a spiritual practice grounded in moments of silence In periods of stillness and Presence From within the core of our being Held in the embrace of Love Chasing away our hungry ghosts

George G
