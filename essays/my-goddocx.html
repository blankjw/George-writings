MY GOD

By George Gondron

MY GOD Is there more than one God, one Creator? Birthing the universe each and every moment Loving creation into existence ceaselessly Called by many names, but nameless Worshiped by Jews, Hindus, Christians, all beliefs, all faiths He hangs on a cross with each death proclaimed in His name She weeps with the abuse of each child It is not worship which my God seeks My God does not need worship, it is the human need In the face of everyone I meet, I see God's face In the heart of every child, beats God life In the cry of the poor I hear His voice In the tears of the suffering I see Her pain In every act of creation is my God's love All is gift, all is grace, all is God A humble God, desiring only relationship We compete for God's favors with our separate beliefs How useless, how arrogant, how blind we are Love does not compete, Love only gives, of Itself Over and over and over again, endlessly Surely there is only one God, a single Creator I know there is Maybe, you know as well Maybe

George G
