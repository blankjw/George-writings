THE NEXT BREATH

By George Gondron | Source: THE NEXT BREATH.docx

THE NEXT BREATH What will the next breath bring Pain or promise Hope or despair Tears or laughter Life or death We never really know, but we act as though we do Most live life unaware, unconsciously, half asleep Swimming in a world of pretend, and pretense Clinging to what in truth is worthless, has no meaning We are truly, the blind leading the blind Most of what we attach to is a result of our programming Our conditioning in a world more interested in things than people And we fool ourselves into believing we have the right path We pursue what we chase for all the 'right' reasons But we really can't tell you why, not really Perhaps if our next breath were as important as our last Which we mostly take for granted Then life would have a different meaning, a different focus Love would not be just an emotional feeling But an action, so desperately needed in a world of 'things' Of competition, success, striving, and struggle We lose and have lost ourselves along the way Honoring our good intentions which are nothing more than Ego gratification, for our own personal safety and security We fear to 'walk' in the shoes of another, especially If in our limited vision they are lesser than ourselves No one is lesser than, no one is more than Another human being, ever It's all in what we see and hear through our role models And sadly, difference has been taught not just culturally But religiously as well, if not more so, God is blamed for so much Perhaps if we would take a few moments each day To simply reflect on our next breath On life and its meaning, on love and its purpose On the wonder of what it means to have consciousness To have a free will, to be able to choose Then maybe, just maybe, our lives might change

George G
