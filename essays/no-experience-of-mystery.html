NO EXPERIENCE OF MYSTERY

By George Gondron

NO EXPERIENCE OF MYSTERY In organized religion, as it exists today It is virtually impossible to have an experience of Mystery For everything is about intellect and behavior We are instructed to learn and or perform Rites, rituals, dogma, and doctrine, think this, do that We have virtually nothing that guides us into the relationship Of Mystery, of Isness, of a Creator Nothing in mainstream Christianity about the importance Of a meditative/contemplative practice Our prayers are about solicitation or petition Memorized narratives simply repeated ad nauseum To a superior being who is omniscient, all-knowing Which means we’re only verbalizing what God already knows Fortunately, the monastic traditions have taught us The importance of listening, you know the two ears One mouth thing – which is problematic between us humans We have fabricated in our minds who we are But in silence with an empty mind, we discover a stranger Perhaps that’s the reason we resist meditation/contemplation Preferring our ‘monkey minds’ instead With their incessant and repetitive thoughts Fear is powerful, especially the fear of unvarnished self-discovery The truth is we are neither the worst person nor the best person We are multi-faceted, both/and complex beings, created from love Our journey is simply a matter of conscientious self-discovery Which most of us choose to ignore, relying only on our ego You know, that part of us that wants to control and manipulate That part that may not always be right, but is never wrong We have, innately, a built-in system for experiencing Mystery An internal reference, an inner core Infused with Divine DNA A choice to develop a daily meditative/contemplative practice Awakens our inner core, gets us out of our ego, our mind That has convinced us all we need are our cognitive abilities To be fully human, fully alive we need much more than ego Take a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Moments to hear the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Our truth-tellers, our true self, they have the answers, they wait

George G
