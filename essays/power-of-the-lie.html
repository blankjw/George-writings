POWER OF THE LIE

By George Gondron

POWER OF THE LIE It literally boggles the mind that people are willing to Believe assertions, rumors, alternative facts, insinuations, Gossip, and hearsay about that which when objectively Examined never approaches any measure of truth whatsoever Yet, they obviously do The explanations for this phenomenon are not too many And are relatively easy to explain The primary reasons are the unwillingness to question, doubt, And use critical thinking In a word, to live in ignorance Ignorance is nothing more than the refusal to learn Ignorance can be overcome, stupidity cannot To live in ignorance is a choice, always It can be described as ‘drinking the Kool-Aid’ Or wearing ‘blinders’, ‘sticking our heads in the sand’ Following the ‘pied piper’, listening to the loudest voices Following all the conspiracy theories Primarily, anything and everything that stirs the emotions It’s like throwing gasoline on an already-lit fire In the end, it’s really a matter of choice The choice of educating ourselves or simply going with the flow The uneducated mind is vulnerable to hype and exaggeration It’s exciting and leads to blame and finger pointing Or any one of the other defense mechanisms To form major opinions based only on one’s emotions Is to play the fool and be easily manipulated The brain is a muscle like any other Without exercise, without use, it falls prey to the emotions Our senses are tools for learning, as is our brain In infancy, we are spoon-fed, but as adults, we are called To be learners, to exert effort for an expanded awareness And greater consciousness, rather than just blind followers Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness and Presence A meditative/contemplative practice is necessary for balance Necessary to awaken our inner core, our internal reference To listen to the whispers of our own hearts, soul, and spirit To discover our true self, to become aware

George G
