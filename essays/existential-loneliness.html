EXISTENTIAL LONELINESS

By George Gondron

EXISTENTIAL LONELINESS Social media and artificial intelligence are contributors To an ever-deepening reality of existential loneliness The tiny acorn that grows into the mighty oak tree Was only able to do so with the necessary ingredients That sustained its life Human wholeness, health, and wellness also require Certain necessary ingredients to become fully human A major component is human interaction, relationship In truth, the best definition of life is relationship Our connections with one another promote growth Intellectually, emotionally, and hopefully spiritually We are sounding boards for one another Providing an atmosphere that enables us to experience All the human emotions, positive and negative We are catalysts for each other, pushing our boundaries Stretching our horizons and fueling our dreams Social media in a sense is a ‘cop-out’, an avoidance Of what is necessary for our expanded awareness and consciousness What pushes us to grow, to become more fully human Is interaction with others If we don’t, we become pawns in someone else's play Living through psychological defense mechanisms Blaming and complaining, never taking ownership For our own lives, our own choices, our own loneliness Loneliness has its beginning when we realize at an early age We are other than ‘mom and dad’, we are separate The beginnings of an ego, I am not you any longer The degree of our experience of loneliness is directly Correlated to our parents and to an internal reference An inner core, which in our culture has for the most part Been totally ignored, supplanted by organized religion, a farce If our level of awareness and consciousness as adults Includes a belief in a creative Source, some call God That relationship to be experiential is from the inside out Connected to the innate knowing of our heart, soul, and spirit They know what the mind cannot perceive Be silent, be still, and listen to your inner guides, they wait

George G
