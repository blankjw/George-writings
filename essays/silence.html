SILENCE

By George Gondron

SILENCE, OUR TEACHER A rational mind, a thinking mind can comprehend Before there was anything, there was nothing There was silence In our Western culture, we do anything and everything To avoid silence, especially silence of the mind There is a resistance by the ego to engage silence Rather, the goal is to avoid silence at all costs One of the greatest benefits of experiencing deep silence Is the opportunity to be present to what can be known In no other way, silence is a teacher Perhaps behind our reluctance to engage silence Is a fear we might discover something about ourselves That is too difficult to acknowledge, to know Or maybe we have an inkling of what might be revealed And like looking in the mirror, we don’t want to see clearly Without a deliberate meditative/contemplative practice The closest we can ever come to experiencing A silence that has the possibility to teach us Is an unfettered use of our senses Taste, touch, hear, smell and see without thoughts and feelings To be fully present to what is without thinking For most people an impossible task All belief systems, indigenous and formal, have within them Teachings about the importance of silence, about being alone Even medical science today has shown the positive results That arise from a consistent meditative/contemplative practice But we resist, like the cartoon character Pogo once said ‘I have met the enemy, and it is me’, we are our own worst enemy A committed meditative/contemplative practice is not happenstance It is a matter of choice and through free will we can choose No matter the conditions of our lives we do have that choice I urge you to spend a few minutes each day in silence Be still and embrace your image of a creative Source, of God Allow deep silence to be a teacher, to awaken your true self To bring balance to your life, inner and outer Breathe in, breathe out a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
