AFTER THE FACT

By George Gondron

AFTER THE FACT Most of what’s important in this journey we call life Is learned and known after the fact And that ‘after the fact’ is experiential Not necessarily experiences we freely choose But more often than not, those that we resist You know, the painful ones, they often are our best teachers Hopefully in your journey you will appreciate the importance Of awakening to the truth, the core beliefs you hold within Given at your birth waiting to be discovered Please, don’t accept the beliefs of others Let an informed conscience be your guide Use all the tools in your ‘toolbox’ Especially your capacity to learn and not merely accept Be your own leader, your own critic, your own explorer We learn best through action, through doing Thinking is mainly a catalyst for performing those actions It is in our nature to progress, to evolve, to accomplish First, we crawled, then we walked, and finally ran It is also within our nature to connect, to be relational Not only with all of nature, but especially with other beings But also, to connect with Mystery, the unknown, the spiritual And again, if religion has a purpose, it’s only to point a finger Toward that Mystery, for religion itself is not the connection The connection is always, always experiential We all are stardust, we carry the DNA of Mystery The name is not important, the experience is All that we take in through our senses, our feelings And through an unbiased and nonjudgmental thinking process Is meant to lead us to our own holiness, our universal connections The bottom line, we become only through experience After the fact The process of becoming whole, holy, fully human Necessarily includes some form of meditative/contemplative practice Moments of chosen silence, stillness, and Presence Time to empty our ‘monkey minds’ of incessant repetitive thoughts That we might hear the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit They hold our true self, have our answers, and they wait

George G
