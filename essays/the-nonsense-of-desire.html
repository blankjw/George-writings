THE NONSENSE OF DESIRE

By George Gondron

THE NONSENSE OF DESIRE The rewards received from the fulfillment of a desire Are only temporary at best and at its worst It only creates an attachment that becomes addictive What we really seek is not accomplished through desire It is only found in being, not doing Learning to be is not something we’re comfortable with We are addicted to ‘doing’ If anything, in the beginning, the process may be filled with anxiety For it is unfamiliar and we resist the unfamiliar As with all things that lead to wellbeing We must move from what is to what is not We must risk and be vulnerable, we must trust As the trapeze artist learns, to get from one bar to the next They must let go We don’t like to let go once our patterns have been established Once our habits and conditioning become second nature Regardless of their imagined benefits Therefore the phrase, ‘I have met the enemy, and it is me’ Is so very true in our resistance to change, all change In a life supported by desire, enough is never enough In a life supported by ‘being’, there is always enough For a life of being is about relationships, not things About giving and sharing, not taking and receiving Desire begins with an impulse based on want or need Being is more about experience About being connected to all of creation An experience of what we can perceive as well as imagine The best path to knowing is through non-knowing, a clear mind Something we seldom experience in our Western culture We manage to fill every waking moment with activity With thoughts, feelings, and actions Our greatest discoveries are revealed in spontaneity, flexibility Not in being tied to the familiar Each day embrace a few minutes of silence, stillness, with Presence A meditative/contemplative practice awakens your inner core The innate knowing you hold given at birth Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold the answers to all your questions They hold your truth, your true self They patiently wait

George G
