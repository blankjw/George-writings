THE MOMENT

By George Gondron | Source: THE MOMENT.docx

THE MOMENT There really is no 'the moment' Only moments, many, many moments Each one as important as the one before, as the one to come The difference being, we miss most of them Not being present to the now Our minds running ahead or lagging behind The present moment experienced on auto pilot, unaware Like the music group, we're 'asleep at the wheel' We miss so very much 'I pass this way every day, that building is new Wow, they must have built it overnight' 'I see her almost daily, when did she change her hair?' The uncontrolled mind, 'monkey mind', is like a kaleidoscope Constant changing patterns and vibrant colors Nothing more than an obscure blur that flashes by unnoticed We see only what we want to see, nothing more Just as we hear, think, and believe What we want to hear, think, and believe The mind alone is biased and prejudiced by nature Conditioned and programmed by our culture, our society We are not an 'each moment' people We are a productive, doing, ever active people We think, therefore we are But we don't feel, we don't reflect, we don't question, we react We listen passively, not actively For the most part, our minds are made up Addicted to our own way of thinking, nothing new gets in We are given life with all the necessary tools to thrive as humans It's as if we've only learned how to use one of our tools We've ignored for the most part, the rest, the others We're great at using the tool called the mind, the ego But we're infants when it comes to the tools of the heart, soul, spirit Without the use of all our tools we look only for the 'big moment' All the while missing the other moments that are just as important The now moments - in the silence and stillness, with the Presence We can become adults, no longer infants with our unused tools Our hearts, souls, and spirits teach us to be present to each moment

George G
