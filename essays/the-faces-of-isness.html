THE FACES OF ISNESS

By George Gondron

THE FACES OF ISNESS In the historical tome called the Bible In the very first book, Genesis, we find the creation myth In those verses, God says let us create, notice us is plural ‘Create in our image and likeness…..male and female he created then’ Our, of course, is also plural The prevailing image of God throughout history has been masculine Often depicted as an overpowering presence You might say, that’s the God of the ego, the male ego Whereas the feminine portrayal of God is better represented By the heart, soul, and spirit The more nurturing softer side of God Both images can be found throughout the Bible Yet it is in the teachings of Jesus that the feminine side of God Really is stressed more and more As a provider, a nurturer, a lover, that shows kindness You won’t find an angry, demanding, hostile God In the four Gospels Consequently, it leads one to wonder Why in modern-day Christianity do we not hear Words of inclusion, togetherness, unity, and cooperation Teaching about kindness, compassion, altruism, and love Why is it so difficult to practice what Jesus taught? Obviously, we much prefer to worship, and forget to practice Orthodoxy over orthopraxy, ego over heart We don’t like simple, we prefer complex and complicated We would rather defend than embrace The finger we point to others points back at us If change is needed, it’s them, not me, not us Every thought and feeling we possess, we choose Every action or nonaction we embrace, we choose All necessary change begins with me, with you, with us Begin a meditative/contemplative practice, a necessary balance Spend a few minutes daily in silence, be still, embrace Presence Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, discern They hold your true self, your truth, they patiently await

George G
