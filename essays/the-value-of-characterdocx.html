THE VALUE OF CHARACTER

By George Gondron

THE VALUE OF CHARACTER In roles of leadership, positions of power, character matters Over the centuries much has been written about the attributes Of and the importance we give to positive human character As with all things human, we must recognize that the spectrum Of human character varies, containing positive and negative Traits and values: let us begin with a few of the positive First is wisdom: which includes creativity, curiosity, perspective, Love of learning and open-mindedness, to see the possible Second humanity: represented by kindness, compassion, inclusion, Social intelligence, and love, more alike than unalike Third justice: fairness, partisan leadership, cooperative teamwork, Equality and freedom for all, not the few Fourth temperance: The ability to forgive, modesty, prudence, And self-regulation, never putting oneself above others Fifth transcendence: the significance of beauty, hope, gratitude, Humor, and mystery, recognizing knowledge is unlimited These are only a few of the potential positive attributes of A fully introspective human character Character eventually manifests itself in the words and actions Of each of us, especially those of notoriety, in the public eye A famous quote from Abraham Lincoln, ‘You can fool all the People some of the time, and some of the people all the time, But you cannot fool all of the people all of the time’ Now, a few of the negative character flaws Arrogance, dishonesty, negativity, self-centeredness, intolerance, Insensitivity, narcissism, indifference, deceitfulness, melodramatic, Vengefulness, disrespectfulness, overcritical, insecurity, Controlling, unforgiving, hypocrisy, ingratitude, paranoid, Pessimism, unpredictability, spitefulness, there are many more The words we hear produce images in the mind Those images have an impact on our choices, choose wisely Use all your human faculties, mind, heart, soul, and spirit To enable you to make important, meaningful decisions Reflection, critical thinking, intuition, and discernment matter A thoughtful response outweighs a ‘knee-jerk’ reaction Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Listen to your inner guides they have your answers, they wait

George G
