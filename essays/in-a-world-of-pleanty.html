IN A WORLD OF PLEANTY

By George Gondron

IN A WORLD OF PLENTY I have a question In a world of plenty, why do so many live in scarcity? The resources are available, no doubt about that There’s really only one answer to the question It is the selfishness of the human mind A mind that chooses fear over love So maybe, the better question Why fear over love? Is it because we’d rather teach fear, than love? Maybe, since fear is connected to control and manipulation Whereas love is about freedom, no strings attached And that kind of love is entirely too much freedom That kind of love is too scary Surely that kind of love demands something in return And not until we experience that kind of love Will we understand, there are no demands, no ‘quid pro quo’ But notice, it’s not an intellectual knowing at all It is experiential, a visceral knowing Once known, unable to be denied And unable to be put into words, it is singular And yet, within each of us, there is a yearning To know this kind of love, this kind of freedom To know a love grounded in a relational experience That has no hidden agenda, is that really possible? It is, to be rich without wealth, a paradox indeed Our mind is conditioned to think of wealth as tangible But freedom from fear is a much greater wealth The journey of life is about embracing change Personal change, but the ego prefers resistance Clinging to a familiar that doesn’t work, has no rewards Embrace this change, a meditative/contemplative change Spend just a few minutes a day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of Presence, your image of a creative Source Listen to the yearnings of your heart, soul, and spirit Experience the freedom of unconditional love Discover your true self, your inner truth You have the answers, your inner guide waits

George G
