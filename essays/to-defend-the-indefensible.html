TO DEFEND THE INDEFENSIBLE

By George Gondron | Source: TO DEFEND THE INDEFENSIBLE.docx

TO DEFEND THE INDEFENSIBLE How do you defend what is indefensible I could put forth a litany of psychological reasons But it would really be just a waste of time Because those who pick and choose don't really care about reasons Here's one, land of the free, home of the brave, equal rights Free for whom, equal rights for whom, bravery for whom Are those who 'scam the draft', are they brave, I think not Let the black guy, the brown guy, the Asian guy Die for me, I'm the rich educated white guy, my life is worth more Is my color okay, my belief okay, my 'side of the tracks' okay Here's another, I believe they're called 'Beatitudes' I don't remember hearing much about those in organized Christianity But I do remember it was the first recorded teaching of Jesus Or perhaps, 'feed the poor, clothe the naked, visit the prisoner Care for the orphan and widow, remove the beam from your own eye' That seems to have been neglected also What you do for the least of these, you do for me, and vice versa And this one is the best Love the lord your God with all your heart, soul, and strength And your neighbor as yourself Anyone who is able to do the last two, regardless of their religion Or their non-religion, is an authentic human being I don't think those words say anything about worship or attendance I don't think they mean just the neighbor next door But, alas we do our best to defend the indefensible Through our justification we say; well that's not really what it means For you see, whatever is inconvenient, uncomfortable, difficult We can and do defend, defend against This last one is really great, how do you defend rhetoric That fuels hatred, bitterness, animosity, distrust, and prejudice Please, give me a rational answer as we call ourselves Christian As we call ourselves people who believe in compassion and kindness Please tell me There is a saying, 'if you're not part of the solution, You're part of the problem', there are no innocent bystanders Think, reflect, question, doubt, discern; don't just react Be a part of a peaceful, compassionate, co-operative outcome, please

George G
