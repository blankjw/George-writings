thoughts and reflections

By George Gondron | Source: thoughts and reflections.docx

Thoughts and Reflections What challenges us more than anything are people. Challenges force us to become more present. Becoming more present rather than being pulled into the unconsciousness. On a physical level exercise is a challenge to the body. Our spiritual growth is challenged by unconscious people. The task is therefore, to not be pulled onto the unconscious yourself when encountering difficult and unconscious people, but to remain present and not reactive but rather be responsive. That is a spiritual practice in itself, just being present to.

Balance, equanimity, congruity - after being in a well lit room and stepping into the darkness, one's vision must adjust to the darkness in order to see the stars. Allowing the mind to be still, is the same process in order to be present to what manifests from within.

Wellness - a healthy balance of mind, body, and spirit that results in an overall feeling of well-being.

The ego, the false self, cannot love unconditionally, for the false self always has an agenda. Which by its very nature cannot be selfless and is therefore selfish. Love with an agenda is not love. The death of the familiar precedes the life of the possible. 1 Contemplation is a sense of coming home. It's not a trance, we do that easily enough. Putting ourselves into a state in which we can actually become aware of receiving grace, becoming aware of the givenness of eternal and infinite love. It's not earned, it's not something we receive for any cognitive reason, it's freely given in every moment, in every breath.

Aware that nutrition, what we ingest, effects our physical, mental, and emotional well-being is enough to give us the realization that our spirit, our soul also needs nourishment. Taking in bad ideas and cultivating a dehumanizing position pollutes the spirit, and leads to sickness of the soul, not just of the individual, but the society as well.

The true self expresses the universal through the heart, and the individual aspect of our character through the mind.

The value of relationship is finding love, again and again and again.

There is no substitute for a healthy relationship, but when taken for granted, they will eventually deteriorate.

The best substitute for experience, is being sixteen.

It's not what we see, but how we see. 2 When human value is not a part of the equation of any business model, then what's important is focus on the bottom line - profit only. The underlying hidden power of money is to enable us to forget our humanity, always.

Words are as mere grains of sand, but acts of compassion are as flawless diamonds.

The person who cannot forgive, is also the person who cannot love.

It is the manner in which we give that has more importance than what we give.

Our behavior, what we do and do not do, in every area of life, our lives, is the touchstone of who we are. There is no other measurement.

Any description of a genuine mystical or enlightened experience is always less than what it is. Any description of genuine love is always less than what it is. It is the value of inner personal experience that has importance.

External teaching is merely a guide, it's not the destination.

3 Words alone are as far from reality as watching a dance from the experience of the dancer, or hearing a song from the experience of the singer. The reality is always from the experience of the participant, not from the words that follow. Words do not suffice.

You cannot change what's dysfunctional in yourself with your own ego.

An opinion without facts is nothing more than a fantasy.

It is the heart that perceives the unknowable, not the mind.

You lose nothing through mere belief, but you also have the possibility of gaining everything.

If you want to go fast, go alone If you want to go far, go together - African proverb

Whatever is received is received according to the mode of the receiver.

The only true indicators of strength; to be able to cry with, to listen to, to show empathy, to be compassionate - yet seen as weakness by many

Religion either moves to the experiential, or to moralism and ritualism 4 You can't think your way into a new way of living, you can only live your way into a new way of thinking; knowledge is not experience; real experience leads to transformation.

If the conditions for peace do not exist interiorly, there will never be peace.

One cannot love too much, but we can love for the wrong motives. Genuine selfless love is not measured in degrees, it is what it is. Asking another how much, why, when, or what they love about you is the wrong question. The question is simply, do you love me.

The ego's addiction is always finite and transitory. The heart's yearning is the Infinite.

Life is not about where you live, it's about how you live.

Words cannot adequately explain or describe Ultimate Reality, yet the fight between various religions is over words.

Why would we want a God that's so small and insecure that they need our worship, rather than a God who is loving, compassionate, and merciful and wants our relationship.

5 A word is never the experience, and many times not the meaning as well.

Stillness and silence are the language of God. Everything else is a poor translation.

All metaphor, as it regards Ultimate Reality, is nothing more than an attempt at describing the Infinite with finite language.

The essence of prayer is attention, not intention. It is not about doing, it is about being present to.

What we call thinking is mostly obsessive, compulsive, mind commentary; internal dialogue which is 90% self-referential.

We don't have feelings, they have us.

Contemplation, the simple delight of the truth. - Thomas Aquinas

Solitude is not the practice of a withdrawal from, but rather a discovery of our own uniqueness and spirituality.

When you cling to the status quo, anyone who is different becomes the enemy.

6 Jesus did not come to change the mind of God about humanity, Jesus came to the change the mind of humanity about God.

As long as we fight what is emotionally, and never accept what is, we can never change what is.

Malicious behavior is almost always a result of unawareness, lack of consciousness, and sensitivity - not intent

The ego does not like differences, but loves uniformity. The Ground of All Being must love differences since from the beginning of creation there have been, are now, and always will be billions of people; each one different.

Postmodern Christianity, militaristic, and capitalistic greed worship their idea of a founder (which Jesus never was) and ignore the message.

Any religion or belief system that thinks dualistically is narcissistic and has a dark side.

The dualistic mind builds walls. The non-dual mind builds bridges.

It's impossible to change dysfunctional behavior or thinking with will power. 7 Self conversion is 99% self-deception.

Addiction equals spiritual illness.

All negative feelings arise from fear. All positive feelings arise from love. Persistent negative feelings are a result of living in the false-self. Persistent positive feelings are a result of living in the true-self.

No significant growth is possible on a human level until we face what we lack, face our shadow, face our perceived weakness, face our fear and our denial.

A safe place is a place where we can fail without fear of being abandoned or judged. Often we abandon and judge ourselves too harshly and too quickly. When we abandon and judge there is no place for forgiveness, and without forgiveness there is no possibility for change or growth into spiritual maturity.

Experiential knowledge is total knowledge. Intellectual knowledge is information only, not transformation. The first half of life is individuation, the second half of life is about transcendence.

Docta Ignorantia (learned ignorance) 8 What one thinks about on a consistent basis becomes their normal. That normal is not the same thing as healthy. In fact in most cases it's unhealthy because it keeps us stuck or with myopic vision. That same normal can then be translated into basic emotional attitudes; i.e. always anxious, angry, sad, jealous, judgmental, etc.

The inner journey is about learning to live without inner disturbances.

How can one ever expect to experience any senses of serenity without ever experiencing some silence and stillness?

If you want to become whole, allow yourself to be partial, incomplete, imperfect, and humble

The most fundamental virtue of the human condition is humility - accepting ourselves as we are, where we are - for we can't change what's not accepted

We are all a story that God is telling We are all a dance that God is dancing We are all a song that God is singing We are all a thought that God is thinking And if God stops, we are not. 9

In the presumption of defending another, a friend, may be protecting us from our need to grow. Seeking false support, seeking people who say we are right. Spiritual adulthood demands personal growth. Protection from personal struggle is not always a help, but often a hindrance to personal growth.

Patterns of addictive thinking - 93% of human thought is repetitive and useless. No problem can be solved by the same consciousness that caused it.

Until grace leads us to a place of uselessness, meaninglessness, depression, a feeling of being totally lost, aimlessness, a situation you can't fix or control, until then faith doesn't really kick-in.

Addictive thinkers are highly resistant to change. The process is to always change others. Transformative religion is also highly resistant. The ego will not allow you to see what needs to be changed or transformed, because is you do see it means the ego must give-in - the false-self must yield. There must be a metanoia, a new mind, new thinking. Addictive thinking is always distorted thinking, through denial, rationalization, and projection. Ironically weakness is the way through, not strength. Most people don't really want self-knowledge because it leads to the revelation of your shadow.

Silence is the common denominator of all religions. 10 Workshops, lectures, DVD's, CD's, books, retreats - all inspirational but necessarily transformative. What transforms you is what you do after the inspiration.

Addiction is nothing more than desire or attachment used up but never fulfilled. It's starts in the mind with a thought and repeats itself over and over unsuccessfully. It seems there's no spiritual curiosity in organized religion today, only an addiction to certain behaviors which never end up in a meaningful transformative place. It's all on the exterior, the surface, a superficial practice which we believe God wants, and in reality it's what God doesn't want. There's only one meaning for 'sin' and that's separation from God, worshipping through ego attachment and addiction. Nothing about that form of religion is transformative, only repetitive.

Emotional tyranny eventually controls your life. When one has a moral victory over a small thing, they remain small.

Accumulation of anything does not lead to happiness. It never has and never will. Properly named, it's called addiction.

The goal, to be able to give without expecting a response, an acknowledgment, a thank you, any attachment whatsoever.

11 True humility of a the giving person is to have no expectations at all.

Programs for happiness have to be rewired, security, safety, and power do not lead to happiness.

Every religion teaches that God is the ultimate ground of happiness and security.

You don't have feelings, most of the time, feelings have you.

Our ideas about another, positive or negative, are not the person, they are our prejudices.

We only hear what we're willing to hear. We only hear well, what we want to hear.

To learn something new, to take a different path requires risk, choosing the strange over the familiar. Becoming active in the process not just a passive participant. Willing to dig through the 'crap' to get to the gold or treasure. Like being on a journey and taking a new road.

Love is non-spatial, it is omnipresent.

12 An experience of the Divine is too self-evident to doubt and too deep to comprehend.

Sustained vulnerability brings change.

Rowan Williams - Arch Bishop of Canterbury Contemplation is very far from being just one kind of thing that Christians do, it is the key to prayer, liturgy, art, and ethics. The key to the essence of a renewed humanity that is capable of seeing the world with freedom. Freedom from self-oriented acquisitive habits and the distorted understanding that come from them. To put it boldly, contemplation is the only ultimate answer to the unreal and insane world that our financial systems and our advertising culture and our chaotic and unexamined emotions encourage us to inhabit. To learn a contemplative practice is to learn what we need so as to live truthfully and honestly and lovingly. It is a deeply revolutionary matter.

Vulnerability leads to freedom. How do I keep from being vulnerable? One way is talk incessantly. Being without words, we feel vulnerable.

Tradition and doctrine in any religion is only translative, not transformative. Transformation only happens through experience.

Belief is an idea, thought, or concept about the infinite. 13 There are no words to express the ineffable - all is metaphor.

Thomas Merton - You cannot love too much. You cannot love yourself or others too much, but you can love in misdirected ways.

To be grounded and realistic about life, existence, one must by necessity include a spiritual aspect to the life and being which is very difficult to do in a consumerist society, having a tendency to confuse religion with spirituality which it is not.

Most prejudice is merely an assumption about someone or something of which I have no direct experience or knowledge. In fact, most of use very little critical thinking at all. We depend on advertising, marketing, and other forms of propaganda to form our values and then become opinionated when things don't work out the way we expect. Have fun with that thought.

When we seek our meaning within ourselves, or rather we become the object of our existence, it only leads to frustration and anxiety. Our meaning has to be found in something much greater than ourselves. Not only found, but in relation with. To relate to only one's self at some point becomes very lonely. Being available is prima facie to being in community.

14 If the universe if God's creation, which includes human beings, then ultimately exclusion and discrimination cannot be compatible with God's plan.

When we encounter that which is different or strange, we are at a choice point. One leads to prejudice which is an easily understood cycle that manifests as follows; what we don't understand creates an immediate difference, that difference leads to doubt, doubt leads to fear, fear leads to hate, and from hate comes violence. Violence of a direct nature or through alienation and separateness. A safe haven for a dualistic mind that only comprehends opposites, being exclusive rather than inclusive. Maybe, just maybe it would be of benefit to understand our prejudices rather than just ignore them and cease operating from a knee-jerk position.
