POINTLESS EFFORTS

By George Gondron | Source: POINTLESS EFFORTS.docx

POINTLESS EFFORTS No amount of compassion, love, or concern Can open a closed mind As the saying goes, ‘You can lead a horse to water, But you can’t make them drink’ Once a mind has settled on a position, right or wrong Heaven or hell cannot break through The position of ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ Is the precursor to chaos, delusion, and dysfunction It’s the fundamentalist mind who if religious Insists on a literal interpretation of scripture The first Bible was printed in 1455 It has been reinterpreted thousands of times Whichever one chooses to support their belief It seems to be used as a tool for judging others And ironically justifying the reader’s own behavior Used more as a weapon than a tool for learning Used more for fear than love If you are a person unafraid of self-examination Unafraid to objectively and critically review your life Then I urge you, to stay focused on yourself For it is only yourself that you can change, no one else If we all stayed in our own lane humankind would benefit It is a rarity to find people who are willing to learn Willing to have an open discussion Able to change their minds when the facts reveal the truth The enemy is always, always, ourselves, not the other I trust the doubter, the questioner, the critical thinker The one able to reflect on their own behavior, thoughts, and feelings Instead of pointing a finger at others Stay true to your journey, the world will become a better place Don’t waste your efforts on the fallacy of another’s belief Allow them to find their own way If they want an honest interaction they will ask Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice, learn to listen Your belief in a Creator always knows what you need For a few minutes each day be silent, be still, be present Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
