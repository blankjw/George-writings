OUT OF SIGHT OUT OF MIND

By George Gondron

OUT OF SIGHT OUT OF MIND For most in our society Serious learning ceases after our formal education We live in a culture that utilizes polarized thinking Dealing with opposites without a middle ground Right/wrong, good/bad, liberal/conservative, etc. Gravitating mostly to the narrative that stirs our emotions Not our intellect We cease to use critical thinking, to question, to doubt We become grounded in information that has no Factual reliability, only hearsay or half-truths Our capacity for compassion, kindness, and understanding Seems to lessen as we become mired in alternative facts Once again, facts that hook our emotions, not our intellect And these attractions have become so strong We are able to deny facts, proven facts Maybe by refusing to see and hear the truth We can rationalize and justify our lack of perception Please, turn off the TV, I don’t want to see that Change the channel, it’s nothing but propaganda For you see, once our way of perceiving, or lack of perception Becomes our familiar, we are hard-pressed to change Our minds have become closed Especially closed to the fact that we just might be wrong We no longer listen to our conscience, our intuition We follow our emotions which are easily manipulated We hear and see what we want to hear and see, it is easier We don’t need to critically think, question, or doubt The truth doesn’t just fall in our lap, it needs to be searched for Only a fool trusts the truth in the hands of another Regardless of the source, political, religious, economic, etc. Find balance, discover the middle way Life is not just either/or, it is always both/and Begin a meditative/contemplative daily practice For just a few minutes, be silent, be still, and embrace Presence Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
