CHOICES

By George Gondron

CHOICES To become aware of the importance of choice Is more the exception than the rule Most don’t readily realize that their entire lives Have been about choices Mostly personal choices No one held a gun to our heads as we made those choices We alone are responsible, no one else And not until we recognize that fact Do we have the possibility For a new frame of reference For our options before that awakening Are limited to either acceptance or blame Making the painful results of some of those choices Someone else’s fault, and not our own Of course, here’s the whole reality of expectations And also, of fairness Show me where it is written That life is fair, and expectations are always met Any decision tied to future expectations Has the possibility to fail It’s called risk and being vulnerable And it’s the nature of a life lived to the full But it seems our ‘go to’ response when there is failure Or when expectations are not met Is look elsewhere for the cause first But never to ourselves To take ownership of one’s own faults Requires a level of awareness seldom seen In this culture The ego is too inflated to admit fault or take blame Leading only to further dysfunction “?{ Imagine what a better world could exist If we all took ownership of our choices Learned from those choices, which is called experience And helped promote a more congruent environment Be aware of your choices and their potential failure

George G
