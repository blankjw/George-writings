IDENTIFICATION

By George Gondron

IDENTIFICATION How we identify ourselves beyond simply being human Becomes our label or labels As a result, at some point those labels become permanent Beyond a permanent identification of gender, of relationship Wife, husband, single, brother, sister, parent, etc. All else becomes a trap, an ego identity A mask, a persona, a façade that becomes a handicap Maybe I identify with my work, what I do So 24/7 I’m the worker, that’s my permanent label Or perhaps I have a poor self-image, low self-esteem So I identify as a victim, undeserving of happiness Or maybe I’ve experienced unhappy relationships So I must be unlovable or unworthy Or better than those I’ve chosen as my relationships The ego’s energy and focus are about how we Compare, contrast, and compete with others And on a human-to-human level is never-ending There are no winners, ever In our human connections, where there is a string attached Where there are expectations, there is no freedom For the labels we give ourselves, we also give to others When the mind, the ego controls We never experience for ourselves labels such as kind, Compassionate, selfless, empathetic, and unconditional love The innate wholeness of each human being Is found in the connection of mind, heart, soul, and spirit It is not just the intellect, the ego The ego alone is the construct of a false dysfunctional self So, how do we begin to operate from our ‘true self’? From the innate knowing and creativity given with our birth Choose a meditative/contemplative path A daily practice of a few minutes of silence, with stillness, In the presence of Presence, your idea and image of God Empty your mind and experience just being, not doing We are so much more than just our thoughts Listen to the yearnings and whispers of your own Heart, soul, and spirit, they hold your healing and truth

George G
