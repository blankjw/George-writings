HYPOCRISY

By George Gondron

HYPOCRISY Who among us has not been a hypocrite? But the more important question is not who, but why To state, expound, declare, and profess That you believe or support strongly any ideology Any doctrine or belief system And act counter to that belief is for one reason only To adhere to the principles of that belief, at all times Is a hindrance, an inconvenience to my lifestyle It's much easier to justify my actions when they are Counter to what I say I believe, to avoid discomfort It's much easier to pick and choose from that belief system What applies to me and what doesn't Which only makes what I believe in, a convenience Not a reality Pretender, fraud, deceiver, backslider, sham, and bigot Just a few of the many definitions of a hypocrite The word in its' original Greek form, 'hypokrisis', or "play-acting" Pretending to be something I'm not, acting a role But even more deadly, more dangerous, more destructive I am deceiving myself I am the 'whitened sepulcher' Clean on the outside, dark on the inside Hiding my doubts, my fears, my distrust and skepticism Not only from others but more importantly from myself I run from my 'shadow' not only in my dreams But in my waking state as well I run from that which in the end, will save me from my hypocrisy I have become, as the song declares, 'The Great Pretender' Pretense does only one thing, it keeps the lie alive It is the distraction from that which I say I believe Because it keeps me comfortable in my hypocrisy The road to 'hell', our personal hell, our hidden hell Is always 'paved with good intentions' That is its' hypocrisy, its' pretense, its' justification The world is run by its' hypocrites Through their power, their wealth, their control Let us hope it survives, let us hope we survive, our hypocrisy

George G
