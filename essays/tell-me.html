TELL ME

By George Gondron

TELL ME It's not what you do It's not where you are from It's not your name These do not tell me who you are If anything, they separate us They put difference first, not similarity What prevents us from wondering, 'how are you like me?' What do we share in common? Beyond our humanness Your heart beats as mine, our blood runs red We breath the same air and feel the breeze We taste the same rain, warmed by the same sun It is your story, that's how I know you It is your history, brief or extended The pain and the joy The loss and the gain Moments of dreams fulfilled, hopes forgotten A tome of experience unique to your life A limited edition, unrepeatable Nothing is more sacred than your story A best seller, written only once Tell me your story that I might know you Tell me your story that I might understand Tell me your story that I can appreciate you Tell me your story that I can express my love Tell me your story Tell me

George G
