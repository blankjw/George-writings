INDIVIDUAL CREATICITY

By George Gondron | Source: INDIVIDUAL CREATICITY.docx

INDIVIDUAL CREATIVITY All creativity is an inside job The catalyst may be an outside source As a result of our sensory experiences, our thinking and feeling But the finished product comes from our imagination From our willingness to think, reflect, research, and experiment Regardless of our field of interest General sciences, liberal arts, humanities, art, poetry, Sculpting, writing, music, dance, the list is endless Our ability to be creative is limitless Even when one may lose what most of us take for granted Our sight or our hearing The human mind is able to compensate and adapt That it might live a satisfactory and purposeful life Another example of individual human creativity However, most of us ‘settle’ Settle for what is and how it is Even out imagination seems to go dormant Many of the world’s great discoveries were made by people Well past mid-life, some without hard research Only their imaginations, their dreams, their hopes Have we ceased to hope, dream, imagine? Just ask ‘Siri’ or find the answer on social media There is a creative spark within each human life That spark never disappears but can and does remain dormant Is it a lack of effort or information overload, probably both Often the path we take at a fork in the road Is the assumed easier path, not the imaginative one Discover your own creativity, your special gift Grandma Moses didn’t begin to paint until she was seventy Leonardo da Vinci was fifty-one when he began the Mona Lisa Einstein formulated the theory of relativity at age twenty four It’s never too late or too early to discover your own creativity Begin a meditative/contemplative practice, find balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Breathe in, breathe out, empty your mind, learn to focus Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
