ENOUGH

By George Gondron

ENOUGH What is enough? Enough, money, food, assets, clothes, shoes, jewelry, guns, toys, things? I suspect the discover of what is enough must arise from need and not want Ah, there's the rub, how to define the difference A slim possibility living in the richest nation on earth Perhaps it's only the poor that can tell us Perhaps true need is only seen in their faces, in their daily struggle to survive What do we know of basic survival? Very little when I must have 'things' labeled as needs When what I gather is more important than human life When what I possess has more meaning than kinship Some say, that the Creator of this universe provides us with all we need Whatever His or Her name If so, how do so many have so little? So few, have so much? I've heard the great teachers of all beliefs espouse a universal law of 'the common good' I'm not sure how that's manifest, but I have a suspicion I suspect the much of the few surpasses the needs of the many And the few with much, build great structures to worship their God that provides all If one dares to reflect, it becomes very confusing The difference between need and want The common good for all humanity One could call it false piety, if they believe in God

George G
