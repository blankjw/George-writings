WHEN TO LET GO

By George Gondron

WHEN TO LET GO When you can’t have an open discussion – let go When it becomes obvious, you’re not being heard – let go When facial expressions and body language clearly reveal There is no interest in your story – let go If you’re constantly being interrupted as you are sharing – let go When the response you receive is to defend – let go When your thoughts and feelings don’t matter – let go When the only important narrative is what The other person has to say – let go When the interaction becomes an argument And no longer a discussion – let go When you’re being told what to think, what to feel – let go When the other person becomes a mind reader – let go When the other person finishes your sentences – let go There is a great difference between an active and passive listener An active listener is sincerely interested in what you have to say A passive listener forms an opinion out of ignorance For they never head you to begin with The passive listener only cares about their opinions Not yours A person interested in learning is an active listener A person only interested in defending is a passive listener Which applies to all the psychological defense mechanisms Denial, projection, rationalization, and avoidance, to name a few A person curious to learn is an active listener with an open mind An unteachable person has a stated agenda and a closed mind Be teachable, be a seeker, be curious, be a learner To bring the needed balance to your life Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice For just a few minutes each day be silent, be still, Be in the presence of Presence, your image of a creative Source Be connected to the Consciousness of the universe Empty your mind of all thoughts and focus on a short mantra I.E. breathe in wellness to yourself, breathe out wellness to others The mind will still that you might hear the whispers of your Heart, soul, and spirit, they hold your truth, they have your answers They reveal your true self, they patiently wait

George G
