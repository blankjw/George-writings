LIFE AS RELATIONSHIP

By George Gondron

LIFE AS RELATIONSHIP What is life, why do we exist? I know one answer, one answer only Life is relationship, communal Meant to be shared, connected I know no other reason, no meaningful purpose We support and care for one another Unless of course, what matters is not people It's really as simple as that, people And of course the reality is often contradictory Depending on what one chooses to do If life is about 'getting, acquiring, accumulating' 'Accomplishing, succeeding', and 'possessing' Somehow, relationship gets lost, shoved in the corner Given just lip service, no longer the 'main event' In the beginning, what was 'quality time' Has now become, 'I don't have the time' The more involved I become, the more activity The less I know myself, who I really am For in the doing, justified by reason, I lose my being I lose my connection with my deeper self And then with others, and soon, it's all external Everything becomes object, no longer a subject The connection is gone Replaced with apathetic complacency Meaningful connection, lasting relationships Require effort, some amount of endeavor Like plants in a garden, they need care and nurture Else they wither and die Ask the question, 'what is life?' You have the answer It's not hidden, it's in plain sight, look in the mirror

George G
