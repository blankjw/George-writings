OPINION VERSUS FACT

By George Gondron

OPINION VERSUS FACT The gullibility of a noncritical mind Is more easily influenced by opinion than fact Ergo, the basis for the age-old saying ‘Don’t believe what you hear, and half of what you see’ Opinion works because it pulls on the emotions Not the intellect It has a repetitive nature Tell a half-truth or a lie repeatedly and it becomes a fact Whether it concerns the individual or the group Where lack of understanding creeps in, fear begins Fear, unabated leads to hate When hate becomes the narrative, love is pushed aside Where there is no love, chaos follows, always If what we believe is because of opinion Because of third-party information and not direct experience Then we unwittingly become perpetrators of the same We take the easy way out We fail to question, doubt, and do our own due diligence It stirs our emotions, we see through a glass darkly It promotes an inflated ego, a self-righteous attitude And we sincerely believe the opinions are the absolute truth We become complicit in the actions that follow Through our nonaction, wearing blinders, our heads in the sand We remain asleep while awake All the aforementioned is the result of a dualistic mind An either/or attitude versus a both/and reality And our closed minds, remain closed A fully human existence demands balance, inner and outer Needs a connection to an inner core, an internal reference In the name of wholeness choose a contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, in stillness, In the presence of Presence, your image of God Empty your ever-busy ‘monkey mind’ of its incessant And repetitive thoughts that go nowhere and do no-thing Become aware of the vast amount of resources Held within your own heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your truth, they patiently await

George G
