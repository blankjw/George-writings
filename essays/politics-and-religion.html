POLITICS AND RELIGION

By George Gondron

POLITICS AND RELIGION It’s interesting to note that there are two topics Politics and religion (many more as well) that we Avoid exploring in our relationships, more interesting is this If we are uncomfortable in that exploration, we limit Our ability to really know one another For whatever our values, our ethics, our integrity They are influenced by our beliefs on anything And everything we hold to be important, the real problem We form these beliefs with very little ‘hands-on’ Personal experience, firsthand knowledge But the emotional connections we make to topics Which are controversial are often stronger than Our ability to discern intellectually what is factually True versus what is obviously false, or distorted truth As long as we give up critical thinking Give up our ability to question, to doubt, to reflect We will accept third-party information, social media content Without any guardrails, without our own research And as a result, whatever attracts and stirs our emotions Leads us to become victims of our own ignorance If we are guarded in our personal beliefs formed from Personal experience then all our relationships will Suffer, for they too are guarded There are several ways to ‘know’ Two of which are discernment and intuition Neither of which are accomplished through ego accomplishments Very few people use the important gifts of either Most of us are trapped in our emotions as well as Dualistic thinking, right/wrong, good/bad, either/or Any topic that can’t be discussed in an open respectful manner Speaks to the maturity of ourselves, not the topic We are called to be fully human, not fractured in our existence But we struggle in our Western culture to live a balanced life One that incorporates mind, heart, body, and soul Choose a meditative/contemplative daily practice Listen in the silence, be present in the stillness, with Presence Embrace your ‘true self’ sustained by universal Consciousness

George G
