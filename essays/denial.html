DENIAL

By George Gondron

DENIAL Of the many defense mechanisms At the top of the list is denial Number one learned at a very young age So common one would think it’s genetic We’ve all used it And most of us still do Regardless of age What we never consider as adults Is the reality that denial is a lie It can’t be defined in any other way We can all see in our minds The young child being asked ‘Did you do that?’ And the immediate response ‘No’ The answer on some level is very revealing It means the child has already learned Somehow, they are flawed They’ve done something ‘wrong’ It’s of course not a reasoned thought More of a ‘knee-jerk reaction Carry this image much further down the road Now that child is an adult, still using denial But now it can be much more damaging Now it incorporates its first cousin, blame Wonderful combination, denial and blame And to emphasize the end result of both All we need do is look no further than Our own relationships to see the evidence It is rampant in politics, religion, education, The job environment Anywhere there is a closed mind, anywhere To escape the denial/blame vicious circle We must be self-reflective, self-critical Change can only happen through acceptance Not through denial and blame Accept yourself, love yourself, change yourself

George G
