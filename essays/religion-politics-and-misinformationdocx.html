RELIGION, POLITICS, AND MISINFORMATION

By George Gondron

RELIGION, POLITICS, AND MISINFORMATION We are currently witnessing how influential social media has become and how little verifiable truth matters There is only one reason for this phenomenon People are more readily influenced through their emotions than through their intellect Or as the famous analytical psychologist Carl Jung once said ‘Thinking is difficult, therefore let the herd pronounce judgment’ It is frightening how we have fallen into a level of consciousness and awareness that cares little about actual facts and truth There are numerous examples throughout history that speak to the power of emotional rhetoric demonstrated by politicians and religious leaders whose only purpose is to manipulate and control others for their own benefit – no other reason Yes, it has become quiet obvious, thinking is difficult We turn a blind eye and a deaf ear to critical thinking, to facts, to verifiable provable truth, our beliefs are very polarized and we are right and they are wrong Obstinate and close-minded positions live by one statement ‘My mind is made, don’t confuse me with the facts. We have relinquished our power to choose by following the loudest voice, drinking the Kool Aid, and blaming others for what is our own responsibility – we remain ignorant by choice One of our greatest gifts has been, is now, and will always be our ability learn, to, further educate ourselves The majority of what’s offered on social media is not for learning it is for manipulation, consumption, power, and control We relinquish the state of our daily lives to the political machine We forfeit our connection to the Mystery of life in following ultra-right wing conservative religion, worship without relationship As individuals we desperately need to connect with our own inner core, our innate internal references – we need balance Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your life For a few minutes each day be silent, be still, be present to Isness Empty your ever busy ‘monkey mind’ of incessant and repetitive thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing – focus on your breath Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, and they wait

George G
