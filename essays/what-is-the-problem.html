WHAT IS THE PROBLEM

By George Gondron

WHAT IS THE PROBLEM Hopefully, you know by now The problem is never the problem It’s always the underlying resistance to solving the problem We have in this nation the economic power To fix all problems we obviously and clearly have Individually and collectively But as it turns out, we simply lack the will to do so Individually and collectively We use favored arguments to support our lack of will It would lead to and support ‘socialism’ It would deprive the individual the struggle Of being responsible for their own lives Regardless of the unknown situations in which they exist Are we really ‘our brother/sister’s keeper’ Not by a long shot, not even close The discrepancies between ‘equality and justice for all’ Or too obvious, too glaringly obvious None so blind as those who will not see None so deaf as those who will not hear In the world’s richest nation, that’s who we are Maybe, just maybe we can stop and reflect on What is important in this nation, as individuals It most assuredly is not the well-being of all its people It is more about production and consumption More about exclusion than inclusion More about division than unity More about wealth, power, and control More about what in the end is unimportant And less about what always is more important, human beings We have become nothing more than ‘guilty bystanders’ Individually and collectively, blind followers Corporate, political, and religious puppets all Adopt a daily meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes of silence, stillness, in the presence of your God The name does not matter, the relational experience does Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, your true self

George
