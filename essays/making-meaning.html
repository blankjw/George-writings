MAKING MEANING

By George Gondron

MAKING MEANING We make meaning through our five senses We make meaning through our thoughts and feelings That meaning is either conscious and purposeful Or unconscious and unaware, but meaning is always present Without a cognitive reflection and understanding of What does this experience mean? The only other option is an assumption We humans, it seems, make a lot of assumptions And as the old saying goes, ‘When I assume, I make an ass out of you and me’ We gather truth through meaning, not assumptions The building blocks of truth are personal experiences Assumptions have the capability of containing Truth, half-truths, or no truth at all Without testing the assumption, the risk is to believe the lie In our current culture, there is more than enough evidence To support the reality that many people, through assumption Through a willingness to be followers versus truth seekers Have been numbed to believing the lie A phenomenon that demonstrates If you tell a lie enough times, it becomes the truth Social media writ large knows how that works very well Advertising, marketing, and propaganda are not there To be truthful, they are there to sell you something Whether that’s a product for your consumption Or an idea to convince you of something And that idea is almost entirely about following them The ‘ole’ don’t do as I do, do as I say do Always question your first meaning Normally a ‘knee-jerk’ reaction that stirs your emotions A mind that never questions or doubts Is easily manipulated, guided, and controlled A guard against a false narrative is the important Balance between the head and the heart A practice daily of silence, stillness, with Presence Time to listen to the whispers of heart, soul, and spirit Listen to your inner Truth, It’s always there

George G
