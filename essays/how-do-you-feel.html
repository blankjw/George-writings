HOW DO YOU FEEL

By George Gondron

HOW DO YOU FEEL? When the question is asked, 'how do you feel?' In most instances it's the mind that answers, not the heart And the answer is given in the form of a thought, not a feeling The examples are endless and are used daily How are you today? 'I'm fine, I'm OK, I'm great' Tell me, what does fine, OK, and great feel like. Or we may be asked, 'what do you think I should do?' Answer, 'I feel like you should......' Instead of, 'I think you might.......' When did thoughts become feelings? Sadly, the issue is much more revealing than it first appears Not only do we confuse feelings with thoughts But most, honestly, don't really know what they feel Oh, correction They do know one very common feeling, anger But if asked, tell me, what's the feeling under the anger? What's that saying, 'a deer in the headlight' look, right? And even though women are much better at expressing feelings All of us struggle to name what we feel Hundreds of feelings are experienced everyday Telling us about ourselves, and they are ignored Shoved aside and not fully experienced And if we don't allow ourselves to feel them As a result, we don't know how to share or express them Perhaps that's why children have such a difficult time Sharing their own feelings, especially after being told 'You shouldn't feel that way', how terribly sad We've just taught them that feelings don't matter In many ways our feelings are much more important Than our thoughts, for feelings never lie, thoughts do It's a ridiculous statement to tell another person You shouldn't feel that way; you've just insulted them It diminishes their reality, so now they hide their feelings For your own benefit, your own wellbeing, your own health Listen to your feelings, listen to your heart, identify them They are who you are, much more than just your thoughts

George G
