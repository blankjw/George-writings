CHRISTIANITY'S STRUGGLE TO CONTINUE

By George Gondron

CHRISTIANITY’S STRUGGLE TO CONTINUE Approximately, every five hundred years Christianity struggles through a significant change In 313 Ad Emperor Constantine declared Christianity To be the religion of the Roman Empire In 476 AD the Roman Empire fell, and chaos followed In 1054 Christianity split between the Roman Catholic Church in the West and the Greek Orthodox church in the East The Great Schism In 1517 we witness the Protestant Reformation Initiated by Martin Luther, an Augustinian friar Nailing his 95 Theses to the church door And now, in the 21st century, we witness the following Whether Protestant or Catholic a narrative stream focused On abortion, LGBTQIA+ equality, all-male leadership, inerrancy Or infallibility of religious authorities, a struggle to maintain White male supremacy, colonialism, patriarchy, and rape Of the planet for profit Right-wing conservative fundamental evangelicalism Straining to join church and state in all matters Seemingly unaware of institutionalized racism, police Brutality, destruction of planet earth, gross economic Inequality, the increase in violence, political corruption, And mounting numbers of mass shootings and homegrown Terrorists Christianity has become a religion of fear, not love A religion of control, not freedom Empty of spiritual content, enforcing religion Millionaire media moguls taking from the poor To sustain themselves, preaching prosperity theology Giving reference to the One they say they follow occasionally Spiritual truth is not found in buildings of brick and mortar It is found in the silence and stillness with Presence It is held within the heart, soul, and spirit of each of us Discovered in a daily contemplative practice A few minutes to empty our minds of incessant repetitive thoughts It is experiential, not intellectual, a sensed knowing The saying, ‘Be still and know that I am God’, is true

George G
