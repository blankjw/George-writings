CONVENIENT BELIEF

By George Gondron

CONVENIENT BELIEF For most, religion is a convenient belief Held in the mind, but not in the heart Practiced on occasion as a temporary precaution Focused on worship, lax on praxis How did religion become a goal? A 'how to' to get someplace called 'heaven' Or a means to avoid a place called 'hell' How did it miss the message of a way of life I believe one religion was originally called the 'Way' And somehow morphed into Christianity Then developed rules, rites, dogma, and doctrine Which its purported founder, unknowingly Never did But he did teach the 'beatitudes' He demonstrated kindness, forgiveness, compassion Talked about a death to the 'false-self' A death to the ego, an awakening of the heart And if you read carefully and see beyond literalism We are blind to heaven now, as well as hell When the goal is to merit someplace to go And avoid someplace to be You miss the now I believe he said, this Jesus, There are only two things that really matter Love God Love your neighbor as yourself Love can only happen in the present moment It can't happen in the past It doesn't dwell in the future Love is always, now

George G
