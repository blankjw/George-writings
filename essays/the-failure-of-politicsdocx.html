THE FAILURE OF POLITICS

By George Gondron

THE FAILURE OF POLITICS One thing is certain, party is more important than policy Party is more important than constituents Of course that depends on your definition of constituent If your definition includes ‘follow the money’ Then corporations and billionaires and millionaires They are the constituents represented by the thousands of lobbyists That roam the halls of democracy That gratuitously pour millions of dollars into the campaign coffers Of current and potential elected officials We need at least three changes in politics if all people Are to be fairly represented in this Republic, this democracy Term limits Campaign reform The elimination of lobbyists It has become glaringly obvious that politicians don’t serve At the behest of our citizenry, they serve at the behest of themselves With a beginning salary of $174,000 per annum Not to mention the many perks they receive as well Our current tax laws benefit the wealthy and corporations And almost all of progressive legislation, legislation that benefits All of our citizens has been passed under the auspices of A Democratic administration – Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid, Civil Rights Act, Suffrage Act, Voting Rights Act, Child Labor Laws, Universal Health Care, workers’ rights, Fair Labor Standards Act, Unemployment Benefits – and many, many more There is obvious abuse on both sides of the aisle But all the aforementioned advances were rejected by Republicans Our current Congress held by the Republican majority Has accomplished less under this administration than Any Congress in the past Currently one party motivates with fear, one with hope Choose wisely, every vote matters Through a meditative/contemplative practice we can learn To listen to our better angels, learn to discern a better choice A few minutes each day be silent, be still, embrace Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
