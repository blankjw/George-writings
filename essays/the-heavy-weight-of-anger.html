THE HEAVY WEIGHT OF ANGER

By George Gondron | Source: THE HEAVY WEIGHT OF ANGER.docx

THE HEAVY WEIGHT OF ANGER Those who live with anger carry a heavy burden It seems they rid themselves of anger through guess what? More anger, and the load gets heavier It's like the patient who goes to the doctor for pain 'Hey, Doc it hurts when I bend my arm' 'Well', said the Doc, 'don't bend your arm' Here's the bottom line as it relates to anger Anger is just a surface emotion, a 'knee jerk' reaction Anger is a very valid, normal, human feeling But when it's used as an excuse for other feelings To cover up the real issue, the underlying problem Then it never goes away What's even more telling is that most of us Become so accustomed to reacting through our anger We never have a hint our anger is really about something else Just a few of the emotions underlying anger are Fear, frustration, disappointment, loss, anxiety, abandonment Rejection, humiliation, hurt, feeling trapped or pressured The list is endless, anger in most cases is a secondary emotion The next heavy burden associated with anger is physical It's a well know documented scientific fact, ignored emotions Or feelings that are merely displaced inappropriately Will manifest themselves in a physical form Which supports the saying, 'healthy mind, healthy body' We do in many instances create our own illness Of course the easiest way to deal with unrecognized emotions Which we simply call anger is through projection It seems to be the preferred method, be angry at someone But, once again it doesn't work It merely helps us hold on to our anger As the Buddha is often quoted, 'Holding onto anger is like drinking poison and expecting the other person to die' There is selfless anger, but most is selfish When anger arises, take time to breathe, to be still What lies beneath the anger is the lingering problem Which in most cases has to do with ego control Anger clings, love lets go to be present to now, always love

George G
