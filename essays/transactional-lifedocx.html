TRANSACTIONAL LIFE

By George Gondron

TRANSACTIONAL LIFE Life for most of us is about the completion of transactions You do this for me and I’ll do something for you Does not matter what the ‘you’ is; another person, a job, an educational goal, etc., etc., etc. And of course, there’s always a spoken or unspoken expectation connected to the transaction Transactional life is very dualistic, black and white with little grey We escape black and white thinking once we experience the capacity for critical thinking, especially self-critical thinking When we question, doubt, examine, research, and approach life with an open versus a closed mind The only words that matter to a closed mind are those that support their own level of awareness and consciousness Once we complete our formal education, be that primary or advanced, we seldom listen to learn, we listen to defend Our patterns, habits, and routines become so ingrained that we begin to operate with a closed versus an open mind Life becomes transactional which is coupled with unspoken expectations and invalid assumptions And the definition of an assumption – I, you, we assume and make an ‘ass’ out of us all Assumptions are nothing more than guesses and almost always wrong When life becomes transactional people become objects They are either for me/you or against me/you Consequently, our level of awareness and consciousness never changes, we cling to what’s familiar even if it’s painful The process of individuation and self-actualization is both outer and inner, always both/and not either/or It is the lack of an internal life, no connection to our inner core that leads to a life of ‘transactions’ not a life of needed experience Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life Just a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Support your inner core, embrace your intuition, wake up Bring balance to your life, focus on your breath or a short mantra The mind will wander, go back to your mantra or breath Rinse and repeat, listen to the whispers of heart, soul, spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
