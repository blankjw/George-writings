TWO COUSINS

By George Gondron | Source: TWO COUSINS.docx

TWO COUSINS Accountability and responsibility, two cousins How are they made manifest in reality, in everyday life? Maybe the better question is, how are they avoided? That’s easy to answer It’s through the use of one or more of the psychological Defense mechanisms, denial, rationalization, intellectualization, Projection, sublimation, avoidance, anything to relieve Ourselves of accountability or responsibility Through the disconnect of a dualistic mind An ego with no connection to heart, soul, and spirit Seldom do we reflect or give critical thought To our own responsibilities as human beings To our own accountability to other human beings The ego is not really focused on other But focused mainly on itself or perhaps family And then there’s the ‘fall back’ position called blame It’s his/her/their fault, the finger pointed at them To be accountable, to be responsible is not an ego ideal The ego is always willing to take credit for what is ‘right’ But seldom able to take ownership of what is wrong Corporate entities as well avoid accountability to protect Their profits, hiding behind their legal representatives Shouting they are not responsible, ‘let the buyer beware’ Without a foundation of basic morality The individual and the group refuse to be accountable Witness the dysfunctional atmosphere of our society today In every arena, political, religious, educational, economic Without a paradigm shift in group consciousness Our only hope is individual change, you and me Be a change agent, stand up for what’s right Choose a meditative/contemplative path A few minutes each day of silence, and stillness, with Presence Empty your every busy mental activity, constant thinking Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold an innate truth, your true self given at birth They will never fail you, be accountable, be responsible Connect inner and outer, become more fully human

George G
