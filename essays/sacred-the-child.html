SACRED THE CHILD

By George Gondron

SACRED THE CHILD The eyes of wisdom, those of the child An infectious smile, is that of the child A laugh like no other, the child's Innocence of expression, guileless emotions They are what they appear to be True to their nature, no pretense Spontaneous in their playfulness Not yet tainted with the deceptions they will learn Not yet shaped to another's liking Not yet forced to follow a path not chosen Totally dependent on the awareness of others The awareness, or lack thereof They learn so much about relationship Like sponges soaking up their environment And who is responsible for what they learn We are We are their teachers, their models, their idols We are their fear, their hurt, their sorrow as well They learn so much from us How to hide, reveal, or stuff their feelings How to relate, communicate, deny, or blame How to share pain, how to weep, how to hurt Most essential, how to love, to forgive, to forget They are sacred, the children We must rediscover our sacredness, we must Like knows like, love knows love The sacred child knows our child within Our child within cries for new life The two can meet But, we first must choose, we must
