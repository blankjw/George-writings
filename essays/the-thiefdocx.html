THE THIEF

By George Gondron

THE THIEF Childhood trauma is a thief It steals from the innocent who are not capable Of dealing with horror and devastation at a cognitive level Those skills have yet to be developed And the traumatic experiences may be indelibly Stamped on their young psyche for life They have not yet mastered the psychological defense mechanisms We adults use repeatedly for our own coping As we witness what’s happening in Gaza at this moment Receiving the news of the thousands that have died We also need to reflect on the impact of this tragedy On those young children who have survived In a real sense, their childhood has been stolen from them As people are uselessly slaughtered in the name of God And words echo, ‘you kill one of mine I’ll kill one hundred of yours’ War has become a pastime and it leads one to think Will the world ever be at peace? Will the mind of man ever be satisfied? All evidence to the contrary, it appears not And who is to blame?, well God of course I think not, it is the selfishness of the human mind In particular, the male human mind And in the meantime, all suffer, women, children, elderly Violence begets more violence, always And factually, it’s only a handful of individuals That perpetuates war for a handful of reasons, all selfish For power, for wealth, for control, and for religion Enough is never enough Our greatest influence in life is not our too many words It is our behavior, what we do or do not do is what matters Choose a daily meditative/contemplative practice Connect with your true self, your inner truth Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, embrace Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Your better self, they have your answers, learn to discern They patiently wait for your attention

George G
