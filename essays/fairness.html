FAIRNESS

By George Gondron

FAIRNESS Like love, it's easier to say what fairness is not Than what fairness is Little children learn soon the words 'That's not fair' What they're really saying is, 'I want that' And what they want, whether thing or action Is unimportant, it is the idea of fairness Better translated as selfishness I deserve what you have I deserve to be doing what you are doing Perhaps the better meaning of fairness is Is it fair that anyone goes without a meal Is it fair that some can't get medical care Is it fair that people live on the street Is it fair our veterans go without Is it fair that young people can't afford college Is it fair that our jobs are shipped overseas Ah, you say, if people worked harder There would be no unfairness Do you truly believe that, really? That is your solution to the unfairness, work harder If so, you are blind with eyes wide open You are deaf with ears that can hear You refuse to see the greed that abounds The hunger for power of those in control The willingness of a few to step on the many Wake-up! see the unfairness If not, you may be next Or your son, daughter, or grandchild Please, for their sake, wake-up

George G
