A CHILD'S GREATEST INFLUENCE

By George Gondron

A CHILD’S GREATEST INFLUENCE A child’s greatest influence in the past, now, and in the future Has been, is, and will always be the child’s immediate family Not the church, not the school, not outside activities It is the family first and foremost Parental guidance and influence doesn’t fail consciously It fails as a result of extenuating circumstances Especially in a culture that is driven by consumption A child’s basic character and personality is formed between The ages of three and five How a parent interacts with their children The quality time spent with each child Is much more powerful than any other influence on the child Adults are role models for children, it’s that simple What an adult does or does not do as a result of their thoughts, Feelings, and actions influences the child Throw into the mix the explosion of social media in all its forms And the problem becomes much more complicated Recent research clearly reveals the negative influence of social media Childhood depression, mental illness, suicides have sky rocketed The things that every parent values held in their hearts, souls, And spirits regarding their children have been overwhelmed By the lies of a culture where humans are nothing but a means To an end – people are secondary, things, material and Otherwise are primary – we are disconnected, even within Our own immediate families Love, compassion, kindness, and inclusion are learned qualities Just as is hate, envy, prejudice and exclusion The most powerful teacher for a young mind is a wounded healer Parents heal yourselves that you might be a positive influence On your own children, on any child Your greatest gift to your own family, to humanity Is to become healthy in body, mind, and spirit – it is possible Add a contemplative practice to your daily life A few minutes of silence, stillness, and Presence, your image of God Breathe in, breathe out a short mantra, or focus on your breath Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your true self, it has your answers, it patiently waits

George G
