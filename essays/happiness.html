HAPPINESS

By George Gondron

HAPPINESS Like love, it is easier to say what happiness is not Than what happiness is For true happiness does not come by intent But by accident, unplanned Trapped in the immediate moment, fleetingly Happiness searched for will never be found It is not a 'thing' that can be purchased Not a course to be taken, not a topic to be learned Planning ahead, you will not find it But wait, this new 'object' makes me happy! That's really called gratification, not happiness Ah, but people, they make me happy What about when they don't make you happy? Don't meet your expectations Fall short of the mark, and leave you empty Are you happy then? The 'thing' we call happiness If in its absence, its loss, we feel sad It was not happiness, it was an attachment True happiness is spontaneous, unexpected The phone call, the card in the mail Watching children playing in the park Feeling the gift of tears as we hear a song A piece of music that brings back a memory Watching a beautiful dancer It is what touches the heart and soul, unexpectedly Connected to an inner yearning If we look for a cause to be happy When the cause is gone, what remains? Always remember, never forget True happiness is always uncaused, always

George G
