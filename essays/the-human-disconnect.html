THE HUMAN DISCONNECT

By George Gondron

THE HUMAN DISCONNECT Without a doubt, we humans are the most complicated of all species Our character includes consciousness of both an intellectual and emotional nature, often at odds with each other, disconnected The wholeness of human personality is inclusive, leaving nothing out Our ability to reason, to critically think, along with our emotional wellbeing, to have empathy and compassion for all life These together make us fully alive and fully human However, currently in our culture, we seem to be motivated more by emotional content than critical reasoning, more by fear than love Our minds, demonstrably, are much more closed than open More judgmental than understanding, more defensive than embracing We have forgotten the importance of active listening, of learning We have forgotten that our wholeness is much more important than our parts, if the only tool we use in our amazing toolbox is a hammer, we consequently will treat everything as a nail We are hard pressed to understand and comprehend what we think, feel, and do or do not do is a matter of personal choice As the cartoon character Pogo said, ‘we are our own worst enemy’ Almost all social media, in one form or another, is about blame, finger pointing, and defensiveness, all of which create division Politics has become the ‘great disrupter’, polarizing at its best and dangerous at its worst, where even the winners lose Currently, the citizens of our nation have ceded their power to a handful of extremely wealthy and egotistical individuals And for them to succeed we only need to follow like sheep, relinquishing our power and freedom to determine or future Not to mention the future of our children and grandchildren As individuals we need to be aware that our wholeness is much more important than our separate parts What we think, what we feel, what we believe as a result of personal experience is much more important than hyperbole and gaslighting Please, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, seek wholeness Connect inner and outer, bring balance into your life Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isnes Focus on your breath, breathe in, breathe out, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They have your answers, and they patiently await

George G
