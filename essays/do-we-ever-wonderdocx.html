DO WE EVER WONDER

By George Gondron

DO WE EVER WONDER About the one in four children that go to bed hungry each night Not in the world, but here in the United States Or that poor nutrition is the cause of 50% of the deaths Of children less than five years old all over the world Do we ever wonder, how is it that we have The most expensive health care system in the world Yet, we are ranked the worst among the eleven wealthiest nations In terms of efficiency, equity, and outcomes Do we question the fact that medical malpractice Is the third leading cause of death, behind heart disease and cancer Or that almost 20,000 deaths happen as a result of prescribed drugs How about the homeless, just in our country Numbering in the hundreds of thousands, almost a million Ten percent of which are veterans who fought for this nation One in five families live in inadequate housing Do we ever wonder about the fact that 25% of all children Live in poverty, here in this country, I doubt that we do Do we wonder about the thousands of college graduates Burdened with the debt of student loans Unable to find adequate employment Do we question why thousands of young people Are imprisoned because of a natural plant A plant that has caused no known deaths While 'big Pharma' is free to sell its death in a pill And the distillers sell theirs in a bottle I could go on about what seems so upside down But most of us don't really care At least enough to question, to challenge, to doubt To reflect and use our minds for a little critical thinking As long as we're not affected, and our lifestyle is safe We can turn a blind eye, and a deaf ear And like the ostrich, bury our heads in the sand And pretend 'those people' are not our problem Their issues are not our issues, we are just fine And we are not the keeper of 'our brothers and sisters' Perhaps we should wonder much more than we do Perhaps we should do more than just wonder, perhaps

George G
