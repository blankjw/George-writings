THE PASSION

By George Gondron | Source: THE PASSION.docx

THE PASSION Two words, a statement, an image Known the world over, regardless of belief The image that's called forth, that dwells in the mind Crucifixion; an inhuman torturous death Evidence of man's inhumanity to man No more, no less Pain beyond belief to the physical body Incomprehensible emotional turmoil to the mind Human, bleeding, broken, ravaged, nakedness And we are told it was to save humankind from 'sin' To save our souls An 'atonement' to the One we call God But I am left to wonder, question, doubt First about this word 'sin' I think we complicate and use words to control To control others with this word 'sin' When in fact, 'sin' is very simple to explain It is the refusal to accept the love of Love Itself That's all - not thousands of little acts of misdeed And there's God's atonement requirement That one's really over the 'edge', too far beyond belief Beyond belief for a God of love, as proclaimed by His son Why would a loving God send His only son to die Intentionally; how can that God be trusted For you see, if as they say I accept Jesus as my savior, accept He died for my 'sins' I'm off the hook, I'm 'saved' I don't need to have a personal relationship with God Love has one requirement; relationship, connection Love, to be love, is reciprocal, is interactive What killed Jesus was power, control, and fear The unwillingness to see and hear What He said and did That's what the Passion was about, and still is Theology is one person's faith experience Mine tells me, God is Love God asks that I reciprocate, with Her/Him, with others, with all

George G
