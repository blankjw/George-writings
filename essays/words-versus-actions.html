WORDS VERSUS ACTIONS

By George Gondron

WORDS VERSUS ACTIONS Conundrum, which came first, the chicken or the egg? The same paradox happens when using words For words are not actions What we hear, what we read Creates images in the mind And those images may be very inaccurate Our reality is created through interaction We first experienced some of life's Most important discoveries Before we ever understood words at all Kindness, gentleness, compassion, and love Learned through actions, not words As well, pain, abuse, neglect, and fear And as we interact with others We learn to trust, not through words But rather, through the living experience Of the other or others in their action or nonaction Life's meaning is very simple, it is relationship Relationship with all that exists But in particular with other human beings Believing and knowing are two very different Dynamics We can profess we believe something Without knowing it But if we know something through experience Believing is secondary, and is a non-question Knowing is always experiential Often, we build a false trust only through words Words that later don't support actions The pain that follows is a result of thinking We've been living with an illusion that was false And the longer the illusion, the deeper the pain Love that is an illusion is often the most painful There is a saying, trust but verify The verification is always in the actions The words, ‘I love you’ Carry truth in action
