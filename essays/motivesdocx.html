MOTIVES

By George Gondron

MOTIVES Motives, the reasons we do what we do The underlying causes, the incentives, and the purposes On a continuum from selfish to selfless More often than not, they are selfish But hardly ever acknowledged as such Is it possible to be totally selfless? To be unattached to what and for whom we do what we do It is very doubtful, if not impossible There seems to be an attachment to every action Even if it's nothing more than a good feeling Or, perhaps an expectation of gratitude, a thank you Yes, motives can be very revealing if we take the time Take the time to examine the underlying impulse or urge When the need to feel good about what I do Overcomes the need to be helpful to another Then the focus is on 'my' good deed And not the reason for helping To be honest about one's motivations Is the path of becoming less and less selfish When something is done out of kindness, out of compassion The most common first thought is, 'why did they do this?' Or, 'I must somehow repay them' Kindness and compassion are their own rewards Like a review of conscience at the end of the day Perhaps a review of our motives for the deeds that were done Would also be appropriate for the clarity of our actions We might be surprised and even alarmed If we include our hearts in the discerning process And not just our minds And maybe that's the key for our best possible actions To be fully human, for the best possible motives Is to include not only our minds But our heart, our soul, and our spirit as well To use all our gifts and not just the one Is to provide more selfless and less selfish motives Something this world needs more and more Something we each need - more than ever

George G
