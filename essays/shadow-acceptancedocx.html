SHADOW ACCEPTANCE

By George Gondron

SHADOW ACCEPTANCE Everything casts a shadow It is the shadow we carry within that becomes our problem There is a saying that what we cannot first accept About ourselves, we can never change Our shadow is not our enemy It dwells in our unconscious revealing itself in our dreams Only to teach us what we refuse to see in the light of day That which aids us in becoming more fully human It’s the shadow we run from in our dreams Attempting to show us what in our behavior needs to change Needs to be brought into consciousness, into clarity In a true sense, it is our defense attorney, not our accuser It’s easy to embrace the parts of ourselves That demonstrates kindness and compassion It’s not so easy to accept the judgmental and biased parts We are not halves, we are wholes, inclusive in our being Wholeness can never happen if we deny or segregate The parts of ourselves that we refuse to accept Pointing fingers at others is almost always a projection A projection of what we are unwilling to see in ourselves Alas, the ‘bad guy’ is always out there, not within It is not ourselves that needs to change It is always he, she, or them Imagine the potential of humanity if all individuals Accepted ownership of their own shadow Their own dysfunctions Our dreams are one tool to aid us in becoming more conscious Another is to choose a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day set aside for the nurturance Of our inner core, our heart, soul, and spirit A time to empty our ever-busy ‘monkey minds’ Of their incessant and often repetitive thoughts Thoughts that keep us stuck in the past or fearful for the future Missing the only moment there is, the now Personal well-being leads to unconditional, love for others Awakening the understanding we are all connected Listen to the whispers within, they patiently wait

George G
