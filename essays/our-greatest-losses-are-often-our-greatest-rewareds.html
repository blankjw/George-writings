OUR GREATEST LOSSES ARE OFTEN OUR GREATEST REWAREDS

By George Gondron

OUR GREATEST LOSSES ARE OFTEN OUR GREATEST REWARDS Those losses which deflate our overinflated egos That brings us an awareness of our own dysfunction That brings us to the ‘end of our rope’ If willingly dealt with, not denied, and embraced Will in the end become our greatest rewards There is no gain without pain, no success without failure But in reality, in this culture, with all its distractions People are able to run until they reach death’s doorstep And never experience the rewards gained from change Personal change from living in the ‘false self’ And discovering the ‘true self’ For deep personal change reveals who we truly are A human being fully alive Connected with an innate knowing given at birth Mind, heart, soul, and spirit all working together Not being stuck in a mindset of certainty, a dualistic mind Unwilling to compromise and cooperate with Their own inner strengths which would free them Of their pain and misery Emotional pain, unlike physical pain, never really heals Unless dealt with in a meaningful and honest manner Self-help groups, twelve-step programs Are all about dealing with one’s own emotional pain But for far too many they become nothing more than Social outlets, as the old saying goes, ‘misery loves company’ Wherever we think we are on a scale of emotional wellness A daily meditative/contemplative practice of just a few minutes Is always a benefit, always a period of grounding Time to empty our ever-busy ‘monkey minds’ Of their incessant and repetitive thoughts The phrase ‘peace of mind’ is really an oxymoron For when you’re in your mind, you’re never at peace It’s when you ‘shut the mind down’ you can find peace Be silent, be still, be in the presence of Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They wait patiently, they bring truth, they heal

George G
