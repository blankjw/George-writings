TO BELIEVE, TO KNOW, TO TRUST

By George Gondron

TO BELIEVE, TO KNOW, TO TRUST To believe can be to know, maybe But most believing is not knowing at all It's merely second hand information Something I may have been told or read in written symbols And the most destructive believing, 'third party' information That's the success of propaganda, advertising, marketing Hoping to convince us what they're 'selling' is valuable And often what they're selling is not tangible, it's ideas Such as discrimination, prejudice, bias, fear, and hatred It may be believable, but seldom is it valuable How many have fought and died over and for beliefs Beliefs not known, nor experienced, only taught Then there's knowing, how do we know anything? We know one way, and one way only Through personal, often hard learned experience Fire is hot, ice is cold, water is wet - how do we know? With our sense of touch, in the experience The sky is blue, the grass green, known through our seeing A whisper, a scream, a shout, a cry, known through our hearing Our ability to taste and smell, ways of knowing The most difficult knowing is knowing another As the saying goes, 'you never really know someone until you've lived with them', and even then we know so little Of all creatures, humans are the most challenging to know For we are only known as we know ourselves, no other way But, we also are the most rewarding to know Lastly, there is trust Which mostly falls between believing and knowing If we never trust until we fully know We become stagnant and lifeless We miss the 'agony and the ecstasy', the juices of life Trust, like faith, gives us courage to risk To be vulnerable and exposed To move away from the familiar and seek the unknown To trust in an unseen Creator For all that we see which is also created Have your beliefs, but always trust what you know

George G
