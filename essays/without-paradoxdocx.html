WITHOUT PARADOX

By George Gondron

WITHOUT PARADOX Life without paradox does not exist When we cannot see the paradoxes within ourselves All our problems are usually the result of something or someone Other than us, you and me If we have the courage to look deep within ourselves We will discover our contradictory nature We will begin to recognize our inner paradoxes For in truth all our real and imagined problems Teeter on the brink of our own thoughts, feelings, and actions On our ability to own our choices without projecting them elsewhere To own what we do or did, while at the same time Being able to forgive ourselves if needed, a paradox For most of what we do that has no merit or that is selfish We also know is discounting to another or others, a paradox Life is a both/and existence, not an either/or journey Fear discriminates, love never does, love unites Love is probably the greatest paradox For we are not called to love when, or if, or maybe, or only We are called to love always, without exception Most of what we do or do not do to avoid emotional pain Will, in the end, be painful, a paradox There is no life without death, not now without then Not here without there, no good without bad, paradoxes We are all living paradoxes Our successes are a manifestation of our failures Our possibilities are fueled by our imagined potential And our gifts to be kind, compassionate, caring, and sharing Or also our yearnings for the same, a paradox Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice Discover your own inner paradoxes, your contradictory nature Perhaps our greatest task is to fully know ourselves Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty you mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing, focus on your breath Breathe in, breathe out – the mind will wander, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
