SELF-ACCEPTANCE

By George Gondron

SELF-ACCEPTANCE It is only through grace that we can embrace our fragility Our total human nature, the dark and the light The yearning and the resistance The honesty and the deception We are always both/and, never either/or What we don’t recognize we cannot accept The path of self-discovery is not straight and narrow It is filled with highs and lows, twists and turns Experiences that open and expand awareness to the reality That we are all more alike than unalike The paradox – we come together in our sameness We grow together in our uniqueness Gifts to one another Every part of us is necessary for our wholeness Nothing is to be rejected, only transformed And it is in the clarity of that perception That we begin to judge less and love more Not only ourselves but all others as well, no exceptions The saying, ‘To err is human, to forgive is divine’ Begins with ourselves first For what we cannot forgive in our human weakness Shackles us in our journey toward wholeness Acceptance of what is, is the first step toward all change For what we cannot accept, we also can never change The greater mystery is that of unconditional love A mystery that clearly supports the fact that love is not a feeling Love is an action, that once experienced is transformative Forever Part of our human journey toward becoming fully alive Is the coming together of mind, heart, soul, and spirit A necessary and required coalescing of all our parts Awakening our inner resources, our innate internal core Engage a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes of silence, with stillness, in the presence of Presence Each and every day, empty your mind of needless thoughts Listen to the quiet whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your true self, your sacredness, they wait

George G
