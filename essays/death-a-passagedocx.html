DEATH - A PASSAGE

By George Gondron

DEATH The great equalizer, death No escapees, no immortals In youth, it's nameless, faceless Too distant to even imagine We do our best to insulate the death experience Antiseptic funerals, preparation by hired strangers No hands on participation We don't ready the deceased We don't dig the grave We don't shovel dirt on the coffin The loss is more difficult, without these experiences The closure lingers , the pain persists As our years pass, death becomes more real The years quicken, time seems to move faster Yet, for most, death is a denied reality Regardless of age, regardless of infirmity Few recognize and have processed it's only a change A transition of form to form Not an ending, a new beginning I read once, 'the kingdom of God is within you', The kingdom of God is eternal' Within and eternal, how fortunate Life, the gift that keeps on giving, only changed "Death, where is thy sting?" It is in the mind only, it has no sting Birth, leads us to death, moment by moment Treasure each moment, savor the now Live the mystery, the problem is already solved

George G
