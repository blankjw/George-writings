THE PROCESS OF UNDERSTANDING

By George Gondron

THE PROCESS OF UNDERSTANDING Understanding, especially that of ourselves Is impacted by our life’s experiences since our birth Shame, blame, the absence of affection All leave deep impressions on our psyche We learn early on the impact of dualism or polarization As they relate to our thinking, feeling, and our actions The ‘how-to’s’ of our life are seldom matters of inclusion But more about exclusion, either/or choices We paint reality with stark contrasts learned from Our families, our culture, and especially our religion All three teach us who belongs, and who does not The negative effects of black/white thinking Are too endless to begin to list, and they are damaging There is a famous line from the book The Little Prince It is as follows – ‘And now here is my secret, a very simple secret. It is only with the heart that one can see rightly, what is essential is invisible to the eye.’ Those moments in our lives when our head is telling us one Thing, and our heart another, are awakening moments When our inner discerning voices remind us Life is never either/or, it is always both/and It is inclusive, reflective, discerning, and soul-searching Every moment of anxiety, uneasiness, apprehension And emotional discomfort is a lesson to be learned Either about ourselves or about our situation All of us have been, are now, or will be in relationships When we will experience discomfort, it’s called being human And here is an absolute truth The only person we can change is ourselves, not the other If you can learn that truth, half the battle is won A choice to engage in a meditative/contemplative practice Will bring new understanding and needed balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, embrace Presence, your image of Universal Consciousness, of God Focus on your breathing, empty your mind Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
