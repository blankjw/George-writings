DARK MONEY

By George Gondron

DARK MONEY In the pursuit of capital, entrepreneurship, ownership If the consumer is not an integral factor as a human being But rather only as a purchaser, a nonentity Then what's most important? Regardless of the rhetoric, it's 'dark money' Profit, the 'bottom line', return on investment People become 'things' to be convinced of their need Their need to purchase something that puts 'dark money' Into the pockets of others To spend what has been hard earned on what is needless As if what they're convinced to buy will benefit their lives And seldom, very seldom does it ever do so When you no longer see people as subjects When they have become only objects to benefit a desire Then there is no concern about how they spend their money The only concern is that the financial projections are met The goal is reached, the stockholders are satisfied A world in which what is of value is not human relationship But a relationship of how can we make more money We being those that in the end have all the power and control Perhaps the saying, 'money is the root of all evil', is true Or, 'absolute power corrupts absolutely', maybe When money controls government what is the result? It's no longer 'of the people, for the people, and by the people' It's a sham of a democracy, now an oligarchy People are no longer represented But wait, corporations, according to the Supreme Court Are now people, which is rather confusing Confusing when you consider that only people have a conscience Not corporations, and that has become very obvious Without a conscience there is no morality, no ethics Only those ethics of power that create more 'dark money' When the majority of the wealth is in the hands of the few That means the power and control are also in the hands of the few The majority are no longer represented and will never be Let us hope the wealthy have a change of heart, let's hope If not, where does it end?

George G
