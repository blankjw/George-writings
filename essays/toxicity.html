TOXICITY

By George Gondron

TOXICITY Seldom, if ever, do we consider the toxicity of the mind Unlike the toxic pollution of our air and water But it is certainly there, very present and thriving As the power of ignorance has become more prevalent Than our need for further and continuing education Education that is reliable and trustworthy Not contaminated with ‘alternative facts’ and half-truths So much easier to simply take what’s offered, whatever the source And believe accordingly without question or doubt Regardless of its toxic nature, and the poisoning of our minds We, it seems, are more attracted to a fear-based narrative Than one of love, kindness, and compassion The explosion of social media affords us easy access to information And it seems we choose what stimulates our emotions Our brain's limbic system versus our cerebrum or thinking brain Consequently, our feelings lead more to a reactive behavior While our thinking leads more to a responsive behavior There is a significant difference between the two As it relates to mental health and wellbeing Or mental health versus mental toxicity Like the air and water we pollute with chemicals and emissions We have the capability to pollute our minds With false and dangerous information or propaganda We have lost it seems, our capability to be discerning individuals Relying on emotional stimulus for our truth Relying on third-party information and gossip for our beliefs The Buddha had a saying, a glass of muddy water Allowed to remain still, will eventually be clear As well, the mind with clarity of perception Will be responsive and not reactive For clarity and balance choose a meditative/contemplative practice Daily spend just a few minutes in silence, be still, be present To your image of a creative Source, or God if you will Focus on your breath, in and out, empty your mind Let go of incessant and repetitive thoughts that go nowhere Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, they have your answers, they wait

George G
