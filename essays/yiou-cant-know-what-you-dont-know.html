YIOU CAN'T KNOW WHAT YOU DON'T KNOW

By George Gondron

YOU CAN’T KNOW WHAT YOU DON’T KNOW A simple enough observation Not knowing what you don’t know The real issue is knowing and nothing changes In every aspect of our lives Our actions, our interactions, our thinking and feeling Our context, our beliefs, and our status quo We, it seems, prefer the familiar to the strange Even if the familiar is painful and uncomfortable This behavior follows a cognitive pattern of ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ The majority of us prefer to follow rather than lead The real problem is not only what or who we follow But more importantly, as a follower, we don’t need to Question, or doubt, or use critical thinking at all We simply accept what is as it is Even in the face of evidence and facts to the contrary Ignorance is the refusal to learn Stupidity is doing the same thing over and over and To one’s own detriment, nothing changes The art of knowing is de facto experiential But not just through action, but through being as well We are very good at doing, not so good at being Those in the East accuse we in the West of Being dead from the neck down Our existence is shackled to our incessant repetitive thoughts Identifying with our egos, our minds, our intellect Completely out of touch with our ‘beingness’ With the knowing of our own heart, soul, and spirit Completely unaware of the significant ‘ah ha’ moments That are beyond thinking we fail to experience daily As a result of not being present to the ‘now’ Dwelling on the past or fearing the future Here’s a challenge – choose a meditative/contemplative practice Each day, for just a few minutes, embrace silence, with stillness, In the Presence of the One, you may call God, no words You will learn to be, to hear the whispers of your inner truth You will discover your true self, you will know Truth

George G
