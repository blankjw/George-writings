POVERTY

By George Gondron

POVERTY To live on a planet that provides more than enough More than enough for every human being Including not just the basic needs for survival But education, medical care, and future promise How is it there's so much poverty? How is it the only ones asking that question, are the poor? And the ones not asking the question don't seem to care There's more than one answer One is greed, one is power And probably the most dangerous, is disinterest They are all great 'pretenders' Confusing fact with fiction Pretending that the poor are poor through their own fault Greed is driven by fear, fear that I'll never have enough At the expense of those that have so little Power is its own adjudicator Making its own rules that maintain its total control And disinterest is the most harmful The ability to turn a blind eye, a deaf ear, to the cries of the poor But the great 'poverty' of greed, power, and disinterest Is poverty of conscience, lack of empathy Islands unto ourselves, adrift in our own selfishness All as a result of living a programmed, external life Dictated by the culture in which we live Which is determinative of what's important And what's ignored Living lives of pretense at the expense of the impoverished If all our 'reference points', of what's important Are always external, and exclude those not important to us Poverty will remain, it will continue, there's no other option Until we see ourselves in the least of the least Until we have a change of mind through awareness of our hearts Nothing changes, it's business as usual We are all 'poor' in one way or another But to be 'poor' of conscience is the worst of poverty The mind refuses to listen to what the heart already knows The heart always carries the truth, we need listen to our hearts

George G
