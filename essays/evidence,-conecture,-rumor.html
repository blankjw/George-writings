EVIDENCE, CONJECTURE, RUMOR

By George Gondron | Source: EVIDENCE, CONJECTURE, RUMOR.docx

EVIDENCE, CONJECTURE, RUMOR All possibilities for believing In addition, we can add, alternative facts, half-truths, Third-party information, scientific facts, demonstrable truth Sadly, it seems that currently in our society most follow All the above except scientific facts, actual evidence, and Demonstrable truth We are much more likely to believe that which stirs our emotions We put aside critical thinking, research, and hands-on experience As a result, we are nothing more than blind followers Gravitating toward the ‘pied piper’, the ‘snake oil salesman’ Of our emotional choice We’re easy to recognize, just ask us what evidence we have To support our beliefs, the keyword is evidence That’s the ‘toe-stumper’, for our evidence is not verifiable at all Like the direct evidence we experience through our five senses Fire burns, water is wet, ice is cold, the sky is blue The risk of course of following anything or anyone That doesn’t provide evidentiary facts, the real truth Is to risk being manipulated, controlled, and bamboozled We become pawns in someone else’s puppet show We become fools in someone else’s narrative All the aforementioned is possible because we believe We may not be right, but we are never wrong We live in a culture of ‘salesman’ Selling us things, ideas, and BS that give them Control and money in their pockets And then there’s the additional fact, we resist change Change is for others, not for me Well, here’s a truth, the only thing we can change is ourselves And if we don’t, we are complicit, not innocent bystanders We don’t take the time to be reflective, to be self-critical Incorporate a meditative/contemplative practice in your life A few minutes each day be silent, be still, be present to Isness Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Dwelling the past or the future and missing the now Breathe in wellness to yourself, breathe out wellness to the universe Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
