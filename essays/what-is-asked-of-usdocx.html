WHAT IS ASKED OF US

By George Gondron

WHAT IS ASKED OF US There is no rule that says we must believe in a creative Source A divine entity some may call God or any other special name We already possess inside each of us An innate knowing that speaks to kindness, compassion, and love It is in our nature to care, a part of our DNA As the mother cares for her newborn At what point does, and what is the juncture Where we lose touch with this innate knowing? It is when there is no longer a connection Between the heart, soul, and spirit and the mind, the ego The ego becomes the ‘Captain of the ship’ And the ego is hard-pressed to ignore the yearnings Of the heart, soul, and spirit Unwilling to allow other aspects of our psyche steer the ship We lose touch with what is asked of us Because that asking requires sacrifice Requires cooperation versus competition Requires inclusion as opposed to exclusion Asks that the starting point be one of love. Not fear We recognize our sameness first and welcome our diversity A community prospers when all its members thrive Whole or handicapped, young or old, all matter Each person is important for who they are Not for what they may possess Discrimination, judgment, bias, and prejudice have no value There is no limit to human potential in a favorable environment Where the norm is love, not fear We all have the power to be change agents, with no exceptions What we do influences others, not what we say All change necessarily includes a connection to our inner core Something more than just ego desire Choose a meditative/contemplative daily practice For just a few minutes each day, be silent, be still, be in the Presence Empty the mind that you might listen to the whispers Of your own heart, soul, and spirit Discover your true self as opposed to the egoic self All truth abides within you, listen carefully

George G
