HOUSTON WE HAVE A PROBLEM

By George Gondron

HOUSTON WE HAVE A PROBLEM Yes, we have our political differences and divides We have economic inequality and injustice Religious biases and infighting The issues great and small are too numerous to count But the real problem lies much deeper It's not one that can be fixed by legislation Remedied by changing the rules, whatever they may be Our nation hangs in the balance The balance between freedom and authoritarianism Of materialism and human relationship Of polarity and equanimity We have traded our sense of compassion and kindness Of equality, freedom, and justice Of neighborly care and concern, of community For smoke and mirrors and empty promises We have lost our soul and replaced it with heady religion Whether that religion is represented by bricks and mortar Or a religion of wealth, power, and greed Worship it seems, is not just about an unseen God It is worship derived from the ego's desires and attachments Including people, places, thoughts, and feelings It is worship devoid of relationship, held at arm's length Too many struggle to survive for the day, quietly We have lost our connection to mystery in a digital age Our focus is on ourselves, our small group What connections we seem to hold beyond the immediate Are mostly superficial, 'hey, how are you', but we don't care We definitely do not connect to humanity as a whole We are motivated negatively through emotional agitation Agitation based on fear, insecurity, and attachment Everything is about the 'externals' Nothing about an internal journey or reference point No notice of the whispers of the heart, soul, and spirit We are grounded in the tangible, the material And we ignore our true essence, our own divinity The kernel of our existence given in love Return to your core, to your original essence, to Love

George G
