DOCTA IGNORANTIA

By George Gondron

DOCTA IGNORANTIA A Latin term translated as 'learned ignorance' What we take in without question, believing it to be true Often times speaks to our own ignorance The reasons we do this are many and varied Perhaps we want to trust it's true Perhaps we're simply gullible Often it's because 'so and so said' And for many of us it's just simply laziness, not checking the facts And who suffers from our 'docta ignorantia'? Firstly, we do and secondly all others as well An unknowledgeable public becomes their own worst enemy To not be a part of the solution Is to remain a part of the problem There is no such thing as an 'innocent bystander' Just as there's no defense to, 'well it's not my fault' Choosing to be uninformed is just another way of saying Yes, it is my fault as well The 'dumbing down' of American is quiet obvious And it's easy to spot those who haven't taken the time Taken the time to become informed They are the ones that shout the longest and loudest When the disappointments arrive, and they will arrive Never trust someone who gives the appearance Of being the solution to all our problems Our work in this life to become authentic human beings To be a responsible participant in this human experience It is an individual task, no one can do it for us Not our family, our church, our nation, just us It is personal, 'to thine own self be true' A choice to remain uneducated, unknowledgeable, uninformed Is a personal choice, no one is holding a gun to our heads Don't be a 'blamer' or a 'whiner' or a 'finger pointer' If we do nothing more than experience something different Experience a little silence, stillness, with an empty mind Each day for a few minutes in the presence of the Creator That is enough to transform us, change us Silence speaks, silence educates, silence heals

George G
