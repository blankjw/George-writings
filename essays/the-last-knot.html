THE LAST KNOT

By George Gondron

THE LAST KNOT There are many threads to our lives They intertwine, separate, come together again And interspersed along each one Are the knots we cling to when the passage gets rough When the winds of change blow us about The choppy seas toss us to and fro We cling to those knots, they are our support The ones we hold fast with the greatest desperation Are those that represent our human relationships Some we will let go of Some we hold on to more than others And then there are those that are the last knot in the thread To let go of those is just too painful For in so doing, we are letting go of our very self We hold on not because of attachment, of the familiar But because of the meaning in our lives, our existence The pain of letting go Is much greater than the hope of holding on It is a paradox, an enigma, a mystery A relationship grounded in the deepest of love Cannot be released as if it never existed This bond is one that can never be broken Be careful, don't fool yourself, these bonds are few They go beyond the meaning of what most call love The mother for her child, a teacher for a special student Spouses for one another, siblings for each other Grandparents for grandchildren And some, friend to friend We will not have many of these in our lives And even though there may be unchosen separation There will always be that bond of love And then there is the bond of the Creator for the created One that exceeds mere beyond human understanding To understand that God cannot not love There have been, are now, and will be Those that do their best to love others in that way We may not succeed, but if we don't try, we never will

George G
