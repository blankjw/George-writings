WHAT WOULD IT TAKE TO CHANGE YOUR MIND

By George Gondron

WHAT WOULD IT TAKE TO CHANGE YOUR MIND ? If we’re honest, there’s at least one thing in each of us, That upon reflection, we’d like to change, probably more But we don’t, and most of us never will, we cling to the familiar Even when the familiar is uncomfortable or even painful For the decision to change requires risk and vulnerability And the resistance, although unspoken and maybe unrecognized Is tied to fear, to the unknown, we want some level of assurance That if we do risk, the outcome will be better than our now Sorry, there are no guarantees, but there is one certainty Without change nothing gets better, no risk, no reward We much prefer a simple answer to a difficult situation, always To grow as a healthy human being, to become more aware And conscious, to discover our own potential and creativity Is not easy, but it beats the alternative, to remain ‘stuck’ To live our lives with a closed mind, to defend and deny Is not to live at all, it is only an existence, very ‘black & white’ Rigidity takes the place of spontaneity and flexibility Today is like yesterday and tomorrow will be the same I offer you a challenge, look in the mirror and ask How am I different today, in all areas of my life than I was One year, five years, or ten years ago Am I more educated, more open, more curious, more inclusive, More kind, compassionate, understanding, less prejudiced and bias Or has there been little or no significant change at all, I hope not One of the signs of a person who embraces freedom is the Willingness to ‘let-go’, to move forward, to choose change A person who is not polarized in their thinking, life is both/and They are not ‘set’ in their ways, regardless of age, they are curious They see life as a journey to be explored, not a puzzle to be solved They are open to Mystery, not clinging to certainty They are few, which are you?, the seeker or the denier? Enhance your life, choose a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra The ego will resist, the mind will wander, go back to the breath Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they are your guides, they wait

George G
