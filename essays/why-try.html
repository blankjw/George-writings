WHY TRY

By George Gondron

WHY TRY Two of the most pernicious words in the English language Why and try To begin a question with 'why' Is like throwing gas on a fire The party queried is immediately placed in a defensive position Being defensive, reasonable communication stops It all goes downhill from there As a matter of fact a 'why' question better stated Is a 'how' question, how did this occur or happen Not, why did it happen We can hear a 'how' question much easier than a 'why' Asking another how something happened Gives them the opportunity to explain Rather than defend Interestingly enough, a why also indicates blame Blamed for something that's easily answered When approached with a how question How doesn't blame, how inquires Why doesn't inquire, why blames And now to our other three letter word, try What the unconscious hears when we use the word try Is that we can fail, and if the 'try' Is something that leads to change, especially inner change Then we definitely will fail, it's a setup If we simply say, in the face of something new I'll do the best I can, there is no failure If the results are not to my satisfaction I merely do it differently or do it again No failure Do, or do not, excludes the word try Success is not measured in the number of times we try But, in the number of times we act, we do, we risk Again, the ego prefers stasis, the familiar And uses the try as a support, as a defense for failure Oh well, I tried, but I failed - the easy way out Replace why and try, with how and do The rewards are much greater, life is much fuller

George G
