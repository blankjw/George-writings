FREEDOM TO LOVE

By George Gondron | Source: FREEDOM TO LOVE.docx

FREEDOM TO LOVE Love only happens in an environment of freedom Where there are no demands, no expectations, no obligations When love is a gift, freely given with no strings attached That’s why it’s so difficult to understand unconditional love The question why do you love me?, is a trap Regardless of the answer, when those conditions are not present The implication is that I don’t love you The sacrifices we make, small or large, willingly Are an indication of the love we hold for others in freedom If you’ve ever experienced unconditional love Then you know what I mean That kind of love makes no demands, has no expectations Then again, maybe one, that we love ourselves Our demons and our angels, our light and our darkness Those who believe in a creative Source, God if you prefer The religion is not relevant Tend to believe in a judgmental God, a punishing God Especially who punishes others, the ones I don’t like The ones that don’t think the way I do, act the way I do News flash, a God that loves unconditionally, loves all No exceptions Everything about this universe is evidence of that love It is only the human mind, the ego, that discriminates The only valid, the only real knowing of God Is, not intellectual or book-knowing, it is experiential It is a relationship, which is the best definition of life It is a heart, soul, and spirit knowing A knowing experienced through the senses God cannot be proven or known intellectually As the great mystic Meister Eckhart said, ‘God please rid me of God’ – meaning his limited understanding Our intellect above all things limits our experience Of Divine reality, of God, of Isness, of Universal Consciousness Add to your life a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day of silence, in stillness, with Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, they have your answers, they wait

George G
