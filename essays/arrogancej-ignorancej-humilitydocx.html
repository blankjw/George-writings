ARROGANCE^J IGNORANCE^J HUMILITY

By George Gondron

ARROGANCE, IGNORANCE, HUMILITY Is there a correlation between these three Ignorance is simply lack of knowledge, education, or information Arrogance and humility on the other hand are states of mind The arrogant person suffers from a lack of understanding The humble person seeks to understand more The former has a closed mind, the latter an open mind The arrogant person is defensive in their conversations They really don’t want new information; their minds are made up The humble person on the other hand is eager to learn Open to new learning, able to acknowledge their lack of information One operates from a mindset of fear and insecurity The other from a consciousness of love and hope The arrogant personality is alive and well in every arena They limit their input to what already forms their opinions And if truth or proven facts are counter to their beliefs, so be it It’s a waste of time and energy to convince them otherwise The humble person avoids, if possible, conflict While at the same time eager to learn more from a kind teacher From a teacher that encourages their curiosity Obviously, our nation, our world, is better off with open minds Minds willing to learn, understand, and challenge their own beliefs A mind that knows, ‘There but for the grace of God, go I’ Where love dwells there is no arrogance, only humility A mind willing to accept what is, and a heart willing to make it better The foundation for everything positive is love and connection The foundation for everything negative is fear and alienation The state of our union is obvious No one is perfect, but there are clear signs of arrogance and fear There are clear signs of a lack of humility, of selfishness Of those whose goals are self-serving versus serving others We have choices, vote, be open-minded, become humble Seek knowledge, learn, educate yourself, understand Change is not easy but it’s always possible, choose balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your ‘monkey mind’, focus on your breath Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
