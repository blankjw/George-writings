THE PRISON WE CREATE

By George Gondron

THE PRISON WE CREATE Four walls, a locked door, no windows, a single naked bulb Our self-constructed prison of the ego, the mind Living with a deception of freedom, dragging our 'ball and chain' Convinced that who we are is what we do, it is our 'persona' As life lives us, rather than we choosing life We all get caught in the flow, there are no exceptions Conditioned, programmed, indoctrinated - the words don't matter But, the outcome certainly always matters Awake while asleep, living unconscious lives Automatons of repeated behavior, day after day We measure our worth defined by cultural criterion We must 'fit the mold', follow the 'norm', don't 'rattle the cage' Just a number, one of the nameless faces, kept safe in our prison Our lives have become an amalgam of external activity Of repeated thoughts and repressed emotions Who is that person I glimpse in the mirror each morning? I, we, have become strangers to ourselves But it's no bother, we're safe in our prison Echoes bounce off the walls of my mind Echoes of the unanswered questions that push at the door The door of my cell, the one to which I hold the key Doubts, gently tugging at the edges of my awareness The barely audible whispers in my few quiet moments And I have heard it said that a problem created by the mind Cannot be solved by the same mind that creates it Perhaps that why we don't change, the mind refuses How do we free ourselves from this prison, it won't be by choice Until the pain we feel is stronger than the ego's distractions Until what we call love is known unconditionally Until we risk the silence, the stillness, the Presence No thing, nothing will change, only more of the same It seems the path to change, to individuation, to transformation Is more often down the road of suffering, mental anguish Deep anxiety, ethical, moral, and spiritual dilemmas Those are the keys to our locked prison door, the door of our mind They lead to freedom, compassion, connection and relationship They lead to life and to Love - use the keys, unlock the prison

George G
