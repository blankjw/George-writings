LITERACY

By George Gondron | Source: LITERACY.docx

LITERACY Simple definition, the ability to read and write However, literacy also includes a level of understanding, Comprehension, knowledge, realization, perception, Discernment, savvy, judgment, etc., etc., etc. Also, the level of literacy must not be overlooked In our culture alone 20% of adults cannot read or write In addition, 54% of adults have a literacy level Of sixth grade or lower, that is frightening Education does matter, it matters greatly But simply attending an education facility Receiving a degree and ceasing a path of formal education Does not equate to a comprehensive level of literacy Learning and memorization are not the same The mind is capable of memorizing without learning Without applying what has been memorized So, if you want to influence adults with low literacy levels The best path is through the emotions, not the intellect The paradox – adults with low literacy levels are easily Influenced by their emotions The great and powerful leaders throughout history Controlled the masses through their emotions Not through their intellect Currently, almost all adult learning is through social media And guess what social media does to capture their audience They stir the emotions, mostly through the use of fear We don’t have the power that social media has We can’t affect the masses, but we can change ourselves We can continue to educate ourselves, read, reflect, Use critical thinking, engage in meaningful conversations If you can find openminded people, which is a problem Most only want to hear the narrative they agree with Listening is an art, and when done well creates a masterpiece When done poorly is only paint by numbers Be an influencer, not a follower Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth and true self, they patiently await

George G
