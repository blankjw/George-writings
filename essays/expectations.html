EXPECTATIONS

By George Gondron

EXPECTATIONS Whatever you do for someone If there’s an expectation tied to your action You’re doing business Not kindness The world is full of one and too little of the other A generous heart has no expectations A kind heart is only concerned about the other Not itself But kindness has an end when it’s abused For in order to be kind to others We must also be kind to ourselves All charity begins from within, if it’s sincere Generosity, when trust is broken, becomes self-abuse The relationships we have in our lives Family and others Are first measured through our spontaneous giving Either through a recognized need Or simply a generous heart Giving is a way of connecting Even if it’s nothing more than the giving of our time Through our listening and our learning For too many, relationships are measured through the fulfillment Of the individual’s personal needs, through expectations Others are treated as objects rather than subjects With the unspoken thought, ‘what have you done for me lately?’ There is an oft-quoted phrase repeated in many ways Throughout history, ‘it is better to give than receive’ In honesty, a cheerful giver does inexorably receive Spontaneous joy results from giving with no ‘string attached’ A chosen path of a few minutes of silence, with stillness, and With Presence each day nurtures our inner core given at birth It awakens an inner generosity sustained by our own Heart, soul, and spirit, kindness to ourselves that flows out To all humanity – a meditative/contemplative practice A time to empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts To listen to the whispers of an internal Source, some call God To be present to an overflowing generosity without expectations

George G
