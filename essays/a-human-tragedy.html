A HUMAN TRAGEDY

By George Gondron

A HUMAN TRAGEDY There is actually only one human tragedy To not become the person, you are called to be Without the masks, the persona, the shoulds and oughts To remain the false self, and not the true self Few there are that can avoid this tragedy For we are programmed, conditioned, and manipulated To be, who we are, without exception Few escape this dilemma, this tragedy It’s natural to have emotional pain in one’s life It’s not natural to experience unconditional love It’s also not natural to know the importance of silence Of stillness, of purposeful times of contemplation Those three Emotional pain, unconditional love, a contemplative practice Are important crossroads in our individual lives Few there are that recognize their importance And even if we have a hint of that importance We live in a culture that through the many distractions The many addictive possibilities that exist Including, but not limited to, prescribed drugs We avoid the experiences that have the possibility To open us up to change, we cling to the familiar Familiar thoughts, familiar feelings, familiar actions All of which prevent us from moving forward
