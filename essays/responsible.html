RESPONSIBLE

By George Gondron

RESPONSIBLE Let’s start with the obvious Only we are responsible for three areas in our lives What we think, what we feel, and what we do or do not do Our thoughts, our feelings, and our actions Fall under the heading of personal choice The norm in our society is to not take responsibility For our choices, we would rather use one of the many Psychological defense mechanisms When our lives are not going so well We’d rather defend ourselves than take ownership of our choices We each, with no exception, defend It is the rare individual that takes full ownership of their life When it turns out we made a choice that was detrimental To ourselves or to others Through choice, we have an opportunity to change We can’t go back but we can go forward We need not remain ‘stuck’ But unfortunately, most people don’t change They prefer the familiar to the strange Even when the familiar is painful They do not comprehend the saying, ‘no risk, no reward’ Or, ‘nothing ventured, nothing gained’ Our go-to response in most cases of needed personal change Is to resist in the form of a defense It’s never our fault, it’s he, she, it, or them Self-criticism, self-honesty, self-examination Is too high a hurdle for most people But it is also the path to deliverance from defending To become self-assured, open, and embracing of change To seeing with clarity of perception That we might also have accuracy of response Rather than ‘knee-jerk’ reactions with closed minds ‘To thine own self be true’ is inclusive It requires input from your mind, heart, soul, and spirit Take time each day for a few minutes of silence, stillness With Presence, listen to the whispers of your inner core Discover your true self, your inner truth, it patiently waits

George G
