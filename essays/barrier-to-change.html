BARRIER TO CHANGE

By George Gondron | Source: BARRIER TO CHANGE.docx

BARRIER TO CHANGE The greatest barrier or roadblock to change Is the unwillingness to let go of the familiar This includes the single individual or the group The familiar can be so powerful That even when one is in pain, especially emotional pain The familiar wins Even when the group is dysfunctional in its purpose The familiar wins If a change needs to happen it’s for the other or others Not for me or my group Regardless of the function or purpose of the group Political, religious, educational, economic, or social What lies behind this refusal to change in the individual Is the ego, polarized dualistic thinking In all other venues, it’s groupthink This is the way it’s always been done, no need to change In politics, the party is more important than qualifications In religion, my God is the ‘right’ God In economics, the poor will always be with us In education, privilege has precedent In society, the haves rule over the have-nots Contributing to an attachment to the ‘familiar’ Are distractions and addictions prevalent in our culture If the pain is too great take a pill, watch social media But never, never question the values, mores, and ethics Of the group or groups to which I belong Never reveal the ‘blinders’ which I may wear The truth will set you free if you have an open mind But it seems we will do anything and everything to avoid truth Through our emotional preferences, we seek alternative truths Choose a path of self-discovery, a meditative/contemplative path Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness In the presence of your image of a creative Source, your God For, after all, life is about relationship, especially with Isness Commit to this daily practice, your life will change positively Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your true self, find your truth, they patiently wait

George G
