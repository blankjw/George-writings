MOTIVATION THROUGH FEAR 2

By George Gondron

MOTIVATION THROUGH FEAR If you study history long enough, you will discover How much more fear is used as a motivator Versus love, and they are total opposites In fact, it can be argued that everything negative Is a result of fear, and everything positive is a result of love If you motivate a child through fear You guarantee that they, more than likely, will do the same When they become adults And the message is compounded when they are exposed To a religion that espouses love but continues to motivate With fear, a perfect paradox Similar to punishing another using the words ‘This hurts me much more than it does you’ To teach through fear is to perpetuate fear And remember, everything we do and say is a teacher Children suffer the most as they are forced to deal with The mixed messages they witness through adult behavior ‘Well, he/she did this, but they said that’ Far too many children learn fear before love And unless as adults they choose to take the path of love It doesn’t change, they replicate what they’ve learned Those who live in the past or the future, do so from fear The past people are always the ‘if only’ people Bring back the ‘good ole days’ The future people, the worriers, filled with anxiety It’s abundantly clear that the positive effects of love Are preferable to the negative effects of fear Fear can motivate the masses, but love’s approach Is different, it begins and ends with the single individual It must be awakened from within From an understanding, we come together in our sameness We grow together in our differentness Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, In the presence of Love, the Consciousness of the universe Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Mostly fear-based and listen to the whispers of your heart, Soul, and spirit – they wait patiently

George G
