THE NECESSITY OF THE PAUSE

By George Gondron

THE NECESSITY OF THE PAUSE Reactor or responder, one is healthy the other is not Unfortunately, in today’s culture, it’s mostly the former Responding to an event, a discussion, individually Or in a group allows for a pause, for some flexibility Before the next interaction Merely reacting does not allow for either This is also a strong indication that little forethought, If any, precedes the reaction In a flash, we literally lash out with whatever unfiltered Rhetoric floods our consciousness Which is almost always a defensive reaction The pause also enhances our potential to listen actively Most of us don’t listen to learn, we listen to contradict Or to defend, or inflate our own ego A passive listener is already formulating a reply before We’ve finished what we’re attempting to share Like the person who finishes your sentence for you Active listening is a learning process Passive listening is akin to a ‘knee-jerk’ reaction The destructive nature of ‘gossip’ is not that we may not Be sincere in what we’re sharing or receiving But because most of us are such poor listeners By the time the narrative reaches the third, fourth, or The fifth person, it’s totally unrecognizable Maybe you’ve experienced that process When a topic is stressful, is tender, is filled with anxiety Pause, breathe, and focus before you respond Of course, if you’re not interested in learning and Enhancing your current relationships If you prefer a closed to an open mind Just ignore the aforementioned, I hope you don’t The world desperately needs better listeners and responders A path of reflection, a meditative/contemplative path Is a ‘master class’ in learning to listen A daily few minutes of silence, stillness, being present to Presence To listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your innate true self, your answers lie within

George G
