MORAL VACUUM

By George Gondron

MORAL VACUUM What happens slowly is hardly noticed Until it becomes too obvious to not notice There are many definitions for the word moral Just a few of which are ethical, virtuous, honorable, Truthful, trustworthy, principled, incorruptible, decent Aboveboard, respectable, and just; but the best is, Capable of making the distinction between right And wrong conduct And then there's the word vacuum An enclosed space out of which something has been removed In our current political atmosphere it is impossible To not notice the moral vacuum that exist within The highest levels of our government Not to see and recognize the moral vacuum that is present In the legislative, judicial, and executive branches One has to wonder where will it lead How will it change and what is the goal Of being immoral It can be several things, more likely than not It is selfishness, power, greed; these all are possibilities Do the participants even recognize the immorality Of their actions, of their 'me first' mentality Are they even aware it has become all too obvious And do they really care It seems that the rallying cry is 'party above people' Money above poverty, white against all others If they believe in a 'higher power', regardless of the name The evidence is nowhere to be found At most they give 'lip service' to the God they believe in And all the while their image of God weeps Perhaps even wondering that maybe 'free will' Wasn't such a good idea after all The only answer to this dilemma is transformation Of the human mind, but that can't be legislated It has to be a choice by each individual, each person I wonder where this moral vacuum will lead I trust, hope, and have faith it will come to a good end

George G
