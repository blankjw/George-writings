THE NECESSITY OF BECOMING

By George Gondron

THE NECESSITY OF BECOMING With all of humanities amazing advancements just in the last few decades, we seem to lack the urgency of becoming as single individuals, of individuating, discovering our true self, our gifts Our days and nights are filled with distractions empty of urgings to push or pull us toward becoming more fully human To connect to our inner core, our internal references So, we rather remain in the prison of our own minds which we create Our lives limited to what we know through our senses Through the constancy of our repetitive thoughts and emotions that change us not in the least, we are the same day after day And the consequent loss is not ours alone, but a loss to humanity The amazing gift we call consciousness, taken seriously, is a demanding taskmaster It asks of us to be critical of our lives, our choices, our decisions Anything worth having is worth working for, our becoming requires effort, it just doesn’t fall into our lap And unfortunately, it seems that most don’t care to expend the effort There is much truth to the fact that without pain, without struggle, without sacrifice, without failure, we seldom become more aware, more understanding, and more conscious, we merely exist Awash in our familiar thoughts, feelings, and actions Rather than expending the necessary energy to learn, to educate ourselves, we rely on outside sources for our truth, and we are fools True knowing comes from experience not from absorption not from osmosis, as choice makers we are also called to discern To be critical thinkers, to question, doubt, and research The signposts at the fork in the road, one reads easy, the other hard Choose the hard, the easy is a fool’s choice, it never ends well I would rather have scars that have given me meaningful experiences, and led me to truth than empty promises that have no worth We are not called to win a war, but we are called to enter the battle The battle of becoming more fully human, more fully alive Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, a practice of becoming Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Mystery Empty your mind, focus on your breath, the ego will resist Simply rinse and repeat, listen to your inner guides They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
