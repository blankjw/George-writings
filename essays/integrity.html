INTEGRITY

By George Gondron

INTEGRITY A quote about integrity by William Shakespeare “This above all; to thine own self be true’” Here’s the catch, first we must know ourselves Especially those parts which we ignore or deny When we blame, point fingers, or judge We are only projecting our own capacity To do or perform those very same actions Dualized thinking has little or no integrity For everything is polarized Right/wrong, good/bad, liberal/conservative Black/white, union/antiunion, gay/straight Not to mention the polarization in religion A life that has integrity is an inclusive life Not exclusive or a segregated one A fully human life includes peace, harmony, Kindness, compassion, justice, and equality For all, not just the few The world’s great philosophers and spiritual teachers Speak of a full life as a both/and life Not an either/or existence The greatest gift we can give to the world Is to know ourselves, there is none greater But oh, isn’t it so much easier to judge others To condemn others, to ridicule others, and To complain about everyone else’s behavior Except of course our own Carl Jung, the famous analytical psychologist Is quoted as saying ‘Every problem we have past mid-life Is a spiritual problem’, not a religious problem If you haven’t noticed Our culture is spiritually and morally bankrupt Organized religion of all beliefs has failed They are big on worship but weak on relationship And that’s what life is, relationship Take a few moments each day for silence, stillness, And Presence – empty the mind, listen to the heart

George G
