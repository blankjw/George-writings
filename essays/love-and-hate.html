LOVE AND HATE

By George Gondron

LOVE AND HATE Born with neither but learned by all Learned through our teaching Modeled in our behavior There are no exceptions, we all learn Both love and hate One unites, the other divides One reconciles, the other demands And, each can be disguised as the other Taken in through our senses The sound of a voice, the touch of a hand What the eyes perceive, the lack of presence All, teach us the difference between love and hate Words are not necessary for this learning They merely reinforce with verbal hypocrisy Or hopefully verbal promise What becomes obvious without the words What fans the flame of hate Is not what maintains the flame of love One is unconditional and promotes our growth The other dies a selfish death, always Hate, like a disease eventually kills its host But in its wake are the innocent and helpless Those who happen to be in hate's path But the stronger is that which first we experienced The bond between a mother and her child The bond of love with the child's first breath The recognition experienced in gaze to gaze Hatred can never defeat that love It has tried and failed and will fail again Be aware what you teach and what you model I hope it is always and ever love, in Love

George G
