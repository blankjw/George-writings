WHAR ARW WE LOSING

By George Gondron

WHAT ARE WE LOSING In a culture formed and founded on the premise of Production and consumption Where we are nothing more than a means to an end We are losing the experience of experience itself As we are pulled here and there by distractions Too numerable to comprehend and understand Almost as if our lives have become an escape from ourselves Too busy to process what is as it is in the present moment Creating an atmosphere of default opposition or dualism We seek, it seems, a familiar that’s comfortable yet painful A place where we can hide from thoughts and feelings Seeking to be recognized, acknowledged, and embraced That we might become more fully human, fully alive What are we losing?, we are losing our very selves We run from our emptiness, we deny our inner yearnings We do whatever we can to ignore the present As we dwell in the past reliving what could have been Or fearing the future for what may be The only real answer is always found in the now Found in the present moment facing our inner demons That only seeks to keep us from becoming our true selves Preferring that we remain an egoic self Dead from the neck down focused on doing and not being We fill our heads with noise, incessant repetitive thoughts That never comes close to relieving our angst or repressed trauma Where do we fit in?, where can we find truth? We have been conditioned to be constantly active Even our so-called ‘downtime’ is filled with distractions Choose a practice to connect mind, heart, soul, and spirit A daily meditative/contemplative few minutes of Silence, stillness, in the presence of Presence, your image of God Learn to be and not just do Allow your mind to become still, empty of those incessant And repetitive thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing Awaken your inner truth, your innate creativity given at birth There never has been, is not now, and will never be another you Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
