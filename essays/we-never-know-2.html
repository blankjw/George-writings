WE NEVER KNOW 2

By George Gondron

WE NEVER KNOW What is so significant about what we do Or don't do? Or so important about what we say Or don't say? Nothing is ever as it appears, ever Others make meaning about our actions What we say, as well as our silence And we, like them, make our own meaning A meaning that more often than not is wrong Is distorted, colored with our limited perception Interpreted through the lens of our own experience Understood with our level of awareness Deciphered with whatever our seeming consciousness We literally make meaning of everything Through our senses, our intellect, our feelings We grasp and comprehend through our total being It is said, wisdom is a result of a clear mind How many of us have a clear mind? How many of us are willing to risk the statement 'This is what I heard you say' 'Your actions meant this to me' Understanding does not emerge from argument Comprehension arises from clarity From asking the questions Before assuming the answers, presupposing a meaning Doubt is almost always preferable to certainty Certainty comes only from experience and Experience changes Be cautious of the meaning you make Leave a little room for doubt, for questions Leave room for love
