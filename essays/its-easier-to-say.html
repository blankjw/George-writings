IT'S EASIER TO SAY

By George Gondron

IT IS EASIER TO SAY Some experiences in life defy concise definition It I easier to say what they are not, than what they are Two of these are the experience of genuine love and The experience of one’s belief in a creative Source, of God A dear friend said to me years ago Nothing is so simple that it cannot be misunderstood Very true words, the ego will resist even the simple It’s much easier to justify our negative behavior Begin with the fact that love is an action, not a feeling It’s appropriate to state love is always positive, never negative Love is always giving, always about the other, selfless A God experience is the same, a carbon copy For one does not exist without the other Two phrases, unconditional love, and free will coexist There are those who decry if God is so omnipotent, So omniscient, so omnipresent Why does God allow _____________(you fill in the blank) If you understand unconditional love and free will The answer is simple, it is we who choose, not God It is we who are in control It is we who are called to love without conditions It is we who make the choices, that’s what free will is But the ego doesn’t like unconditional love or free will Because the acceptance of both means we are responsible For our behavior, our lives, our relationships Not the one we may call God So we are at the same time, both a blessing and a curse to ourselves We choose to disconnect from our inner resources From our own abilities to discern as opposed to blaming Our minds disconnected from our hearts, souls, and spirits Our egos are important but it is their nature to be in control To resist change, to shut out any input from our internal core If we are made in the image and likeness of, we carry Divine DNA Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life For just a few minutes embrace silence, be still, be present Empty your ‘monkey mind’ that you might hear the whispers Of your heart, soul, and spirit – they hold your truth, they wait

George G
