EYES WIDE SHOT

By George Gondron

EYES WIDE SHUT Most of us see what we want to see, hear what we want to hear Creating a reality that fits our particular level of awareness Or, put another way, our lack of awareness Our unrecognized attachment to the limited understanding of who we are also limits our perceptions We become trapped in polarized dualistic thinking Trapped because our options are limited to either/or Which by definition doesn’t allow for a real choice For if we don’t choose either, we must choose or For life to be full it must be a both/and proposition Either/or only leads to judgement, exclusion, bias, etc., etc. Unfortunately, a person whose life is governed primarily through their ego, has very limited vision Their minds are more closed than open, more defended than welcoming, more asleep than awake The ability to say no, to let go, to choose change is almost nonexistent to the life that has no internal reference No recognized inner core, everything is about the mind We all have free will, we all have the power to choose But if those choices are uninformed, made only because of emotional attachment and not through personal experience Then we choose illusion over reality We choose fiction over fact, following the carrot on the stick We forfeit clear perception in exchange for smoke and mirrors Peace at any price is never peace, only a self-made prison All choices have consequences and if our choices are made based on someone else’s experience so also the consequences Critical thinking, research, reflection, continued education just some of the requirements for healthy well informed decisions Drinking the Kool Aid and allowing our emotions alone to guide us never ends well An intelligence that includes not just the mind but incorporates intuition, discernment, heart, soul, and spirit knowing as well Can easily course correct when it is necessary Everyday spend time in silence, stillness, and be present to Isness Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, and patiently wait

George G
