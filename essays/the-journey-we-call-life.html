THE JOURNEY WE CALL LIFE

By George Gondron

THE JOURNEY WE CALL LIFE Hopefully throughout our lives, we now and then Take some time to reflect on the very purpose Of our existence, the meaning of our being And in those moments we reflect on our own ethics, Morals, integrity, ideas of social justice, our spirituality All of nature has a built-in innate sense of its purpose Is able to seek and provide itself with the necessary Means for its survival and continued existence You would think that with the added benefit of Free will, consciousness, and choice, we humans Would be very adept at kindness, compassion, empathy, Care, inclusion, peace, justice, and equality for all But with evidence to the contrary, we have failed Those aspects that set us apart from other life forms Have turned out to be, in far too many instances. Not a blessing but a curse Our behavior clearly reveals, when we have no Sense of the sacred, of mystery, of spirituality When ‘ego’ is king, and we have no internal reference We can and do become ‘wolves in sheep’s clothing’ There is only one species responsible for the suffering That exists in all its many forms, the human species We alone are the cause for all that is dysfunctional The fact that we have intellect obviously doesn’t Mean we are intelligent, far from it We it seems, have not only numbed our brains But as well we have neglected our psyches, our souls For balance adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still Hear the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
