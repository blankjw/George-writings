WITHOUT LOVE

By George Gondron

WITHOUT LOVE As horrible as circumstances seem at times From our small circle to the world as a whole Without love, they would be much worse For it is love that sustains and maintains All that we know, all that we experience All that we can imagine A reality that we seldom think about And much less even take time to consider Something much greater than the human mind can comprehend Is responsible for this vast universe And this small mind of ours insists on projecting Onto this unknowable source various names which Cannot possibly come close to reality Names in themselves are nothing more than labels Attempting to describe something called God In a lame attempt to intellectually know the unknowable Benevolent unconditionally Love is a paradox, a conundrum For it is known only through personal experience It asks for nothing in return, makes no demands And as St. Augustine said, ‘is closer to us than we are To ourselves’ or as Voltaire said ‘God is a circle whose center is everywhere and circumference Nowhere’ It is not with the mind that we know the Isness of the universe As the fox told the little Prince ‘It is only with the heart that one can see rightly; What is essential is invisible to the eye’ The Western ego can never understand unconditional Love Through the intellect, only through the heart Choose a meditative/contemplative daily practice For just a few minutes sit in silence, experience stillness, Be in the presence of Presence – a daily commitment Empty your mind of its incessant and often repetitive thoughts Thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your inner truth They patiently wait, become aware

George G
