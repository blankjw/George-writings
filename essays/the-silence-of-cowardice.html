THE SILENCE OF COWARDICE

By George Gondron | Source: THE SILENCE OF COWARDICE.docx

THE SILENCE OF COWARDICE It is always a risk to speak out when silence is easier Easier to chose the path of least resistance But when a voice is needed, to remain silent Is the choice of the faint-hearted, the 'cowardly lion' The person who turns away and pretends they don't hear or see The one who says to themselves, 'it's none of my business' Afraid of what the cost may be; that's easy to answer The cost is an increase in selfishness, a lessening of self A shrinking of the human spirit, a compromise of integrity Just an added little personal shortcoming Added to the hidden others which we may carry A heavy burden for the soul, consciously or unconsciously One of the better excuses to remain silent, to not confront In the face of an obvious abuse, a clear harm or exploitation Is the following internal dialogue, 'they wouldn't understand' 'They'd just laugh it off or ignore me entirely' Yes, we can find any number of excuses to assuage our conscience In order to continue to feel good about our denied cowardice And of course the underlying emotion is nothing more than fear Plain, pure, unadulterated fear - the basis of all negativity Ironically, if we knew how to deal with and process Our own emotions congruently, we would have no fear That's the real issue, how to deal, face, and process our emotions Without clinging to defense mechanisms, making excuses Believe it or not we are not just 'talking heads' Believing that the intellect, the mind, the ego are all that matters That is a conditioned, programmed, trained, learned belief It is a lie, it is the doctrine of those who seek power and control We are also physical, emotional, and spiritual beings And without the inclusion of all our human faculties We cannot call ourselves human at all, half human at best Perhaps it's my age, or maybe lack of fear I refuse to remain silent in the face of abuse, of any kind I refuse to be a coward, regardless the cost For I know the harm I may see does not stop with just my seeing It is like a ripple in a pond, it continues Just as does compassion, speak out, don't be a coward

George G
