DUE PEOCESS

By George Gondron

DUE PROCESS Soon after our Constitution was ratified the first ten amendments were added called the Bill of Rights The Fifth Amendment states ‘no person’ (not citizen) shall be deprived of “life, liberty, or property, without the due process of law” The Fourteenth Amendment was ratified later to include the states as well as the federal government Those included more specifically are “all persons within U.S. territory, including corporations, aliens, and presumptively, citizens seeking readmission to the United States.” When any or all of the above are not given due process our Constitution has been violated So, what’s the lesson to be learned given by our founding fathers? Careful who you judge without due process, you may be next And honestly, we all fail the requirements of due process when we embrace conspiracy theories, blame, defend, and point fingers with only our emotions to support our unfounded beliefs, our fragile egos It’s always the other or others who are at fault, never ourselves We are all guilty of scapegoating others without reliable evidence Psychologically there are two primary emotions, fear and love All that is negative falls under fear, all that is positive is based on love We, without a doubt, are a fear-based culture, deeply divided And consequently, we have anti negative attitudes, lacking kindness, compassion, empathy, and love for all people, not just the few We unconsciously have formulated guidelines for who is acceptable and who is not, which only supports ‘exclusion, bigotry, prejudice, and continued discrimination’ Just maybe it would be wiser to look in the mirror and be self-critical And further ask ourselves how we avoid “due process” Passing judgement without evidence based on emotional reactions And if haven’t noticed, our current leadership motivates us with fear To connect with our own innate inner resources, our internal core adopt a daily meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes in silence, stillness, with Isness each day Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Breathe in, breathe out, the mind will wander, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your inner guides; heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
