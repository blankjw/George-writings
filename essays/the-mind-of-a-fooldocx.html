THE MIND OF A FOOL

By George Gondron

THE MIND OF A FOOL Begin with this thought, It’s easier to fool a person Than it is to convince them they are being fooled In other words, it’s more difficult to talk someone Out of something that they already believe But very easy to reinforce what they believe Even if what they believe can be disproved No one wants to be proven wrong Even in the simplest matters much less the larger ones As the saying goes, ‘birds of a feather, stick together’ There’s no such thing as a group rational mind That applies only to individuals Groups have no rationality, they are easily controlled Especially rhetoric that stirs their emotions Fear is a stronger control generator than love, always And fear is alive and well in this society The old adage, ‘divide and conquer’ is alive and well A common cry from every arena, political and religious The reins of power in a democracy is the right to vote And yet half the people qualified to vote, don’t The mind of fools The teachings of Christ and Buddha have similarities Kindness, compassion, altruism, and love The vast majority of those who call themselves ‘believers’ Don’t believe at all, as evidenced in their behavior The mind of fools When division, exclusion, separation, and discrimination rein What’s the solution, is there a solution? When the love of power, control, and the material Is stronger than the power of love for humanity Who will stand in the breach and say, ‘enough is enough’ What exists now will never work Groupthink doesn’t change, only individuals change Be a change agent, embrace a meditative/contemplative path For a few minutes a day be silent, be still, be in the Presence Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They have your answers, they hold your truth, they await

George G
