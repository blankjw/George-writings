CLOSED MINDS

By George Gondron

CLOSED MINDS The most dangerous mind is a closed mind A mind that doesn’t listen to learn but only to defend There are many psychological defense mechanisms, projection, denial, repression, regression, sublimation, compartmentalization rationalization, displacement, reaction formation, intellectualization, Closed minds are polarized and dualistic, minds that can’t grasp the idea of situational ethics but one that perceives reality as an either/or possibility, never a both/and possibility All of life’s choices then, especially the more difficult ones, stand on a chair with only two legs, and the chair falls Whether we acknowledge it or not, choice has many possibilities If good/bad, black/white/ right/wrong, etc. are our only perceived choices, we deceive ourselves for if we don’t choose one we are forced to choose the other, that’s not choice There are always at least three choices and actually many more Polarized and dualistic thinkers mostly live in the past out of anger and resentment, or in the future because of fear and insecurity They miss the now, the most important moment there is The real dilemma is obvious, closed minds resist change Closed minds are rigid preferring the familiar to the strange even If the familiar is painful Change, especially an increase in awareness and consciousness is an indication of positive progress, the refusal to change is death So, how do we encourage people to change, to be self-critical Especially when the ego’s ‘knee jerk’ reaction is to resist change A great motivator for personal change is pain, but the mind will use whatever distraction is available, the next shiny object, to avoid pain Even if we have enough willpower to embrace a path of change we must adopt a new context Choose a meditative/contemplative practice, something so few do So simple, so powerful and so rejected by the mind A few minutes each day be silent, be still, be present to Mystery Empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out When rhe mind wanders, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
