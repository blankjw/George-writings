EMPATHY

By George Gondron

EMPATHY Empathy is a step beyond sympathy Sympathy is to feel sorrow for the circumstances Empathy is to feel sorrow with To imagine oneself in the condition of another Sympathy is the sharing of one’s emotions intellectually Empathy is more vicarious, more visceral Almost like taking the place of the other Sympathy requires a certain amount of understanding Empathy is more spontaneous and immediate One could easily say Sympathy is of the head Empathy is of the heart The more individuated a person, the more empathetic For they have engaged their own darkness They know that we are all more alike than unalike That judgment and condemnation are matters Of the ego, lacking the heart’s compassion They see sameness before differentness Those who have never suffered never struggled Never yet faced their own human weakness Are more capable of sympathy than empathy There is a connection that is missing One which can say, ‘I have been where you are’ ‘I too have reached the end of my rope’ One of the greatest motivators for change is pain Deep unbearable emotional pain The type of pain that produces the ability To be empathetic, to be at one with Ironically, we live in a culture that avoids pain Take a pill, seek distraction, anything to not Face our pain, deal with our pain, learn from our pain We learn empathy through learning ourselves To the depth of our innermost being A path that most avoid and never engage Take a few minutes each day for quiet reflection Embrace silence, and stillness, with Presence Empty the mind, listen to the whispers of the heart

George G
