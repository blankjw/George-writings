IT'S EASIER TO ASSUME THAN TO KNOW

By George Gondron

IT’S EASIER TO ASSUME THAN TO KNOW Far too much of what we claim to know We don’t know at all, it’s merely an assumption To believe without ‘knowing’ is to play the fool The inability to not be able to distinguish the lie from the truth Is to remain the fool, and alas we are millions of fools We all have the power to question, doubt, reflect, research But unfortunately, most prefer to just remain sponges And absorb what flows our way based on our current beliefs And please, remember most belief is not experiential Especially as it relates to other people or groups It’s more the result of he/she/they said, some call opinion Or others call it ‘gossip’, with no verifiable evidence at all Our best teachers are our five senses, they are immediate Taste, touch, smell, hear, and see But our thinking and feelings are very different They can be easily manipulated, stimulated, controlled Advertisers and marketers know very well how to attract us Most people in power, selfish and selfless, know how to Stir our emotions through love and fear Through hope or through doubt, through facts or through lies Here’s a fact The less educated we are, the more likely we will believe the lies The more educated, the more likely we will see that most Political narrative, most religion, is about power and control Not about empathy, kindness, compassion, love, and inclusion As for me, I prefer the latter, not the former Please, do yourself a favor, and check your beliefs Are they merely assumptions without factual basis? Without the benefit of personal experience? Is your life not worth knowing the truth, or is it living an illusion? Remember, ‘alternative facts’ are just that, alternative Add to your learning, choose a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, be in the now Empty your mind of incessant and often repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or choose a brief mantra Be present to your image of Isness, Universal Consciousness, God Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
