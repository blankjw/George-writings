THE CASE FOR CHANGE

By George Gondron | Source: THE CASE FOR CHANGE.docx

THE CASE FOR CHANGE Why change, why doubt, why seek, why question? Because the alternative is nothing more than quiet defeat Acceptance that what is, is all there is We don't love others hoping they will change We love others so that they might change Would that we loved ourselves the same way We convince ourselves there's no need to change And besides it's too difficult, upsets the routine All which are excuses, we much prefer the familiar Oh, and there's another reason to resist change It broadens our horizons, pulls off our blinders And worst of all, causes us to think Change asks of us to respond versus reacting It asks of us to listen with an open mind And for most to have an open mind is way too frightening We are contradictions to ourselves Dealing with life in our parts rather than our wholeness We are proficient using our egos, our minds But we are sorely lacking in the use of our innate abilities Called our hearts, our souls, and our spirits They have laid dormant for far too long We barely know they are there And when they do occasionally push our egos aside Being touched by some urge of compassion They soon lose their influence as the ego pushes back No, if I let them in, then I'll surely have to change It is the human dilemma The ego takes, while the heart gives The ego is about self, the heart about other The ego is about the usual, the common, the defined The heart is about the unknown, the mystery, the divine Change is inevitable no matter how much we resist If nothing more our death will bring change But no need to wait for the 'grim reaper' Better to change and reap the rewards, now Better to listen to our hearts, our souls, and our spirits They are truer guides than just our ego

George G
