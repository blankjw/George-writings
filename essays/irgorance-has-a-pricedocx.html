IRGORANCE HAS A PRICE

By George Gondron

IGNORANCE HAS A PRICE Ignorance is measurable since it has to do with one’s level of literacy In our nation one out of five adults, twenty percent, are illiterate Cannot read or write Another fifty-four percent of adults have a literacy rate of sixth grade or lower, both facts are astounding Ignorance is directly correlated with education Compulsory education is required through the 12th grade Education after that is voluntary, either through undergraduate, graduate degrees, or self-study It is not the absorption of social media that solves ignorance Even now, there are restrictions being imposed on every educational institution from kindergarten through college regarding what can or cannot be taught As a result, history is distorted, or even eliminated altogether When what can or cannot be taught becomes the norm then so also controlling what people think and believe as well The price of ignorance becomes first, loss of personal freedom Followed by, in our nation, the erosion of our Constitution and our democracy, history is witness to both these facts Another significant result of ignorance is dualistic and/or polarized thinking, closed minds You know the type, ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ Among the many gifts we have as human beings are free will, the ability to choose, curiosity, imagination, and intuition To relinquish these gifts is to allow others to control our lives That’s too big a price to pay, our personal experience matters If our lives become nothing more than sponges, merely accepting what stirs our emotions at the expense of our own critical thinking then we are willing participants in our own ignorance Often what we need for our own well-being is what we resist The ego will cling to the familiar, even if it’s painful, resisting change I recommend a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day be silent, be still, be with Presence Empty your ‘monkey mind’ of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
