SELF LOVE

By George Gondron

SELF LOVE Is self-love important? Not only is it important, it is absolutely necessary If we are to love others in a healthy meaningful way We must learn to love ourselves selflessly, not selfishly The gift of what we call our life demands it Mind, body, heart, soul, and spirit, our whole being Our humanity includes not just our strengths But our flaws and weaknesses as well Most relationships don’t fail because of some flaw In the other They fail because of a lack of self-love in both parties Two individuals expecting each one’s needs to be net By the other, it won’t work and never will At best we may grow old together in quiet tolerance A terrible price to pay for lack of self-love When two beings join in a primary relationship They are agreeing to enter into a crucible of vulnerability To be challenged by each other for the betterment of each The friction and anxiety generated in the relationship Becomes the proving ground for their success or failure Important relationships are partnerships not ownership One is not meant to rule over the other they are co-equal In the tension, seldom do either ask the question How am I personally not loving myself? More often the question is, why are they not meeting my needs? The truth for each is the same The only person they can change is themselves, not the other A lesson that is seldom learned Sometimes the better choice is to walk away, as hard as that may be One person can’t make a healthy relationship, it takes both Self-love necessarily includes some alone time Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for needed balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Presence being your image of Mystery, of God Choose a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out, stay focused Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
