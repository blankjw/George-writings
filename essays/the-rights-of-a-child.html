THE RIGHTS OF A CHILD

By George Gondron

THE RIGHTS OF A CHILD Let’s begin with the fact, we are the richest nation in the world Every child deserves an education geared to their innate gifts Every child deserves proper nutrition for adequate growth Every child deserves medical attention when needed We have the means to provide all the above But we don’t Thirteen percent of children will not graduate high school Eleven million children live in poverty Among US households with children under the age of 18 Seventeen percent of those households were food insecure Twenty million children lack sufficient access To essential healthcare We are the richest nation in the world Given the opportunity and the resources I have a strong suspicion that these deficiencies Would probably decrease sharply, if not disappear Directly correlated to the rights of children and their education Are the insufficient resources financial and otherwise Provided to those who are their teachers What they do for the little they receive is beyond charitable For the last five decades, there have not been adequate Wages paid to most hourly workers But then again, we’re the richest nation in the world
