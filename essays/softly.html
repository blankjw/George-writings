ESSAY

SOFTLY

By George Gondron

SOFTLY Nothing so soft as water Yet able to wear away a mountain of granite A single drop falling onto a tiny silver bell Brings forth a wondrous sound All from its softness Able to flow with effortless ease Eluding resistance, conquering all barriers Quenching our thirst Sustaining our life There is another softness The softness of silence In the silence the spirit awakens The heart loses its hardness The soul's thirst is sated by the stillness To resist silence is to resist awareness To avoid silence is to be defended What is the fear of silence? The aversion to stillness? The avoidance of Presence? It is only the mind The mind that resists, avoids, denies The mind that clings to control Silence, stillness, Presence, all uncontrollable Always available, always ready, always near Embrace the possible Choose the softness of silence The stillness will follow The Presence is waiting

George G
