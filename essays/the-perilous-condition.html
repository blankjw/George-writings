THE PERILOUS CONDITION

By George Gondron | Source: THE PERILOUS CONDITION.docx

THE PERILOUS CONDITION I've learned the opposite of love is not hate Hate carries energy and can be felt deep within The opposite of love is apathy, where love has died The condition of no emotion, a dullness When I've reached the place of not caring My sails may be full but my compass has no direction A sense of uneasiness and discomfort dwell within Lack of interest seems to be the fore An indifference to even the simplest existence My successes and strivings have become empty and hollow Ashes tossed and lost in the wind Attending thoughts of, 'who am I really?' Perhaps, maybe, I've chased the wrong rainbow Possibly I needed to pay more attention to those subtle nudges in the recesses of my mind The gentle push, the soft whisper, of change direction, change course Sometimes it's much better to pay attention to the not so obvious, to the seemingly hidden To revisit a word or sentence that once may have attracted, ever so briefly A perilous condition is the condition of apathy It seems beyond despair, beyond hope, even beyond redemption Is it possible, just maybe, that I can return to the faith of the small child I once was? The child who knew that in the end, all would be confirmed Can my memory carry me back to that time, long ago? Can that small child save me, awaken me? Teach me again what is truly important? Perhaps it can Perhaps

George G
