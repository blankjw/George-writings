MY PRAYER

By George Gondron

MY PRAYER How do I pray to you my Lord? The words seem so useless and unimportant Empty and hollow with no substance The mind has no words, no thoughts, nothing of value That can possibly express what lies within my heart, my soul No need to ask for anything You already know what I need In Your greatness There is no need for my gratitude You don't seek sacrifice or worship In quiet moments I have hints of what prayer truly is It is relationship, it is being with It is the soft beating of my heart, the warmth of my breath In the silence, in the stillness, in the solitude In Your Presence, in contemplation We pray, 'Thy will be done' What is Your will oh Lord? I know the answer, I feel the meaning, I sense the reason It is the well-being of Your creation It is relationship to and with all that is The well-being of all that You provide as gift Given through Your grace, Your love So, my prayer also must be action Action that springs from my contemplation Action, given in the form of love for all For all that You have created and are creating still You create life, out of love, for relationship How could it be otherwise? Your Son taught us these things, with His life He showed us how to live, how to relate, how to love We are slow to learn, we prefer rules instead We are slow to follow, reluctant to practice But, You trust us still, You have faith in Your creation You give us Your Spirit, bound to Your Love We will learn, we will relate, we will love I know we will, we must I promise, we will, we can

George G
