INTROSPECTION

By George Gondron

INTROSPECTION The ability to look within The process that few in Western culture Take the time for or have the ability to do The serious examination of one’s own behavior Connected to critical thought The understanding that all change Begins with personal change Our ‘go to’ behavior is first and foremost Pointing fingers and blaming Nothing can be my fault So, it must be your fault Thinking which is normal to a young child Or even adolescents But no longer are adults the exception Now, all it seems, participate in the blame game Lack of introspection is directly correlated To an inability to have a personal relationship With one’s image of a creator Being Regardless of one’s belief system Rather, that relationship remains cognitive only And never reaches the heart It remains a belief about rules and regulations Rites and rituals, and dogma and doctrine Polarized thinking at its best Do good get rewarded, do bad get punished Sincere belief is not about good or bad It is about relationships, pure and simple That’s what life is, relationship That is its best definition Beginning with the honest relationship about myself And then with all else, not just other people Introspection demands that I examine my own behavior And how that impacts others We don’t live in a vacuum What we do or do not do matters We truly are a part of the problem, or of the solution For your sake, for all’s sake, be introspective

George G
