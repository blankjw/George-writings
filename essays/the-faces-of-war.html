THE FACES OF WAR

By George Gondron

THE FACES OF WAR The blessings and curses of the digital age Allow us to see in real-time The ugly faces of war Along with the depravity of the human mind All we need do is access the nearest TV, iPhone, tablet, or computer The images are often too much to comprehend From the total destruction of the landscape To the broken and mangled bodies of the innocent Men, women, and children sacrificed As a result of a megalomanic A soulless individual inflated with his own importance As we witness from afar We must reflect on how do we end this travesty? How does the world end this debacle? Is it a simple matter of fear That prevents a deterring response? And does that fear justify the tremendous loss Witnessed each and every day? Fear is always the enemy Can this indescribable horror Only be ended through retribution Of a similar nature? Are we and other nations doing enough To save the people of Ukraine? Are is what we are doing going to be Too little, too late? I don’t have the answers I wish I did Don’t turn away from the images Allow them to sear into your memory As a reminder of future possibilities Even within our own nation Don’t take freedom and democracy for granted Pray daily for the people of Ukraine Give what you can for their aid The smallest amount matters

George G
