CIRCUMSTANCES

By George Gondron | Source: CIRCUMSTANCES.docx

CIRCUMSTANCES Our current circumstances or context will only change Through choice Especially how we think and feel about our circumstances Jokingly, what we most experience could be described as Premeditated carelessness But once we acknowledge and take responsibility for our Circumstances, our choices, we then also awake to the fact That we have always been in charge of our choices That awakening, that self-revelation is the greatest gift We can ever receive For now, all the psychological defense mechanisms we’ve Been using can fall by the wayside Repression, denial, justification, projection, rationalization. Avoidance, passive-aggression, etc., etc., etc. All can be seen in the clear light of day as ways we defend Our own dysfunctional behavior, repeatedly What we think, feel, and do or do not do is a matter of choice Figuratively, no one is holding a gun to our head Taking ownership also means further educating ourselves Education beyond most social media Which is more interested in controlling than educating We can cease to blame and point fingers, actions motivated Through an underlying fear and a closed mind Learning that expands our awareness and consciousness Only happens in a context or circumstance related to the Topic of our choice, what we’re attempting to learn For example, religion and spirituality are very different One is more about intellectual knowing, the other experiential Knowing - each within a different context The commonalities for spiritual experiences are more about Being than doing – more about emptiness than fullness More about relationship with Mystery than knowledge of religion Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your life A few minutes each day be silent, be still, be present, be empty Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in breathe out Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
