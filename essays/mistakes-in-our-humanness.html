MISTAKES IN OUR HUMANNESS

By George Gondron | Source: MISTAKES IN OUR HUMANNESS.docx

MISTAKES IN OUR HUMANNESS Are there choices, decisions, mistakes From which we can never recover, never recoup? Yes, there are If they involved another life, another person Perhaps, if we're fortunate Those mistakes can be worked out, discussed, even healed Perhaps not It may be an issue of forgiveness Or simply one of misunderstanding More often than not, these kind of mistakes Are a result of assumptions, unspoken fears Misinformation and unanswered questions The saying, 'what we don't know won't hurt us' Is very false, it always hurts us Especially in human relationships When we don't know, we guess Our guesses are almost always wrong What remains silent and unspoken Becomes the 'elephant in room' In human relationships, in potential partnerships We seldom, especially in newly formed encounters Say what we feel and think on deeper levels We are 'testing the waters', 'keeping our distance' Unwilling to ask for what we want Fearful of taking a risk Assumptions abound with little factual evidence It is the cost of being human, of having a heart Of wanting to trust without being vulnerable It takes the willingness of a wounded spirit The compliant soul and open heart To heal the wounds of past mistakes For those who are no longer in our lives It may not be possible For those that are, near or far With honest caring and sharing What was broken, can be mended Mended in many different ways

George G
