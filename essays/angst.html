ANGST

By George Gondron

ANGST

A general feeling of dread, apprehension, or fear That’s angst And the more divided we become The stronger the anxiety And the more negative our thoughts These prolonged feelings lead to health issues Both physical and emotional The ego rules more than the heart And our relationships suffer How can anything positive result from A continual atmosphere of angst? Worry sits on our shoulder Whispering messages of doom and gloom Thoughts of ‘If only’s’ are our constant companions If only he, she, they, would What? Maybe, just maybe the question needs to be asked Of ourselves, individually How do I change my thoughts of angst? Whether we are willing to accept it or not We can’t change others We can only change ourselves My ability to live in the present moment To let go of the past and resist fearing the future Depends only on me – no one else If we decide to take ownership of our condition Stop seeking distractions Stop medicating, in all forms Maybe we can face our pain, our angst And begin to heal Begin to appreciate ourselves rather than Waiting for approval and acceptance from others We are stronger than we know And better than we believe We are connected, we are relational, We are human beings, gifts to one another

George G
