WHO'S SPECIAL

By George Gondron

WHO'S SPECIAL You are, she is, he is, they are, we all are, special So few of us realize the simple fact that we each are special Not in an inflated, egotistical way, but in a humble reality Not until we understand that we each have a unique gift That we give to each other, in a sharing caring way Will we see sameness before difference Before color, before gender, before any perceived Difference, only sameness Sameness in our connections, our interdependence Our need for each other, with no exceptions But then, we create standards, and guidelines Criterion and yard sticks, and regulations Prefixes and suffixes to our names That point out we're not special Because we don't measure up We don't meet the standardized model We're not 'Barbie' enough if we're female Not 'Ken' enough if we're male Not intelligent enough if we don't have The approved necessary education Then we begin to compare and contrast And if we don't meet this 'imaginary bar' Whatever it may be, whoever it may be We don't matter, we swallow the lie And in some small or large way We become our own worst critic We buy into what's being sold We live in our self made prison Constructed with the help of our religions, our politics Our educational institutions, our culture We each are a 'diamond in the rough', like no one else Contained within each of us is something special Meant to be shared with all others You can't find it in a book, a classroom, a job But in silence, stillness, and Presence you'll discover it You'll find it within yourself, between and among others You'll discover the gift that you are, to be shared with all

George G
