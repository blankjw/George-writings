THE NATURE OF LOVE

By George Gondron

THE NATURE OF LOVE The nature of love is to give, not receive For genuine love is not a feeling but an action We use the word love to express fondness for a person or I love that meal, that movie, that book, etc., etc., etc. All of which is saying, ‘I experience pleasure from that event’ But that pleasure is not love in its true sense Our actions for others performed with no strings attached No expectations, no reciprocity, no ‘tit for tat’ Is the real nature of genuine love, unconditional love The Greeks have eight words for love as follows Eros – romantic, passionate love Phila – affectionate love Storge – familiar love Manis – obsessive love Ludus – playful love Pragma – enduring love Philautia – self love Agape – selfless. universal love Agape love, unconditional love is the nature of love in action A giving, a reaching out without conditions But under conditions of abuse, physically, emotionally Spirituality, economically, intellectuality, or psychologically That unconditional love can be withdrawn By defending abuse in any form, we lessen ourselves Unconditional love does not mean you can be used As a ‘doormat’ What we do for others, especially through sacrifice With no expectations, and no strings attached is love It all begins with unconditional love for self For those who are honest, not an easy task A starting point, a meditative/contemplative practice A daily few minutes of silence, stillness, with Presence Presence being one’s image of God, Isness, Source A time to empty the mind of its incessant thoughts Most of which are repetitive about yesterday or tomorrow A time to listen to the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit They hold our ‘true self’, our healing, our truth, they wait

George G
