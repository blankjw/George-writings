BELIEF SYSTEMS

By George Gondron

BELIEF SYSTEMS The core of all belief systems is about relationship Not meritocracy, dogma, doctrine, rite, or ritual But a relationship with the source of one’s belief And subsequently, a relationship with all that is Especially the relationship with one’s self If we as individuals are not self-actualized Whole within ourselves as a result of the integration Of mind, heart, soul, and spirit In psychological terms, the individuation process Bringing unconscious contents into consciousness Then all our relationships are lacking in purpose Without that ‘wholeness,’ there will be something missing Our connections will be fraught with unconscious expectations Acting counter to genuine love and real freedom Relying on psychological defense mechanisms for survival Living through the intellect, the ego, the mind alone Is not a recipe for human wholeness, free from fear Life is relationship and relationship is a result of love The proof of unconditional love rests in two words Free will Even being loved unconditionally, you and I, Are not required to return that same love, we can refuse Either through denial of a creative Source Or choosing our own particular version of acceptance Which in most cases is about worship and not practice Our fragile egos would never acknowledge it But the only real enemy we have is ourselves We are very adept at justifying what we do or do not do, easily Back to belief systems – the holder of a valid belief system Incorporates some form of reflective practice in their life Basically a meditative/contemplative practice A daily few minutes of silence, stillness, and Presence A time to empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts That we might hear the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit That we might learn to live from our ‘true self’ That we might find the answers we seek and the truth we hold Our inner core, our internal resources wait for our attention

George G
