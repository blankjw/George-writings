THE POWER OF OPEN DIALOGUE

By George Gondron

THE POWER OF OPEN DIALOGUE In order to have a dialogue concerning an issue That on its surface is problematic Several prerequisites must be established, must be present Whether it’s a one on one or a group dialogue We must have an open and not a closed mind We must listen to learn and understand, not simply react And defend - defensiveness is incapable of hearing We must know that life is not just either/or, black or white Life is always both/and, and mostly gray We must be able to recognize that difficult problems Have no easy answers and the solutions are almost Always a compromise, a negotiation, a give and take All the aforementioned have their polar opposite Revealed in all human relationships intimate or otherwise Revealed in the personalities of those in position of power Those with wealth and status with their degrees and pedigrees They have closed minds, they are always right They care less about truth and more about their opinions In fact, the only truth that matters is their truth They do not listen for the only voice that matters is their own They ignore the facts and are blind to any reliable evidence They are expert in the use of being defensive, blame, denial, etc. Their narrative is filled with ‘what about ism’s’ and comparison They live in the past, the ‘good ole days’ Or they fear the future through a life of worry They miss the now, the only moment that matters I hope you’re one of the former and not the latter I hope you seek to learn and further educate yourself I hope you search for balance in your life, inner and outer I offer this suggestion -adopt a meditative/contemplative path Include a few minutes of silence, stillness, and Presence daily Presence being your image of Isness, or Universal Consciousness Of God , if you prefer – empty your mind of all thought Focus on your breath or a short mantra to avoid distraction Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They are as important as your mind, if, not more so They hold your true self, have your answers, and patiently wait

George G
