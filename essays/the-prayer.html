THE PRAYER

By George Gondron

THE PRAYER We all have one, a special prayer One that's just ours It has meaning only for us, no one else We may bring it to mind often Or perhaps very seldom But it's a prayer that's personal, about relationship It's not about asking for anything Not about being thankful for anything Not about praying for someone else In fact it is wordless, empty of all thought For words, more often than not Are only a hindrance, they get in the way The mind cannot comprehend What the heart speaks without words What the spirit knows, unspoken The most important prayer, the most meaningful prayer Is always about being, not doing, not speaking, no formulas It is always about being present to Nothing more, northing less The spirit knows much more than the mind ever will The heart knows what the mind cannot fathom The greatest gift I can give of what I call myself Is nothing less than being fully present to another To know one's God experientially One does not have to hear voices, or see visions One only needs to be silent, to be still To be present to what already exists within each of us 'God is closer to us than we are to ourselves', - St Augustine There is no special time or no special place to pray It is always now, always where you are Always in the presence of the One you may call God There is always time, for there is always now We don't pray in the past, nor in the future But only in the present moment That's what's so valuable about the present moment It's always now, God is always now, always present I have my special prayer, you find yours, perhaps we'll meet

George G
