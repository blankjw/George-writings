TO MAKE THE COMPLEX SIMPLE

By George Gondron

TO MAKE THE COMPLEX SIMPLE A good teacher has a unique ability To make the complex simple We, for the most part don't like simple We prefer the hard way, the complicated way A good teacher has a way of engaging the whole person Not just their mind, their ego, their intellect But the whole human being, body, soul, and spirit A good teacher has the ability to create doubt Just enough doubt, enough curiosity, to arouse the mind Simple reasoning has a way of challenging the ego Whereas complicated explanations create closed minds It's more difficult for the ego to defend against simplicity For example, most of those who 'wave the flag' And espouse 'patriotism and nationalism', above and beyond When asked if they've ever read the Constitution Or the Bill of Rights Fall in the majority response of, 'no' It is usually those that shout the loudest Beat their chests the most, that know the least Living within a mind that knows not itself Yet, has all the answers Giving irrelevant responses to important questions Simple questions They seem to fall in the category of 'I' and 'me' As opposed to 'we' and 'us' Except of course if the 'we' and 'us' are likeminded They are addicted to their way of thinking Which is usually narcissistic and not thinking at all Just a 'knee jerk' reaction, with no clear perception 'Me and mine', us 'gun tote'n' good ole boys' Simplicity teaches us 'less is more' Strength lies in our weakness Real love is not selfish, but selfless And humanity is whole only in connection and unity The ego alone, thrives on division and separation The life lived to the full, thrives on togetherness, simplicity Thrives on the premise that 'I am my brother's keeper'

George G
