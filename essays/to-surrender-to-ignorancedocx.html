TO SURRENDER TO IGNORANCE

By George Gondron

TO SURRENDER TO IGNORANCE The dues we pay for surrendering to ignorance Are costly indeed, very costly Ignorance does not expand, it contracts It pulls us backward to a supposed 'better time' Rather than pulling us forward to the possible It reminisces on prior memories Questioning future possibilities It speaks in the language of 'remember when?' Rather than the language of future hopes and dreams Living in the past, clinging to what's lost Stuck in what was, missing what is We can never improve what is, by living with what was We don't expand and grow by holding fast or shrinking Life is about becoming more, not less Evolution moves forward, not backward Awareness calls us to increase not diminish It makes no sense to become more unconsciousness And less conscious, less knowledgeable The signs of growth, of development, of progression In all areas of life Intellectual, emotional, and spiritual Are not by being attached to what was But by being present to now and what can be The past exists for the purpose of history Not for a 'place' in which we live The past is a teacher of the 'good ' and the 'bad' Revealing what needs be dropped and not repeated As well as the promise of what can be better And if we don't learn the lessons taught by the past We merely repeat them again and again Ignorance may seem too strong a word But it simply means unawareness and inexperience And we can't become more aware, more experienced By living in the past and clinging to what was For whatever the reason, however desirable Life can only be lived in the now, not in the yesterday Be present, be now, be alive; not dead, living in the past

George G
