WITHOUT FAULT

By George Gondron | Source: WITHOUT FAULT.docx

WITHOUT FAULT There is no one without fault, of some kind But for most, it's unintended The fault lies in our unawareness, not in the noblest of our ideas It happens in our need to feel secure and in control It happens as a result of not being 'okay' with ourselves Accepting how we are, and who we are, now, not later It lies in not what we see, but how we see Not in what we hear, but how we hear Without blinders, without prejudice, without discrimination The purest of motives without the inclusion of the heart Will always contain some fault, some error The heart knows far beyond the intellect, the mind The heart is able to discern the impact of our choices on life Life beyond just our own In the matter of having to chose between two seemingly 'goods' Or two seemingly 'bads' Whatever the decision, someone will see fault Whether in periods of desolation or consolation Fault is always present without the discernment of the heart The wisdom of the clearest mind cannot compare To the knowledge, the balance, the reason of the heart The evidence of fault individually and corporately is easy to see It abounds wherever there is injustice, turmoil, and destruction Where people are pitted one against the other For economic, educational, political, or religious reasons And in the end there is no winner, but many who suffer I am at fault, you are at fault, we are at fault I wish there was a magic elixir that would instill heart knowledge But there's not, one does not exist But there is a process, a path, a practice that yields heart knowledge And it is simple, it is free, and it is always available, to all It is found in silence, in stillness, and arises from Presence It demands only one thing, choice Intentional, committed, daily choice, for only a few minutes In matters of the heart, it is the necessary alternative It is the essence of Love which sustains our life Moment by moment, breath by breath, heartbeat by heartbeat

George G
