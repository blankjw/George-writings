DON'T WAIT

By George Gondron

DON'T WAIT The one who crossed your mind Followed with the thought, 'perhaps I will call' Do it, don't wait, make the call You promised someone a visit, keep your promise If it's something we mean to do, and yet we don't With someone we care enough about Then we fail ourselves and the one for whom we care Our deepest regrets, more often than not Are, not what we've done But, what we haven't done To hear a voice, to feel a hug, see a smile Means so much more than words on paper Than a preprinted card with a signature only Than a text on a screen with a 'smiley face' We can forward images, sounds, and words on a screen But we can't forward ourselves We can't forward that hug or touch that means so much There's a feeling we get when we see little ones playing When we see a child hug an adult, as only a child can Unafraid to show their feelings, their caring, their love Somehow, as we become adults, that impulse get lost Or it becomes buried and hidden, but it's always there There is a yearning that abides within each one of us Contained within every human life, every human soul Regardless of race, regardless of gender, regardless of belief That yearning is to be connected, to matter, to be of value Heartache happens mostly because of people And a heart only mends through people as well Life is too short to let those moments slip by Those moments of the ones that cross our minds There is nothing more important than all relationships Whether fleeting or strong, they keep us connected Connected to our yearnings For ourselves and to others So, please don't wait Make that call, keep that promise Please

George G
