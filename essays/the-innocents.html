THE INNOCENTS

By George Gondron

THE INNOCENTS Who are they, the innocents? The young who are defenseless, dependent upon others Perhaps the unborn who will never know life as you and I Those are the obvious ones What about the not so obvious innocents? The single mom who depends upon the honesty of others For her needs, as auto or home repairs Or the young adolescent who trusts the teacher, the preacher The priest or the pastor, and lose their innocence How about the elderly? Who without question accept the doctor's diagnosis The 'pill-pushers' for big Pharm They become simply a number of the thousands who die Die from over medication or wrong medication, the innocent Or maybe those who have lost their jobs Only because the entity they worked for wanted more profit Let's go overseas, labor is cheaper, taxes are fewer These unemployed then spend their savings, lose their homes Forced to seek welfare in order to survive, but they were innocent And then there are the reluctant warriors Those who are sent into harm's way at the behest of others Mostly for selfish reasons, again money, power, control Losing their lives, maimed for their country, body and soul Returning home fragile and broken and abandoned Thank you for your sacrifice, but we can't help you now The innocent are those who have no right of decision Their decisions are made for them, or taken from them Made by others, controlled by others, decided by others I wonder, how many innocent will continue to suffer Through decisions made framed in profit, power, and desire Based on dividends for the stockholders Bonuses for the CEO's, corporate perks for the few The innocent will continue to suffer until Until people are more important than 'things' Than money, than power, than control, than desire Perhaps it will never end, perhaps it will I hope that it does, I shall pray that it does

George G
