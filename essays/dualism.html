DUALISM

By George Gondron | Source: DUALISM.docx

DUALISM We live in a society of polar opposites As witnessed by our thoughts, feelings, and actions Right/wrong, good/bad, liberal/conservative, Openminded/closeminded, givers/takers, etc., etc., etc. And then there are the ‘winners and losers’ Which ironically, in a dualistic approach to life is a fantasy Ultimately there are no winners, only survivors On the other hand, a pluralistic society is one in which There is a diversity of ethnicities, races, religions, And social groups maintaining and supporting one another Within the confines of their culture, unity among diversity When examined objectively, there are enough resources Of all gradations, of all types, of all sources to provide The individuals of our society with all the necessary requirements Needed to fulfill their health, happiness, and wellbeing But it’s not even a close reality toward equality and freedom In addition to a polarized mind what other attributes Add to the bias and discrimination that plainly exist If we’re honest, I’m sure we can all think of a few I’ll offer two; you can reflect on your own reasons My two – maleness and whiteness Historically, prove me wrong -if we’re willing to see clearly History supports my premise When there is a connection between mind, heart, soul, and spirit Plurality succeeds, dualism has not and never will It remains me against you, us against them A dualistic mind is anti as opposed to pro Within the dualistic mind, a threat always lingers Always a part of the problem rather than the solution We can’t change others but we can change ourselves Embrace a few minutes of silence, stillness, with Presence Each day – a meditative/contemplative practice Awaken the innate core given at birth that is your true self Empty your busy mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They have your answers, they hold your truth, they wait

George G
