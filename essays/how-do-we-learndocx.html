HOW DO WE LEARN

By George Gondron

HOW DO WE LEARN? To ride a bike, play a sport, drive a vehicle, sail a boat, Be a spouse, a parent, have relationships, perform a job Only one way teaches, experience, hands on learning We can read a book, take a class, listen to a lecture But genuine learning is always in the doing, the failing, And hopefully the succeeding The simple definition of ignorance is lack of knowledge and learning Ignorance remains ignorance if its source of information Is opinion, ‘gaslighting’, third party information, gossip, etc. I’ll clean this next comment up There are two things we all have ‘navels’ and opinions The first is always true, the second, seldom In a democracy, we have legitimate power in one of three ways Equal justice under the law, the right to vote, and collective bargaining If we are not ignorant we can easily recall when all three have failed Lack of financial means for legal aid, restricted voting rights And unlimited money thrown at ‘busting’ unions The saying, ‘power corrupts and absolute power corrupts absolutely’ Is alive and well, thanks to ignorance The character, or lack thereof, between the two presidential Candidates should be evidence enough for a valid choice If opinion is your support for your choice, then you sully And defile your sacred right to vote, opinion is not evidence If you are a ‘one issue’ voter, you are a problem Unable to see the forest for the trees Unable to be concerned about our children and grandchildren Be a critical thinker, do your own research, question, doubt Alternative facts are not facts, half-truths are not truth Use your intelligence, don’t he ‘hooked’ by your emotions Don’t be the one who says, ‘I just don’t like her’ If you are willing to examine the failures and accomplishments Of both candidates, the choice is clear, make the clear choice Choose actual learning over the ‘Kool Aid’ flavor of the day Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for important balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your mind, listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
