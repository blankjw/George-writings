CROSSROADS

By George Gondron

CROSSROADS It has become abundantly clear that Christianity Is at a crossroads At least as it pretends to be a practice of what the human Jesus taught as it relates to all humanity The stark differences between practice and preaching Are too obvious to ignore Something along the following narrative ‘Don’t do as I do, do as I say you do’ The phrase, ‘In name only’ is very applicable When it comes to many who call themselves Christian Christianity as it’s practiced in this society Is not a unifying concept, it is very divisive Very judgmental, very condemning, and very critical As the great German theologian Meister Eckhart once said ‘I pray God to rid me of God’ The danger of White far-right Christian Nationalists Is too obvious to ignore If you listen to their rhetoric it’s about separation, not unity One would think they have a ‘straight line to God’ Their narrative is about fear, not about love and inclusion The message they proclaim through their megaphones Is the exact opposite of the human Jesus without a doubt What sells to the uneducated mind is fear, not love And these fundamentalists are very good at spreading fear Fear by any other name, is still fear The great stumbling block to the human condition is ignorance The refusal to learn and the focus on the negative Sadly, since religion became an organized institution It became about worship, rules, rites, rituals, and doctrine The main point, relationship, got lost in the translation The teachings of the human Jesus have become bastardized Replaced by the need for male power and control Over the years millions have suffered and died as a result Choose a meditative/contemplative daily practice Experience a few minutes of silence, be still, embrace Presence Awaken your innate inner resources, discover your true self Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
