LOOK, LISTEN, LIVE, LOVE

By George Gondron | Source: LOOK, LISTEN, LIVE, LOVE.docx

LOOK, LISTEN, LIVE, LOVE Small words, difficult concepts As an example - 'There are none so blind as those who will not see' 'There are none so deaf as those who will not hear' 'Let the dead bury their dead' 'Love one another as I have loved you' The human Jesus Or - 'The world is blind! There are so few who see things as they truly are' ' Do not go upon what has been acquired by repeated hearing' ' Without health life is not life; it is an image of death' ' If you truly love yourself, you could never hurt another' The human Buddah And yet another - 'How jaded have we become to turn a blind eye to the suffering around us?' ' Whoever shuts their ears to the cry of the poor, they also shall cry, but will not be heard' ' One of the greatest diseases is to be nobody to anybody' ' Love is a fruit in season at all times' The human Mother Teresa We look, but we do not see, blinded by marketing, advertising, and fantasy We hear, but not the small whisper, overwhelmed by constant noise, inside and out We breathe, but we are asleep, not awake to life We trade happiness for constant gratification and call it love Without peace within, how can there be peace without? Without concern for the one who goes hungry, how can there be concern for the millions that starve? How can we look, listen, live, and love? How can we? How?

George G
