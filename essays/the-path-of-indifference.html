THE PATH OF INDIFFERENCE

By George Gondron

THE PATH OF INDIFFERENCE Of all the paths we can travel in life The most dangerous is the path of indifference The opposite of love is not hate It is indifference, apathy, unconcern People of indifference are much more dangerous Than those of strong feeling, positive or negative For their attention to life, to security, to safety Revolves around their small existence, their small group With feigned concern for those unlike themselves And as a result they are always a part of the problem And not a part of the solution Their religious beliefs manifest as external behavior As specified by the rituals of their belief system Beyond the external appearances, it's nothing more than passivity Which is an attribute of indifference, passivity People of indifference have lots of opinions, with little action Few based on personal experience, and most very critical And none concerning their own behavior, or lack thereof They are 'finger-pointers' who have done nothing on their own But quick to blame others for their ideas of what's wrong And of course, the person of indifference is never wrong They are extremely adept with the use of defense mechanisms Able to justify all of their actions The path of indifference never ends well It will eventually lead to loss of freedom and justice For those who don't care, who are indifferent Will be controlled by those who do care, but for the wrong reasons And suddenly the indifferent who where once the insiders Have become the outsiders, now looking in Indifference never has a positive end Never results in a healthy solution, for the few or the many I have, you have, we all have been indifferent at times Don't let indifference become a routine, or your habitual normal It may not be us, but someone will pay for our indifference It may be a child, a grandchild, or just the stranger Don't let indifference lead to the misfortune of anyone Or it soon may be us

George G
