SLOW LEARNERS

By George Gondron | Source: SLOW LEARNERS.docx

SLOW LEARNERS We are you know – slow learners There are many reasons this is so The greatest of which, we don’t like change If change is necessary for our well-being Then it must be someone else that needs to change It’s so much easier to cling to what’s familiar Even when that familiar may be unpleasant After all, we can avoid emotional pain quite easily Take a pill, find another distraction, zone out Learning for most people after formal education Is not learning at all It’s merely absorption We take in what’s offered and either accept it or reject it Maybe that’s why the literacy level of 54% of adults is only Sixth grade or lower equivalent, frightening Another frightening statistic, 20% of adults can’t read or write Yep, we are slow learners And we’ve been warned As the saying goes, ‘Don’t believe what you hear And only half of what you see’ It seems we don’t heed that warning Following the social media outlet of our choice Primarily the one that stirs our emotions So if we’re not using our intellect to be critical thinkers To not be self-reflective and expand our awareness To become more conscious, connected to our heart, Soul, and spirit, our internal core How can we possibly have a clear perception? An accurate response requires a clear perception Not a ‘knee jerk’ reaction Choose a meditative/contemplative path For just a few minutes each day, be silent be still, Be in the Presence of your Image of Isness, of God Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold the answers to all your questions They hold your innate true self, not the egoic self They hold your truth, they patiently await

George G
