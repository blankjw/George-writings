PLEASE^J NEVER - PLEASE^J A;LWAYS

By George Gondron

PLEASE, NEVER – PLEASE, ALWAYS If you have a halfway decent memory You may recall quite easily, those voices that are clearly Saying, please never do that, or always do this What follows those requests are endless scenarios of Do’s and don’t’s, hopefully, most of which were ignored For who you and I became and are still becoming At least some of us, is the result of our personal experiences Our failures and successes, our bad decisions and our good ones Sadly, there are far too many who still as adults Live by and through the ‘shoulds’ of others Whether that other is a single individual, a group, a belief system Or the ever-popular’ third party’ narrative, including social media What we don’t realize or reflect on is the unspoken message We are telling ourselves by following what he, she, they, or it said That unspoken message is, they know more than me They are smarter than me, I trust them more than myself Especially if they use fear and doubt to promote their rhetoric To promote and encourage self-development is vastly More important than third-party information or social media Especially the development of our own values, ethics, and morals Our guide to rightness and wrongness, to good or bad To discern the truth from the lie, is never our ego It is the inner guides we all have, heart, soul, and spirit As the fox tells the Little Prince, ‘It is only with the heart That one sees rightly’ Life is not just about our thoughts, our too many words It is more importantly about balance, about a connection To our inner core, our internal references, our very essence To embrace a meditative/contemplative practice Is to awaken our inner guides so needed for balance To take a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence To empty our ‘monkey minds’ of their incessant and repetitive Thoughts – to learn how to be and not just do To trust our own innate abilities that promote our wholeness If we but take the time, we are the energy of the universe Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
