ESSAY

BY EXAMPLE

By George Gondron

BY EXAMPLE Early learning is a process that is accomplished Through the examples that influence our lives In other words, it’s not the words we hear Nor the verbal teachings or what we read That has the greatest impact on our character It is rather what we observe, what we witness Which ironically defeats the axiom of ‘Don’t do as I do, do as I say do’ So, it’s not until we begin to develop An individual sense of awareness and consciousness That we start to learn through our own experiences As opposed to learning by example If we don’t develop a healthy sense of ‘self’ Learning by example now applies to not only What we witness but what we hear as well We fail to develop our own sense of reality Our own reality of truth and belief We are still leaning on the narrative that attracts Our attention, especially through our emotions For example, ‘well it’s on the internet so it must be true’ There are thousands of other examples that are similar All beginning with ‘so and so said’ There is an internal reference within each human being That holds the ground of all truth That also contains the important ethics, virtues, and morality Contained in the crucible of unconditional love We have the opportunity still, as adults, to learn by example Reflecting on the behavior of current and past teachers of Spirituality, of positive religion, and human relationships And if we’re truly interested Interested to access the internal resources mentioned above There is a simple path A daily meditative/contemplative practice A process of emptying the mind of its incessant and Repetitive daily thoughts, clearing our ‘monkey minds’ That we might hear the whispers of our heart and soul That we might experience the Ground of All truth

George G
