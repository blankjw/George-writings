WHO ARE WE

By George Gondron | Source: WHO ARE WE.docx

WHO ARE WE? An impossible question to answer, who What are we; much easier to answer Flesh and bone, conscious; some of us Able to think and feel and sense Being able however doesn't mean we do so A few are more aware than most Most are asleep and think they're awake Human beings with free will acting like robots Free to love, free to harm, free to chose Free to label, separate, discriminate, and question With that said, we still don't know who we are Why are we? I assume that answer turns upon what you believe Notice, I said believe, not know For few know why they are, really Assuming it's the result of a physical act called sex And that's not totally incorrect, just partially correct But for some the why is much deeper For some we have been in the mind of a Creator Long before we were born Refusing to believe we're just accidents Which only comes from knowing But, back to who are we, there is no pat answer Our who is a becoming, a process, a journey A discovery of the gift of our uniqueness Moment by moment, day by day, year by year Who we are could easily be defined as, we are love For we come from Love, and will return to Love We come from silence, from stillness, from Presence We come from what cannot be defined, ever But only experienced in the mystery of our who-ness

George G
