NO DOEVER'S

By George Gondron

DO OVER’S Unfortunately, in life there are no do-over’s What we do when we do it, can’t be undone What we say when we say it, can’t be unsaid And if we’re honest, we can all say to ourselves ‘I wish’ - you can fill in the rest Too often, too late we realize the consequences Of our actions, or perhaps our inaction A life without regret is not a life, it’s a lie It’s pretense in its worst form, its strongest denial We all have our ‘shoulda, coulda, woulda’ moments Our attempts to justify what we’d like to do over But in truth they seem to fit at the time After all, you can’t know what you don’t know The toughest ones to get over are the ones When we ignored that quiet inner voce Warning us ahead of time, ‘hey, are you sure about this?’ Yeah, you know the ones I’m talking about So, when those tough decisions come around Those choices that may seem a little ‘iffy’ Maybe take a little extra time to reflect, question, doubt Include the input from your heart, soul, and spirit as well Making decisions with input from the ego alone Is an indication of selfish and self-centered behavior You know, ‘my way or the highway’ mentality Even with an open mind, deep reflection, and critical thinking We will still occasionally make decisions we may regret later But they will be far fewer and not as harmful to others To give yourself the best possible opportunities For wise choices, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, & Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They have your answers, hold your true self, they wait

George G
