DEFENSIVE TO DEFENSELESS

By George Gondron | Source: DEFENSIVE TO DEFENSELESS.docx

DEFENSIVE TO DEFENSELESS Exclusive of physically defending oneself There are currently, in psychological parlance Fifteen amazing emotional defense mechanisms Denial, regression, acting out, dissociation, projection Compartmentalization, reaction formation, sublimation Compensation, repression, displacement, undoing Intellectualization, assertiveness, and rationalization All easily 'googled' for a precise clear definition A veritable panacea of ways to defend, emotionally The minds deliverance to safety, for the helpless And for the not-so-helpless, an evasion, an escape An avoidance of and for the false-self, the encased ego The mind that refuses to let go, to see another view The mind that offers innumerable excuses as a buffer Countless justifications for being 'right' and not 'wrong' But it is not until the mind ceases defending Until the inflated ego is forced to take a 'back seat' That change can hope to have a possible chance Remember, the last bastion of the ego is security Safe within its own limited, small worldview On every topic, on every agenda, all easily defended To the defended, there is only one view, their own And for them, their stance makes perfect sense For they are protecting their ego, their sense of 'I' But, to discover the true-self, the intrinsic connection One must be open to change, to the different To be willing to become undefended and awake To become aware of what already and always exists To be open to eternal, infinite Love To become defenseless, to become wounded Emotionally, by Love

George G
