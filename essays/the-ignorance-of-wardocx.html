THE IGNORANCE OF WAR

By George Gondron

THE IGNORANCE OF WAR There are essentially only a few reasons for war My God is the right God – self righteousness I want what you have – greed and selfishness I am more important than you – my rights exceed your rights All war falls within those three parameters And no matter the reason, there are no winners And the ones that suffer the most are the innocent We send the innocent to die and call it virtuous It is not a mystery that the danger to all life is committed By us, the human being and in particular, the male of the species What is the antidote to war? It has to be, it must be, a change in human consciousness And therein lies the problem for it seems, at least until now That there is nothing that can change group consciousness That task is the responsibility for each individual, each of us If war is the chosen alternative to ‘waking up’ To an increase in awareness and consciousness then our Dysfunction, our continued reasons for conflict will remain Since the beginning of time, war has become our familiar Where do we receive encouragement to grow psychologically, Intellectually, emotionally, and spirituality? Certainly not social media, and less and less educationally It comes back to, and will always be, individual transformation In an environment of negativity, fear, and ignorance Where do we discover kindness, compassion, and love? ‘Omnia vincit amor’, a phrase from Roman times, love conquers all When there is a disconnect between head and heart When we no longer listen to our own inner core Our power to discern and intuit, love lies dormant War is always the result of a deeper problem, never the solution Part of the solution is to learn to love ourselves, beyond what Can be described as selfish love, egoic love, but a humble love A meditative/contemplative daily practice is a path of discovery To discover what we innately already possess Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, they wait

George G
