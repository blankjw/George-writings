ESSAY

TO KNOW

By George Gondron

TO KNOW I wonder , if we spoke only of what we know Would there be less conflict? Less confusion, less noise What is the mind's attraction to believe something it does not know? Is it to appear to be more than I am? The ego's way of importance and inflation Or maybe it's to fit in with those that are attractive Those that seem to be more knowledgeable Those whose inner conflicts remain hidden behind their masks of virtue and righteousness I sense that the way of 'knowing' is a whisper that arises within me A whisper that gently tugs, 'trust your experience' Trust your senses, they will not deceive you The mind is a wonderful gift and a deadly disguise Left to the whims of the ego it becomes a harsh and solitary judge Separating and dividing Left to the whispers of the heart it is compassion, kindness, and love Unity among diversity The mystery lies in knowing I am both My prayer is to trust the whispers Real knowledge comes from experience I trust what I know Until a new 'knowing' arises from a new experience I trust

George G
