LIVING ON CREDIT

By George Gondron

LIVING ON CREDIT What does it mean, to live on credit? To postpone the inevitable To believe that temporary gratification, Is better than long-term stability We live in a credit culture What's important is now, not tomorrow But sadly, the credit culture of finance, of money Spills over into the rest of our lives We begin to live on credit in other areas of life Educationally, intellectually, emotionally, Relationally, matrimonially, spiritually Living on credit is always about compromise Always about the expedient, about me That's really what it means About me first, you second Us first, the others next All the while attempting to convince ourselves It's not about us at all, but it is We jeopardize all for a little credit We accommodate our education, ignore our intellect Bargain with our emotions, abuse our relationships We take our partners for granted, and our spirit Our spirit is appeased with sterile religion Eventually, payment will be made for our credit lives Not necessarily in money, but in other ways In the emptiness, the loneliness, the brokenness Payment is given, along with a blessing If the lesson that's learned is not forgotten That a credit life is not worth the living You know what is; it is within, it is between, it is among It is you know, and have always known, simply Love

George G
