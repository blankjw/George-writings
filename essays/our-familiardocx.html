OUR FAMILIAR

By George Gondron

OUR FAMILIAR Our familiar brings a certain level of comfort Even when that familiar may include some level of pain Primarily emotional pain Once that comfort level is established No amount of truth outside that comfort zone Has any meaning, we are closed to change We are impervious to the possible Unwilling to risk or be vulnerable Whatever the reward, real or imagined we cling to our familiar As the saying goes, ‘We’ll dance with the Devil we know, Rather than the one we don’t know’ As human beings, our greatest resistance is against change The prisons we build are self-made, they are personal Only we have the key that will set us free Once we can manage the hurdle of moving beyond our familiar We realize the risk is worth the reward It becomes easier to continue forward Rather than staying stuck, for after all being stuck Is nothing more than being in a rut The rut is like a grave with both ends open that we might escape To embrace life is to learn from our experiences The negative as well as the positive And if we refuse to learn one of two things will happen We will cling to our familiar or simply repeat the behavior That creates our discomfort over and over again When we have no connection to our own internal resources When the mind, heart, soul, and spirit are disconnected We simply continue to live external lives clinging to our familiar A never-ending cycle of ‘rinse and repeat’ A daily few minutes of silence, stillness, and Presence A meditative/contemplative practice is a change agent A catalyst to move us beyond our familiar, our stuckness A few minutes to empty our ever-busy ‘monkey minds’ Of their incessant repetitive thoughts That we might hear the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit Discover our true self as opposed to the egoic self Our inner guides hold the answers, hold our truth, they wait

George G
