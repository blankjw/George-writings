WHAT IS LOVE

By George Gondron

WHAT IS LOVE? It’s easier to say what love is not than what love is Hopefully you’ll read to the end of this reflection and I’ll give you my thoughts on genuine love To demean love to the status of an emotion we become guilty of depreciating its true purpose We love the meal, the movie, the book, the activity, the person, etc. These are all feelings such as gratitude, satisfaction, fulfillment, pleasure, excitement, joy, appreciation, etc., etc., etc. They are not love The Greeks have seven words for love Eros, passionate love – Philia, affectionate love – Storge, familia love Agape, universal love – Ludus, playful love – Pragma, everlasting love Philautia, self love – all of which require some form of action regarding self or other/s When the definition of love is limited to a feeling, when that feeling is absent does that mean we no longer love The question of ‘why do you love me?’ is a trap For when the circumstances that define the ‘why’ are absent the assumption is you no longer love me The circumstances that surround the question of love for myself and other/s is easily revealed through actions, not feelings What we do or do not do, our actions, clearly demonstrate our love for all that is, especially other human beings We will know that we are loved by what others do, not what they say At the top of the list of what’s important in healthy relationships is congruent communication, active versus passive listening, something few people know how to do but something that can be learned Some of the simplest things we can do for ourselves become the hardest, only because of our own ego, our own illusion of ‘I’, of ’me’ Not until we engage in a practice of self-discovery will we ever find inner peace, the wisdom of doing nothing is to discover something Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, awaken your inner core Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, the ego will resist, the mind will wander, simply rinse and repeat, embrace nothingness Listen for the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
