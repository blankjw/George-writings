OUR PRISON

By George Gondron

OUR PRISON We all have one, a prison, a special cell Not made of brick and mortar Without steel bars, no swinging door There is only one jailor, ourselves The prison, our own mind, limits our possibilities The key to our release is in our grasp It is only our mind that holds us back With self-talk that's convincing, convincing us to Cast aside our dreams, our hopes, our yearnings The mind reiterates we're not good enough The possible goal is too risky, bound to fail Best to keep to the normal, repeat the usual We're not smart enough, talented enough Never enough to reach the hope, or the goal Within each of us is a special gift For that, we were given this life An artist, a writer, a dancer, or singer A sculptor or composer, a tinker or tailor A scientist, a farmer, an educator, or inventor The possibilities are endless, yet not pursued We instead are taught to follow the program The program of what some call success But who writes the program and defines success? It's from the prisons of others who follow the crowd The mind alone is never the answer The mind alone doesn't even know the questions It is with the heart, the soul, and the spirit That our gifts can be found, our dreams discovered If we listen to these, our goals will change We will use our gifts, pursue our dreams And at last, be freed from our prison

George G
