PAIN OR POSSIBILITY

By George Gondron

PAIN OR POSSIBILITY A truth; pain is unavoidable Whether physical, emotional, or spiritual One can learn to live with physical pain But emotional pain, through our attachment Lives us, controls us, cripples us Spiritual pain is the feeling of unfulfilled meaning or yearnings Which most of us manage to push away, through distraction Or through external behavior and precepts that avoid connection Our emotions are meant to be our teachers, not our enemies A blessing not a curse, a part of being fully human When the human condition is left to the mind alone Peace fails, justice is discriminatory, and exclusion prevails Pain is in a true sense, a gift All of us have had and do experience emotional pain We do so in the conscious awareness of suffering Clueless that's it's possible to experience emotional pain Without suffering, without drowning in what seems to be Unending emotional turmoil The fact is that one thing only clings to the pain The mind, in its inability to see clearly, respond accurately The mind would rather be miserable in its perceived pain Than become healthy through the possibility of change The reality that the pain is a teacher, is not on our agenda Almost always, the first reaction to emotional pain is blame Not, what is this teaching me about myself, or about others Spiritual pain is quiet simply lack of a relational connection A connection to the Source in which I claim to believe A connection substituted through rites, rituals, dogma, and doctrine Not through the needed silence, stillness, and Presence of life Whispers cannot be heard through the noise Presence cannot be felt among the distractions Pain offers us endless possibilities Possibilities of potential growth and transformation Away from the conditioning and programming of the unreal Toward the compassion and loving kindness of the Real The path of pain always offers an option Either move forward or remain stuck, the choice is ours

George G
