CROSSROADS 2

By George Gondron

CROSSROADS Intersections and turning points of life What appear to be only a few, ultimately countless With limited vision, wrapped securely in our safety A safety of nothing more than a self-imposed prison A prison of existence, of survival, of continuity Did I miss a turn somewhere along the way? A turn that might have led to life, not just existence? As I risk to reflect As I chance to ponder As I venture to consider Small whispers in the recesses of my mind tug gently I sense the small whisper carries great meaning I grasp for the import of that small whisper Another voice, much louder, says 'ignore the whisper' It has no value, it is a mere distraction A crossroad A choice point A chance, perhaps for life, not just existence It often is the ignored, the missed, the overlooked that carry confirming meaning That carry what the noise attempts to drown out The smallness of life, the whisper, awaits our attention I reject the loud voice I choose the small, the whisper I recognize a new crossroad A crossroad of Life and not just an existence

George G
