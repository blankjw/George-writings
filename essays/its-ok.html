IT'S OK

By George Gondron

IT’S OK It’s Ok to remember those experiences in our lives That had a significant meaning at the time Whether of joy or pain, of success or failure The impact of those events forever changes who we are They helped shaped us regardless Of the memory, of the occasion, of the event They are a part of our history, a history that includes Tears of joy, tears of pain, and they all matter We do harm to ourselves when we deny our experiences When we can’t ask ourselves, what have I learned Especially about myself, when we reflect on the past When we deny the experiences ever happened One of the most difficult exercises around the issue Of broken relationships, is objectivity The courage to ask ourselves, what did I do wrong If we can’t learn from our mistakes, we repeat them There is a saying, ‘you can’t know what you don’t know’ And many of our past experiences were simply because We didn’t know how to be or, how to do We take a lot for granted, especially in our relationships But we seem to grow too soon old and too late smart When it comes to relationships With ourselves and with others In some of life’s experiences, there are no do-overs I wish there were For sure most of us are willing to admit If I had it to do over again, I would be more present I would be different, at least I hope so To blame ourselves or blame others is senseless But to learn is of great significance, what did I learn? If we’re willing to sit in silence, in stillness, with Presence A few minutes each day to empty our ever-busy ‘monkey minds’ With their incessant and repetitive thoughts that are wasted energy We may discover in those quiet moments that our hearts, Souls, and spirits whisper truth we’ve not been able to hear In our busyness, our outward focus, take time to listen Take time to heal, take time to learn, take time to be

George G
