CHOOSE

By George Gondron

CHOOSE The old saying, ‘you can pay me now or pay me later’ is certainly applicable to the process of change Change, like time, cannot be stopped but it can be chosen We contribute to our own dysfunction, our own closemindedness through personal choice, in no other way And we will use any and all means necessary to cling to those choices It’s so much easier to blame, point fingers, and use all the psychological defense mechanisms to prevent us from owning our own choices As human beings we possess no greater gift than that of free will, yet we will seldom, if ever, acknowledge that it is given with no strings attached, no payment required Every hurdle we encounter in our individual journey to become more fully human is a gift, a teacher, and it’s up to each of us to learn the lessons presented, not to bury our heads in the sand When, where, why, what, and how we choose matters Few there are that reflect upon the importance of their choices Many there are that choose more as a reactor than a responder In our culture, most of our choices are more likely to be influenced by our emotions than our intellect More likely to be influenced by what’s familiar, even if that familiar is painful, we don’t like change Generally, the Western mind, the ego, abhors silence or inactivity Production and consumption are the norm in our society Yet, to become healthy, to be balanced, inner and outer, some form of practice is necessary which the ego will resist with vigor A meditative/contemplative practice is a wonderful example Simple to follow, virtually impossible to do for the Western mind Just a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Presence being your image or idea of a benevolent Creator Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out An example for a mantra – breathe in wellness to yourself, breathe out wellness to the universe The ego will resist, simply rinse and repeat, repeatedly Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait We are all stardust, we all carry a Divine spark, we are connected

George G
