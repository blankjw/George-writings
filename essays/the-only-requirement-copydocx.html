THE ONLY REQUIREMENT - Copy

By George Gondron

THE ONLY REQUIREMENT There is only one requirement for becoming Becoming aware, conscious, awake, fully human - do the work And if we embrace that task deliberately, consciously We soon discover, it’s not easy For it necessitates that we drop our defenses Acknowledge we don’t have all the answers Take off our masks, our personas, our disguises Grapple with our weaknesses, our illusions, our need to be right Especially confront our own ego that resists change The ‘great pretender’, a mind that is closed and judgmental A mind that formulates its beliefs and opinions not from Its own research, reflection, or critical thinking But from ‘third party’ information, social media, he/she said Too many have become ‘Kool-Aid’ drinkers, puppets on a string ‘Carrot on a stick’ followers – in a word, lazy Simply tell me what I need to know, ignorance is sublime Bigger is better, more is better, faster is better Turn on the TV, open the iPad or phone, and there’s the answer We are fools, we are our own worse enemy, we are not smart We are prisoners of our emotions abusing our greatest gift Our free will, our ability to choose, as we abandon all reason In exchange for being ‘spoon fed’, no need to question or doubt No need to fact check or do our own critical thinking or research Just accept what’s on the screen blindly, unconsciously I hope you care more about truth, about personal growth, About becoming a kind, compassionate, caring, sharing, loving Human being connected to all of creation, I hope We allow the opinions and beliefs of others, individuals and groups To poison and distort what is true, factual, and actual Part of becoming fully human is to connect with our own Inner core, our internal resources given at birth, they are there We need balance, inner and outer, mind and spirit Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Life is relationship not only with what we see but also with Mystery Focus on your breath or a short mantra, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
