WHAR'SHEAR TO SAY

By George Gondron | Source: WHAR'SHEAR TO SAY.docx

WHAT’S HARD TO SAY? If you have eyes to see and ears to hear You can easily recognize that we live in a culture That is very polarized, with many minds that are closed Just a few statements unable to be uttered by closed minds ‘I’m sorry, I don’t know, I’m willing to learn more, ‘Can you point me to information that is factual?’ ‘I apologize, please help me understand’ The danger is not just a closed mind, but a mind that doesn’t Want to know, they already have the answers In common vernacular, they drink the Kool Aid flavor of the day They live through their emotions and not their intellect They’re more ‘anti’ than ‘pro’, more against than for They’re quick to find fault, unsubstantiated fault They cannot determine the truth from the obvious lie Refuse to question or check out ridiculous exaggerations Discernment is a foreign idea to them, they’re ‘knee-jerkers’ They feed off of hate and division If you don’t think like they do, you are a problem They’re really not interested in what you have to say, ever What matters is only what they believe, never forget that They’re OK to call you a friend as long as certain subjects Are closed to discussion – is that really a friend? Is that someone you’re willing to trust with your deepest Thoughts, feelings, and experiences? True friends can agree to disagree as long as there is open discussion True friends are the exception, not the rule Unfortunately, humility is not the hallmark of most Americans But rather, ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ When we say the ‘hard’ things, we risk, we become vulnerable But that’s the only way we grow, the only way we change The only way we become more aware and conscious Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Breathe in, breathe out, your mind will wander, rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, be discerning They hold your true self, have your answers, and they wait

George G
