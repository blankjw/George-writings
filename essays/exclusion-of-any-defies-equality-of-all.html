EXCLUSION OF ANY DEFIES EQUALITY OF ALL

By George Gondron

EXCLUSION OF ANY DEFIES EQUALITY FOR ALL Where people are excluded equality is absent, always Personal growth and change happen on the edges On the fringes, the boundaries of assumed acceptability Without tension, or a little anxiety, nothing changes Our level of awareness and consciousness remains the same As a result the level of ignorance in a culture where Factual information is readily available to all, but not embraced Remains constant and unwavering, blind leading the blind The faults we refuse to see in ourselves, we see in others They carry our shadow for us as we remain unblemished Our tendencies to judge others and not ourselves Creates a wide gap in equality for all without exception In our society, the norm is blame versus understanding We each profess to belong to the ‘right’ group Which of course is the hallmark of dualistic polarized thinking When we only see opposites, right/wrong, good/bad, Just/unjust, like/dislike without full comprehension We will never understand life is never either/or It is always both/and A closed mind is always the most dangerous weapon It has no concept of situational ethics, it is condemning It is either far left or far right with no middle ground It is a mind that does not care to explore the truth Nor to question, doubt, or use critical thinking For, in fact, it thinks with its emotions, not its intellect It would rather divide than unite, blame than accept Exclude rather than include, preach rather than listen In fact, it already has the answer to any question before it’s asked What’s lacking between far left and far right is balance A connection between head and heart, comprehensive inclusion A path to inclusion is a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence A time for relationship empty of our too many words Time to awaken our internal resources Time to become aware of our inner yearnings To listen to the whispers of our hearts, soul, and spirit To discover our true self, and find our truth, they patiently await

George G
