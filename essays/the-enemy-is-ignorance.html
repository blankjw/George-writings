THE ENEMY IS IGNORANCE

By George Gondron | Source: THE ENEMY IS IGNORANCE.docx

THE ENEMY IS IGNORANCE It’s truly amazing how so many know so little Supporting the phrase, ‘none so blind as those who will not see And none so deaf as those who will not hear’ And at the same time there are more than enough resources Available today to educate ourselves and seek the truth It has become obvious, people don’t want the truth They much prefer to be ‘spoon fed’ their favorite nourishment Which is nothing more than words that stir their emotions Blaming he, she, it, or them for their ill-conceived thoughts The easy path taken never ends well But then again, it’s the path that requires no deep reflection No honest self-criticism, little doubt, little questioning, And no research or examination at all – easy A mind-set of, please don’t confuse me with the facts A closed mind is an ignorant mind, a dangerous mind It survives on negative versus positive energy On fear, doubt, exclusion, and defensiveness Rather than love, inclusion, acceptance, and vulnerability Pogo is right, ‘We have met the enemy, and it is us’ In all of nature, humans have the greatest potential for Creativity, for care, compassion, empathy, and sacrifice As well we have the greatest potential for destruction and chaos It’s interesting to note that those who pass judgment Do so with only half the truth at best, the half they prefer It’s really easy to know if someone has an open mind Is willing to listen without replying to every comment with Words such as, ‘well what about, yes but’, or refuses to Acknowledge proven facts as if they didn’t exist Facts, proof, easily supported don’t matter to ignorance Ergo the statement, ‘willing to cut off their nose in spite of their face’ They live through their emotions, not their intellect They are motivated through fear, not through love Please, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, in stillness, with Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
