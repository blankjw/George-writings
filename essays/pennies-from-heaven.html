PENNIES FROM HEAVEN

By George Gondron | Source: PENNIES FROM HEAVEN.docx

PENNIES FROM HEAVEN For every positive thought, for every positive feeling I receive a gift of a penny For every negative thought, for every negative feeling I lose a penny How wealthy, or perhaps, how poor am I? Is my bank account overdrawn? Or do I have savings? A story Two children, one from a wealthy family The other from poverty It's their birthday today, both are excited Behind door A lies a surprise for the wealthy child Behind door B, a surprise for the child in poverty Room A reveals a pile of beautifully wrapped gifts The wealthy child rips of the wrapping to the last one Exclaiming in an angry voice, 'what I want is not here!' Room B reveals only a floor covered with horse manure The child in poverty is ecstatic! Shouting, 'there has to be a pony under all this crap!' What is wealth? Perhaps it's not measured through material goods Perhaps it's seen only through the eyes of love Perhaps my bank account grows the more I love Less the more I complain, judge, and criticize Perhaps my wealth is carried within my own heart Perhaps it's the 'pearl of great price' hidden in the field Once discovered, never to be relinquished Search for your true wealth, your 'pearl of great price'
