RELIGIOUS BELIEF

By George Gondron

RELIGIOUS BELIEF Let’s begin with the obvious that’s not so obvious When any belief system moves from its original Basic tenants to a more complicated system of Dogma and doctrine, it fails If one is always attempting to show how their belief system Is different, better, and more inclusive than others The approach is divisive, not unitive Compare, contrast, and contend ignores cooperation Any philosophical or religious belief system that separates And divides is not a system that follows kindness, Compassion, peace, equality, and most importantly, love In a system that compares the ideals of caring and sharing Only internally, lives in a closed system In a networked world, how do you gain credibility If you listen to only one source If you are closed to any input different than your own It’s a clear way of saying, ‘I am and right and you are wrong’ A pattern repeated throughout recorded history To be at home in one’s own religious belief is a relationship Question, a connection with an originating Source Called by many names and not a communal source Who we become as a person is an individual responsibility Just as who we are in relationship, with our image of a Creator source is singular, one on one We know one another through our individual relationships Which is the only way to know another Most belief systems have become entertainment focused Rather than a focus on internal transformation We are born with resources we never explore, never experience We have the freedom to choose to become more Choose to spend a few minutes each day in contemplation Only in silence do we learn to listen Only in stillness do we learn to be present Only in the Presence do we become spiritual Only in caring and sharing do we learn to receive The only belief that matters is the one discovered from Personal experience, don’t focus on the finger pointing at the moon

George G
