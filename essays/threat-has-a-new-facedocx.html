THREAT HAS A NEW FACE

By George Gondron

THREAT HAS A NEW FACE Throughout history threat has been used as a means of control And the threat is always in the form of some kind of punishment Religion is a perfect example of control through threat To burn in ‘hell’ forever if you fall short of the ‘mark’ is the Christian threat, the other mainstream religions have theirs As well, follow the rules or suffer the consequences In our own nation’s history, it’s easy to support the fact that if You were anything other than a straight white male you were Subjected to some form of punishment if you didn’t comply With the ‘norm’, or became a ‘troublemaker’ by their definition As a result, people of ‘color’, women, foreigners, handicapped, Native Americans, LGBTQ+, and disenfranchised have been and Still are subject to punishment through threat Oh, and let us not forget the poorly educated, they suffer also Thankfully, many of our laws are written to protect those who Failed to comply as defined by those in control The right to collective bargaining, child labor laws, the Suffrage Act, Women’s rights, Civil Rights Act, Voting Rights Act, antidiscrimination Laws, anti-trust laws, handicap laws, EEOC legislation, etc., etc., etc. And now in our current political environment we have threats from Would be office holders to be punished if we don’t comply with Their wishes, you know small things like withholding aid if you’re Associated with the opposing party, or perhaps let’s eliminate the Department of Education for after all educated people are a threat Or let’s alienate our allies and isolate ourselves, living in a vacuum Success through deceit versus success through integrity matters The primary initiative of threat and control is separate and divide It is not freedom, justice, and equality for all, but only the few When the seeker of a political office operates from a position of ‘It’s all about me and not about you’ be very concerned We live in perilous times, please educate yourselves, use critical Thinking, question, doubt, research, don’t drink the Kool Aid Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life Spend a few minutes in silence, stillness, with Isness Empty your ‘monkey mind’, focus on your breath or a brief mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Your inner guides, they hold your true self, they have your answers

George G
