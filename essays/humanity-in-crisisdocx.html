HUMANITY IN CRISIS

By George Gondron

HUMANITY IN CRISIS One word fragments human consciousness, the human psyche The desire for more, always wanting more Whether it’s in the realm of the physical, the intellectual, The emotional or the spiritual It’s about acquiring, getting, hoarding, and controlling And the desire is so strong that evidence to the contrary Reveals that enough is never enough As a result of this ‘more’ mentality, humanity suffers The ‘haves’ turn a blind eye to the ‘have nots’ The ‘haves’ are not only individuals But also groups of a social, political, economic, religious, Educational, ethnic, or gender ilk You know, ‘birds of a feather’ sort of thing When did sacristy become such a motivator for more? When did ‘getting’ become more necessary than ‘giving’? When did selfishness become more the rule than the exception? The common connection to the above three questions Fear, what happened to ‘it’s better to give than receive’? What happened to ‘I am my brother/sister's keeper’? Put religion aside, it’s not about religion It’s about seeing sameness before difference Wanting more, fearing there’s not enough, blinds Love, generosity, charity, and kindness, are clear-sighted There are enough resources in nature and financially To feed, clothe, provide education, housing, and medical care To every human being on the planet, but I, we, us, they want more, always more Would that that there could be a paradigm shift in consciousness Worldwide, but alas, it seems to happen one person at a time We all experience a ‘lack of’, a lack of connection To our own inner core, our internal reference We live in our heads, our egos, our minds Ignoring the innate truth we hold within Embrace a daily meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes in silence, stillness, in the presence of Isness Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your original self, your truth, they patiently wait

George G
