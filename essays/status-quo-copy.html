STATUS QUO - Copy

By George Gondron

STATUS QUO Everything as it is at a point in time – status quo The problem lies with a status quo that never changes One that is static and not dynamic And when all remains the same, day after day Freedom becomes an inoperable word It has no significant meaning For we become trapped in the familiar Afraid to risk, to be vulnerable, to grow, to change Another word for freedom is liberation The first step toward liberation, toward freedom Is letting go For change to happen something must be given up And in most instances, it’s those thoughts and feelings That we cling to that are of no benefit whatsoever The routines and ‘ruts’ in our daily lives That have no meaningful value And as the saying goes, a ‘rut’ is a grave with both ends open We alone are responsible for what we think and feel No one else For example, if I say to you now, right now Don’t think of a pink elephant If you’re honest, you will, however briefly, do the opposite You will visualize a pink elephant So, what you think, and feel is always a matter of choice No one is holding a gun to your head, only you How we manage or do not manage our individual lives Is up to us We can remain in our ‘status quo’ or we can choose to change We can let go – we can seek balance in our lives I recommend a daily meditative/contemplative practice As a beginning point – and your ego will resist, guaranteed For a contemplative practice is about letting go A time to empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts A few minutes each day of silence, stillness, and Presence A time to awaken our better self, to heal what needs to be healed To listen to the small whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit They are your guides, they wait patiently

George G
