RELUCTANT BYSTANDERS

By George Gondron

RELUCTANT BYSTANDERS You know, that’s what most of us are, reluctant bystanders Outside our comfort zone, our small circle We seldom challenge the obvious dysfunctions of our culture Bury our heads in the sand, turning a blind eye, Don’t make waves, stay in your lane Denying to ourselves what is plain to see Making any number of excuses for our unwillingness To speak up and speak out All of which is bolstered by our own fear The greatest lie we will ever tell Is the one we tell ourselves as reluctant bystanders The imagined price to pay for not taking a risk Outside our comfort zone is too high, too frightening But the greater price is not speaking out at all Fear wins, love loses Do you ever notice that all arguments to maintain The status quo, both pro and con, are fear-based Not love based Not about inclusion but exclusion Not about unity but division and separation Not about understanding but demanding Denial, along with all the other psychological defenses Is simply a means of ignoring the truth, supporting the lie To change, become more aware and conscious Is a personal choice, an alternative to what is not working Without that alternative, the future becomes more fragile The slippery slope becomes steeper and more deadly If we remain, reluctant bystanders, we ignore with peril The outcome of our future for our children and grandchildren They deserve better, we are their role models Add a new daily practice to your life Spend a few minutes each day for, silence, stillness, and Presence Awaken your inner core given at birth, your truth The voice that stands for what’s best in your being Refuse to be a reluctant bystander Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They have your answers, they hold your ‘true self’, they wait

George G
