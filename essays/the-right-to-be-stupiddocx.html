THE RIGHT TO BE STUPID

By George Gondron

THE RIGHT TO BE STUPID Of all the 'rights' we acclaim in our land of freedom Gun rights, civil rights, voting rights (well for some) We also, it seems, have the right to be stupid Not because of some natural disorder or birth handicap But rather, a chosen stupidity We who choose stupidity are easily identifiable We are the ones that shout the longest and the loudest We are clueless about words such as compromise Conciliation, negotiation, nonpartisanship, and objectivity We don't know how to listen, but we know how to be 'blind' And we do know how to argue from our chosen stupidity We don't 'straddle the fence', right or left Our opinions are strong, though very shortsighted Liberal or conservative, sacred or secular We form our beliefs, for the most part, with few if any facts No wait, that's not right, we use other people's imagined facts We are the gullible people whose lives are grounded in fear Fear and insecurity, following the rhetoric of higher stupidity We are 'stirred up', 'inflamed' by those who manipulate Who play on our emotions and not our supposed intellect We love' propaganda' and 'dirt' on other people But ours remains hidden as others carry it for us Yes, we have the right to be stupid To 'wave the flag' and 'beat our chests' As if we have all the answers, before the questions are asked From our vast store of unlimited knowledge Our inexhaustible experience, just ask us, we'll tell you There is a saying, 'you can't fix stupid' But we who are stupid don't believe we need fixing For we are 'right' and everyone else is 'wrong' How did what's stupid become the norm for what's smart? I think I know, but I hate to admit I don't reflect very much or think critically at all I don't follow the trail that might lead to the facts I prefer to remain stupid and hope that in the end That my lack of awareness is not a just cause For all the dysfunction in this land of 'stupidity'

George G
