NOW

By George Gondron

NOW What is most significant, important, and meaningful Is always now, always Not in the past, nor the future But in the present moment For most, the present moment is nonexistent Except perhaps in moments of concentrated 'doing' Focused on a particular task, but even then Through repetition, routine, and habit we're usually absent Our job is already completed in our mind's eye And we're off to the next venture in our oh so busy day Even in our conversations our minds are not present to the other They are thinking ahead about how to respond or react And consequently we miss most of what's being said, if not all Seldom if ever are we in moments of just 'being' Simply present to the immediate sights and sounds Sincerely present to the person before us Really seeing the 'forest and the trees' Shutting out the distractions, being aware of what is, who is In the now moment Most of life is lived with and through repetitive thoughts In the past or the future, always missing the now And when we remember our happiest experiences Our fondest moments, our most precious memories They are always with an inexplicable clarity and simplicity For they are always about relationship About spontaneous behaviors of love, caring, and compassion That bring a warmth to our hearts, a smile on our lips A tear to our eyes Those kind of memories are only reminders of the importance The importance of living in the present moment, in the now The importance of connections, of relationships, of people When we are so preoccupied, nothing more than a human doing That we can't remember what happened just a few days ago It's only because we weren't present in the moment Life lived to the full is always in the present moment We are born human beings, not human doings Live life, love often, and always experience the now

George G
