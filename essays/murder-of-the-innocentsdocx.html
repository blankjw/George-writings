MURDER OF THE INNOCENTS

By George Gondron

MURDER OF THE INNOCENTS Don’t look away Let the images be seared into your memory For they represent a reality that can’t be denied That can’t be ignored or excused A clear representation of man’s inhumanity to man And for what reason? Many will blame religious ideology But it’s much deeper than that It represents clear evidence of how the mind can be controlled Of how a person can be indoctrinated Of how the spark of love in human consciousness Can be snuffed out Of how conscience itself has no importance whatsoever It has turned to rotted wood and dried blood To a lesser degree, we also suffer a loss of conscience A loss of an inner truth innate with our very birth As we buy into the false narratives that are so prevalent In our society today, truly a social dilemma And the more we accept without question or doubt The less we reflect and seek the truth The closer we come to a ‘murder of innocents’ Much closer than we care to realize It only takes a small crack in the damn for the resultant flood To be swayed through our emotions And ignore our intelligence, our ability to reason, to think Is to become in the end, the fool Once the point of return has been reached The snowball rolling down the hill becomes an avalanche And we will be buried beneath the lies and lose the truth What can we do, how can we be instrumental for the good? We can change ourselves, increase our awareness, our consciousness We can connect our mind with our heart, soul, and spirit Awaken our internal reference, our inner core given at birth Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, and Presence Empty the mind, give it a break Listen to the yearnings of your heart, soul, and spirit They have the answers, they hold your truth, they wait

George G
