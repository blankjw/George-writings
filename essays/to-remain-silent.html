TO REMAIN SILENT

By George Gondron | Source: TO REMAIN SILENT.docx

TO REMAIN SILENT There is always a price to pay for remaining silent in times of obvious turmoil, dysfunction, endless anxiety, and political unrest Throughout history there are those, male and female, who have had the courage, even at the risk of their own lives, to speak out and act against those who through their actions only want to use and abuse other human beings for their selfish desires And yes, many paid the ultimate price of losing their own lives by doing so, but their message never dies Their belief in kindness, compassion, inclusion, and unity within diversity lives on, their belief in love echoes throughout history To remain silent and ignore what is an obvious travesty is first to deny our own importance as a part of humanity and second to live with the excuses we use to compromise our own beliefs The Greek philosopher Plato once said, ‘The price of apathy toward public affairs is to be ruled by evil men’ It’s important to remember that what we must share concerning the lack of integrity, ethics, and values so apparent in our culture is like throwing a pebble in a pond Its initial impact may not seem significant, but the ripples created may indeed be very important We do violence to ourselves when we ignore the whispers and inner yearnings of our own internal resources We do violence to ourselves when we believe that what we think does not matter, it does, an open mind is better than a closed one Another Plato quote, ‘the measure of a man is what he does with power’, does he attempt to empower others or make them compliant and obedient to his authority It is obvious what our current administration intends, ‘bend the knee’ We are nothing more than ‘cogs in wheel’, a ‘means to an end’, ‘passive, voiceless followers’ expected to do what we’re told Plato again, ‘ignorance is the root cause of all difficulties’ Culturally our problem is ignorance, our refusal to learn Connect your mind with your inner resources Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your mind, listen to the whispers of heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
