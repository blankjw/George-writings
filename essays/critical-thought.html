CRITICAL THOUGHT

By George Gondron

CRITICAL THOUGHT Little evidence of critical thought today Lots of evidence for gullibility Maybe it's the unreliability of all the hype But, it would seem most prefer the sensational Whether it's true or false I think I remember someone saying 'Don't believe what you hear, and half of what you see' It's mostly impossible to confirm what we pass on But, if it fits our agenda, we do it anyway We actually can become addicted to half-truths Un-truths, propaganda, evangelism, and doctrine Addiction is not just a stimulant for the body But for the mind, the ego as well Wanting to belong to the 'right' group Follow the 'legitimate' crowd Even though they have questionable ethics And maybe a little paradoxical behavior Not exactly practicing what they 'preach' It seems those that are 'right' shout the loudest Speak for God, and denounce everyone else Denying their own 'shadow', and seeing it in others Critical thinking, quiet reflection, self-analysis All, worth more than the hype, the sensationalism Associate with those that are willing to question Willing to challenge the status quo Nothing is so perfect that it can't be improved So infallible that it can't be examined Risk being a critical thinker Seek more than one source Especially the Source that dwells within Where truth always abides

George G
