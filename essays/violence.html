VIOLENCE

By George Gondron

VIOLENCE When we overemphasize the law, sacred or secular We open the door to violence, a historical reality Death, destruction, and chaos in all forms Relies upon laws written and unwritten to support Its actions, witness the result of the narrative ‘White is right’ or ‘power corrupts and absolute power Corrupts absolutely’, or the golden rule, ‘those who Have the gold make the rules’ Underlying all violence are three motivators Wealth in all its forms, power, and control All three are able to justify their violent acts To satisfy their desires by defending their abhorrent behavior Even though the law may be broken their acts are an exception For they are committed for the people, not for themselves All acts of violence, are in the end, selfish Unless it’s in the defense of a child As adults, we can eventually remove ourselves from An atmosphere of violence, but to ignore violence In any form is to be complicit, an unspoken approval Unwittingly we do violence to ourselves as well When we accept without question information That stirs our emotions without any objective verification When we cease to learn and only listen to defend If we listen at all If we never question or doubt our favorite media source We become subject to manipulation and propaganda And fall into the trap of believing the ‘alternative facts’ Or the out-and-out lies, the truth is buried beneath a false narrative It is much easier to motivate people through fear than love Fear sells when love, kindness, sacrifice, and compassion don’t To be a discerning person we must engage our inner resources A perfect way is to embrace a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, be present to now Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on a short brief mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, they help you discern, they wait63

George G
