WITHOUT FAIL

By George Gondron

WITHOUT FAIL One of the mainstays of the human condition is our ability for Critical thought, to reflect, question, doubt, and discern As opposed to a ‘knee-jerk’ reaction when confronted with Situations that would normally require at least some small Amount of investigation, some modicum of examination If not, without fail, we are more likely to be part of the problem As opposed to being a part of the solution All evidence to the contrary, it would seem that critical thought Has become a lost art, a very necessary quality of being human What then takes the place of critical thinking in making decisions? Our emotions, our mind’s resistance to change, our focus on What we’re against rather than what we’re for Without fail, if we are ‘led’ by our emotions we are easily Influenced, we can be convinced of the most outrageous ideas Ideas, when closely examined are based in fear, are against, Are ‘anti’ something and we are never the problem, it’s always ‘other’ There is no wonder the unending list of conspiracy theories The ‘deep state’ propaganda, without critical thought we can be Convinced of literally anything, including the fact that decency, Integrity, morality, honesty, truthfulness, and selflessness Don’t matter – we are nothing more than sheep led by wolves We fall prey to the leaders who say repeatedly, ‘I love the poorly Educated’, to those who demand loyalty no matter the cost We appoint a ‘fox’ to guard the ‘henhouse’, happily And sadly, the real enemy is ourselves as we abandon our true Responsibility of being ‘our brother’s keeper’, being selfless When love is no longer our purpose, fear takes its place And where fear reigns love never abides We become forever ‘fault-finders’ and ‘finger pointers’ We don’t need a change of heart, we need a change of mind Seek balance in your life, connect inner and outer Adopt a meditative/contemplative daily practice For a few minutes experience silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they bring critical thought They have your answers, and they patiently wait

George G
