NEED VERSUS WANT

By George Gondron

NEED VERSUS WANT Probably the most misunderstood words in our vocabulary Misunderstood because of their meaning, need and want Most of the time when we use need in our conversations It’s totally out of context, indicating a necessity we can’t Live without, almost as if our well-being depended upon Whatever follows the words, ‘I need’ In truth what we need are the things that provide for our Survival; food, clothing, shelter, access to medical care Too often, even our relationships are needy, codependent We necessarily are social beings But healthy relationships are based on want, not need We place a burden on others when need is the expression of Our connection, rather than a choice A choice without expectations, ‘I want to be with you’ ‘You are important to me, but I don’t need you to survive’ The only person who can objectively hear those words And not be offended, not take it personally And still knows they are loved Is the person who has been broken apart and has painstakingly Put themselves back together again in order to become More fully human, fully alive, more whole A person who has experienced the ‘dark night of the soul’ A person who has clear perception and accurate response To see and hear what is as it is, with no pretense In this culture, they are few and far between A culture that convinces us daily what we ‘need’ Which ironically leads to all our dysfunctions, all our ills It’s partially the result of having no balance in our lives No connection to an inner core, an innate internal reference Given with our birth, it’s part of the package Choose a path of meditation/contemplation each day Spend a few minutes in silence, with stillness in the presence pf Presence, your image of a creator God, Isness, or as quantum Physics says, universal Consciousness Commit to a daily practice, listen to the yearnings To the whispers of your own heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, your healing, your wholeness, they wait

George G
