I AND ME

By George Gondron

I AND ME A powerful partnership, I and me The ego's preference, the mind's delight After all, what is of greatest importance If it's not I and me? A much better relationship than we and us Maybe, we can be a we if you think as I Perhaps there is an us, if I'm in charge Only one can be right, and it must be me Tolerance? is that a foreign word? Surely you welcome my beliefs You must approve of my inflation My righteousness After all, see how secure that I am Safe in my myopic world view My bubble of safety and protection Shielded from reality as a horse with blinders Please, don't confuse me with facts Don't present me with what I wish not to see Protecting what I have, isn't that enough? Surely my needs are the most important Then, sometimes, unawares a thought creeps in A little doubt of all I believe Allowing my gaze to linger a little longer On those less fortunate than I and me Maybe in their shoes, I might see an us Understand their history, hear their story Have I been too quick to judge? How long can I quiet the whispers of 'we and us?' How long will I cling to only 'I and me? Maybe I'll never find the answer And then, maybe I will, maybe

George G
