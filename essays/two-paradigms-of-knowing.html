TWO PARADIGMS OF KNOWING

By George Gondron

TWO PARADIGMS OF KNOWING Knowing through ideation, a mental process The observer and the observed are separate Or knowing through being, spiritual perception When the duality of observation vanishes and Now the observer and the observed become one The very definition of a mystical experience The first we do every day; it is our normal The second requires a new and different context than our normal Different descriptions are used by different belief systems But generally, they are some form of initiation rite Some form of ‘desert ‘experience or the development of a Meditative/contemplative practice As the saying goes, ‘different strokes for different folks’ But the focus is the same, regardless the path To get out of our heads and connect with ‘Other’ Unless of course you happen to be one that believes This amazing universe is just an accident and has no purpose I am fortunate, for like the great Carl Jung when ask by a TV Journalist if he believed in God, his reply was “I don’t need to believe, I know’ That knowing can only be accomplished experientially Not intellectually, so the name doesn’t matter Mystery, Isness, Universal Consciousness, Love, or God As the saying goes, ‘a rose by any other name would Smell just as sweet’ The practice of meditation engages the senses, hear, see, Taste, touch, and smell – be fully present to the meditative scene A consistent practice of meditation eventually leads to contemplation Emptiness, silence, stillness, nonverbal experience of Presence An experience which is too self-evident to deny And too deep and profound to put into words, it simply Is I encourage you embrace a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your ‘monkey mind’ of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or a short mantra, when the mind wanders Rinse and repeat, stick with it until it becomes natural Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
