WHEN WE SEE ONLY DIFFERENCE

By George Gondron

WHEN WE SEE ONLY DIFFERENCE When what matters is difference, and not sameness Common ground is difficult, if not impossible to find It becomes the 'needle in the haystack' Or as 'scarce as hen's teeth' What is not the same; different skin color, culture, belief Only gets in the way, becoming the 'elephant in the room' That no one wants to talk about, at least in positive terms When similarity is left to the mind alone Difference always wins, always The mind is in solidarity only with those that it perceives As the same, and not different Therefore the relevance of the saying 'Walk a mile in my shoes' The person who 'knows' and is not just a 'believer' Sees beyond what most see as difference only They see sameness, similarity, connection, and commonality Belief is only for those who do not know Do not know especially themselves And as long as I don't know myself Intimately, in detail, without reserve I can never know another For what I don't know about myself What I'm too blind to see, too afraid to perceive I project onto the other, seeing them as unlike myself They carry my shadow, which in the end Is a very heavy burden for both of us Difference builds walls, not bridges Difference leads to exclusion, not inclusion Difference separates and does not unite We are unique, different, in one measure only In the gifts that we bring to the table of life We all have a special offering that is beneficial to others We are interdependent, not mutually exclusive We are relational beings, or we are nothing at all If we are born from Love It is for relationship, there could be no other valid reason To know the Giver of life, of relationship, is to know love

George G
