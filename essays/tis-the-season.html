TIS THE SEASON

By George Gondron

TIS THE SEASON What do these words mean, 'tis the season' If you asked someone many years ago that question You would have received a much different answer than today The answer then would have been connected to a man The incarnation of the Jewish god Yahweh as an infant An infant named Jesus Slowly over the ensuing years it has evolved Evolved into something very different The trappings of the celebration of Christmas The tree, the gifts, the decorations All emerging from pagan rituals And, slowly over the decades the message, the meaning Of what lies behind the celebration Has been replaced by the God of Capitalism The Prince of Profit The lights, the glitter, the wrappings, the bows Take the place of a miracle some 2,000 years ago In years past, the tree was decorated on Christmas Eve But now, an urgency beginning the day after Thanksgiving Black Friday, Cyber Monday, it's all about the gifts The success of the season, measured in dollars The Giver has been forgotten, lost in the shuffle What once was a celebration of life and of love A time of renewal with family and friends Has become a burden on so many levels What to buy, how much to spend, who did I forget More depression, frustration, anxiety, and debt An increase in abuse, suicide, and death When did the definition of love get changed And become what I give or receive, to and from another Yes, something has been lost through all the distractions Something very precious, very rare, very valuable A free and infused gift that comes with the package The package called our life and created from Love It makes relationship possible, the only reason to be And it 'tis the Reason for the season' Spend a little time with the real Reason

George G
