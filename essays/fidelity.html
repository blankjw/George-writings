FIDELITY

By George Gondron

FIDELITY Fidelity – to be loyal or faithful to one’s core beliefs Only one definition of fidelity, there are many others The real issue is one’s core beliefs What are they, can I identify them, what is their origin Are they the result of personal experience Or are they beliefs that have been imposed upon me To accept anything without question or doubt Is to play and be the fool But that’s the culture we live in Marketers, propagandists, advertisers, and social media itself Are experts in their abilities to influence and manipulate They know that the way to influence people the most Is to play upon their desires and attachments Not their intellect, but their emotions, their feelings The process related to the addictive choices of human beings Is not intellectual, it’s psychological Of the emotions, not of the mind, both pleasure and pain There is no rationality at all to two questions such as What is it specifically you don’t like about that person? Answer, ‘I just don’t like them’ Or, how do you know that? Answer, ‘I just know’ Very difficult to have a meaningful informative conversation With that mentality, the epitome of a closed mind A mind that refuses to acknowledge facts and circumstances The beginning of an awareness of our personal core beliefs Is self-examination, self-reflection, self-doubt The question, ‘how is it I know what I say I know?’ Or do I merely assume because it satisfies me emotionally? Maybe we don’t want to know, for if we did We might have to change or admit we were wrong One of the benefits of a meditative/contemplative practice Is self-revelation or self-understanding Through a few minutes of silence, and stillness, with Presence We begin to peel back the onion of ourselves And discover our true essence as we listen to the whispers Of our heart, soul, and spirit – they hold our truth, our healing

George G
