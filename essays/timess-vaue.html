TIMES'S VA;UE

By George Gondron

TIME’S VALUE In one moment, we are born In one moment, we die It’s the millions of moments in-between That creates our life The journey will demand many choices, many decisions Many forks in the road and different paths along the way The first organ to form in our body, our heart There are no instructions telling it how to beat It’s not gender-specific, contained within every person In its formation, there is already an innate knowing And to this day science has no idea how all this happens They can follow the mechanics of organ formation But they lack an understanding of how life begins We seldom, if ever, look to the heart for guidance But science does now know The same type of cells in the brain Are also found in the heart We will never know how time is lost As a result of ignoring the wisdom of the heart Great spiritual teachers, poets, and authors Have taught and written about The knowing of the heart for centuries But we much prefer listening to our minds Our intellect, our egos before our hearts We waste time, run out of time, lose time, Time flies, not enough time, want to turn back time The best manager of time is the most resisted The consistent and dedicated practice Of a meditative/contemplative daily exercise Just a few minutes each day of silence, stillness With Presence, our best time investment A time to empty our minds of their incessant And often repetitive thoughts Which go nowhere and accomplish nothing We can then hear the whispers of our hearts An internal guide that patiently waits Take the time to listen, it never fails

George G
