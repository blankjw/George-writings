ESSAY

MYSTERY

By George Gondron

MYSTERY There are those things in our lives that cannot be understood Academically, intellectually, or even sensually They fall in the realm of mystery, of ‘ah ha’ moments Those moments that are too self-evident to be denied Yet too deep and profound to be put into words Their meaning is singular, for the individual alone Yet they are a common phenomenon for human beings Not subject to gender, race, or ethnicity Not subject to place or time, they are universal and eternal Moments that are calling us to pause and reflect They can happen in any context, any situation, any moment Those of unbearable pain and those of immense joy Our only bridge, our only hope, our only requirement To connect to those ‘ah, ha’ moments, that mystery Is to be aware, to be conscious, of that ‘now’ experience In our Western culture most respond through denying Like the saying, ‘out of sight out of mind’ Our lives are so outwardly focused that unless there’s Some clear external evidence we grasp through our senses, Our thoughts, or our feelings, we pursue the ‘ah ha’ moment No further, as if it never happened and our soul and spirit Languish in loss and emptiness for the meaning of that mystery We seem to forget that as humans, one of our greatest gifts Is the gift of choice, we are choice makers in all that we think, Feel, and do or do not do Our opportunities to know ‘mystery’ surround us each and Every day, but when life is viewed as a problem to be solved Rather than a gift to be explored, we’re blind to the opportunities And we fail to experience ‘mystery’ which is an inner yearning The best definition of awareness is to wake-up But many of us never do -add a meditative/contemplative path Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Mystery Isness, Source, Universal Consciousness, God, the name doesn’t Matter – our human mind is unable to comprehend the Mystery We can only be present to ‘ah ha’ moments Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, they hold your true self They have your answers, and they patiently wait

George G
