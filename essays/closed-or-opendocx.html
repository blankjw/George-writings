CLOSED OR OPEN

By George Gondron

OPEN OR CLOSED To be open holds the possibility of expansion, of the willingness to at least explore the unfamiliar, to be curious, to learn To be closed is to reject any and all that is not familiar, regardless of the potential benefit, especially if it’s counter to our beliefs The ego does not like change, the ego’s go to position is to resist Curious minds have brought us from living in caves with our light and heat being that of a fire To living in houses using gas and electricity for warmth and light From horses, buggies, and canoes to autos, trains, and ships As the saying goes, ‘necessity is the mother of invention’ Closed minds are almost always negative minds, complaining about what they perceive as being wrong, which is nothing more than being a part of the problem and not the solution And they normally follow those who do the same, blame and complain, separate and divide, and defend and reject You know the type, they may not always be right, but they are never wrong, just ask they them, they’ll gladly tell you There are many psychological terms that define these types, but the ‘tell tell’ signs are the ‘beating of their chest’, the inflation of their own ego, and their delusions of being a ‘savior’ And the bottom line, ‘what’s in it for me’, they are the clear example of what it means to be totally selfish We really don’t matter, just paws in their crooked chess game As of now, the Republicans, or they used to be, are enamored by the modern day ‘Wizard of Oz’, afraid to pull back the curtain and reveal the ‘charlatan’ that wants total power and control I hope enough of them come to their senses before it’s too late I hope they can examine their own conscience and shine a light on their own integrity, values, and decency, it matters I hope that we all have an open mind now and for the future It is desperately needed if this experiment, our founding fathers called democracy, is to survive for future generations To bring balance to your life, to connect inner and outer Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice, each day for just a few minutes, be silent, be still, be in the presence of Isness Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They awaken your discernment, they have your answers, they wait

George G
