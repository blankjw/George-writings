FREEDOM OF SPEECH

By George Gondron | Source: FREEDOM OF SPEECH.docx

FREEDOM OF SPEECH On September 17, 1787 our Constitution was formed Two years later, September 25, 1789, the Bill of Rights was formed The first of those ten amendments was The Freedom of Speech Since then seventeen more amendments have been added Under the current administration several of those amendments are being attacked, especially Freedom of Speech When freedom of speech is curtailed, we approach joining the ranks of Russia, China, Hungary, Iran, Venezuela, nations that are autocratic and dictatorial Evidently, our founding fathers considered free speech important enough to be first in our Bill of Rights That importance is being threatened almost daily as restrictions are being levied on most forms of social media, including the individuals that represent them In other words, regulations on what we say and write Only under dire circumstances is free speech limited, i.e. shouting fre in a crowded theater Political satire has never been restricted, not until now Operative free speech protects expression, including expressions of dissent But more and more our free speech is being attacked beginning with intimidation, the threat of governmental funding being withheld from colleges and universities used for research in science, medicine, and education And then there’s the issue of our own national history, ‘white washed’ and distorted regarding our treatment of Native Americans, African Americans, Mexicans, Chinese, to mention a few A verbal and nonverbal form of speech representing unacceptable behavior, which to this day many refuse to acknowledge Most will never understand the importance of free speech until it is lost, that reality looms greater day-by-day Spend some time reflecting on your own ability to speak freely Cherish that ability and exercise the same when needed Trust your intuition, use common sense, trust your inner guides Each moment that passes when we should have spoken out brings us closer to losing the opportunity in the future

George G
