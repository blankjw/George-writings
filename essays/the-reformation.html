The Reformation

By George Gondron | Source: The Reformation.docx

The Reformation, a necessary and meaningful historical event, had both positive and negative results. It was the precursor of bringing religion all into the head, and away from an experiential event. Away from what Jesus actually taught, toward a system of rights and wrongs to replace the rights and wrongs being committed at the time. A dualistic system, in many respects, simply being replaced by another dualistic system. The one big loss was the loss of contemplative prayer, by the masses, but retained by a few monastic orders. The importance of some form of prayer involving a practice which incorporates silence, stillness, and presence is becoming more recognized today. Perhaps out of necessity, perhaps from the meaninglessness of life for so many, or perhaps the recognition that there must be more to life than just constant gratification. Or, perhaps just the simple question of St. Francis, 'who are you God and who am I'.   "If you can't explain it to a six year old, you don't understand it yourself."   -   Albert Einstein   Running from our inner voices, we'll never understand ourselves, perhaps we don't want to. Seek awareness, stay present, live in the now.   Love & Peace,  George G
