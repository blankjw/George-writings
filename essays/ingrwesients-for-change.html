INGRWESIENTS FOR CHANGE

By George Gondron

INGREDIENTS FOR CHANGE Unfortunately, we live in a culture that resists change of a personal nature rather than embracing change to become healthier Statistically, most who do seek counseling do so to change someone else and not themselves And ironically, history is filled with teachers that stress the importance of self-actualization, individuation, and becoming whole And almost without exception, the ego will resist, the mind will not yield to being vulnerable to letting go You know the thought, ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ The necessary ingredients that offer us the opportunity to choose a path that would be beneficia personally, enter our lives daily Anxiety, stress, all forms of emotional turmoil, senseless worry, constantly living in the past or fearing the future There is a consensus among the spiritual and psychological teachers of the world, past and present, that three experiences afford us the opportunity for personal change Deep emotional pain, to know unconditional love, and some form of meditative/contemplative practice, each can be a catalyst for change Our psychological health and wellbeing are personal choices, always It is not the responsibility of others; it is ours alone To blame or defend our circumstances toward another or others is to grant them power or renounce our own power How we perceive, understand, learn, and become more aware is always about choice, not about chance Education, like ignorance, is a choice To merely accept what’s offered through social media is a choice for ignorance, we each are responsible for our own ‘fact checking’ A valid education demands critical thinking, reflection, research, intensive study, discernment, and intuition, we’re not just sponges soaking up information as it’s offered without question We alone are responsible for our own thoughts, feelings, and actions The better option is always about chosen change, to risk A suggestion; adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Connect inner and outer, choose balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
