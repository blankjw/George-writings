UNTOLD STORIES

By George Gondron

UNTOLD STORIES Since the beginning of human consciousness The untold dead, the living now present Each with a story, most unspoken, unknown Individual histories lost, unshared memories It is in the telling of our story that we are kin It is in the sharing of our humanness that we connect We each, have our pain and sorrow, our losses As well, we have our joys and celebrations Our times of weeping, our moments of laughter The doubtful instance, the hopeful longing The fearful moments, some short, some longer A yearning for acceptance An aching to belong Some fulfilled, some gone astray In our uniqueness we still are alike In our alikeness we survive Our hopes, our dreams, our expectations Some achieved, some lost forever, some never meant to be Tell your story It is important It is a part of the cosmic pattern A thread in the tapestry of creation Don't rob history of your story It has meaning, it has purpose, it is yours It is like no other It is you

George G
