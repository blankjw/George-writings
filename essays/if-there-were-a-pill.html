IF THERE WERE A PILL

By George Gondron

IF THERE WERE A PILL Wouldn’t it be great If the drug companies Could produce a ‘kindness’ pill Or a ‘compassion’ pill A pill that would have a dual effect Kindness toward oneself and toward others Compassion for oneself and others Maybe it would eliminate the need for Anti-depressant and anti-anxiety pills Who knows? Maybe a great deal of our ‘ills’ Would be eliminated as well Healthy mind, healthy body Kindness and its first cousin compassion Are probably the two most important tools We can have in our toolbox In general, we don’t really realize How negative we are not only toward others But also, toward ourselves The words poor self-image and low self-esteem Are more established than those of a positive nature In truth, how can we feel good about anything If we don’t feel good about ourselves? I hope you reflect on that question Of course, a big part of the problem Is, we compare and contrast ourselves to others Constantly, in every aspect of our existence Physically, intellectually, economically, financially The list is endless of how we measure up to others It is no wonder our need to develop And exercise kindness and compassion Do yourself a favor Develop a meditative/contemplative practice A time each day for silence, stillness, and Presence A time for self-reflection and positive affirmations To appreciate your uniqueness and value To know you are here because of Love, to be love George G
