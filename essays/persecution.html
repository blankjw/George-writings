ESSAY

PERSECUTION

By George Gondron

PERSECUTION Wherever human beings are not free to be And that freedom would not impinge upon the rights of others There will be persecution Ill-treatment, torment, and abuse will follow In one form or another And it is always those in positions of less power Less control, less influence, and less importance That will be persecuted It's always a long road and a hard fought battle For the persecuted to truly be free, if ever But it is tribalism and ignorance that demands its freedom They are the same ones that persecutes recklessly, unfairly Not long ago persecution was more overt than covert It was more direct and 'hands on' Burn the witch, hang the negro, scalp the Indian Beat the slave, shoot the labor organizer, burn 'them' out But now persecution is more covert and hidden Discriminate, segregate, ostracize, shame, isolate Direct or indirect it's still persecution It still encroaches upon and lessens the freedom Of the persecuted If freedom is real, it is a right afforded to every person Within the confines of our current statues, ordinances, and laws Of our legal system, of our Constitution But historically we know, we see the evidence, that's not true Not true for everyone Freedom seems to be on a continuum Maximum freedom is afforded to wealth, power, and 'whiteness' Lesser freedom afforded to those of color and those without Study your history, past and current, you'll discover the truth The real problem is not a matter of the law, the law is clear The real problem lies in the heart which can't be legislated Freedom, equality, kindness, and compassion for all Are matters of the heart, of the soul, of the spirit Current justice is not focused on restoration It is focused on retribution, and eye for an eye And some eyes are more important than others

George G
