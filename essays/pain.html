PAIN

By George Gondron

PAIN It is said there are two types of pain in this world One is of the physical body The other is of the mind, both can and do hurt But only one has the power to alter To alter character and personality To alter belief and lifestyle To alter one’s sense of value, ethics, and integrity To alter truth And most importantly to alter how one Understands and comprehends The true nature of relationship With oneself and with all else, without exception It is this second type of pain that the world So desperately yearns for So desperately needs Especially at this time in history From all objective indications We are alarmingly approaching an abyss From which we may not be able to return The planet will easily survive But will humanity? There is little indication that globally We will awaken to our dangerous plight Sure, there are small groups here and there That are aware and conscious enough To see the dangers that face us But are those small groups enough? The world is awash in ignorance With little evidence that anything will change The Dalia Lama once said That if we would teach all eight-year-olds To meditate The world would change in one generation Meditation is nothing more than a practice That leads to awareness and greater consciousness A process of ‘waking up’ Let’s do our part, let’s ‘wake up’

George G
