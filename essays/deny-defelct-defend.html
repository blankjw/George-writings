DENY, DEFELCT, DEFEND

By George Gondron

DENY, DEFLECT, DEFEND More and more in our society the communication process Follows a path of first denial, then deflection, and always defending It’s especially noticeable in politics and religion It’s almost impossible to ask a politician a direct question That requires only a yes or no answer and expect A yes or no response, virtually impossible Now with the far-right evangelical Christian movement The same phenomenon is evident more and more Especially when their diatribe is anti and fear based As opposed to being inclusive as their leader taught Seems I remember reading ‘Love one another as I have loved you’ When communication between and among humans Is fraught with denial, deflection, and defensiveness Truth, along with trust, is abandoned and becomes irrelevant We, it seems, no longer know how to communicate With an attitude of openness and welcoming Instead, we have become close-minded and defensive Stuck in our either/or thinking Choosing one or more of the psychological defense mechanisms In our everyday communications, even with our families Friends and acquaintances When we are guarded in our thoughts and feelings For fear of rejection or not being understood We become silent, losing the ability to have meaningful relationships Where will it end, when we can’t have open discussions Without alienating and belittling one another The ego’s need to be right is very dysfunctional indeed The opposite of the heart’s desire to be inclusive and kind That it seems, is the real problem We have, for the most part, no internal reference or inner core No touchstone that is the dynamic of the heart, soul, and spirit We are all external, living from the ego alone Please, spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness, And with Presence, your image of a Creator Your truth dwells within, not without Your true self is in the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Take time to listen each day, they patiently await

George G
