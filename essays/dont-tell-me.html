DON'T TELL ME

By George Gondron

DON'T TELL ME - SHOW ME When will we learn that words are empty? Words, more often than not, forms of manipulation Rarely, reasons for change Forms used to convince, persuade, and attempt to prove Words cannot demonstrate, they can only insinuate When words are worshipped, the message gets lost When words are believed wars are created Lives are lost, innocents die, no one wins Words are just labels and labels are not truth At best just descriptions, not transformation How many times have the words proved false? From the politicians, the vendors, the marketer of lies In the end, we believe what we want to believe Nothing more, nothing less If it keeps us secure, with little discomfort, little distraction Whether it's fact or fiction, we will believe And the words which we hear Whether half-truths or lies If heard often enough become our beliefs So, how do we know what is true or false? How can we tell what to trust and believe? The truth can be known in the behavior that we see The truth can be known in the results of the actions The truth can be known in our personal experience It is not what we hear in the too many words It is what we observe and the results there from There we find truth more trusting than the words It is personal experience that matters the most For personal experience cannot lie Critical thought, reflection, and contemplation These bring awareness more than just words We trust what we know, we know what we experience Nothing can be fully believed, trusted, or known Without putting into practice what we have learned Without experience, and not just words Have faith in your knowing, don't doubt your own truth Even if you stand alone

George G
