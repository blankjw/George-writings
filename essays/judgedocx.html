JUDGE

By George Gondron

JUDGE Without exception, we all judge, we all express our opinions Judging comes in different forms There is a vast difference in 'passing judgment' As opposed to expressing, after critical examination After study, after experience, after behavioral evidence A clear statement of fact or disagreement And the fact or disagreement is presented without any rancor But with compassion, without putting the other person down Without a 'gotcha' To 'pass judgment' is usually supported or backed up with Someone else's narrative and that narrative is in many instances Supported by religious belief, some creed, some 'ism' Discernment is a lost art in our current culture Which ironically is an important part of religious belief In the Christian religion Jesus is the perfect 'discerner' Repeatedly saying, 'they say but I say' But when worship becomes more important than practice The practice of compassion, kindness, mercy, and love Then we pass judgment and no longer, if ever, discern Instead we have a 'knee-jerk' reaction to almost everything Everything that lies outside of our experiential knowledge Everything that asks us to be open to another point of view The underlying feeling to rebuttal without experiential evidence Is nothing more than fear, we must be right We have to be right in order to defend our position A position empty of personal experience Simply founded on someone else's narrative Other words for judge; to decide, conclude, resolve, assess So in reality, we judge 24/7 in one form or another Don't be too quick to reject what you don't know Be open to expanding your horizons, your knowledge All of our positive emotions are grounded in love All our negative emotions are grounded in fear Fear restricts, love expands Fear is static, love is dynamic Expand, be dynamic, be love Build bridges, tear down walls

George G
