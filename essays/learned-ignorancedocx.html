LEARNED IGNORANCE

By George Gondron

LEARNED IGNORANCE The risk of consciousness is learned ignorance Arising from clinging to the shortsightedness of others Refusing the innate tug toward awareness Believing the words and not the experience All, contribute to our learned ignorance When is the time to stop seeking, stop growing? Intellectually, emotionally, spirituality, relationally To cease questioning, to cede curiosity? To be satisfied with the status quo To stop being a part of the herd, having all the answers Answers that are only half truths Half truths that have no experiential basis Seen only from our limited perspectives Trusted because of another's words, their propaganda And not their actions, not what remains hidden The time to stop is never, for knowing is infinite The trap is the ceasing as if all is concluded The snare is the smug satisfaction Our comfortable, safe, and impenetrable existence In our learned ignorance we pretend to live But we are not connected, not to the whole At best, we are alone with those of like interests Undaunted by the new, the strange, the different Reacting against, close-minded in our smallness Chained to the familiar to avoid the possible There is a whisper that calls us forward Urging us to cast off the fetters that bind Pulling us from our self-made morass Let us, let go of the anchor The anchor of learned ignorance Let us sail beyond, before we drown

George G
