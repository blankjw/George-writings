REASON

By George Gondron

REASON We live in a culture that believes reason is enough But reason alone will never suffice For reason does not comprehend the unknowable As an event to be experienced, as a reality In fact, for reason the only necessary truth Is that which is a result of the intellect, the mind, the ego Nothing else has more merit than facts And dare not mention intuition So, if reason is formulated from facts How do all the conspiracy theories gain ground Have credence or hold true if they’re not supported With facts, with hard evidence, with exposed truth And with obvious and clear results Oh, but wait, I know They’re called ‘alternative facts’ They arise from conditioning, programming, Propaganda, marketing, and targeted advertising And if you’re exposed to social media You’re exposed to all the above We can’t escape it But we can be reflective critical thinkers We can be people with open minds We can refuse to drink the ‘Kool-Aid’ And most importantly, we can awaken The power and creativity we hold inside We can choose to question, doubt, and educate ourselves From reliable sources and resist the rhetoric of the Snake-oil salesmen, the con-artists, the Pied Pipers For reason to have merit, have value There must be a connection between the head and the heart Between the known and the unknown Underlying all true beliefs is a personal experience And unfortunately, many there are that believe

There is a saying – trust but verify Spend a few moments each day in silence, stillness, With Presence – discover your truth, it is there

George G
