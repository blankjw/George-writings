EGO

By George Gondron

EGO The given is, we all have an ego, a sense of ‘I’ However, what we don’t realize is that ego will do whatever is necessary to protect itself from self-discovery That self-discovery is ‘double-edged’ Unwilling to acknowledge or embrace not only our ‘devils’ but our ‘angels’ as well For the individual there is nothing more difficult than an honest inventory of character How and what we think, feel and do or do not do When we look in the mirror, when we ‘pull the curtain back’ objectively, who are you, who am I Individuation is the process by which the wholeness of the individual is established through the integration of consciousness and the collective unconscious – a necessary task to become fully human and fully alive The ego doesn’t like change, it will resist, even while in pain Few there are that reach a level of awareness and consciousness that results in the understanding that what we think, feel, and do or do not do is a matter of choice, not the fault of others Any time our narrative is defensive, we are stuck in our ego We reject the possibility that we might be wrong, be in error We cling to our familiar even if that familiar is painful Just further evidence we don’t like change If you have a person you trust, a close friend Ask them to share with you what they see about your character that you may not see yourself, your ‘blind spots’ Perhaps it’s your judgements or your defensiveness or perhaps it’s your inability to be open minded and accept facts not your own If they are willing to do this, you can only listen, not respond Your ego will resist – I promise In addition, add a meditative/contemplative practice to your life A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Presence being your image of a Creative Source, of Isness Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
