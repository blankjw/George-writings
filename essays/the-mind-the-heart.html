THE MIND, THE HEART

By George Gondron

THE MIND, THE HEART It’s interesting to note The heart sees sameness first But the mind sees differences first Within that sameness dwells a uniqueness We are much more alike than unalike in many ways But we are also unique Each with a different gift, a different creativity All a part of the greater whole, made of stardust Carrying within ourselves the entire universe In the book, ‘The Little Prince’ The fox shares this important line with the prince ‘It is only with the heart that one sees rightly What is essential is invisible to the eye.’ As well, right seeing is a matter of conscience, Of soul, all carry the same purpose Prior to about the age of two, the child Perceives those in his/her immediate surroundings As an extension of themselves It’s not until later they develop a sense of ‘I’ It is that development that begins dualistic thinking Everything now is oppositional Right/wrong, good/bad, selfish/unselfish All the major belief systems and philosophies Teach about the importance of nondualism Or if you prefer situational ethics Meaning there are situations when what may appear To be absolutely right, could be wrong And vice/versa A life guided by the heart, the conscience, the soul Is always both/and, not either/or It is plain to see in our current environment We are of the mind and not the heart, the conscience Our society abounds with discord, separation, exclusion Extreme polarity and oppositional thinking Take a different path, return to the heart Spend a few minutes in silence, stillness with Presence Each and every day, seek the wisdom of your soul

George G
