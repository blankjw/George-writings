INSTITUTIONAL EGOCENTRICITY

By George Gondron

INSTITUTIONAL EGOCENTRICITY Institutional egocentricity is more insidious and treacherous Than individual egocentricity for it has no conscience Institutions can exclude, ostracize, condemn, and isolate Both individuals and groups at will They can justify war, racism, xenophobia, and white nationalism Nothing is exempt from institutional egocentricity We all fall prey to its influence, power, and control Our nation is not founded on the precepts of life, liberty, And the pursuit of happiness, at least as it now exists But rather is founded on production and consumption Spend more of what we don’t have on what we don’t need Our democracy is not managed by three branches of government It is managed and controlled by corporate entities Who pad the pockets of politicians for their benefit alone We worship the ‘golden calf’ of consumerism Institutional egocentricity touches every area of our lives Both sacred and profane, i.e. right-wing TV evangelism Taking money from widows living on Social Security Our values have turned upside down What we possess is more important than how we relate We abandon truth, morals, and ethics for self-righteousness Kindness, empathy, compassion, and love replaced By dualistic and polarized thinking without conscience We deny the shadow side of our history, wearing blinders Burying our heads in the sand unable to see the forest for the trees Our ability to discern, listen to our conscience and intuition Has all but disappeared, drowning in our own egocentricity We desperately need balance in our lives, inner and outer We fear silence and aloneness, needing constant stimulation We have become strangers to ourselves As faint whispers ask, ‘With whom am I having the pleasure?’ Choose a meditative/contemplative practice Just a few minutes each day be silent, be still, become aware Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts That you may hear the quiet whispers of your inner guides Your heart, soul, and spirit, they patiently wait They hold your true self, have your answers, give you truth

George G
