LET-GO

By George Gondron

LET- GO The bane of being unable to let-go Is the greatest obstruction to personal change It is the roadblock, the barrier to individual growth The most destructive weakness of the ego That which keeps us stuck and mired in the past A past that forever impacts the present A thought, a feeling, a previous experience that's like an anchor A memory of shame, of betrayal, of personal failure In truth whatever it is that we can't let-go of Is not to our benefit, but our detriment, always If there is one guarantee, change is inevitable Like it or not Yet we shackle and hobble ourselves With what we refuse to release from our past The benefit of history is to prevent us from repeating the past But we cling to what's no longer beneficial In a real sense, through our clinging We refuse to learn something new, something better We prefer the familiar to the strange Even if the familiar is painful Unable to let-go we limit our chances for new awareness Reduce the possibility for deeper consciousness We remain the same today, tomorrow, into the infinite future Making ourselves and those around us miserable The common internal dialogue with those unable to let-go 'If only they' But the real solution is 'if only we' Those who proclaim they're powerless to change, to let-go Are really saying, 'I don't want to change, I want to cling' They really don't want a solution It's much easier to blame The energy required to cling, to hold on, is much greater Than that to let-go, to change There is no win to clinging, there is only loss To limit one's own possibilities, one's own potential Become healthier, become happier, become whole not half Let-go

George G
