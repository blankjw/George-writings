WHEN GIVEN A CHOICE

By George Gondron

WHEN GIVEN A CHOICE I know, funny title for a reflection For we do have choices about what we think, What we feel, and what we do or do not do But for now, when given a choice to be right or to be kind Please, choose kindness, the world’s in short supply Acts of compassion, empathy, inclusion, mercy, justice, And equality are seemingly evaporating before our Very eyes – no connection between our heads and our hearts No law can be passed that will legislate the heart How we feel about ourselves and others is an ‘inside job’ The only person we have the power to change is ourself No other, just ‘me, myself, and I’ Blame doesn’t work, the defense mechanisms don’t work They are only excuses for not doing the right thing When given the choice and there’s lots of opportunities Just observe, they’re easy to see, not easy tp accept We all, with no exception, have the power to be change makers In order for that to happen we must first change ourselves All positive progress starts with the single individual And what initiates positive change?, all that falls under the Heading of love And what slows or halts positive change?, all the negatives That fall under the heading of fear When given a choice, choose love, reject fear Through experience we know the difference between fear and love Fear is like a cancer that eventually destroys its host Love is like an elixir that supports the essence of being Let the positive changes in yourself be instruments of change For others in your life, even the strangers The simple choice to adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Is significant toward becoming more alive, more fully human A few minutes each day of silence, stillness, and Presence A time to empty our ‘monkey minds’ of repetitive thoughts To learn to focus and be present to now To listen to our own heart, soul, and spirit, our better angels To discover our true self as opposed to our egoic false self All are answers are hidden within, they patiently wait

George G
