THE POWER OF CHOICE

By George Gondron

THE POWER OF CHOICE Free will affords us the opportunity to choose Seldom a realization for those who prefer to live their lives Without ever taking ownership for what they think, feel, or do And I might add, for what they don’t do as well Many go through their entire lives functioning through the Adaptation of the psychological defense mechanisms Such as denial, blame, rationalization, justification, etc., etc., etc. If we never gain a level of awareness or consciousness That awakens us to our own choices, we will continue to blame We will point fingers, we will live through our emotions Ignoring our intellect and rejecting a path that leads to understanding We will be closed and not open, preferring our familiar Even when that familiar is painful, for after all we can’t be wrong To continue to complain is just another way of saying I choose not to change I’m basically placing my situation in the hands of another Or others, positive or negative, and that’s also a matter of choice To give up your own humanity, your uniqueness Is a dreadful choice, it’s an unspoken message, ‘I don’t matter” This personality type doesn’t want empathy, they want sympathy And there is a saying, ‘you can find sympathy between shit and Syphilis in the dictionary’, sympathy never changed anyone If anything, it allows them to remain stuck and be the victim What we choose really does matter because we each matter As the saying goes God does not make ‘junk’ We’ve all made some bad choices, maybe not intentionally But guess what? We can make new choices Life is continuously about ‘letting go’, about change About living in the now, in the present moment About awakening to the awesome responsibility of our humanity Our ‘broken places’ only make us stronger, they are teachers Self-care requires balance inner and outer to be fully human Adopt a daily meditative/contemplative practice For just a few minutes experience silence, stillness, be present Empty your mind, focus on your breath, in and out Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
