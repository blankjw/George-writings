DO YOU EVER WONDER

By George Gondron

DO YOU EVER WONDER Ever wonder what it must be like to be handicapped? Physically, intellectually, or emotionally Ever ask yourself what’s the story behind The addicts need for their particular drug of choice Which may not be a drug at all At least not something that they might ingest It could easily be a thought, an emotion, or an attitude Which is their constant companion A handicap which is motivated and supported by an angst A fear so subtle it can’t be defined, but it’s there There is one handicap that is more deadly than all others It is the closed versus the open mind The position of not only not wanting to ‘know’ But not ‘needing’ to know The position of ‘don’t confuse me with the facts’ ‘My opinion is all that matters, I know the truth’ It is a condition that ‘runs rampant’ in our society The belief that ‘I might not be right, but I’m never wrong’ The saying, ‘trust but verify’ has no meaning We tend to trust our emotions and not our intellect We seem to no longer question, doubt, or critically think We simply accept that which appeals to our feelings The greatest gift we can give to humanity at this time in history Discover our own personal handicaps Especially those related to our intellectual and emotional Wellbeing, or lack thereof Those handicaps related to our closed minds Our opinions based on emotions and not facts Based on ‘he said, she said, they said’ We hold the truth inside ourselves, we have the answers Choose a meditative/contemplative practice daily Spend a few minutes in silence, with stillness, with Presence Commit to this daily, discover your ‘handicaps’ Experience the gift of discernment, an innate gift Empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your ‘true self’, they patiently await

George G
