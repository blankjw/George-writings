YESTERDAY, TODAY, TOMORROW

By George Gondron

YESTERDAY, TODAY, TOMORROW Only one represents the present – today Yet most of us dwell in the past or the future In the past holding on to anger and resentment In the future because of fear and insecurity You might say, we’ve never lived our lives at all Because everything that matters Happens in the now But if we’re not present, we miss it So, all of our memories about yesterday The so-called ‘good old days’ Are our measuring stick for what’s happening now We view everything through The rose-colored lens of yesterday As well, our future fears are also In the recesses of our minds Lurking in the shadows Lingering in the darken hallways of our future We of course hold on to the important lessons Of yesterday, so they are not repeated As well, we need to plan for our future Like the grasshopper and ant story But if asked, ‘what are you thinking?’ Rarely is the response about now, the present I sometimes believe that’s why it’s so difficult For people, for us, to identify our feelings Those feelings seem to always relate to The past or the future, and not now And even then, the feelings are almost always negative A healthy life is always about change And if we don’t change with it If we don’t embrace the now We simply remain stuck with a closed mind And some would say, ‘a closed fist as well’ We are our own worst enemy Living in a self-made prison in which we hold the key Take time each day for critical thinking, self-reflection For moments of silence, stillness, and Presence

George G
