PERSONAL VALUES

By George Gondron

PERSONAL VALUES The evidence of who we are and what we value What and whom we include and what and whom we omit Is always found in what we have done And what we are doing still Not in what we have said nor what we are still saying Who we are, not the prefixes or suffixes of our names Not the titles or pedigrees Is evidenced through how we incorporate, connect, include Indicators of what we value, where our treasure lies Our repetitive thoughts, reoccurring emotions All signs of what's important To ask the stranger first, where are you from and What do you do? is a wall, not a bridge builder Our labels are only subjective identifiers They cloud and hinder our true knowing of another As well as ourselves If we believe this universe is benevolent If it has been created by a benevolent and loving God Regardless of one's philosophical or religious belief How do we manifest that love which we've received If we are not aware, we don't and never will Religion is then just a matter of going through the motions It has no life and is by observance, very passive It is no more than membership, following the rules It has morphed into only worship Forgotten the words of, 'follow me' If we do 'it' the right way, at the right time, in the right place That's all that matters, but not really What does matter? What I know for certain about life It was created from Love for one important reason For relationship, not to become human 'doings' But through our spiritual birth to become Fully human beings, in relationship with one another In relationship with our image of a Creator Through the discovery of who we are, in relationship With all that has been , is now, and will ever be

George G
