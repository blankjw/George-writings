SELF-REFERENCE

By George Gondron

SELF-REFERENCE Self-reference, referring to oneself Or looked at from another point of view How we think and feel about, or don’t think and feel about Anything and everything What really matters is the clarity of our own perceptions, always Especially about ourselves For if our perceptions are ‘colored’ or tainted because of Our own bias, prejudice, and judgments Our own lack of awareness and consciousness Can we be objective about our own subjectivity? Not really, no matter how much we say we are To be able to stand apart from our own desires and attachments Is rare indeed, it is the exception, not the rule We seem always, as the saying goes, ‘to have skin in the game’ Every moment of our life is being impacted Directly or indirectly through our five senses Through our thoughts and feelings Through our context and interactions, we don’t live in a vacuum To see clearly is to respond accurately, about ourselves Because of personal choices, almost everything we do Or do not do, is the result of a focus on the externals That which influences us from the outside A good example is social media in all its varieties We no longer think for ourselves Those outside resources think for us Few of our personal beliefs are held because of personal experience It’s someone else’s experience we follow, we trust, we commit to Examine your own conscience, what are your deep beliefs That are the result of your own experience, honestly A more important question, what is your internal reference Clarity is not just a matter of mind, but of a connection with Heart, soul, and spirit as well, balance inner and outer I urge you to choose a few minutes of silence, stillness In the presence of Presence each day – your image of God Empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers and yearnings of your true self Every question has an internal answer, patiently waiting

George G
