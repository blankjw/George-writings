JUST BENEATH THE SURFACE

By George Gondron

JUST BENEATH THE SURFACE Our history is sacred Our memories essential Together, they lie just beneath the surface Memories for some, sadly, a dwelling place Stuck in the past, mired in resentment Memories for others, importantly, a reference Of lessons learned and joys cherished Some left behind, others retained Lessons that create our history Without a history, we have no story Without a story, we have no existence Our stories are collages of words They paint the pictures of our lives Some with strokes of stark blacks and whites Some, the vibrant colors of a rainbow Others, soft pastels from the Artist's brush What lies just beneath the surface For each of us is different, yet the same We all have pain, loss, regret, and disappointment As well as joy, happiness, laughter, and love Some more of one than the other, yet still the same I have a hope That you tell your story Tell it to someone who will listen To someone who will be present Tell them what lies just beneath the surface For in the telling, we heal old hurts a little more We celebrate some wonderful joys once again We consecrate our story, anoint our humanness It all lies just beneath the surface Tell someone

George G
