JUDGEMENT -- EVALUATION -- DISCERNMENT

By George Gondron

JUDGEMENT – EVALUATION – DISERNMENT Our most immediate way of understanding is through our senses Taste, touch, smell, hear, and see Our senses give us instant recognition of our environment One could state that our senses form immediate judgements There’s no need to evaluate or discern if something is hot or cold, If something is sour, a noxious odor, a blinding light, or loud sound Now add thought and feeling, and both without objectivity Without critical thinking, reflection, question, doubt, research, Analysis, and a sincere desire to know almost always lead to Judgements – knee jerk reactions grounded in all the psychological Defense mechanisms utilized to support our judgements You know, the fault never lies within me, it’s always the other And when you further challenge another’s judgement with Verifiable, factual, proven data, you also are judged The hallmark of the judging personality is a closed mind Their unwillingness or inability to reason or listen Evaluation opens the narrow lens of judgement With the ability to include as much data as possible for An objective result, not influenced by their own emotions Seeking to arrive at an informed decision absent preference It could be described as a ‘vetting’ process especially if it Relates to an individual seeking a position of power What are they really interested in, my/our wellbeing or their own And finally, there is discernment, that which few of us consider The ability to perceive, understand, and discriminate on a deeper level Usually referenced in a spiritual context but applicable in any Endeavor to arrive at a necessary decision Often it may be about choosing between two supposed ‘positives’ Or two supposed ‘negatives’ when both are not possible Between judgement, evaluation, and discernment The third is the most inclusive and preferrable With practice it can become our normal, more objective Please, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Become a discerning person, listen to your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, and they wait

George G
