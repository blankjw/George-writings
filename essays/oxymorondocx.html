OXYMORON

By George Gondron

OXYMORON 'Land of the free, home of the brave' For some, but not for all Land of opportunity and equality Also for some, but for fewer and fewer Oxymoron, a combination of opposite and contradictory ideas Here's one, 'peace of mind' When you're in your mind, you're never at peace Another, 'out of your mind' Now, that's when you're at peace Our lives are full of contradictory terms Most of which we seldom ever question For, in so doing, we'd have to examine our own behavior When it's much safer and much easier to ignore For we all know Ignored behavior will eventually extinguish itself Kinda like, 'out of sight, out of mind' Yes, the ego never questions its own motives For they are beyond interrogation In no way are they ever wrong, just ask yourself If the question is even posed 'Could I be wrong', answer; 'well of course not' To live in a world of contradictions Is to live a fantasy, a half-awake existence Assuming what is false is real, and what is real an illusion The train doesn't stop at 'Welcome to Truth' It must be discovered, by each one of us And sadly, for most it never is, the train moves on Focused on an external existence only, and always If we're lucky, the 'house of cards' will collapse The 'walls will come tumbling down' There may be a 'crack in the egg' that allows the light in And if not, we just keep 'running on the wheel' of distraction Speeding down the track, going nowhere Question the oxymoron's, hope the train 'derails' Allow more light to come in There is really nothing to lose, everything to gain We are a gift from Love, meant to be loving, always

George G
