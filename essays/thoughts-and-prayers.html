THOUGHTS AND PRAYERS

By George Gondron | Source: THOUGHTS AND PRAYERS.docx

THOUGHTS AND PRAYERS Maybe, just maybe after one of these mass shootings The first words out of a politician's mouth will be We need to pass some common-sense gun legislation Rather than their first words being, ‘our thoughts and prayers’ There have been 565 mass shootings this year We know it’s not a matter of if it will happen again It’s a matter of when And so far, thoughts and prayers have not solved the problem The problem is obvious As long as the NRA keeps padding the pockets of politicians As long as the ‘fear mongers’ shout to the rooftops ‘They’re gonna take our guns’ Nothing will change, absolutely nothing, and people die The latest poll shows that 90% of people support universal Background checks Another 65% support the ban on assault weapons and Large-capacity magazines, but people continue to die Isn’t it obvious that what’s important is being re-elected? Continuing to be bought off by the NRA Maybe if images of the massacred were put on TV And social media, things might change Or maybe if every politician were required to visit the scene Of the massacre before the bodies were removed Maybe that would do it, somehow I doubt it When compassion, kindness, empathy, and the belief In the sacredness of life, all life is nonexistent The result is random violence, disregard for others We should be evolving, but alas we are devolving Becoming lesser than our potential for community, for love I do think about the families that suffer I do pray for their healing and recovery But again, that won’t solve the problem Compared to the rest of the world we excel in gun violence Spend a few minutes each day in reflection, in silence Be still, listen to the whispers of y our heart, soul, and spirit If we open our minds the solutions are clear Be a part of the solution, not a part of the problem

George G
