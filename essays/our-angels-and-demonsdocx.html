OUR ANGELS AND DEMONS

By George Gondron

OUR ANGELS AND DEMONS We all have both you know, our angels and demons And if we are aware and conscious of that reality Then they can co-exist quite well within us It is when we are not aware that our character becomes flawed And we live so to speak, out of balance And if we believe we are more one than the other We lean on our weaknesses, not our strengths I’m sure you must be familiar with those weaknesses Our thinking is polarized and oppositional Duality reigns, right/wrong, good/bad, liberal/conservative We point fingers and become reactors versus responders We embrace all the psychological defense mechanisms And in either case, kindness, compassion, and love Is for the few and not the many, if at all And religion, all religion, throughout history Has been one of the major causes of this imbalance Only because it remains a cognitive reality And not a lived experience, all, with no exception Well perhaps one, indigenous spirituality Their lives reflect more than any organized religion Their connections to all reality, everything is sacred Like the cartoon character Pogo, when will we admit We have met the enemy, and it is us Since the beginning of recorded history, this is documented All religions, all beliefs, all mankind Our angels and demons both recognized in the clear light of day Keep us grounded, keep us humble, keep us fully human When we deny one or the other, we immediately are less human We are disconnected, less equal, less free, more judgmental Social media is filled with negativity meant to separate and divide But the real divide is within ourselves A disconnect between our conscious and unconscious Our inability to integrate our own shadow Choose a meditative/contemplative practice For just a few minutes each day be silent, be still, embrace Presence Empty your mind, listen to the whispers of heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, they have your answers, they wait

George G
