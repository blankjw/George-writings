RELIGION

By George Gondron

RELIGION I hesitatingly venture to offer a few thoughts About a topic since before recorded history began that Has been the perpetrator of more death, destruction, Division, and chaos than virtually anything else Organized religion, of all varieties It all rests on the misguided belief that religion is a Matter of the head, of intellect Of rules, regulations, rites, rituals, dogma, and doctrine And oh yes, let us not forget worship How is it even imaginable that a being, an entity Powerful enough to create this amazing universe Would need to be worshipped? Religion has been, is now, and will be forever A matter of relationship, a matter of the heart That is what all the great teachers, prophets, and poets Have been telling us for centuries To understand and experience the meaning Of unconditional love Of that which cannot be put into words And yet can’t be denied is beyond comprehension It is tasting both the breadth and depth of Possible human suffering and love Knowing that; that which Is, cannot not love And through that experience, great humility follows Along with great love for all humanity and creation Those that call themselves Christians Profess that the One in whom they believe Was both human and divine If that is so, Jesus experienced every human emotion And every action He pursued was about human relationships He left this world with two requests Love God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength And love your neighbor as yourself, that’s the ‘kicker’ I know of few that love themselves ‘warts and all’ Accepting their own ‘shadow’ material with humility Take a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Be in relationship with the One you believe is the Creator

George G
