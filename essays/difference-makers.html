DIFFERENCE MAKERS

By George Gondron

DIFFERENCE MAKERS Encounters, experiences, events, happenings Moments that make a difference Some profound Some seemingly insignificant Etchings on the soul that remain forever Absorbed through the senses When the mind ceases its chatter Being present to what is, not what was or will be The people that wander in and out of one's life Creating memories, some cherished, some forgotten All carry purpose What mattered at the moment was meaningful The intersections of life, the crossroads of chance Are there really any accidents? As I reflect on the difference makers, I must ask What did they teach me? What did I learn? I watch the memories play out on the screen of my mind, in the theater of my life Yes, the difference makers are many, I owe them much They are the catalyst of my existence People, places, sights and sounds All a part of the weave, an unbroken thread Perhaps in a quiet moment you can ask What, who are my difference makers? Can I appreciate all that I experienced? Can I be at peace? Can I be? Can I?

George G
