DUALISTIC THINKING

By George Gondron

DUALISTIC THINKING Few people in Western culture are non-dual thinkers They have a polarized either/or approach to life Which results in the inability to understand paradox None of their behavior is self-contradictory For example, they can call themselves patriots And still support a ‘draft dodger’ who makes fun of Wounded veterans Or a person who sells Bibles and has never read one A dualistic thinker is unable to see their own ‘shadow’ Their thinking is very black and white, right and wrong And their way is the white right way They call themselves Christians because they may attend Church services on Sunday and pay their fire insurance But beyond that, the Beatitudes have no meaning Feed the hungry, give drink to the thirsty, cloth the naked, Visit the prisoner, take care of the widow and the orphan No thank you, that’s asking just too much A dualistic thinker’s life is filled with words and ideas They love to complain but offer few reasonable solutions Especially if those solutions incorporate kindness, compassion, Inclusion, non discrimination, equality, and love for all Dualistic thinkers are ‘finger pointers’, blamers, deniers, And basically lack humility and patience, they are never wrong It’s virtually impossible to have an open discussion With a dualistic thinker for their minds are already made up They follow what supports their thinking which is basically What stirs their emotions, indoctrination through fear Clarity of perception brings the awareness that life is not Black/white, it is grey – not either/or but both/and Everything is connected, everything belongs, All is a matter of choice, love is a matter of choice Self-love means I will not be manipulated or used as a door mat I will not put my creative energy in a subordinate position Please, spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, Be in the presence of your image of a creative Source, of Isness Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
