VALUE OR VALUELESS

By George Gondron

VALUE OR VALUELESS How does what once had value, become worthless? Or, how does what has no worth become valuable? There is a positive and a negative image to value Through increased awareness, consciousness What once may have had value May no longer be significant or important As well, through lack of awareness or consciousness The insignificant remains important What is valuable on a human level is simple to see It is revealed through the culture in which we live And what is it our culture, our society holds as valuable? It is something that most refuse to see Most are afraid to admit It goes by the phrase, 'production and consumption' The ego ideal, the 'sine qua non' of capitalism at its best More is better, bigger is better, faster is better We value the lie more than the truth We trust our heads to the detriment of our hearts We waste as much as we consume There is no 'pot of gold at the end of the rainbow' There is only the 'carrot on the end of the stick' A carrot that encourages us to spend what we don't have That abhors the concept of delayed gratification And the most telling value A culture that places 'things' before persons A society in which people have only two major functions To produce the goods and spend the money The value of relationship has been replaced Replaced with and by power, profit, and greed The idea of capitalism at its root is not the problem It is when it became dehumanizing When people became just a number, a business statistic To con and dupe through advertising and marketing To spend what they don't have on what they don't need A capitalism that is devoid of spirit feeds on the souls Of those producing the goods, or selling their services "And all the King's horses, and all the King's men........."

George G
