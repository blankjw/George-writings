HISTORY INTERPRETED OUT OF CONTEXT

By George Gondron

HISTORY INTERPRETED OUT OF CONTEXT What is spoken, what is written always has a context A time, a place, a person, or persons at a point in history It seems to be the nature of a dualistic mind To find something from the past to support a current position That does not enjoin the same context in the present moment It follows the perennial argument of ‘what about’ When ‘what about’ happened many, many years ago It happens when we’re unwilling to see the fallacy of our position When we’re so closed-minded to even have an open discussion When we’re unwilling to accept a truth that is counter To our personal beliefs, beliefs not supported by experience All beliefs are subject to change when our minds are open It is the closed mind that can easily deny reality Can defend the indefensible ‘Yes but’, and ‘what about’ concern the past Not the present The prophetic voices throughout history Always spoke about change The last thing a closed mind wants to do is change Consequently, most prophets were killed Even the one Christians say they follow, Jesus Not because he was not a good Jew, he was But because he talked about and taught about change It’s impossible to be alive and not be in a context The problem becomes comparing the current context To past history, ignoring the reality of what is now The now moment is all that can be dealt with, not the past How to approach a current dilemma with a past solution Is a like attempting to reinvent the wheel, it’s already been done The current dilemma is about the now moment, not yesterday It’s about a lack of critical thinking, a preponderance of ignorance The inability to include the intellect of the mind With that of the heart, soul, and spirit in union with one another We have lost connection with our own inner core An innate internal reference where our true self abides Spend time each day with some silence, stillness, and Presence Listen to the yearnings and whispers of your inner truth

George G
