CREATIVE SELF-CRITICISM

By George Gondron

CREATIVE SELF-CRITICISM I’ve lost some of you already, just from the title For few of us like to be self-critical, or find it necessary to change Change for most of us is for the other, he/she/it or they, not me We’re just fine, thank you, while living inside our little ‘bubble’ Doing whatever it may be throughout the day to keep us busy Distraction is a wonderful companion, even if it’s religious distraction As long as we can point our fingers elsewhere, we need never point Them at ourselves, how convenient, and how dysfunctional All positive change starts and ends with me and with you, each of us Unfortunately, as of yet, we don’t have the power to change the group A reality that organized religion has yet to learn There is no such thing as ‘group conversion’ or transformation However, there is more than enough evidence for mass ignorance Sorry, maybe that’s a little harsh, maybe it’s mass manipulation Accomplished by playing on the emotions and not the intellect Afterall, who wants to be confused by facts and circumstances Hyperbole and ‘gaslighting’ are much more effective Easier to control the mob through nonsense than common sense With no exception, we all resist change, the ego fights against change Never realizing that change is a great gift, a future promise It allows us to let-go of that which restricts us and prevents Us from becoming, becoming more fully human and alive Awakening us to our own creativity and potential, our gifts Admittedly it rakes courage to be self-critical, it’s not for the timid It’s not for those that prefer to cling to their familiar Even when that familiar may be painful The first step to becoming more, trust what you know through Your own personal experience, not through what you only believe Our conscience is formed through experience, not through belief Just as a ‘knowing’ of the Mystery of the universe is experiential Not through logic or belief alone, ‘you will know them by their fruits’ A powerful way to get to know ourselves, a meditative/contemplative Practice – just a few minutes each day of silence, stillness, with Presence, your image of God, the name doesn’t matter Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out The mind will wander, the ego will resist, simply rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, and spirit

George G
