ESSAY

WHEN

By George Gondron | Source: WHEN.docx

WHEN In reference to ourselves, of all the W's that follow Who, what, why, where, and when When is probably the most difficult to answer The most difficult to answer in relation to people We know who they are, what they are and For the most part, why they are and where they are But in what circumstance is it necessary to let go When, is it no longer healthy to stay connected Connected in the usual and accustomed manner Regardless of the relationship Spouse, child, parent, relative, friend When it's no longer a reciprocal endeavor When there's no longer sharing and caring No longer give and take, ownership and not partnership But, only human advantage, a feeling of being used When there's no longer healthy communication Merely talking points of a shallow nature When the reality of being a nonperson is more obvious Than the reality of being in relationship Changing a relationship is difficult But, not changing an emotionally injurious relationship Is discounting oneself and the other It's unspoken permission to allow continued abuse And it's saying to the other, I don't respect myself If you can talk it through, you're fortunate If there's a compromising position, there's hope If not, then the last question remains, when? When do I walk away, let go, change the connection Do I value myself enough to alter the relationship And also hold hope for the future If not now, when?

George G
