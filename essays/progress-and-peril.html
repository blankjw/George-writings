PROGRESS AND PERIL

By George Gondron

PROGRESS AND PERIL In this age of social media, instant connection What's happening to our relationships? Is it simply more distraction? Added to an already over stimulated mind All progress carries within it a peril as well Have our relationships become voiceless? And in so becoming, less meaningful Surface acquaintances with very little depth Only words on a screen, images with no sound The media explosion is a seeming paradox Bringing awareness of the world's great tragedies Yet at the same time we insulate ourselves Even in our closest relationships With hand held devices that have no warmth A text, an email, an instagram, a twitter More preferable now than a simple phone call Which is free, with no long-distance costs Words on a screen carry no emotion, a voice does Words on a screen can't share a meal, a person can Words on a screen can't give a hug, arms do There is definitely peril in progress When it weakens human relationships When the trade-off for convenience Is more important than sacrifice The sacrifice of taking time to 'be with' Text to text cannot replace 'face to face' Emails can't compensate for human contact Digital expression can't replace emotion or feeling To know another is to know their whole being Not just their signature on a screen In relationships, balance progress with its perils, always
