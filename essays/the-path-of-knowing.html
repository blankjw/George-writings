THE PATH OF KNOWING

By George Gondron

THE PATH TO KNOWING How does one know? Much of what we think we know is unknown I heard about that I saw that on TV or the internet I read about that - all unknowing The only way to truly know something Is first hand experience All else is dependent on mediated information Data taken in through the mind only Not verified, not tested, not validated, not proven Yet, how emotional we become How judgmental, and decisive we appear Through what we don't know at all 'So-and-so said', then it must be true I heard (third party information) The source was reliable - how do you know? It seems, we love to accept the ridiculous Unable to think beyond the obvious Call into question the embellished exaggerations We gravitate toward 'naysayers' not proof-sayers It's easier to be critical than accepting Why give them the benefit of the doubt They are the wrong color, the wrong gender Wrong belief system, wrong culture All ploys to feel better about myself and, My limited knowing, my inflated educated ego The path of knowing is and will always be experience Contact, connection, and sharing with the other Listening before we judge, learning before we condemn Chose the only path to knowing Chose experience

George G
