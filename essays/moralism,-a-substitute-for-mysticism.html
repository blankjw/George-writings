MORALISM, A SUBSTITUTE FOR MYSTICISM

By George Gondron | Source: MORALISM, A SUBSTITUTE FOR MYSTICISM.docx

MORALISM, A SUBSTITUTE FOR MYSTICISM A mature belief system is not about morality About right or wrong, good or bad, who has the right God What we call our ego, our ‘I’ is very limited in understanding To any significant degree the importance of and the existential And relational nature of spirituality or mysticism For most, religion, in particular the Christian religion, is an Arm’s length performance activity of do’s and don’t’s based on Prescribed guidelines formulated through rites, rituals, dogma, And doctrines All the aforementioned relate to a cognitive understanding A teaching about one’s belief system that has nothing to do With an existential experience, a relational experience On a grand scale the attempts to ‘know’ the God that is Professed or proclaimed are all about ‘doing’ something Whether that ‘something’ is about membership or some Religious formula or activity, some action versus a way to be In order to ‘know’ All the great mystics and spiritual teachers are about ‘being’ Not about ‘doing’ About being ‘empty’, embracing the ‘nothingness’ Less about action and more about being acted upon Organized religion’s primary purpose is to be a catalyst Toward the greater objective of an experiential relationship With what our unspoken and often unrecognized internal Yearnings are really all about – like coming home The great mystics and spiritual teachers throughout history Did not reach their levels of awareness and personal Relationships with Mystery or God because of formal education It requires no education at all, it only requires a choice That choice is more about doing nothing than doing something A meditative/contemplative practice of becoming empty In order to become present to That which is seeking us A few minutes each day to be silent, to be still, to be present Focus on your breath, let go of all thoughts, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They makeup your innate inner mystic, your true self They bring balance to your life, have your answers, they wait

George G
