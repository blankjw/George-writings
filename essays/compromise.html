COMPROMISE

By George Gondron | Source: COMPROMISE.docx

COMPROMISE An important process in all areas of life, compromise Except of course when we’re one of the parties After all aren’t we always right, correct, without error? I’d be willing to wager we seldom, if ever Reflect on how we compromise our own values, our ethics, Our beliefs in equality and integrity Especially if those values, ethics, beliefs, and integrity Create an inconvenience in our lifestyle, desires, and attachments For you see, it’s so much easier to defend when we compromise When we turn a blind eye to obvious injustice When we support a narrative that we know is false When we judge another before ever knowing their story When all that’s wrong with the world has nothing to do with me Or let’s narrow that down to the relationships in our lives If we believe in compassion, kindness, altruism, and love Do we compromise those relationships when it’s inconvenient? When we’re unable to self-reflect and see our own shortcomings? Maybe compromise begins with examining ourselves In the clear light of day, with honest self-criticism Few there are that choose that path We survive in a culture where fault always lies elsewhere Even within our families and personal relationships We seem to expect other’s values, ethics, and integrity To remain firm, but what about our own Do we defend the person or persons treated unjustly? Do we offer kindness and compassion to the marginalized? Do we speak up when speaking up is a risk? Do we find excuses for defending the indefensible? Admittedly we are not perfect but there is a way To strengthen our hesitancy to remain firm to our better nature Spend a few minutes each day in silence, with stillness In the presence of your image of a creative Source of this universe The name doesn’t matter, God, Isness, Consciousness, etc. Be committed to a daily practice, eventually spending thirty Minutes to each sit, in the same place, around the same time Listen to the truth held in the whispers of your heart, soul, spirit They hold your true self, without compromise, they wait

George G
