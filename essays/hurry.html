HURRY

By George Gondron

HURRY The American pastime, hurry How much we lose, in our hurried lives Surface emotions with no depth, no substance Fleeting thoughts running ahead of the moment Arriving at our destination before we are there Completing a task before it is finished No time to reflect, no time to enjoy, we're in a hurry And the risk of it all, of our hurried activity Only one It is the risk of a life that's never been lived For being in a hurry, guarantees one fact We cannot be present to what is, in a hurry We can only continue with what is familiar And in so doing we miss the strange We miss the unusual, the possible, the new Our only pause is in our sleep That brief respite of rest and recharging So that we might return to 'hurry' tomorrow And if I ask, 'why do you hurry?' The common reply, 'I must finish this task' 'I have something to do or somewhere to go' It's never, 'I just need to be' For 'to be' and 'to hurry' don't fit together How does one stop or cease all the hurry? Most don't, and most never will, ever Unless they stumble, or fall, or fail in their hurry Get knocked down by some unfortunate event It's seldom by choice But those few that choose another way A life without hurry, a life of presence They discover Life before the end of the journey

George G
