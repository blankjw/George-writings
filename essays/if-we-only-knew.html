IF WE ONLY KNEW

By George Gondron | Source: IF WE ONLY KNEW.docx

IF WE ONLY KNEW We are, each one of us, amazing beings We have approximately thirty trillion cells in our body One hundred million neuros in our brain And sixty thousand miles of blood vessels The heart beats more than 2.5 billion times in a lifetime Yet there are those who believe this is all happenstance There is no supreme being, for after all A being that allows for childhood cancer, deafness, blindness And other debilitating diseases can’t be very concerned About this amazing universe, about pain and suffering About constant war, genocide, and natural disasters Obviously, many terrible atrocities have been committed As a result of what is called ‘free will’, one’s ability to choose Also, there was a man some two thousand years ago That reminded his followers of the following statement ‘For truly I tell you, if you have faith the size of a mustard seed, You will say to this mountain, ‘Move from here to there,’ And it will move, and nothing will be impossible for you’ Free will and faith, both powerful individual choices Understood by a few, lost to the awareness of most For after all isn’t it so much easier to blame and point fingers Than it is to question our own choices on every level And of course, if we don’t believe in a benevolent supreme Being Then faith is never an issue for it’s useless Only, it seems, a personal numinous experience awakens us To the importance of personal choice, free will, and faith As it should be, for the important discoveries of life Are not taught, they are experienced They resonate with our innate inner core, our internal reference Which few without critical thinking, reflection, and some form Of meditative/contemplative practice never realize consciously We in the West suffer a disconnect between head and heart All ‘things’ in a broad sense are more important than people Please, spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still Be in the presence of Presence, your image of a creative Source Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They are your guides, they have your truth, they wait

George G
