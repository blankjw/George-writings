IMAGINATION

By George Gondron | Source: IMAGINATION .docx

IMAGINATION Imagination, the lynchpin of all creativity The butcher, the baker, the candlestick maker Science, medicine, all manner of exploration The beginning of all forms of artistic endeavor Music, sculpting, colors on canvas, words on paper, dance In all the arenas of performing, stage, film, TV, etc., etc. Imagination is the beginning point, the power to dream The willingness to take an idea and turn it into a reality The refusal to give up, no matter the cost, imagination Over and over again, history has shown us the power of Imagination and the benefit of its potential Success is always the result of failure at first Imagination does not perceive failure as a deterrent But only a challenge, the opportunity to try again Would that our own imagination were strong enough To push us to grow, to change, to explore What the ego refuses to even acknowledge, our creativity Our innate ability to know Mystery, Isness, Universal Consciousness To think outside the ‘box’, to refuse to ‘settle’, to imagine We too soon become creatures of habit, habitually limiting Ourselves to familiars that have no meaning whatsoever Never lifting the curtain of our delusions and illusions To see clearly our own connections to all of creation Our unlimited ability to ‘know’ and not just do To ‘be’ and not just produce and consume Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life It’s not just some ‘airy fairy’ practice to reach nirvana or know God It has been documented to show benefits to the mind and body That medicine alone cannot do It will awaken what lies asleep within yourself, your own imagination Your potential and creativity, and yes, your connection to Mystery Quantum physics now hypothesizes the universe is conscious Is evolving and expanding, is benevolent and we are its ‘stardust’ Take a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Focus on your breath or a brief mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, your imagination

George G
