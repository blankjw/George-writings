THAT WHICH IS UNLIMITED

By George Gondron

THAT WHICH IS UNLIMITED As unfortunate as it may be, we do have an unlimited Source and supply of ignorance in our nation If it could be transformed into wealth, we’d all be rich, very rich The sadness of this reality is that it can be remedied But to do that we’d have to educate ourselves But that seems to be too difficult Much easier to depend on that wonderful source we call Social media – a reliable, fact based, scientifically proven source And if you believe that, you are one of the truly ignorant It should shock you to know that 56% of adults Have a literacy level of 6th grade or lower But then again if you watch some of the interviews Of the average American on the street it’s not surprising at all Ignorance seems to be the new normal A frightening prospect for the future for all of us We have traded intellectual learning for emotional stimulation Trusting our feeling and not our intellect, we need both The most important decisions are the result of research Of critical thinking, deep reflection, with an open mind They are not the result of emotional ‘knee jerk’ reactions Just as wisdom is not the result of age but of a clear mind An emotional mind is never clear, it’s already biased, prejudiced When we process from a place of ignorance beliefs and opinions Are formed almost instantaneously without the benefit Of reliable information, input, or data Our familiar coupled with our emotions prevents us from being Objective, from being interested in knowing the truth For a closed mind already knows the truth, facts don’t matter Once a mind is fixed in its beliefs, the possibility of change Is useless and unproductive, objectivity has been lost Life is viewed from a polarized dualistic lens, no possibility for balance Currently we desperately need balance Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for focus and balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Empty your mind focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
