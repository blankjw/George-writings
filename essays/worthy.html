WORTHY

By George Gondron

WORTHY The one who discriminates, excludes, separates, and passes Judgement without experience is using nothing more than Projection, seeing in others what they fail to see in themselves The blame game, the denial game, all the defenses we use to Justify our own behavior reveal we are unworthy of ‘patting’ Ourselves on the back for our exemplary behavior, for it is Not worthy of imitation, we are not so-called ‘model citizens’ To be worthy is correlated with our personal level of morality As we mature, as we become more aware and conscious So also does our level of morality become more attuned to The social dimensions of humanity in general We are not islands unto ourselves, but we are connected to Each other through our common humanity All of life, physical, intellectual, emotional, spiritual, is about Continued growth through various stages and states Lawrence Kohlberg, a renowned psychologist, theorized six Levels or stages of morality, the first stage begins in infancy We do good we get rewarded, we do bad we get punished Stage two, behavior driven by self-interest Stage three, behavior seeking social approval Stage four, behavior obeying authority conforming to social order Stage five, balance of social order and individual rights Stage six, universal ethics, behavior driven by internal moral principles As we approach an important moment in our nation’s history We will vote for a presidential candidate and down ballot choices Use Lawrence Kohlberg’s six levels of morality and reflect on the Worthiness and morality of your particular choices The future of our democracy, of our children and grandchildren Is at stake, use critical thinking, listen to your conscience Don’t be led by your emotions alone Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Seek a discerning mind, be a critical thinker, reflect Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out When the ego resists, the mind wanders, rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they wait

George G
