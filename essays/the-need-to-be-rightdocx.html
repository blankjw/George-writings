THE NEED TO BE RIGHT

By George Gondron

THE NEED TO BE RIGHT Probably more than all else, the need to be right Has been responsible for anything and everything That in the end, had a less than favorable result In truth a result that created a more negative than positive Outcome, bent to the will of a need to be right The human mind, or better referenced the human ego Is so inclined to only see its own point of view That all indications to the contrary fall by the wayside Or get swept under the rug for a polarized ego is impervious To rational reason if that reason is opposed to its own view And the initial source for the ego’s point of view is endless Political, religious, economic, educational, etc., etc., etc Oh, and let us not forget the realm of relationships How many relationships dissolve or live in quiet tolerance Because of the need to be right And what psychological traits can we assign to this need Probably the most notable is a closed mind A lack of awareness and consciousness that is so dramatic That the possibility of an outcome, other than the one the ego Holds and defends cannot be imagined or considered Like the old saying, ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ Cooperation, conciliation, compromise, objectivity, goodwill, And reconciliation all falls by the wayside to a closed mind Truth doesn’t matter, facts don’t matter, even experience Contrary to the ego’s need to be right – all don’t matter The ability to ‘let go’, to embrace change, to have an open mind Are all lost on the ego’s need to be right, regardless the outcome Sad but true, a closed mind lives in ignorance, refusing to learn It is a self-made prison living in the past or future, missing now There is a way out of the prison, a way that leads to balance Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence And naturally the ego will resist, even more reason to do so Just as the need to be right, it’s a matter of choice, a better choice Empty your mind, focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, and they wait

George G
