GROUP DYSFUNCTION

By George Gondron

GROUP DYSFUNCTION It is not only the dysfunction of the individual Which of course includes all of us sometime and perhaps Most of us much of the time, but the dysfunction of the group Can be just as dysfunctional or even more so For groups don’t have consciousness, only individuals do Like the committee that was tasked with designing a horse And ended up with a camel Group think can be and often is very dangerous For seldom does any one member have the courage To stand against or even survive the animosity of the group We witness it in every major organization; politics, religion, Education, corporate board rooms, etc., etc., etc. And sadly, it’s often the lone voice that speaks the truth That becomes the scapegoat and is ostracized You know that mentality of, ‘please don’t confuse us with the facts’ Especially if the facts effect our ‘bottom line’ in a negative way And that includes money put in ‘plates’ on Sundays Not until the mission statement of all corporate endeavors Includes some recognition of their intent toward the betterment Of humanity will the primary motive for their existence, which Is more and more annual profits for CEO’s and shareholders Ever change, the unspoken narrative, profit first people last Greed, without exception is insatiable, enough is never enough More is better, bugger is better, faster is better, always When ‘things’ are more important in our individual lives And our communal and corporate lives, dysfunction exists If we all don’t matter, then no one of us matters For we are only one part of the whole, all connected If the groups to which you belong do not allow for open Discussions, questions, or challenges, leave They are not about progress but about control A full life is a balanced life, inner and outer Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for inner balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out When the mind wanders simply return again and again Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, they have your answers

George G
