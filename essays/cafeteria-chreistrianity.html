CAFETERIA CHREISTRIANITY

By George Gondron

CAFETERIA CHRISTIANITY What follows can be applied to all formal organized religion And to other human belief systems such as Buddhism Which is not a religion but does postulate a way of living Many are proud to proclaim we are a Christian nation The great Indian leader Gandhi once said, ‘I like your Christ, I do not like your Christians. Your Christians are so unlike your Christ.’ The attraction of a cafeteria is that we get to pick and choose What we want to eat, and Christianity is much the same way Our society, our culture in general is anything but Christ like All religion or belief system that postulates the sanctity of human life Automatically assumes individual personal sacrifice, it’s called love But come on, really? That’s much too difficult so we, like the cafeteria We pick and choose what we want to practice in our Christianity Personally feed the hungry, give drink to the thirsty, clothe the naked, Visit the prisoner, care for the orphans and widows, turn the other Cheek, walk the extra mile, love our enemies, welcome the stranger, Let me relieve myself of what’s ask of me by occasionally putting my Butt in a pew and money in a plate and call myself Christian You know, a Christian that supports a modern day Ceasar Whose character and personality are as far away from being Christian As A is from Z, one would have to be blind, deaf, and ignorant to not Recognize the obvious, isn’t it interesting how we justify our choices Genuine love is hard, it is a sacrifice, it is selfless not selfish It’s so much easier to defend and make excuses for our dysfunctions To point fingers and to blame when we each are to blame As we slowly descend toward a place we will only regret later And we will say, ‘we didn’t know’, but we do know, we deny There’s always a price to pay for chosen ignorance but unfortunately, The innocent will suffer as well, those who don’t fit Ceasar’s approval Or maybe, our approval, those of us who are cafeteria Christians In ways large and small we are all guilty, but we can choose change Add to your experience a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Your ego will resist, the mind will wander, rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, inner guides They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
