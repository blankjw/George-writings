DO WE KNOW

By George Gondron | Source: DO WE KNOW.docx

DO WE KNOW As time passes, as the years slip by If what we know does not change, we have not lived What we believe and what we know are often opposites If our beliefs are based on someone else's ideals On some external practice, some intellectual understanding We will never know who we are, in our deepest core To know is to experience, to not know Is throwing the gift of life back into the face of the Creator It is never knowing, for this I have been born, for this I have life Knowing happens through failing and falling, being knocked down And getting back up Not once, not twice, many times Knowing happens through defeat more than through success It happens though doubt, want of faith, lack of confidence Through touching the inner poverty and hunger I carry inside It happens through risk, through being vulnerable Not just playing it safe, grasping at security, for security's sake It happens not through joy alone, but more often through pain But then, we can build our own prisons, wall ourselves in Safe in an illusory existence, empty of significant meaning Void of empathy, compassion, deep connection I prefer the pain of truth, to the lie of false gratification I am present to now, I let go of the past, the fear of tomorrow A fully lived life experiences many small deaths and resurrections A life that's willing to crawl through the mud to discover the lotus To die to the useless facade that covers my yearning A pretend life is not a life at all, it's simply an existence A pretend life is lived only through the mind Stripped of heart, soul, and spirit A life that knows no humility, no meekness, no modesty A life that is self-absorbed, lacking in otherness That does not understand that in weakness there is strength In less there is more, and wealth does not lie in riches When you have lived a purposeful life You will know When you have not, you will also know Don't reach the end, knowing you have not

George G
