THE HEALING PROCESS OF HUMAN CONNECTION

By George Gondron

THE HEALING PROCESS OF HUMAN CONNECTION Our healing, not physically, but psychologically, emotionally, the healing of our very souls or psyche happens person to person Without human interaction we experience an emptiness, an ever present yearning for connection, no man/woman is an island We are social beings meant to be in relationship In fact, the best definition of life, life is relationship With the surge of social media in all its many forms many are losing their personal relationship skills, supplanted by handheld brightly lit screens, desktop computers, or giant TV’s We are attempting to fill our human yearning for connection through an addiction that only demands more and more screen time but which can never really satisfy, we remain empty, unfilled In the early 1940’s infant mortality rates of newborns in hospitals was higher than normal As it turned out, there were too few nurses for too many babies The children were not being held, touched, embraced enough As soon as more nurses wear employed, the mortality rate dropped A clear indication of the importance of human connection Most who enter nursing homes at an elderly age die within a year Could it be because of inadequate human interaction? Admittedly there are degrees of connection, some better than others Women who go the ‘half-way’ houses to escape spousal abuse, often want to return home within a few weeks, familiar is hard to break It is incumbent upon all of us to become the best human ‘connector’ It begins with examining not only our personal strengths, but even more so, our personal weaknesses, our expectations and defenses Maybe it’s our ‘black and white’ thinking with no room for grey Are our ‘either/or’ thinking with no room for both/and Or maybe me, mine, and us and no room for they or them We need balance in our lives, inner and outer We need to awaken our own inner core, our internal resources A suggestion, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out When the mind wanders, rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit

George G
