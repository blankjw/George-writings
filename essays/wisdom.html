WISDOM

By George Gondron

WISDOM The path of wisdom is self-knowledge True wisdom has no bias It is the result of a clear mind Accepting with clarity what is, as it is The saying, ‘the wisdom of a child’ Is the result of a mind as yet uncontaminated With programming, conditioning, and manipulation Age, therefore, is not a precursor for wisdom When childhood is lost, and the ego is formed We then gain wisdom in the lived experiences Found in the crucible of our lives The burning away of the lies and half-truths Accumulated along the journey The tortured letting go of what we have clung to That now seems useless and unimportant Careful to not be too hard on ourselves For our foolishness and gullibility in the past To be self-reflective and critically examine Our journey requires courage and strength Not condemnation and retribution As the saying goes ‘We cannot know, what we do not know’ But, once we know we must accept The responsibility for change, positive change We can no longer pretend and blame Pointing our fingers elsewhere It never works, it only keeps us dysfunctional The clarity that wisdom brings is not without pain In fact, pain can be expected For that clarity will reveal our own patterns and habits That contributes to our own impaired functioning Including those relationships that keep us stuck Clinging to hopes and wishes That may never manifest nor come to fruition Our choices are ours and ours alone The sooner we become aware of that fact The sooner we can heal and move forward

George G
