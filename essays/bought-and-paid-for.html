BOUGHT AND PAID FOR

By George Gondron

BOUGHT AND PAID FOR Our so-called democracy is for sale Our Constitution and rule of law are superseded Currently, and in our recent past, bought and paid for By corporate America through their hired 'guns' Their representative lobbyists, in plain sight Too clear to be denied, yet we do We stick our heads in the sand Turn a blind eye and a deaf ear To what is right in front of our faces I do believe that most of us do care But our political situation has become so corrupt We don't know what to do It's like trying to put out a raging forest fire With a plastic water gun Here's the question, is it too little too late? Are we on a decline that we can't stop? A slippery slope that is destined to implode Is extreme wealth, stacks of 'greenbacks', Unsatisfied greed, and unlimited and unchecked power The root of the problem? And if it's not, what is? We want to point our fingers at others The 'other' is the problem, right? But the situation is much more sinister than blame The culture itself is sick, is 'ill', is empty of substance Some say we're morally bankrupt, perhaps they're right Some say all we care about is our 'material' existence Others say we have lost our ability for compassion For kindness, cooperation, and collaboration Are they also right? Whatever the problem, whatever the answer It begins and ends with self, with me and with you Without a paradigm shift in consciousness and awareness We, as individuals, hold the hope for the future Be a part of the solution, not the problem Question yourself first, before questioning others We each can be a change for the better, the better of all

George G
