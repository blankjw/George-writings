WHILE ROME BURNS

By George Gondron

WHILE ROME BURNS Indifference, the nemesis of compassion No longer a small neighborhood, now a global community Access to world news in just minutes Apathy, lack of concern, empty of emotion Perhaps if I don't know, it doesn't matter Surely I'm not responsible for what I chose to ignore Does my silence convict me? What does being a part of humanity mean? How long can I detach myself from those who suffer? It seems I can, forever In my small 'fireproof' existence My secure enclosure as all burns around me The needs of "Mine" outweigh the needs of "Theirs" Like the horse with blinders, I chose to see in a narrow frame I can't widen the lens too much or too far It may create concern, it may awaken my conscience It may rekindle a flame within that has long since become only the blackness of coal Love is hidden in the piece of coal It can be reignited, relit, brought back to life Compassion renewed, apathy rejected To once more feel my connection to all of humanity The kinship of all life The power of unity The reality of hope
