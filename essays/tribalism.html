ESSAY

TRIBALISM

By George Gondron

TRIBALISM The consciousness of tribalism extends no further than the tribe When awareness and consciousness have limits They are by definition, exclusionary, limited For what my tribe defines as normal Makes all else not normal, different, unacceptable And who are these tribal people? They are many, they are manifold, they are legion They 'bump up' against one another Creating friction, disharmony, and malcontents They are the professors of 'ism's', secular or sacred My 'ism' is much better, much truer, much wiser Than your 'ism' They prefer the familiar, the routine, the habitual Clinging to their melancholy of the past Fearing the possibility of the future And missing the now entirely Tribal consciousness is low level consciousness Compassion, loving kindness, and empathy Apply to only my tribe, and maybe not even then They are only words in the mind, but not in the heart We, us are the tribe, not they or them Life is given to all equally, not specifically Value is given to all universally, not individually It is only the human mind that creates difference, nothing else We are taught to be discriminatory, tribal, not the same We are taught fear by not understanding the perceived different Whatever, whoever that different may be The heart unites, the mind divides The heart listens to all, the mind listens to the tribe And in the end our little tribes are like islands Floating on an ocean of humanity meant to be connected If we come from a loving Creator If the universe is a benevolent place Then we are one tribe, all human beings Loved by the One we may call God Not because we are good But, because God is good

George G
