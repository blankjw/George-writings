TENSION OF THE OPPOSITES

By George Gondron | Source: TENSION OF THE OPPOSITES.docx

TENSION OF THE OPPOSITES Meaningful growth, increased awareness and consciousness, Happens between the tension of all opposites Knowing and not knowing, yes or no, right or wrong Between what is obvious and mystery A curious mind is the foundation of a meaningful life Our common connection to all of humanity, we want to know Our motivation to know is built into our psyche, our soul It’s easily observed in little children with their ability to ask Endless questions, their comprehension of the answers Is really unimportant, it’s the questions that matter As we become adults it seems we question less and The trade-off is, we assume more, forming opinions Without reliable information or facts, without discernment The importance of discernment cannot be overstated Honest discernment, choosing between right and wrong, Perhaps between two rights, even two wrongs, when attached To some underlying expectation, contaminates the process With an underlying bias or prejudice In our Western culture, it’s probably very true that most of Our decisions are made with an underlying preference Making discernment a useless unnecessary endeavor The tension or anxiety we may experience in our lives Is not our enemy, it is our silent supporter, encouraging us To grow, to move beyond our familiar, to become more To reject fear and negativity and embrace possibility But as we become habituated to take in more and more Information from social media without any filters We fall prey to half-truths, deception, propaganda, and lies To develop a discerning consciousness, to have balance Embrace a meditative/contemplative practice Spend time each day in silence, stillness, with Mystery Focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to your inner guides, they hold your true self

George G
