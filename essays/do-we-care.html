DO WE CARE

By George Gondron | Source: DO WE CARE.docx

DO WE CARE Have we lost all belief in kindness, compassion, love? Is it important to only our small little group? Is the game now just maintaining the status quo? Organized religion is losing its believers Churches are closing, but not all Mega churches, TV evangelists they survive, they thrive They've learned how to stir the emotions Like the used car salesman, they sell Christianity And they really do; 'pass the plate', send money, tithe Buy your 'fire insurance' Prosperity Christianity is now the name of the game Bolstered by 'be good' and God will reward you The message of the Beatitudes dies a slow death The message of 'follow me' holds no promise 'It's harder for a rich man to enter the kingdom Than for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle' Falls on deaf ears, or, that can't possibly apply to me Any belief system that separates and divides is false If it doesn't unite it's dangerous Life can best be defined as relationship If not, then what purpose for life How we care, matters How we connect, matters Religion seems to survive only through membership It's become a club to which we belong But there's no challenge to change, to grow, to transform Only to belong, follow the rules, don't step out of line Life is 'messy', to grow is to be challenged It's about action, not platitudes Security is the enemy, it allows us to rest on our laurels The first step toward change is acceptance Even if the acceptance is, 'I don't want to change' For without awareness, change will never happen Learn the difference between religion and spirituality It's slumbering inside of you It only needs to be awakened Be still, be silent, listen and you will begin to care, for all

George G
