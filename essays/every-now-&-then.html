EVERY NOW & THEN

By George Gondron | Source: EVERY NOW & THEN.docx

EVERY NOW & THEN It may be something we've read Perhaps a conversation overheard Observing a small act of kindness Watching a child at play Or absorbing a beautiful sunset Moments when something breaks through Something slips past our certainties Our absurdities weaken Our convictions aren't so filled with guarantees If we're lucky, if we're blest A great gift is doubt, is lack of certainty Especially the doubt of what's not experienced The doubt in believing another's propaganda Ah, 'don't believe what you hear' 'Only half of what you see' I can hear, see, taste, touch, smell, feel and think My senses are my trust, my truth I don't deny your truth, don't deny mine I honor your experience, please honor mine I am open to you, be open to me We are connected, we are not opposed We are unique and also the same Every now and then something breaks through Every now and then there's a crack in the shell Every now and then truth slips in Every now and then I hear the Whisper Every now and then

George G
