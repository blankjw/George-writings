HOW WE LEARN

By George Gondron

HOW WE LEARN The ways we learn are unlimited But there is only one way That leads to our personal truth It is the learning gained from personal experience To abandon that significant learning Is to abandon ourselves So learning is never a matter of Why, when, where, or what It’s a matter of how Even more significant is learning gained From not doing, but from being And that in itself is a process For most of us don’t know how to be We know only how to do We can properly be called ‘human doings’ Versus ‘human beings’ We have learned through repetition Through conditioning, through programming That our importance is about what we do Not about who we are And with that mindset we remain objects And not subjects From this reality conflict arises For genuine love is about subjects, not objects The most important learning is often the result Of non-doing Simply being still and silent Emptying the mind of its incessant repetitive thoughts Probably the most important activity we could embrace Is a daily meditative or contemplative practice To escape that which in the end Is of little importance and no value Without balance in our lives Between doing and non-doing We remain only half human at best We cannot do anything to our full potential Including most importantly, loving others

George G
