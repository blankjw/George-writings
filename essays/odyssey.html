ODYSSEY

By George Gondron | Source: ODYSSEY.docx

ODYSSEY We all have within us a yearning for an intellectual or a Spiritual quest, but most never heed the yearning We fail to pursue our own odyssey, unique to our life And unfortunately we don’t live in a culture that fosters An idyllic inward journey, a discovery of self Which is probably the most important journey of our lives Even if we have experiences of that yearning The ego loudly shouts, ‘you don’t have time, it’s useless’ ‘Focus on the outer world, it’s much more important’ If we were to examine the single most important issue We have as human beings in our Western culture It would be we have no connection to our innate inner core The internal resources we all have, more prevalent in women Than in men, for example ‘woman’s intuition’ We seem to believe that all our problems are either The fault of others or they can be solved intellectually But without a connection between inner and outer, they can’t With all the apparent chaos, division, and discord In our nation today, have you ever seen on social media Hey, we need to wake-up, individuate, recognize our own Individual shortcomings rather than pointing fingers elsewhere Wish that were true but I don’t see it happening Our egos are too defended, too resistant to change or growth As the saying goes, ‘I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’ Open minds are a rarity in this culture, and it’s getting worse Not only have we ignored an inner connection We also refuse to further educate ourselves We sadly, follow social media for our education, frightening Which translates to a life lived in fear as opposed to love In fact most people equate love to an emotional feeling Which is a fantasy, for genuine love is an action, a sacrifice Discover your own odyssey, become fully human, not half Become a human being, not a human doing Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your life Each day spend time in silence, be still, embrace Presence Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Discover your true self, it has your answers, it patiently waits

George G
