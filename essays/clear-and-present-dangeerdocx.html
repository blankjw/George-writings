CLEAR AND PRESENT DANGEER

By George Gondron

CLEAR AND PRESENT DANGER At the risk of being labeled an alarmist this brief observation Is the result of much reflection and deep thought Concerning the current and ongoing situation in our nation Recently I went to see the movie Civil War A current day depiction of what could realistically happen In our own United States I use the term realistically as a result of several observations As well as the reading of various publications available to all Much of what is depicted on social media from all sources Is opinion versus fact and most of those opinions Are meant to generate fear, doubt, or conspiracy theories Opinions which divide, separate, and exaggerate the negative To listen to the responses of what would seem to be simple Questions of the ‘person on the street’ by journalist concerning Our own history, current politics, religion, etc., etc., etc. Is more than frightening especially when confronted with Actual factual data that supports the correct answer The answers attest to the importance of an education meant to Teach history in an accurate and factual manner The answers also reveal that ignorance is our greatest enemy As the saying goes, ‘you can lead a horse to water, but you can’t make them drink’ We seem to be more easily convinced about almost anything And everything, not because of our intellect, but because of Our emotions, especially emotions grounded in fear or doubt We are being divided primarily by two major institutions Religion and politics, championed by voices who seek power And control, which is ironic when we recognize that our Democracy is suppose to be about ‘we the people’ and religion about ‘love one another’ Please, educate yourselves, don’t be swayed by opinion Research, reflect, listen to your ‘better angels’ Choose a little silence, stillness, and Presence each day For a few minutes meditate, focus on your breath in and out Choose balance, mind, heart, soul, and spirit together They reveal your true self, have your answers, they wait Clarity of perception is needed now more than ever

George G
