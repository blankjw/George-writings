IMAGINATION

By George Gondron

IMAGINATION The elixir of creativity, our imagination Brought to life with the brush, the chisel, the pen, the hands It is said that what we can imagine we can create We can become Without the humanities, especially art and literature Philosophy and history, where would we be? There is a fear that without them we will become only robots Concerned only with that which makes us less than human Our attention focused on what we acquire, our material desires More about what we do than who we are Surrounded with things that give limited gratification Things to which we become attached And in the end they control us, they own us, not we them What the heart can imagine is vastly different Different from what the mind imagines What the soul and spirit dream of, what they envision Throughout human history there have been a few A few who imagine with much more than just their minds Their creativity is more about relieving the suffering of life More about finding peace within ourselves So that we may find peace with others Those that turn our attention to the present moment The now of life as it relates to ourselves and to others They are the peacemakers, the arbitrators, those of empathy Their imaginary tools are kindness, compassion, and selfless love They are more concerned about giving than receiving They are few in number, but their message is strong Their imagination encompasses the universe, all of humanity It unites, embraces, and speaks to a common core in each of us What it imagines never separates, never divides, never isolates One life from another, all are included, all have value The only difference they see with each and every life Is the unique gift that each of us carries They have the ability to awaken that gift if we but listen The parts are never greater than the whole But the whole is great because of the parts We are the parts, we each have a gift, we all can imagine

George G
