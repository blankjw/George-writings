PRAYER

By George Gondron

PRAYER A sensitive subject, prayer So many definitions, so many different opinions A simple matter actually Three overall categories Petition; an asking, for myself or others Praise; gratitude, acknowledgment, and thanksgiving Presence; a sought personal relationship The same in all major faiths and beliefs I wonder if an omniscient Creator doesn't already know my and other's needs? I somehow believe, all-knowing means all-knowing I wonder if a benevolent First Cause desires my praise? I suspect a humble and charitable God is not waiting anxiously for my affirmation But presence, presence is central, fundamental Presence is the key that opens many doors The prayer of presence is transformative The prayer of presence is to be silent, still, and empty It is the demonstration of my willingness to be in relationship with With That which gives and sustains all of life Right relationship presupposes appropriate response It is the first desire of the Ground of All Being To be in love with that which Love has created It is the desire of the created to reciprocate that love as instilled in the soul Mutual relationship, mutual compassion, mutual humility In love with each other, eternally, 'ad infinitum'

George G
