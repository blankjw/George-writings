COLLABORATION

By George Gondron

COLLABORATION Have we lost the ability to be collaborative with each other? To compromise, to cooperate, to be conciliatory Perhaps to an outsider looking in Witnessing our cultural interactions The answer might be yes The divide between us, among us, within us Seems to be growing more and more exaggerated Our minds are more and more polarized Extreme myopic thinking, very dualistic thinking The exception to the rule seems to be The individual who can comprehend That a fully human life is always both/and As opposed to either/or Life is situational Actions taken today may be considered advantageous But tomorrow, absolute wrongs Human thinking, the evolution of consciousness An increase in awareness has always been When the individual or small group of individuals Perceives through a happenstance experience Something new, something beyond the status quo Human nature’s first response is to resist We’ve all resisted what is opposed to our familiar Even when that familiar is dysfunctional Those few, who offer a new point of view Are more often than not, rejected or ignored Whether those beliefs were scientific, educational Religious, political, social, or economic And of course, the extreme rejection is death History is replete with those who have died for their Beliefs, especially religious and scientific beliefs They were unwilling to reject their experiential truth Human potential, creativity, and truth Are internal, not external processes Discovered in silence, stillness, with Presence A meditative/contemplative daily practice Necessary moments to hear the whispers of Isness

George G
