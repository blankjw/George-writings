OPEN TO GRACE

By George Gondron

OPEN TO GRACE Some would define grace as a Divine favor granted freely Just as easily, what follows could have been titled OPEN TO CHANGE, open in both cases being the operative word For a closed mind neither experiences grace nor change If a mind is open, grace flows abundantly Most minds remain closed as a result of teaching Teaching that suggests we can know all simply through intellect Through knowledge, not true All knowing happens as a result of experience The conundrum is, the ego resists change Especially the adult ego It’s analogous to being thirsty but refusing to turn on the tap The water is readily available but the resistance to change Is so much greater, that’s the culture we live in The greatest motivator to change is deep emotional pain But the ego will do anything and everything to avoid pain Addiction and distractors are great avoidance techniques Take a pill, have a drink, smoke a joint, grab a shiny object They do work and for many, it’s the only game in town There’s nothing more dangerous than a mind that refuses To change, dangerous to themselves and others Once we have one success of breaking out of our Self-made prison, able to have an open mind Change comes more rapidly, grace flows more freely We experience with new eyes and ears what has always been We take the ‘road less traveled’, make new choices Our awareness and consciousness expands We put possibility and probability before negativity and failure We no longer live in the past nor fear the future The moment is now Part of the change journey is balance – inner and outer To engage all our faculties, not just our minds, our egos Begin a meditative/contemplative practice daily Spend a few minutes in silence, be still, and embrace Presence Empty your mind, focus on your breath, be here now Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they await

George G
