LOVE'S SXARS

By George Gondron

LOVE’S SCARS The most meaningful scars you will ever experience That are held for a lifetime Are those of a deep and significant love That type of love always knows pain Whether the love endured Or perhaps it ended Matters not Those relationships are never forgotten A piece of the heart is given away A part of the soul belongs to the other And it could not have been any other way It is the type of love that is experienced In the very first encounter In the psychology language of Carl Jung It is the male’s connection to his anima And female’s connection to her animus In some sense, it is an experience of wholeness Found in no other way A light in the darkness revealing a certain sanctity A flawless diamond discovered in the deep A coming home to a familiar yet unknown place A glimpse of love without lust But a physical experience when joined in love, There is no separation, no distinction between the two It is as it should be, a wholeness like no other Spiritual teachers, poets, and mystics Have written throughout all of history Of a similar love experienced in the intimate Relationship with one’s image of God The author of the Biblical book The Song of Songs professes this love with elegance To know and experience this manner of love Is to take time to be – to cease doing To sit still in silence with Presence With Unconditional Love – each day everyday Twenty to thirty minutes is all that’s required What patiently awaits is worth the practice

George G
