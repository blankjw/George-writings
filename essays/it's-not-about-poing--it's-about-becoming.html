IT'S NOT ABOUT POING - IT'S ABOUT BECOMING

By George Gondron | Source: IT'S NOT ABOUT POING - IT'S ABOUT BECOMING.docx

IT’S NOT ABOUT DOING – IT’S ABOUT BECOMING In our Western culture we are better defined as Human doings rather than human beings And usually when we meet the stranger we are concerned about Three things – their full name, what they do, and where they’re from All of which distort and color our unspoken opinions about Who they are, before we ever give ourselves the opportunity To know them, our minds are contaminated with preconceived Ideas around name, occupation, and location If we are able to see the forest and the trees, both/and We are less likely to prejudge without knowing Unfortunately, that doesn’t seem to be our current reality When 54% adults have a literacy level of 6th grade or lower You can be assured we live in a nation that prejudges A nation where most of its citizens get their information From that reliable source we call ‘social media’ Better know as ‘social propaganda’, third party information And the only people we can blame are ourselves We become what we attach ourselves to in thought, feeling. And deed – rather than becoming we remain puppets Dancing to the beat of someone else’s drum, it’s so easy We don’t have to critically think, doubt, question, research Just accept what we’re being fed that stirs our emotions To hell with intelligence, discernment, intuition, He/she said it, so I believe it, motivation grounded in fear Works every time after all love has no place in politics And obviously love, truth, honesty, integrity, morality, decency, The rule of law, equality and justice for all no longer matter at all They are only words lost on a mind impervious to learning Please, don’t ask me to ‘become’, I enjoy my familiar Even though my familiar is dysfunctional and keeps me stuck Becoming is much more difficult, but as the saying goes, Everything worth having demands change, a letting-go But the ego doesn’t like to let go, it would rather resist Please, spend some time becoming, learn to meditate A few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, your inner core They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
