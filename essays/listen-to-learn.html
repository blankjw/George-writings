LISTEN TO LEARN

By George Gondron

LISTEN TO LEARN Now this is a novel idea, listen to learn For as you probably know Most listen to respond, not to learn In fact, most listening is passive, not active And a passive listener really has no notion of what you’re saying For they are busy either finishing your sentences for you Or, formulating a ‘knee jerk’ response Without processing what has been said in any depth Studies conducted on how we listen Bring us any number of examples of our passive listening Once our minds have been closed to anything counter To our own beliefs And notice, most of our beliefs are not the result of experience Personal experience, but someone else’s narrative We become emotionally attached to the message To a message that has no intellectual basis at all In other words no validated truth, third-party information Honestly, we make meaning out of everything that happens And unless we question that meaning, we make assumptions Most of which are in error Supporting the fact, that we don’t listen to learn Mostly, we listen to either defend Or accept what we already agree with And consequently, our world grows smaller and smaller Hearing and seeing only what fits our agenda Here’s a challenge, whatever you are thinking, feeling, Doing or not doing, understand it is a choice If you can learn that, you are on your way to wholeness On your way to becoming fully human, fully alive You cease to defend and begin to understand, to change And we all need to change, our potential is infinite A valuable tool that will help us change A meditative/contemplative practice just a few minutes a day Silence, stillness, in the presence of Presence, your image of God A time to empty our mind of its incessant repetitive thoughts It is a time to know our true selves, discover our truth Listen to the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
