QUESTIONS

By George Gondron

QUESTIONS We must learn to live with our questions without answers For often it’s the questions we ask That have more value than the answers we might receive To question, doubt, to seek has more validity Than to merely settle with what is To settle and accept without criticism is to stagnate Ask your questions with an open mind A mind that’s closed cannot accept an answer That’s opposed to its mindset, it already has an answer We don’t grow without a challenge Without a little anxiety in our lives We need to be constantly letting go of our familiar In order to move forward toward the new Complacency is a state of mind that is threatened By almost anything that asks them to change They don’t like to be questioned about their opinions To be questioned is a threat to their intellect, their position In most cases their support for what they believe Is nothing more than ‘third party’ information He said, she said, they said It is an insult to our intellect to accept what is without question It demeans our personhood to abandon reason A disrespectful or hostile response to a question asked Speaks volumes not about the question But about the respondent There are no unfair questions in an atmosphere of freedom Only the lie makes excuses, the truth never does In our culture, we lack balance in our humanity All our thoughts, feelings, and actions relate to externals Nothing about an inner core, an internal reference No connection with the innate ‘true self’, a giving self Connected only to the egoic ‘false self’, a needy self Take a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Empty the mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold the questions you need to ask They hold your truth, your healing, they wait patiently

George G
