WHAT HAS VALUE

By George Gondron

WHAT HAS VALUE? A lost airplane, a capsized ferry, lost lives Hours of daily media attention Untold dollars spent searching, asking, discovering Mourning, deep sadness, loss of loved ones But what about? 16 million hungry children in the U.S. One out of eight go to bed hungry each night No daily media, no news, no outrage Every 3.6 seconds someone dies of hunger Where is the mourning and sadness? 800 million on the planet suffer from malnutrition 15 million children die every year from hunger Nearly one in four people live on less than one dollar a day Where is the value? I wonder why there is not daily media coverage of these realities? And the most sorrowful fact of all, it's easily solved Those with the power, the finances, the resources Where are they? How do they, do we, not hear the cry of the poor? One does not hear nor see what one does not wish to hear or see This news does not make money, or create wealth, or increase one's power Perhaps the question is not, 'What Has Value', but rather, 'What Does Not Have Value'? All of life, all of creation has value We discover the value by looking, hearing, experiencing We discover by being, not merely existing We discover by loving Through being loved By That which loved us into being That which connects us to all of life, all of creation Within us, between us, and among us Love Itself

George G
