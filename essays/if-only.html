IF ONLY

By George Gondron

IF ONLY We all have them, there are no exceptions Those times of 'if only' And the regrets we carry attached to the 'if onlys' Are of different degrees, have different meanings Each has a significance known only to ourselves At least for the most part, if not denied But make no mistake, we all have them Some relate to the risks not taken, the missed opportunities Others for our inability to simply say, 'I'm sorry' If only I had said, 'forgive me, I didn't mean it' Or maybe it was my urge to give you a hug, but I let it pass Or say, 'I appreciate what you did, it wasn't necessary' Not enough time, that's a big 'if only' If only I had more time, it would have been different But you know I'm in charge of my time, no one else And as usual, blame is always the solution For not enough time, for missing the 'if onlys' It's never my fault but someone or something else Isn't that the cure for all defense, blame Yes, we all have our 'if onlys', but most of us want to forget Second thoughts are always a betrayal Of what might have been, or could have been And the saddest part of our 'if onlys' is very clear There are no 'do-overs', we can't go back That's a funny thing about life We can't change what's already happened, or not happened We can only deal with now And that is an option, really the only option Dealing with now, the present moment Don't let another day pass with regret With another 'if only' Whatever the reason, the purpose, the goal, the person That yearning within that asks for our attention Don't let it pass, there may be no tomorrow Be silent, be still, listen to the whispers Life is not an 'if only', it is now, not yesterday nor tomorrow Please, don't let it end with a trunk full of 'if onlys'

George G
