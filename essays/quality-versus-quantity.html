QUALITY VERSUS QUANTITY

By George Gondron

QUALITY VERSUS QUANTITY The meaning of time, if time has a meaning Is more about quality than quantity More about the wonder and amazement of our very existence In particular, the things we take for granted Our innate qualities we call our senses Taste, touch, smell, hear, and see And then we add consciousness, our ability to think and feel Sadly, hardly ever do we stop, rest, and reflect About these amazing and wondrous qualities Rather, our time is about this, that, or the other Always chasing something, someone, somewhere, some place Life follows a pattern of more is better, bigger is better, and Faster is better, always When do we lose our ability to wonder? At what age do we cease to be curious? When does our energy turn to producing And no longer cares about being? And if we lose these qualities how do we have deep conversations About mystery, about beauty, about value and virtue When all around us is meaningless input and constant noise All we encounter adds not an ounce of value to our existence On a merry-go-round hoping to grab the brass ring Distracted by the next shiny object Clothed in our protective armor for fear of being vulnerable We judge others before we know them when we ask What do you do, what’s your last name, where do you come from Our lives rush before us as we focus on quantity versus quality To be fully human and fully alive is to know one’s self first To humbly know we are all more alike than unalike, all connected One part of the entire fabric of creation, a spoke in the wheel Through a meditative/contemplative practice we touch Mystery We discover our own creativity and purpose, we consciously connect We are joined in our sameness and grow in our differentness Take time each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Embrace the quality of your thoughts, feelings, and actions Not the quantity, less is more, too many is too much Empty your mind, listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
