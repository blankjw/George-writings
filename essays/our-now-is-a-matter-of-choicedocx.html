OUR NOW IS A MATTER OF CHOICE

By George Gondron

OUR NOW IS A MATTER OF CHOICE Joy, true happiness is uncaused, it is spontaneous On the other hand, unhappiness is a matter of choice It is not until we own our choices, that our now will change Love is never the reason we remain in unhappy circumstances Only fear holds us hostage, with an endless list of excuses And if our unhappiness is shackled to a relationship We will cling with the hope of ‘what if, maybe, perhaps’ He/she will change, the situation will change, something will change Change means moving from the familiar to the strange And for most, the strange is too risky too vulnerable So, they remain with the pain of the familiar The ego will always resist change Even in the face of pain, it does not allow itself to let go Pain is neither a curse nor a blessing, it is simply a messenger As are all our emotions, saying ‘pay attention to me’ ‘I’m attempting to tell you something, show you something’ Always remember a once good choice that ended badly Is not a life sentence, we can choose again, we can let go In our culture, most people don’t change as a matter of choice They may change when their circumstances become unbearable This is sad since all life is about change, all of nature But most live lives of quiet tolerance Once a person decides to embrace change, take a risk And subsequently, realize the benefit of that choice They will look forward to future change, future personal growth Hopefully, they will pursue as well their own spiritual growth Embrace their internal core, their inner resources And choose a reflective meditative/contemplative practice A necessary balance in our frenzied distractive world Choose inner peace, a daily few minutes of silence, stillness, And Presence, being in the presence of your image of God Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Thoughts that go nowhere and do nothing Breathe in and out a short mantra to maintain focus Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, your inner truth, they wait Remember, no choice is a choice

George G
