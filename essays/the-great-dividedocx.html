THE GREAT DIVIDE

By George Gondron

THE GREAT DIVIDE Division seems to be the norm, the acceptable The dual mind reigns supreme Permeating every venue, all arenas, regardless of content Never before has agreement on almost any topic Been so fractured Truth is losing ground The lie is now the standard, the model, the pattern We seem to be in love with fantasy, untruth While reality falls by the wayside We trust those in power who say they understand Tell me, how can you understand without experience? How can your wealth, especially inherited wealth Understand poverty, need, and lack? How can the pedigree behind your name Understand the difficulty of acquiring a higher education? Without the means, without resulting in enormous debt How can your Sunday morning sermons and exhortations Adequately relate the spiritual message If you don't live it yourself? Why should we trust at all? The division between those who govern And those they represent Between those who teach and those that seek learning Between those who preach their religious doctrine And the souls who seek true spiritual nourishment Is an ever expanding gulf, a divide impossible to bridge Is life devolving rather than evolving? Is it now one step forward and two steps backward? Is it now entirely, 'I've got mind, you get yours'? Or is this simply the result of extreme consumerism? The amalgamation of excessive power, stress on individuality Rather than individuation If my life matters, so does yours, and theirs, and all life Maybe this experiment in our Republic must fail In order to discover the new, maybe Or perhaps we will come to our senses, if it's not too late Perhaps we will understand our need for each other, perhaps

George G
