EXCUSES

By George Gondron

EXCUSES There's a great divide between an excuse and a reason As great, many times, as that between fact and fiction And we can defend the excuses as deftly as the reasons Defended so well, that no one suspects Yes no one, at times not even ourselves What is it that may be offered, so obviously harmless And yet is too much of a threat to be chanced To be examined, weighed, or open for consideration The excuses arise always as a defense, as a protection Protecting the ego, the small guarded self That prefers not to change, wrapped in its fragility Projecting an image of strength and importance But it's all for show for the outside world And someone asks, 'have you heard about this process?' 'About this path of change and well-being' You listen with feigned interest and false attention Already the excuses forming in your mind The inner voice of, 'I'm perfectly fine as I am' The voice that whispers, 'you'll regret this' 'You'll have to sacrifice, give up something' 'Be less than what you now are' 'You'll no longer be the captain of your own ship' The excuses are endless and always well stated Presented as reasons but wrapped in deception For those who make their excuses and proclaim them as reasons Who refuse to risk, take a chance, or be vulnerable Life always carries a certain hollowness An emptiness avoided through repeated behavior Through a steady, incessant, continuous 'doing' Seeking constant gratification and calling it happiness And in the end It's clearly no more than life lived in pretense, lived as a lie The heart knows, the soul knows, the spirit knows While the mind continues to make just excuses Be loving to yourself, value yourself Listen to Who knows, hidden in others, hidden within Hidden in plain sight, look, listen

George G
