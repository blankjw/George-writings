GRATEFUL

By George Gondron

GRATEFUL A truly humble person is always grateful For they know intuitively that every life experience Holds a lesson, a lesson to be learned The choices we make intentionally or otherwise Are nothing more than teachers About what to do, what not to do, what to continue to do To discern the necessary paths we pursue As a result of the many life experiences we will have Is more often than not, a matter of the heart, not the ego As the fox tells the Little Prince ‘What is essential is invisible to the eye, It is only with the heart that one sees rightly’ To be grateful for every life experience is to understand And learn from whatever the resulting outcomes To see beyond the ego’s desires and attachments That in the end are nothing more than illusions and pipe dreams Who’s underlying motives are based on ‘getting not giving’ About a false self formed and molded from ego needs Absent the wishes of the heart, soul, and spirit Wherein lies our true self connected to all creation We are all more alike than unalike As the Country song says, ‘we all bleed red’ In every experience, we play a part actively or passively Become a discerning person and ask what part did I play? And further, what lesson did I learn? cease to defend A defensive nature is nothing more than being in a ‘rut’ And a rut is like a grave with both ends open We can escape through either end through better choices We each are responsible for our own well-being, no one else Pursue a meditative/contemplative practice each day Spend a few minutes in silence, with stillness, in Presence Presence being your image of a creative Source, a personal God In those few minutes empty your ‘monkey minds’ Of their incessant and often repetitive thoughts that go nowhere Dwelling on the past or the future and missing the now Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold the answers, your truth, your true self, they wait

George G
