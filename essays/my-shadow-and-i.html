MY SHADOW AND I

By George Gondron

MY SHADOW AND I We all have one, a shadow Seen only as a result of the light Cast upon the ground on a bright sunny day Racially neutral, not gender specific, common to all Ah, but what about the shadow we all have within That which we run from in our nightmares Always dark and foreboding Seen in our judgments and condemnations of others It is they, not we, who are wrong, they are enemy If we only knew, our shadow is not our prosecutor, not our nemesis There to befriend, there to welcome, there to teach us Aid us in seeing our own shortcomings projected onto others Aid us in seeing the unrecognized gifts we hold apart Our internal dialogue, we're not good enough, smart enough, or whatever enough Our shadow is our conservator of humility Our guardian of our ego inflation Our protector of our assumed differences The light cast on our internal shadow is easy to find It is found in the silence and stillness It is found in quiet reflection It is found in the presence of the All In the discovery I am led to accept In the acceptance I am led to change In the change I am led to Life

George G
