LEFT, RIGHT, MAYBE MIDDLE

By George Gondron

LEFT, RIGHT, MAYBE MIDDLE How is it possible, or practical, or beneficial To be totally left Or totally right There must be a middle way Whatever the venue Either/or is destined for failure My way or the highway will not succeed Compromise, negotiation, understanding Is the path to inclusion, not exclusion Most differences that exist, when examined Are the result of a lack of awareness Incorrect information, false data, propaganda Cultural bias, and conventional belief To be taught with prejudice To be surrounded by discrimination Is to fall far short of reality The other's subjectivity becomes our objectivity And on either end, left or right, truth is lost Some say it is in the middle that change happens In the tension of the opposites Not in polarization, but only with both/and Reason without faith denies the mysterious Faith without reason is only subjective They are the two sides of one coin, faith and reason Just as mind, heart, soul, and spirit Are all, parts of the whole, existing together We separate for examination and analysis But we unite for the better and best choice Love does not discriminate, love unites Avoid the left or right Choose the middle way, choose love

George G
