A POINT OF REFLECTION - THE LACK OF

By George Gondron

A POINT OF REFLECTION – THE LACK OF Maybe it’s not just a matter of closed minds Of low levels of literacy, or even of ignorance That has created such a wide divide in our nation today A nation in which there is more than enough for everyone Including the immigrant, the handicapped, the disenfranchised, The homeless, the uneducated, the jobless, the gay and lesbian, The black person, the brown person, the Asian, native American The atheist, the unchurched, the widow, the orphan, the addict, Those different from me, from you, from us Maybe, just maybe it’s more a lack of empathy, of compassion, Of understanding, of awareness, of kindness, of inclusion, Of connection, of sameness, of humanity Maybe it’s too much ego and not enough heart Too much selfishness and not enough soul and spirit Maybe enough is never enough for those that have When in truth there is more than enough for all If we are our brother’s keeper, it seems to be limited To those like ourselves, our small circle Not until we understand that we are more alike than unalike Will there ever be enough for all, with no exception Power that benefits the many is true power Power that benefits the few is called greed and selfishness Much of the above is the result of a deep conversation With a very dear and important friend of mine I hope you have a friend like that, one you can listen to I write a lot about closed minds, illiteracy, and ignorance But little about matters of the heart, soul, and spirit Perhaps it’s a sign of the disconnect between our heads And our hearts, no sense of our own inner core No internal reference, all ego, all ‘monkey mind’ Choose to embrace a meditative/contemplative practice It will connect you to your inner guides Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breath in, breath out Your ego will resist, your mind will wander, simply rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
