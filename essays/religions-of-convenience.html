RELIGIONS OF CONVENIENCE

By George Gondron

RELIGIONS OF CONVENIENCE The three primary religious belief systems Islam, Christian, and Jewish have been responsible for more Death over the past 6,000 years than any other reason We exclude Buddhism for two reasons One it’s not a religion but a philosophy of the mind And two it’s by nature a nonviolent system Interestingly, the ‘big three’ believe in the same God With one primary difference and several lesser ones Christianity claims the human Jesus as its teacher Islam claims Muhammed as its teacher And the Jewish faith follows the Old Testament teaching Believers of all three easily defend their dysfunctional And judgmental actions towards any and all who are Not in their ‘flock’ through picking, choosing, and Interpreting literary passages to fit their purposes Similarly, all three also have passages concerning the care And wellbeing of its followers and of strangers All having to do with kindness, compassion, inclusion, and love Yet it’s more convenient to point out the ‘don’ts’ than ‘dos’ Within each belief system, individually and collectively It seems the easier choice for most formalized religious belief Is to condemn, judge, blame, and deny others, within and without All the three major systems that follow the precepts of Care, kindness, compassion, inclusion, empathy, fairness, and Not the least of many, love, seem to do so with little major emphasis From the entire membership, only lip service, absent action Individual members, and few there are, profess and practice Without a paradigm shift in awareness and consciousness universally There is little hope the future will be any better A six thousand year history is proof enough of its failure, all sides The task falls therefore to the individual, a personal transformation Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your existence Just a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence A time to empty our ‘monkey mind’ of its noise To focus on our breath or a short mantra To hear the whispers of our heart, soul, and spirit They bring balance, have answers, hold our true self, they wait

George G
