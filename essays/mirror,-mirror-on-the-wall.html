MIRROR, MIRROR ON THE WALL

By George Gondron | Source: MIRROR, MIRROR ON THE WALL.docx

MIRROR, MIRROR ON THE WALL A daily exercise, look directly into the mirror for one minute Merely observe, without judgment, being objective And if you're able, you just may find If you can be honest, a stranger peers back A silent reflection of many personas None of which is really you For who we say we are, who we think we are Who we present to the outside world For many if not most, is not at all who we really are Rather, it is the person we wish the world to see Either through our own expectations or The perceived expectations of others And living behind the mask for so very long We lose who we where, if we ever really knew And we truly have become strangers to ourselves And the fear of that admittance, even for a moment Is so very strong that we continue the facade Feeling trapped in a prison of our very own making Of our own choosing, for it is a choice to pretend But the person in the mirror, beneath the reflection Has one simple yearning, no more no less To be known as they truly are To be known as the One who breathes, dances, sings Our life into being heart beat after heart beat The One who knows us at our deepest core In our brokenness and mistakes In our selfish choices and our fickle motives In our distractions and busyness In our full humanity with its weakness and strength It is only through this yearning that we can be mended The game can't be changed with just a partial yielding Without a spiritual connection of heart and soul Without a letting go into the abyss of Love With the courage through grace to say 'I can do this no longer' There is more to me than the mask that I wear The Mirror on the wall, and in my soul, tells me it is so

George G
