INTERPRETATION

By George Gondron

INTERPRETATION Truth and reality don’t fail Perhaps that’s what’s behind the saying ‘Don’t believe what you hear and half of what you see’ What often fails is our interpretation All of our life’s experiences color our interpretations How we think, how we feel, what we do or do not do On a cloudy day, the sun still shines The symbol for water is H2O, whether in the form of liquid, Steam, or ice – how we hear and how we see is as important As what we hear and see Do we ‘see through a glass darkly’ or with clarity? Repeated behavior, healthy or unhealthy, right or wrong Leads to trusting that same behavior Just as repeated lies will eventually become the truth The choices we make about what we read, who we listen to And what we observe, are extremely important Because they all influence our interpretations, our reality As the old saying goes, ‘When something seems too good to be true, It usually isn’t’ Under close examination, a lie can’t survive the truth But first we must question, doubt, reflect, and research A willingness to accept without critical thinking Is an insult to our own intellect, our ability to discern To be controlled by our emotions is a treacherous path Which almost always leads to an unfavorable end Relying only on our emotions is akin to ‘the tail wagging the dog’ Without balance in our lives, between inner and outer Something in our ability to be fully human will suffer It’s liking attempting to complete a puzzle with pieces missing For balance, for clear interpretation adopt a path of daily Meditation/contemplation for just a few minutes Awaken your own inner lie and truth detector Experience silence, be still, and be present to Presence, to Isness Learn the power of relationship and connection to all creation Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self as opposed to the egoic self They have your answers, they hold your truth, they wait

George G
