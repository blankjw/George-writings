I LOVE YOU

By George Gondron

I LOVE YOU When you speak those three little words Either out loud or silently in your heart Understand their significance, most don't Don't take them for granted and ask yourself What do they mean? Not only for yourself but to those you say you love For far too many love is simply a feeling Albeit a different feeling for a friend, a parent, a sibling A significant other, a mate But if we can comprehend that love is an action And not a feeling That doesn't diminish the feelings we have for others So maybe if we can find another word to name Those feelings, then perhaps describing love as an action Can and will make more sense If love is wanting the other to become all that they can be Physically, intellectually, emotionally, and spiritually And if our actions can help in that endeavor We can better understand love as an action We know in our own hearts When someone does something for us If it's an act of love Or simply an obligation, a have to, a necessity We know also if there's a string attached To what we have received in the name of love If there's an unspoken expectation hidden in the act The act of love, then is not really love It is the rare person indeed that understands love Unconditional love, no expectations love But they do exist, it is possible, for all of us When one has experienced that kind of love In that experience, there is no choice but to reciprocate For other people, the stranger, and even the assumed enemy Follow a contemplative path, you will discover it In the silence, in the stillness, Love will come It has always been there And It whispers, I love you, unconditionally

George G
