THE FAILURE OF RELIGION

By George Gondron | Source: THE FAILURE OF RELIGION.docx

THE FAILURE OF RELIGION In our current society if you were to ask the average person On the street, ‘what is the purpose of religion?’ Seldom would you hear, ‘It is to love and care for all people’ Ironically, when we examine religious history There’s not a lot of evidence to support that statement We it seems, especially the male of our species, are very adept At manipulating and interpreting religious writing to fit Our desired end/ends – with little proof it’s ‘to love and care for all people’ And isn’t it interesting how many people, and in particular men, Speak for ‘God’ – a direct line to their particular deity One could put forth an argument that literalist organized religion Is actually an enemy of the loving Father that the human Jesus Taught about, who was not a Christian but a faithful Jew Attempting to challenge the Jewish leaders of his own time Ah, but I regress Have we forgotten the millions that have died in the name of God? The Crusades, the burning of witches, the Inquisition, etc., etc., And then there are ‘mega-churches’ built at the expenses of Millions of dollars, and millionaire TV evangelist fleecing Widows living on social security in the name of ‘God’ As if we can buy our way into their idea of ‘heaven’ It would appear that Christianity as it is practiced today Is not about relationship at all, it’s about worship About putting people in pews and money in plates Not about the Beatitudes, the Sermon on the Mount Not about the words of Jesus, ‘love the Lord your God With all your mind, heart, soul, and strength and your Neighbor as yourself’ Christianity teaches worship, not relationship Christianity’s teachings are cognitive not experiential If you are a believer of a creative God, a relational God Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, be present To Presence – empty your ever busy ‘monkey mind’ That you might hear the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your God DNA, they have your answers, they wait

George G
