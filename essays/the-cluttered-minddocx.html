THE CLUTTERED MIND

By George Gondron

THE CLUTTERED MIND Distraction, repetition, rumination, preoccupation Better known as the cluttered mind Monkey minds, fly-paper minds, obsessive minds Never present, never focused, never aware Most of our waking moments are spent this way We miss the now; living in the past, worrying about the future There is no peace of mind, for in your mind you're never at peace We become our thoughts, identify with our thoughts We are so much more than our thoughts The mind desires first to control, even in passivity The mind desires to be right, even in acquiescence The mind, consciousness, the ego, inventors of progress, comfort, and gratification At the same time, destroyers of humanity The mind, like a cancer, can and will kill its host Perhaps not in physical ways, but in emotional ways In the ways of compassion, mercy, peace, and justice To have a healthy mind, there must be a balance There must be heart knowing as well as head knowing There must be a time for silence and stillness, not just activity Down time, empty time, presence time The moments to discern what is real and what is waste What is significant and what doesn't matter What needs to change and what is changeless To be and not just to do We have what we need If we but take the time

George G
