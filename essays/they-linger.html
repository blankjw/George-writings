THEY LINGER

By George Gondron

THEY LINGER In the shadowy corners of my mind, they linger Unanswered questions, partial pieces Mysteries that exceed comprehension Tugging at my consciousness Pulling me forward, in a misty fog As the morning sun burns away the dew They slip back into the abyss of consciousness In the hidden places, seldom revealed Faint movements, barely perceptible They remain, a felt unchanging fascination Muses of perceived importance Patiently waiting for my mindfulness These yearnings, these anxious desires Hold the answers to questions of life They await in the silence The hush of the early dawn The deepening shadows of an approaching evening These alluring riddles linger in the stillness Emerging from the nothingness From the depths of my soul I can almost savor their importance Breathing in their inestimable essence These lingering questions Who poses these queries? I do And who answers? That within me that knows That has always known Found in the silence Discovered in the stillness Revealed in the Presence

George G
