WHO WE ARE, NOT WHAT WE SAY

By George Gondron

IT IS WHAT WE DO, NOT WHAT WE SAY The measure of a human life is in what we do, not what we say Is the ability and willingness to learn, not to teach We know others through our interactions, our story telling And yet, the evidence reveals we’re more interested in telling Others what they ‘should’ be doing, which is just another way Of saying, ‘this is what I expect from you’ Probably the nexus of the saying, ‘don’t do as I do, do as I say do’ It’s also interesting to note that what most of us call learning Is not learning at all, it’s merely absorption Like sponges we soak up what’s offered with no filters, no critical Thinking or reflection – monkey see, monkey do Attracted to that which stirs our emotions, especially negative ones Remember. an uneducated mind is an easily manipulated mind Anxiety, tension, frustration, apprehension, are gifts They are asking us to take another look, look beyond the obvious They are not our enemies but motivators for personal change If we are willing to cling only to our familiar, even though painful We are proclaiming in another way, we’re unwilling to change

A closed mind is an indication of a mind that resists change A mind that ‘may not be right, but is never wrong’ When there is not a strong connection between head and heart Then our sense of kindness, compassion, and charity Is manifested not as direct participation with those in need But participation at a distance, people without names The true nature of love is to sacrifice, to embrace the suffering Of other, to be in the fight for their wellbeing, their freedom There is an eternal truth, actions always speak louder than words There are also some psychological truths called defense mechanisms All about defending our personal dysfunctions, in particular Around personal interactions, our relationships Balance is so very important to attain a life of wholeness A connection between inner and outer, mind, heart, and soul Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Focus on your breath or a brief mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit You are not your thoughts, you are your actions

George G
