SILENT COMPANION

By George Gondron

SILENT COMPANION Watching, listening, always present This neutral observer, my silent companion They give no advice, offer no counsel But yet, just their closeness gives me pause They are more than conscience, so much more How, I do not know It is neither a contrary nor an affirmative feeling Not my mind, or ego if you prefer For there is no judgment from their presence No attempt to fashion my choices No opposition to whatever I do Or, do not do It is as if in the awareness of my breath They give me pause Pause to ask the unknown questions The ones I'm always too busy to ask Pause to reflect before the next word The one I'm so anxious to utter Pause to see what I overlooked before Before turning away and losing the moment Pause to listen with more than my ears That I might fully hear what is not being said Pause to respond and not just react Pause to acknowledge my deepest feeling And not just my thoughts That must be it, that must be the reason For this silent companion of mine I am more than grateful, I am very humble For they bring me awareness Awareness to pause

George G
