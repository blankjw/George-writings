MORAL FIBER

By George Gondron

MORAL FIBER The title of any narrative brings to awareness Word pictures in the mind For in reality words are nothing more than symbols Symbols of alphabetical letters thrown together Which create the images we think about For we don't see words in our imagination We see the images the words bring forth So, what are your images about the phrase, 'moral fiber'? Throughout recorded history Great teachers of compassion and justice Of what it means to be an authentic human being Have arrived at their conclusions through their own Experiences, positive and negative, regarding moral fiber The capacity and the courage to do what is morally right Under any circumstances is not an easy path But it is the path of becoming fully human What's interesting in most modern cultures Is the fact that we don't teach much about morals We seem to leave it up to our religious belief system But I sometimes think it's because the real teaching Is seen in our behavior, not our words Morality simply means doing the right thing All the time, under any circumstances The words 'except when' don't apply And, if you're like me those 'except when' moments Do come around, but as I grow older, and perhaps wiser They don't come around as often as they used to The fear sometimes connected with 'doing the right thing' No longer holds sway I've learned, through grace, to listen to my heart And not just my head We all at times have put morality on the back burner For whatever the reason, but in truth There is little that is more important than having Strong moral fiber, a nature of compassion and resolve To do the right thing, to stand against what's wrong To be willing to risk for what our hearts know is moral

George G
