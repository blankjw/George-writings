THE PERIL OF UNIFORMITY

By George Gondron | Source: THE PERIL OF UNIFORMITY.docx

THE PERIL OF UNIFORMITY To be the same in thought and action as all others Is to be nothing more than a follower, an imitator An unspoken agreement of accepting the Conditioning, indoctrination, and programming Of one's group, one's gender, one's religion, one's culture There are very few matters that can be called uniform Beyond our basic humanity, our fundamental origins To follow the crowd, any crowd, blindly Is to be a coward, a pessimist, a deserter to Our own innate ability of understanding truth Of being a participant in life and not just an observer There is no such thing as an innocent bystander To remain silent in the face of injustice is cowardice Equanimity is not defined as noninterference Equanimity is the ability to remain calm In the face of trying circumstances Uniformity is not about values, integrity, or ethics It is merely about following without question Unity, on the other hand Is bringing diverse groups into harmony Harmony for the greater good Being able to put aside our differences In order that the whole may benefit, not just the parts Ignorance is born of not thinking, not questioning Never doubting, never risking or being vulnerable Those who seek uniformity don't like the above Those who 'make waves' are their unspoken enemies To seek unity is welcome difference It is to understand that our diversity is necessary For the success and benefit of all, regardless of appearance Regardless of belief, of gender, and of culture It is to know that in our sameness we are also unique It is to know that we are one, connected, and interdependent No one is any better on any measurement, than any other We all carry the essence of life We all are born from love Avoid uniformity, seek unity, be the love that you are

George G
