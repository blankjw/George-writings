INFINITE SPACES

By George Gondron

INFINITE SPACES Science reveals that we are more nothing than something Every human being on the planet Is made up of millions and millions of atoms Those atoms are ninety-nine percent empty space If you were to remove all the empty space contained In all the atoms in every human on the planet and compress them You end up with an object no bigger than a sugar cube Attempting to comprehend that bit of information Riddle me this What connects literally everything in existence In the entire cosmos? What holds all those infinite empty spaces together? Quantum physics now postulates that the universe is conscious Is purposeful, benevolent, and evolving intentionally Panpsychism is a fancy word that means all matter has consciousness Where do that consciousness and intent come from? To look up at a clear night sky, unpolluted with man-made light To view images taken by the Webb telescope How do we comprehend the magnificence of the cosmos Without some inkling of a creative Source? Even if one has not had a powerful self-evident experience Of the numinous, a felt knowing, beyond intellect How can one believe that this vast cosmos is accidental? There are those great mystics and spiritual teachers Who declares that perhaps everything that is Including ourselves is a part of that Consciousness We are all a part of an energy called Consciousness Energy never dies, it transforms Maybe the saying is true, we are all stardust To return from whence we came It is the heart, soul, and spirit that comprehend these mysteries The ego, the mind, and the intellect are too dualistic Unwilling to think outside the box, to imagine the unimaginable A full life demands balance; inner and outer Choose a meditative/contemplative practice daily A few minutes of silence, stillness, with Presence Listen to the whispers of your inner Truth, your Essence

George G
