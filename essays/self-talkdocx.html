SELF TALK

By George Gondron

SELF TALK Self talk, nothing more than internal noise Lost moments of convincing myself that I am right Or, that someone else is wrong Guess what? A life devoted to who's right or who's wrong, has a name It's called a wasted life, a conundrum Another word for self talk is worry, imagine that Took me forever to realize that worry doesn't work Guess what, again? You can't change anything, predict anything, fix anything Stop anything or heal anything or anyone, with worry For some reason it just doesn't seem to work Unless I missed something along the way If it works for you, please, give me the secret We humans waste untold hours, worrying And what do we worry about the most, other people 'Does she still love me, is he gonna call me Do they like me, am I good enough, who cares for me'? Guess what, once more? If you truly love yourself, your strengths and your weaknesses If you've forgiven yourself for whatever you may have done And you can look in the mirror and see love and humility See brokenness, pain, suffering, and failure Along with joy, happiness, thanksgiving, and success It doesn't matter what others think, if you see those things You know, you know what a spiritual experience is You know what an encounter with the one you may call God is You know that's what's most important What's of significance is known with the heart, through experience Not with the mind, and that makes life special Known with the soul and spirit, not the intellect Because it's always about connection and relationship Relationships known through encounter, through being present to And all life, all relationships, all connections Spring from one Source, and return to the same Source Self talk is useless, a dead end road leading nowhere Heart knowing is real life, real love, real encounter, is God

George G
