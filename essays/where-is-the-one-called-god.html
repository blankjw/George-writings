WHERE IS THE ONE CALLED GOD

By George Gondron

WHERE IS THE ONE CALLED GOD? God, just one name of the many for what can't be named If the universe is benevolent, is kind and generous Then, also is the Creator Love creates, love by its nature is relational Therefore the Creator is love and is also relational How did we learn that worship is relational? Because it's not at all, and never was Worship is not an 'I - Thou' encounter Not a subject to subject , but rather a subject to object An 'I - It' attachment How did God get confined to a place of bricks and mortar? As if what breathes creation is restricted in space How do you become reverent in one place? And not in all places, what is not sacred? Who determines what's important and what's not? How could any person, any life not be important? We play God, at the expense of death And no one wins So, where is this one we call God? Between each breath, each heartbeat, each thought There God is In the silence, in the stillness There God dwells Always present, always near, always The laughter in the child's joy The tears of a broken heart There God is found God lives in the 'in-betweens' The quiet listener to every discussion, for good or for ill The silent observer of all our actions, accepting of each Where can I go, that God is not? There is no place I can go For this one thing is true, for every life That which creates leaves its mark A yearning within that calls to Itself A yearning for union, completed only in Love

George G
