DEATH

By George Gondron

DEATH A subject seldom discussed in our culture, death One of those topics avoided by most Out of sight, out of mind - so to speak And of course what we're unfamiliar with, we fear We seem to protect ourselves from the death experience As if it could be 'catching' or contagious Even our mourning process is in a way very sterile Strangers prepare the loved one's body Strangers dig the grave And in most instances carry the coffin Excluding immediate family members as pallbearers And lastly, strangers again, bury the loved one How can one have closure with this antiseptic process How does one mourn properly without 'hands on' experience More than once have I heard, 'I wish I would have' It has to be in some manner an avoidance of the reality of death Death begins the moment we are born But few reflect on its meaning And most pretend they will live forever At least through the denial of their own demise And sadly, those who holler and scream at the moment of death Do so for one simple reason They suddenly realize they have never really lived What is the difference between the person who fears death And the one who doesn't There's a very simple answer regardless of one's religious belief Those who have no fear have made a connection A spiritual connection with their idea of their God They have experienced a personal relationship Which they cannot explain, nor can they deny They know beyond belief that death is not the end It is only a transition, just as was birth They have been present to life, they have embraced its love They have sensed the wonder of all creation They know death does not win, it has no 'sting' Their essence will continue with the One that is Love They don't just believe, they really know

George G
