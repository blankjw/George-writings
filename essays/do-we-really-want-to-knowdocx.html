DO WE REALLY WANT TO KNOW

By George Gondron

DO WE REALLY WANT TO KNOW Here’s a statement that’s more true than false Most only want to know what they already believe So, as a result, we really don’t want to know The unfortunate fact is that what we believe for the most part Is not from our own research, our own learning, our own Critical thinking, our own doubts, questions, or efforts For the most part it’s what we’ve been spoon fed It’s about a preconceived belief influenced by many things But absent true clarity and discernment Influenced by social media, third party information Sources that care less about truth and more about follow Not what challenges our intellect, our true self Rather it’s what stirs our emotions, our inflated ego self And we’re very particular about our focus If there are many issues to be considered relating to an Important decision, we find the one that feels comfortable Defending and ignore the rest, it’s much too difficult To see the forest for the trees, so we pick our favorite tree It might be immigration, the border, or maybe inflation Or foreign conflicts, isolationism, health care, socialism, Perhaps abortion, women’s health care, the right to vote, Worker’s rights to unionize, building a wall, minimum wages Free health care, or God forbid, free education Of course, if you’ve read this far, you know what this about Politics, religion, economics, rule of law Our very democracy, separation of church and state Please, don’t be a one issue voter, too much is at stake See all the trees, the whole forest, put aside ego Include mind, heart, soul, and spirit in your decisions Consider character, integrity, honesty, equality, and justice In your decisions, seek what unites not what divides For balance adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Connect inner and outer, empty your busy mind Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen for the whispers of your better angels, your true self Heed the yearnings of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
