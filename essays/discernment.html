DISCERNMENT

By George Gondron | Source: DISCERNMENT.docx

DISCERNMENT Seems to be almost a lost art – discernment It has to do with the acuteness of judgment and understanding To be able to perceive beyond the obvious To recognize that common sense is not so common To comprehend the underlying motives behind What may seem to be clear-cut actions To know that the ‘devil is in the details’ To see through the ‘smoke and mirrors’ Realizing that all is not as it may seem Advertising, marketing, and propagandist Are very adept at disguising their true purposes A discerning person is interested in the message Behind the message What is actually being promoted but not clearly stated Hidden within the ‘word salad’ of the promoter Our greatest weakness against being a discerning person Is to resist becoming emotionally involved In the message being offered Whatever the method of presentation For once the emotions take over There is no longer a needed objectivity To arrive at a realistic solution, response, or answer To what’s being offered for our consideration When what may seem to be an offer Is nothing more than a disguised demand Hidden in the conditioning, programming, and manipulation Of the presenter Politicians and certain types of religious leaders Are very good at the aforementioned Experts at their craft, ‘silver tonged devils’ Or some would say ‘snake oil salesman’ The awakening of your inner core, your inner truth Is also an awakening of your ability to discern To see through the illusions, look behind the curtain Spend a few minutes each day in contemplative prayer Time for silence, stillness, with Presence To empty the mind and listen to the whispers of the heart

George G
