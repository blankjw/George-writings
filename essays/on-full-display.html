ON FULL DISPLAY

By George Gondron

ON FULL DISPLAY One of the benefits of the explosion of social media Is immediate availability, unlimited sources, and resources For almost any topic that strikes our fancy, all on full display The problem is not the availability, it’s the reliability Or the verifiability - faced with hyperbole or truth Fact or fiction, without critical thinking and a curious mind We easily can be led astray when what we hear and see Plays on our emotions rather than our intelligence Through targeted propaganda, marketing, and advertising We are easy prey for what the ‘snake oil salesmen’ are pushing Every commercial on the ‘airwaves’ is an attempt to sell you Something = radio, internet, TV, movies, etc., etc., etc. After all we must keep ‘greasing‘ the wheels of capitalism Everything is for sale – goods, services, ideas, even religion When those are our focus, our emotions are easy to manipulate For if I can’t have what social media keeps telling me I need Then someone must be to blame Please tell me where to place my anger, point my finger It can’t be my fault, guess what? It is our fault The important things we can’t buy, they’re not even for sale Honesty, integrity, kindness, compassion, altruism, inclusion, Unity, compromise, conciliation, cooperation, conscience, Sharing, caring, justice, equality, common decency, respect, And the most important, love – connection, relationship But we already possess those things They are innate with this gift we call our life, hidden within We only need awaken them, allow them freedom That can only happen when we seek to know ourselves Beyond our incessant and repetitive thoughts What the mystics and spiritual teachers call our ‘true self’ Not our egoic false self, our polarized and splintered self We need balance, inner and outer, a contemplative practice A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, for Presence Before we were born, we were, after we die we still will be Born from stardust, our energy remains Focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, they patiently await

George G
