ESSAY

LEVELS OF CONSCIOUSNESS

By George Gondron

LEVELS OF CONSCIOUSNESS Yes, there are levels of consciousness, of awareness The lowest, the most basic level is dualistic It is also the least aware level of consciousness Life is black/white, right/wrong, win/lose, us versus/them There is no room for compromise or conciliation A very defensive stance, ‘my way or the highway’ mentality What matters is always an either/or proposition, never both/and Supported by sayings such as ‘can’t see the forest for the trees’ “I may not be right, but I’m never wrong’, ‘head buried in the sand’ ‘Wearing blinders’, ‘if you don’t choose A, you must choose B’ Dualistic thinkers are motivated more by fear than by love Narrower minded and myopic and more unlikely to change Firm and unwavering in their thoughts and beliefs Even with evidence to the contrary, they reject facts They prefer to be literal rather than speculative or imaginative They limit their capacity to understand for their thinking is polarized The highest level of consciousness or awareness is nondual Everything belongs, life is always both/and Right and wrong are not absolutes, they are situational They are concerned more about inclusion than exclusion Concerned more about understanding than self-righteousness More about love than fear, compassion than judgement More about learning and change than clinging to the familiar More about serving others than being served They have curious minds and view change as a positive They believe the human potential for the better is unlimited That the only thing that prevents us from becoming more From reaching greater understanding, awareness, and consciousness Is ourselves, the choice to live with possibility versus peril To see sameness before differentness, connectedness versus division Unity with diversity versus exclusivity and judgement Choose to become a nondual thinker, a both/and thinker Reject polarized, judgmental, defensive behavior Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath or a short mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
