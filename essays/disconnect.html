DISCONNECT

By George Gondron

DISCONNECT If nothing else is obvious in today's culture It's the disconnect between fact and fiction The leaders of religion living in million-dollar homes Speaking of Christian charity Avoiding the part of the narrative that's uncomfortable The politicians who are 'war hawks', for profit motives only With no 'skin in the game', sending the young to die Then, the young return, broken and maimed, body and soul Left homeless, jobless, with no aftercare The 'fat cats' of Wall Street, living in luxury At the expense of those they cheat, deceive, and hoodwink The banks, 'too big to fail' Bigger, richer, more powerful than ever Total disconnect - humans, numbers only, not souls The discrimination hidden in false equality I'm sorry, you don't quite 'fit the bill', color or gender Then there's the justice system, but justice for who? How is it the saying, 'if you do the crime, you do the time' Doesn't apply to all? If you're the right color, if you have enough money If you run in the right 'circles' And now, if you're a corporation, your justice is different It is privileged, yes, there is a disconnect And then, there's you and I, the common folk Living day-to-day, for the most part, safe and secure While the family across town struggles to have food It is a fact, that the poor help the poor More than the 'haves' help the 'have-nots' The poor don't live by 'I've got mine; you get yours' Yes, we live in a disconnected world, in a bubble of our own How does that change, how do we connect? It is only through awareness, self-understanding Seeing ourselves in the other, that is the way Our common connection is in our humanness, our sameness With That which gives life, dwelling within is our connection The name does not matter, it's just a word, but it is Love And in the end, Love will win, it always has, it always will

George G
