THE ONE WE CAN NEVER LEAVE

By George Gondron

THE ONE WE CAN NEVER LEAVE There is only one person we can never leave Ourself But even more frightening Most never even know who they are They live their lives defined by others By external sources, ‘ism’s’ Collective groups such as religion, especially faulty religion Religion that divides and doesn’t unite The same can be said about almost any group Those that are for and those that are against When we compare and contrast ourselves with others We live with an inner yearning that is never fulfilled For we each are unique and different Similar in many ways, but unique in our creativity Spend a few minutes each day looking in the mirror Who is the self that lives behind the eyes that stare back? What are the questions that self wants to ask? And how will you respond? To be defined by the externals of life Is to live a lie, compare, compete, contrast To be attached to thoughts and feelings without critique Is to be disconnected from your true self If our perceptions are formed by the externals of life With no connection to an inner core, an internal reference Then we can’t possibly see and respond with clarity We remain stuck in our egos, our false self Clinging to the influences of a culture whose focus Is more is better, bigger is better, faster is better Consumption is what really matters, not people Choose an alternative path, awaken your inner resources Spend a few minutes each day in a contemplative practice Experience silence, stillness, and Presence Experience the necessary balance between inner and outer We are not just a mind, an ego, an intellect We are more importantly heart, soul, and spirit We are stardust connected to the Consciousness of the universe Listen to the whispers and yearnings of that connection

George G
