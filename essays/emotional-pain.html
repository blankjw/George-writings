EMOTIONAL PAIN

By George Gondron

EMOTIONAL PAIN – PERSONAL CHOICE When the pain of your current situation Becomes greater than the fear of change Only then will you choose a new path Only then will you let go Emotional pain is far more difficult to heal Only because it is self-inflicted Through our thinking, we embrace emotional dysfunctional It is natural in our humanness to feel The range of human emotions is unlimited But to cling to the pain of certain emotions Is nothing more than a choice To become the victim is nothing but a defense against change Allowing me to remain in a ‘blaming’ frame of mind Finding fault elsewhere but never in myself The words are ever so familiar, ‘if it weren’t for you’ Which is nothing but another way of saying I allow you to keep me ‘stuck’ I’m unwilling to move on, I give my power to you As the comic character Pogo sates ‘I have found the enemy and it is me’ To cling to any emotion that creates our own ‘angst’ Our own anxiety, fear, frustration, and dread Is a personal choice, always But most have become so addicted to their familiar They refuse to acknowledge the dynamic of their choices What we focus on determines our wellbeing Our success or failure toward becoming fully human Being at peace with ourselves is our responsibility Incorporate a meditative/contemplative practice into your life Spend a few minutes each day in silence, in stillness In the presence of Presence, your image of God Take time to empty your mind of its incessant and often Repetitive thoughts that go nowhere and do no-thing Discover your true self, the self connected to your heart, Soul, and spirit, not the false self of only the mind, the ego Listen to the whispers of your inner yearnings They hold your truth, your creativity, they patiently await

George G
