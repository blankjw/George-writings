SELFLESS OR SELFISH

By George Gondron

SELFLESS OR SELFISH Difficult to find, selfless people Yet, the most abundant lifestyle is a selfless one There is a stark distinction between the selfish and the selfless One is more comfortable in a three-piece suit The other, just fine in the rags of a pauper For one there is a string attached to their proclaimed charity If nothing more than, ‘after all I've done for you’ For the other, life itself is about being charitable, about giving About what more can I do to relieve your suffering? Young children have a greater sense of sharing, of giving Than do adults, they are wiser than adults Selfless people could care less about power and control About manipulation and biased rhetoric They are like an open book and care not About the judgment of others Their behavior is witness to their kindness, compassion, and empathy What they do care about are the disenfranchised, the outcast The homeless and poverty-stricken The orphan, the widow, the handicapped, the abused They care about the fog of disillusion that permeates our culture About the ‘mantra’ of spend more of what you don’t have On what you don’t need, endlessly They care about the lies, alternative facts, conspiracy theories And ‘deep state’ rhetoric which flows freely on social media Meant to keep a few in power, a few in control, a few in wealth Selfishness is like a cancer that eventually devours its host But in the process, those without power, suffer needlessly The wealthy’s gain is the poor’s loss Would there could be a paradigm shift in human consciousness From being selfish to selfless, but I fear that’s not possible Change is always an individual process, please seek change The selfish say yes but, the selfless say yes and Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, Embrace your image of God, of Isness, of Universal Consciousness Breathe in, breathe out a brief mantra Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your selfless self, have your answers, they wait

George G
