UNKNOWABLE

By George Gondron | Source: UNKNOWABLE.docx

UNKNOWABLE How is it possible to know the unknowable? It will never happen intellectually, cognitively, its wasted effort If it were possible, then someone would have already published a manual on the necessary steps to accomplish the task The failure of most organized religion is its focus Its insistence on the formulation of rites, rituals, dogma, and doctrine that faithfully followed will bring you enlightenment In truth, it’s the opposite of what’s necessary to ‘know’ All the great mystics and spiritual teachers knew as a result of their own struggles that the accomplishment of an experiential relationship with that which can’t be known intellectually is a process of ‘letting go’, of surrender, of ‘I can’t do this through my own efforts’ – they learn that it in the end it is the lack of effort It is learning how to be, the path of becoming with no manual As with all personal change, the first step is letting go of what does not work and embracing something new That ‘new’ when attempting to know the unknowable begins with a meditative practice, using only the five senses to be fully present to some spiritual writing or scripture, no thought just be present Experience the sights, sounds, smells, tastes, and physical sensations A committed consistent practice will eventually morph into a release of all sensory perceptions into a contemplative practice Where the attraction now becomes emptiness, silence, and stillness As the religions in the East have taught, everything arises from the emptiness, the silence Even in the Christian religion how often did Jesus go apart to be alone He said to the crowds, ‘when you pray go into your inner room shut the door and pray to your Father in secret’ Volumes have been written over the centuries about the importance of silence and stillness in the maturing of the human psyche, both from Eastern and Western authors, the ego resists A meditative/contemplative practice will lead to an experiential knowing of the ‘unknowable’ An experience that is too deep and profound to deny and as well one that is unable to be put into words, it is personal Give it a try, there’s nothing to lose and everything to gain Silence teaches, silence heals, silence connects to Isness

George G
