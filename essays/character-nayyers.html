CHARACTER NAYYERS

By George Gondron | Source: CHARACTER NAYYERS.docx

CHARACTER MATTERS Decency, honesty, integrity, truthfulness, all matter The ability to unite rather than divide, matters The quality of being presidential as opposed to acting presidential Matters – empathy, kindness, inclusive language matters The differences between the character of the two presidential Candidates is very clear, very pronounced The differences in their experience is also very obvious One a lawyer, a DA, an attorney general, a senator, A vice president – the other a twice impeached, convicted Felon who had 26 failed businesses and 6 bankruptcies One has ‘mocked’ a handicapped journalist on live TV Been sued more than 3.500 times and lied over 30,000 times His four years in office, and those lies continue today Dishonored our fallen service personnel, calling them Losers and suckers – a person who avoided the draft His fifth time because of bone spurs yet was able to participate In sports with no problem at all This coming election on November 5th is without a doubt The most significant election in the history of our democracy If you believe character matters, the choice is obvious If your only reason to reject the decent choice, the experienced Choice is ‘I just don’t like her’, then you are the problem At seventy-eight years old character, short of a miracle, Does not change, what you see and hear is the real person The future of our children and grandchildren are at stake The future of our Republic is at stake At this time in our history, we are experiencing what Germany Experienced during the 1930’s Let us not repeat history, let us not be asleep at the wheel This is probably the most serious vote you will ever cast Use due diligence, research, question, understand what Causes deficit, inflation, increased debt, it’s not a person In the voting booth use your intelligence not your emotions Vote for integrity, decency, morality, and unity Vote for our fallen and wounded warriors, that their sacrifice Might not be in vain And first and foremost, vote for the rule of law and democracy

George G
