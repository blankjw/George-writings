FRICTION

By George Gondron

FRICTION Friction is not a detriment, harmful, or a disadvantage It is a catalyst, a leaven, or a causal agent for change A pearl is produced by an oyster because of an irritant called a grain of sand – that becomes its agent for change A giant oak began as a small acorn withstanding all of nature’s frictional challenges, wind, storms, snow and heat The process of becoming for us humans, the individuation process, the self actualization process, is never ending Immediately the newborn begins to experience friction Hunger, discomfort from a soiled diaper, the urgency for human touch and interaction And most importantly, the learning experienced from the adult role models in its new world In a very important way, friction is a blessing in every transition of our lives from birth to death The world itself becomes our oyster and how we navigate the frictions we encounter, social, economic, psychological, interactional, and spiritual is instrumental in the pearls we do or do not create All personal experiences are teachers, but only if we use the ability of choice to examine at depth the experience itself To simply have experiences without any critical thought, without reflection, without any analysis is a loss All experiences, especially those that are negative are our greatest teachers, they are gifts to our waking up, increasing our awareness The person who seeks to understand is the same person who discovers that blame and the pursuit of one’s own growth, one’s own wellness, are not compatible, blame doesn’t work They begin to realize we are ‘choice makers’ to the extent that we choose our own thoughts and feelings The friction in our lives, anxiety, frustration, stress, are disguised blessings enticing us to ‘pull back the curtain’ and discover how we, through our lack of awareness, create our own reality Adopt a daily meditative/contemplative practice for balance Embrace a few minutes of silence, stillness, with Presence Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They hold your your self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
