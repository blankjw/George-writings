BE AFRAID, BE VERY AFRAID

By George Gondron

BE AFRAID, BE VERY AFRAID We are currently living at a time in history When we can no longer take for granted our Republic No longer assume our Constitution is unassailable Democracy is in peril, which means our freedoms are as well Our legislative, judicial, and executive branches Are being eroded away in a play for power and control And if this ‘erosion’ is successful the losers will be us The majority of citizens at the expense of the few The few who are willing to ‘white-wash’ our history Drag us back to times of inequality and discrimination Of taking away hard fought for rights with more stringent controls Of mandating we should be a Christian nation We are on a very slippery slope, so much for religious freedom We are being told in direct and indirect ways what we can read Returning to the days of ‘book-burning’ If women’s rights have not been restricted enough They are being restricted even more The right to vote is being manipulated and lessened Ridiculous ordinances, and laws aimed at people of color To make it almost impossible for them to vote And there’s talk of restricting Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid, and health benefits Our elected representatives have become ‘fat cats’ With their outlandish salaries, retirement benefits, And unmentionable ‘perks’, not to mention ‘insider trading’ Politics has become a profession with unlimited benefits Politicians voting themselves a raise with no shame at all But refusing to raise the minimum wage for ordinary workers We are nothing more than a means to an end Programmed to consume endlessly, programmed to blame To wear blinders and stick our heads in the sand Be afraid, be very afraid – but most of all wake up Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your daily life For a few minutes be silent, be still, be in the presence of Presence Discover your innate true self, not the egoic self Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
