THE RIGHT THING FOR THE WRONG REASON

By George Gondron

THE RIGHT THING FOR THE WRONG REASON Human reason has a tendency to critique, analyze, judge, and dissect Subject or object before they have a chance to reveal themselves As themselves, in themselves, and for themselves This phenomenon falls under the purview of addicted thinking Which we all do, denied or otherwise The same applies to seeing or hearing It's not what we see, it's how we see Just as it's not what we hear, but how we hear Because an experience can't be put into words Does not make the experience invalid, or wrong, or unbelievable In fact, most transformative experiences cannot be put into words The weakness or disadvantage on the human level Is to exist in an environment where experience is second hand In other words, believe me, take my word for it, I have the answer Which results in one reality, an unaware and unconscious culture Quick to blame for the effects which I don't like Unable to own my responsibility for the outcome
