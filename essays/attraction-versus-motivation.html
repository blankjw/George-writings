ATTRACTION VERSUS MOTIVATION

By George Gondron | Source: ATTRACTION VERSUS MOTIVATION.docx

ATTRACTION VERSUS MOTIVATION Sadly, attraction wins over motivation in our culture, almost always Attraction stimulates the senses, motivation challenges the intellect Have you never realized that hyperbole, gaslighting, and propaganda are much more appealing than ‘cold hard facts’ The ‘snake oil salesman’, the ‘silver tongued devil’, the ‘accuser’ are much more enticing than the ‘truth teller’ We, it seems, love to have someone to ‘blame’ Afterall what’s wrong with the society is he, she, it, or they, not us We do our ‘due diligence’, right? We research, critically think, fact check, question, and doubt Not really, the excitement of having one’s emotions aroused seems to be much more important and enticing than having one’s intellect challenged, we are thinking less and accusing more We ae throwing rationale out the window in favor of emotional dysfunction and accordingly give up our ability to be human To be kind, empathetic, caring, sharing, inclusive beings And as a result, bigotry, prejudice, racism, judgement, blame, finger-pointing, and exclusion have become the norm Unless we wakeup and resist the forces that are doing their best to divide us, we will in our lifetime suffer the consequences Not to mention the futures of our children and grandchildren Supposedly human beings have a higher level of consciousness than other sentient beings, that seems to be in doubt We have lost the desire, not the ability, to educate ourselves We insist on being ‘fed’ BS rather than discovering truth Are we really that lazy? Or are our distractions more important? For sure, ignorance, in all its many forms has taken control The power of social media has reared its ugly head, algorithms win And now with the advent of AI, the sky’s the limit Our future is truly frightening, led by men without conscience They say a rat trapped in a corner will fight, are we there yet? I encourage you to adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Awaken your inner guides, connect head and heart Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breath in, breathe out Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
