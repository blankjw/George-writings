FEED US

By George Gondron

FEED US When we were infants, we could not feed ourselves We had to be fed for our survival, now as adults In a real sense what we take in through our awareness and Consciousness is feeding us as well And what we feed our minds is as important as what we Feed our bodies, more important As the saying goes, ‘garbage in, garbage out’ Without critical thinking, continued education, reliable filters, If we merely take in without any form of discrimination What we receive from social media, from third party information, Following the individual or individuals who stir our emotions Never doing our own research, our own ‘fact checking’ We fall prey to being manipulated and controlled Especially if the narrative we receive is ‘gas-lighting’ Filled with anti-rhetoric and negativity meant to divide us Meant to turn us against one another and point fingers We unintentionally fail ourselves by being irresponsible Allowing others to give us our answers, mentally lazy Yes, feed us To be led by our emotions, to reject our own intellectual potential To fall for ‘conspiracy theories’ and ‘deep state’ propaganda Is to be the fool, is to relinquish our responsibilities as adults We are no longer infants needing to be fed Throughout history, the ‘Kool Aid’ drinkers, the follow the ‘carrot On the stick’ mentality, marching to the beat of another’s drum Never ends well, never Please, as the old saying goes, ‘don’t believe what you hear And only half of what you see’, do your own due diligence At this juncture in our history it matters, it matters greatly If we accept out-of-hand, without any critique what we take in We become part of the problem and not part of the solution Don’t be fed - question, doubt, reflect, research, listen carefully Include your inner resources, your internal core in your decisions Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Isness Empty your mind of its incessant and repetitive thoughts Focus on your breath, listen for the whispers of your Heart, soul, and spirit, your inner guides, they wait

George G
