DON'T LOOK BACK

By George Gondron

DON'T LOOK BACK There's nothing there, looking back The fond memories that may come to mind Are there only to reinforce our ability to form more, now On the other hand, the pain of the past Hopefully teaches us what not to do in the present Teaches us to say what we need to say now Do what needs to be done now Love now, don't wait; but most of us repeat our pain Through our inability to critique our own behavior Through our refusal to learn Through our resistance to grow Through our insistence on clinging to the familiar How often I've heard the phrase, 'the good ole days' How can we make now, 'the good ole days'? Those who live in the past Can't handle living in the present, the now The excuses are many and there's only one result Compare and contrast what used to be, always Rather than being present to what is now And that's really all there is, now How do we make now better? How do we as individuals become part of the solution And not part of the problem How do we nurture and manifest the unique gift? That is a part of who we are How do we wake up? How do we not see the energy wasted on the past? Change is not easy, but it is possible It only requires one action, choose When we choose, we must remain true to the decision Push away the words of the ego, to stay the same After all, if everyone else changes, we'll be just fine Just another lie, another deception, another distraction Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, they hold your truth You are, I am, we all are a part of the Divine That patiently waits, waiting to be heard, waiting to love That whispers your name in the recesses of your soul

George G
