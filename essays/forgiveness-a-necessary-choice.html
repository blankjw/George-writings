FORGIVENESS A NECESSARY CHOICE

By George Gondron

FORGIVENESS, A NECESSARY CHOICE How can necessary be a choice? Just as easily as, how can forgiveness be withheld? Perhaps an issue of a wounded, inflated ego The unforgiving mind is self-absorbed, unable to be logical, rational, or objective Holding on to whatever the experience, whatever the pain, is akin to living with a cancer that could be cured If pardon is given, the mind must relinquish fault If it is not given, then blame is the winner, resulting in essence with no winner To ask for forgiveness, to offer forgiveness, the process that leads to well-being, for all concerned When all focus is about me, and not about we, how can wounds be healed, peace be fostered. The reluctance, the resistance to forgive is a stuck place Nothing positive results from being stuck Life is by its nature relational In our humanness we will wound others In our humanness we must forgive ourselves, forgive others for our wounds In our forgiving, awareness grows, consciousness grows, love grows

George G
