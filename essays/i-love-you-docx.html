I LOVE YOU

By George Gondron

I LOVE YOU Three little words, that in the end Have no meaning if not supported through behavior Genuine love is not about feeling It is about action It is the result of what we do or don’t do toward other Other being all that exists Love connected to expectations is not love at all For it is conditional, there is a string attached It incorporates the narrative such as If you love me then Then translated, ‘I expect’ Few there are that are able to love without conditions Only one experience can sever the connection of genuine love The violation of trust And even then love can still be held in the heart If not in action In order to love unconditionally We must love ourselves first, unconditionally Therein lies the real problem We must be willing to do for ourselves That which brings us to wholeness, honestly To clarity about our own thoughts, feelings, and actions About how we defend our own behavior In order to avoid needed personal change We will do anything and everything to avoid our own shadow Which, in the end, is the answer to all our problems For what we refuse to see in ourselves, we see in others Both negative and positive The paradox, we are the same, even in our differentness Make a change for your betterment Choose a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Presence Presence being your image of a creative universal Source Empty your ever-busy ‘monkey mind’ of its incessant and Repetitive thoughts that go nowhere and solve no-thing Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your truth, creativity, and ‘true self’ They are teachers of genuine love

George G
