PRESENCE

By George Gondron | Source: PRESENCE.docx

PRESENCE Being present is a universal concept Present to another person, to nature, to a thought or a feeling It is basically the ability to be focused on ‘other’ singularly While having no distractions, no reference to self All attention is given to ‘other’ selflessly It is a learned ability, an acquired experience developed Through consistent practice, in a sense, to be the other When presence is used in the context of spirituality It is the awareness or realization that, That which creates The Originator of this universe, Mystery, Isness, Creator, Universal Consciousness, Essence, or God Is always and ever present to all that is in every moment Including and especially ourselves It is we who lack the ability to experience Presence And the main reason for that failure is the inability to be Present to our very selves, you know the thought Looking in the mirror and asking, ‘With whom am I having The pleasure?’, or ‘To thine own self be true’ One of the primary goals of a human life is the unvarnished Knowledge of who we are, the good, the bad, the indifferent Self-acceptance at its deepest level, the darkness and the light Is a gift, an attainable goal that is possible for each of us But it is a choice, it’s not a given, it requires effort It requires being vulnerable and taking a risk, becoming defenseless As opposed to being defended and oppositional It also requires a change in context, following the habitual patterns Of our everyday lives doesn’t work, clinging only to what’s familiar Doesn’t work, change is necessary, adding something new We can’t unlearn what we already know, but we can add new Learning, new experience, reflection and critical thinking A full life demands balance, action and inaction, doing and being It’s the being part that is the most difficult in Western culture Adopt a meditative/contemplative practice for balance Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Focus on your breath or a brief mantra, empty your mind Listen to your heart, soul, and spirit, they hold your true self They have your answers, they patiently wait

George G
