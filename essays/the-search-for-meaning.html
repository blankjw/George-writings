THE SEARCH FOR MEANING

By George Gondron

THE SEARCH FOR MEANING Most of us are ‘runners’, not in a literal sense But figuratively we do whatever is necessary To avoid knowing who we are and the meaning of our life It’s not necessarily a conscious choice, it just happens Especially in our culture, our society For our lives are filled with distractions, the next ‘shiny’ object Some unintentional, but most very intentional All focused on personal consumption, people, places, things There’s always a ‘better mousetrap’, or a scam convincing us What we ‘need’, not want, for our own wellbeing Physically, emotionally, intellectually, relationally, spiritually In every area of our lives we are ‘marks’ for some advertiser, Marketer, propagandist, manipulator, or plain ‘con-artist’ They are alive and well in our extreme capitalistic culture, In our politics, and fundamental organized religion There are very few areas of our lives which are exempt from The ‘push-pull’ of buy this, go here, or do ‘that’ Everywhere we look , someone has the ‘right’ answer Or the ‘perfect’ solution for whatever ails us If you want to get a small glimpse of who you are What you are about, your meaning and purpose I offer you the following challenge Spend a few days alone, totally alone With no outside distractions, no external input No music, no TV, no social media, no phone, no conversations No chores, no projects, just your thoughts and feelings And maybe a pencil and blank paper to memorialize the experience If you’re honest, I bet it’s not an exciting prospect Which should be a good indication of its necessity I sincerely hope you accept the challenge, it’s worth it When we risk moving from our familiar to the strange The possibility of a whole new awareness opens before us What we resist that has the potential to increase our consciousness Can only be for our benefit, our wellbeing, our growth If we cannot be present to ourselves Look in the mirror and ask, ‘with whom am I having the pleasure?’ We cannot be fully present to others, I hope you take the risk

George G
