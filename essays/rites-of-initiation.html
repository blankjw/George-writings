RITES OF INITIATION

By George Gondron

RITES OF INITIATION Important transitions in the human journey Were symbolized through rites of initiation Both female and male Powerful experiences of moving from Adolescence to adulthood Often involving physical endurance More importantly those first significant steps of A psychological, emotional, and spiritual nature Facing fear and the unknown with trepidation Awaiting a shift in consciousness alone in nature The vision quest resulted in a powerful shift in awareness Bringing a new clarity and comprehension Of one’s personal path and journey The importance of initiation rites has been lost Has been forgotten in modern culture Along with the opportunity to have a visceral Experience of the sacredness of life, all life And it appears that what has filled that necessary void Is violence Violence to the individual and to others The struggle and fight within Accomplished through the initiation rite The facing of one’s own darkness and fear Has been supplanted with external violence With disconnection, isolation, and separateness We no longer feel a part of the greater whole An alternative for the vision quest as adults Is a committed consistent meditative/contemplative Practice – a few minutes each day for Silence, stillness, and Presence A period during the day to empty our ever-busy Monkey minds of their incessant and repetitive thoughts A time to listen to the whispers of our hearts To the Truth that we each hold within To become aware of our eternal connection To the Isness of the universe To a benevolent Consciousness, to Love

George G
