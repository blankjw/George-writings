THE FOCUS OF OUR CULTURE

By George Gondron | Source: THE FOCUS OF OUR CULTURE.docx

THE FOCUS OF OUR CULTURE If it is not obvious that the focus of our culture Is about producing and consuming, then we are illusory We are not only blind we are deaf as well What’s important is not religion, not politics, not social policy It’s the economy, or more bluntly, money And of course, we bever question our own participation In the never-ending struggle of more is better, bigger is better, And faster is better In fact, we scapegoat the less fortunate, the disenfranchised, The underprivileged, the ‘welfare queens’ And consequently they suffer double jeopardy For not only are they inadequate producers But they consume far too little, blame is the name of the game Our virtue, our ethics, our values are impeccable People without, are by default, placed in a ‘double bind’ And the gap between the haves and the have-nots grows wider Ask yourself, who benefits from a culture of produce and consume It’s not the poor, for their life is about surviving Why do the tax laws favor the wealthy and the Fortune 500? You ever wonder what happens to products that become obsolete, Excess food preparation, outdated ‘fads’, the old replaced by the new All excess production, all outdated production, is essentially Destroyed – not given freely to those in need, that’s too charitable The grease that lubricates the wheels of this economy – money Money is king, money is power, money is control Spend more what you don’t have to give more to those Who do have – this culture depends so much on consumption That if we ceased to spend for just one week The economy would collapse The greatest motivator for marketers and advertisers is fear Fear that we will stop spending, nothing to fear, we never will Social media is made for marketing, the content is secondary Focus more on becoming fully human, less on being a consumer Choose a little silence, stillness, and Presence each day Provide more for those who have not, less for those that have Discover your true self, your inner truth, learn to listen Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit, they wait

George G
