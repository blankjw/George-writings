RESISTANCE TO CHANGE - Copy

By George Gondron

RESISTANCE TO CHANGE That which resists change, is more often than not What most needs to change Whether it be individuals, groups, or institutions Realistically, change is unstoppable Our physical bodies change moment by moment, uninvited Which in a way, is a clear indication, our bodies are smarter Than our minds, for our ego is the greatest ‘resistor’ What we term ‘conscience’ embraces change easily When situations call for it, when love is stronger than fear For the ego the power of the ‘familiar’ cannot be overstated People will often prefer the familiar to the strange Even when the familiar is painful What the ego doesn’t know is more dangerous or frightening Than the ego’s familiar, even though it may be dysfunctional In general terms, change is always about moving to an Unfamiliar place, a new vulnerable or risky experience ‘Group think’ or institutional think has no conscience Only individuals have a conscience Consequently, what motivates groups or institutions Has little to do with what’s right or wrong In a compassionate sense, in a what’s good for humanity sense Their focus is more about their own self preservation Regardless of their narrative, who belongs and who does not Their mission almost always revolves around control or money Many times both – not what’s good for the individual but What’s good for the institution or the group That’s why most important change throughout history Put forth by individuals was considered radical So radical some were even crucified, burned at the stake, Or eliminated by genocide as we attempted to do In our occupation of this country, to its true Native Americans Please, be a change maker, a person of conscience Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your journey Awaken your own kindness, compassion, and love For yourself and all creation, all humanity Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, be present Listen to your true self, your inner truth, it patiently waits

George G
