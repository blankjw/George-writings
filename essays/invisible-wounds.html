INVISIBLE WOUNDS

By George Gondron

INVISIBLE WOUNDS Words and actions from the past Scars on the psyche Wounds of the heart No bandage, no balm can heal these wounds Words can be so deadly, so poisonous In a brief instant they reach their mark To lash out with venom To injure another with words Words, sharper than a two-edged sword Be careful with your words They fly swiftly, they can't be taken back Unlike a loosed arrow, they always hit their mark Matters not their lack of intent to harm Matters their being said at all How do I remove those scars, those memories? How do I forget? I'm not sure I can do it alone I'm not sure my heart can hold the forgiveness Maybe in the silence Maybe in the stillness Maybe in the Presence I can heal, I can learn to forgive, I can learn to forget Maybe

George G
