THE BOOK

By George Gondron

THE BOOK At birth we are given our book Beautiful, leather bound, gild edges The title, The Book of Me In the beginning, just empty pages Pages that day after day begin to fill with my experiences My thoughts, my feelings, my encounters Those that are painful, those that are joyous Filled with some disappointment, some love My history, pieces of my past, patterns of my life I am more than my book My book is just a pointer to who I really am It is not me I am much more than my book There is another Book Some call sacred scripture, divine writing, inspired word It also is a history A history of a God and that God's people But, it also is a pointer A pointer to a greater Reality than words on paper When on a clear night, a child points to the stars The child knows the greater Reality Do we, like the child see the stars, the greater Reality? Or, do we see just the finger pointing? I fear we've lost the Reality and cling to the words I fear we kill the Messenger and miss the message I cling to hope beyond the fear Hope that Love will prevail

George G
