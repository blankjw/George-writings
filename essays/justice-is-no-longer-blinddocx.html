JUSTICE IS NO LONGER BLIND

By George Gondron

JUSTICE IS NO LONGER BLIND When did the phrase 'justice is blind' disappear? By all accounts in America, it is no longer true Justice purports to be based on who is right, who is wrong Innocence or guilt No bias or prejudice as to the type of person you are Or where you come from Therefore, justice should be blind, but it is no longer It seems justice for the poor and justice for the wealthy Are vastly different, with an occasional 'sacrificial lamb' But even those have greatly reduced sentences And when did the color of a person's skin become their guilt? When is it just, to ignore sexual abuse, any abuse? On college campuses, in the work place, serving our country How is it just, that a white man serves less time than one of color For the same crime? How is it that the ones who make the rules The rules that you and I have to follow They don't have to follow ? And finally, how is it women have lesser rights than men? Yes, Lady Justice is no longer blind There is a great inequity in our justice system Money, power, politics, status, and position Have a different measure of justice than everyone else It's a pretend justice hidden behind a smoke screen With more protection than teeth A slap on wrist, a 'pat on the ass' One of the 'good ole boys' laughs up his sleeve Watch yourself, we may have to use you as an example If the masses become too unhappy But for now, 'you got away with another one' When freedom is no longer free for all When justice is no longer just for all What will be next? Who will be next? Justice needs to be blind again, the same justice for all Don't be part of the problem Be a part of the solution Speak out against injustice, always

George G
