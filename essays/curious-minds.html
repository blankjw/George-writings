CURIOUS MINDS

By George Gondron

CURIOUS MINDS More and more it seems, we are losing our natural inclination to be curious, preferring the consistency of clinging to our familiars We are attracted more to what we already believe, not what we know, than what we don’t believe or know, creatures of habit we are Curiosity and necessity are first cousins, both ‘mothers of invention’ A closed mind, on the other hand, cares not about expanding its awareness, consciousness, or understanding It already knows, without any background experience, no need for doubt, questions, critical thinking, or curiosity It’s not attracted to intellectual ideas, only emotional stimulation It cares little about further education, only consistent repetition If your source for daily information is what appears on a screen, your every action is being tracked by some form of algorithm Algorithms that know exactly how to entice, influence and manipulate you, about anything and everything No need for critical thinking, research, questions or doubt All the answers are readily available at your fingertips Answers to every problem, social, political, economic, environmental, religious, relational, psychological, educational, free for the taking In other words, we choose to remain uninformed, unchallenged, trapped in habits we have chosen unawares, remaining ignorant And those that know how to ‘play the game’ shower us with insincere praise to keep us in line, to control us An unshackled mind is a precious gift, one that can’t be influenced by hyperbole, gaslighting, propaganda, and alternative facts Our ability to think is both a blessing and a curse A curse when it is disconnected from our heart, soul, and spirit A blessing when it’s connected to our internal resources Please, do not allow your curious mind to die, to languish in the patterns, habits, and day to day familiars, never cease to learn Add a meditative/contemplative practice to your context Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Mystery Bring balance into your life, connect inner and outer Focus on your breath or a brief mantra, breathe in, breathe out The ego will resist, the mind will wander, simply rinse and repeat Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit They teach you discernment, they hold your truth, they wait

George G
