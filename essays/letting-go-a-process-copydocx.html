LETTING GO - A PROCESS - Copy

By George Gondron

LETTING GO – A PROCESS In the realm of relationships When there is an awareness that enters consciousness That an impasse has been reached Between one or the other in the relationship One that has been slowly developing over time Perhaps reluctantly, hesitatingly, unwelcomed The thoughts of letting go begin to arise Images are formed in the mind of walking away And the process has then begun It’s not specific to a certain type of relationship It can be between spouses, significant others Brother or sister, parent and child, or a Cherished friend or close acquaintance For the self-aware person, a sadness is felt Almost immediately When the process is congruent There is no judgment, no condemnation And no blame attached to the decision As the saying goes, it is what it is And if the relationship held value Was important and had deep meaning It will remain in memory with kindness It will be painful, it will bring sadness But there will be no blame, no finger-pointing Not even fault finding Only a conscious recognition That change happens We are not clones of one another Our values may change Our experiences may change If our thoughts, feelings, and actions Support our decision to end the relationship There will be no bitterness, no anger, Nor recrimination or culpability Over time we will heal and hold in memory Those moments of joy, laughter, and love Letting go is a process, and the process takes time

George G
