A PATH THA LEADS TO KNOWING

By George Gondron

A PATH THAT LEADS TO KNOWING Among all the tools that we use to ’know’ Our five senses, our thoughts, our feelings There is one that arises from an inner yearning To know what we don’t know It is the pursuit of that yearning that will inevitably lead To an expansion of our awareness and consciousness To touch without touching the ‘mystery’ of this universe That cannot be known intellectually, cognitively But can only be imagined at best with our small-mindedness And consequently, this ‘knowing’ can’t be taught One can point to a book and say ‘here it is’ read this Or ‘I can lead you to a person that can show you’ Or ‘if you follow these instructions, you will know’ The truth and validity of an experience of that yearning Is always a singular event Known only to the beneficiary of the experience One in which the reality is so self-evident It cannot be denied, no matter how much the ego resists As well it cannot be explained, described, or put into words It is beyond all definition, all reasoning, all explanation The mystics and spiritual masters of the past call it ‘grace’ And it is as it should be The experience itself can never be forgotten For it touches much more than our senses, our thoughts And our feelings – it infuses the essence of we are In our very core which probably has always been there But now has been awakened It is an experience of extreme benevolence, kindness, Compassion, mercy, forgiveness, and most importantly Unconditional love There is a saying, ‘you cannot know what you don’t know But once you know, you never forget’ As a result, there is a necessary daily practice of reflection That lingers in the recesses of the mind for a few moments Of silence, stillness, with the Mystery each day Moments to empty the mind and listen to the whispers Of the heart, soul, and spirit – our truth and our healing

George G
