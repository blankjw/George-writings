THE ONLY ANIMAL

By George Gondron

THE ONLY ANIMAL In all of nature, there is only one animal that needs to be regulated The human All others follow their natural instincts which is not to destroy But to survive, to maintain a balance within their environment If allowed Unlike other species, the human is greedy Enough is never enough, willing to have and desire more At the expense of others, especially those in need It seems that of all life, the human is the least adaptable It will destroy its own habitat without a second thought For nothing more than selfish reasons The human gift of free will, of choice, has often become its enemy Rather than something positive and progressive The mystery of human selfishness is a conundrum When the world, nature, provides more than enough For a comfortable existence for all humanity Kindness, compassion, inclusion, freedom, and equal justice Are ideals hoped for by most people But there are always the few that live through their ego alone Disconnected from their hearts, having no internal reference No inner core to aid in their intellectual decisions Which in the end are only decisions of ignorance Willing to cut off their noses in spite of their faces All the great philosophies, great spiritual teachers Write and speak of a Source beyond themselves That Source goes by many names, many descriptions When quantum physics speaks of a universe that is conscious, Evolving, and purposeful it makes one wonder Has science become more spiritual than religion? But then again when we observe what organized religion Has done throughout the centuries, it is no surprise at all Evidence to the contrary, religion has failed spiritually Please, choose a meditative/contemplative path Each day spend a few minutes in silence, be still, embrace Presence Connect inner and outer, empty your mind, focus on your breath Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
