CONSEQUENCES

By George Gondron | Source: CONSEQUENCES.docx

CONSEQUENCES We do choose you know, every thought, feeling, and action What we do or do not do is a choice, but seldom do we stop And ask ourselves, what are the likely consequences of my choices And assuredly if those consequences are negative, or harmful We are hard pressed to admit we are at fault Not us, the fault must lie elsewhere of course, almost always More importantly, there are also consequences for what We fail to do, what we take for granted Accepting controversial information without critical thinking Without fact checking or research, with the lamest of excuses Like, ‘I just don’t like he/she/ or them’, having no apparent reason Oh wait, maybe the apparent reason is racism or prejudice Or heaven forbid just plain ignorance, having a closed mind Can easily be called ignorant, choosing the lie over the truth And if we cannot discern an obvious lie over the truth We are the problem, and we will suffer the consequences The failure to know is personal, blame doesn’t work It’s really not difficult to recognize hatred, division, exaggeration, And an inflated ego, the ‘emperor has no clothes’ The greater problem is falling in ‘lockstep’ with the ‘emperor’ To ridicule or denigrate others is the first and last argument of A fool, always – They are never wrong, and are always dangerous To give power to those who thirst for power is to be the fool In so many ways we are, as the cartoon Pogo says, our own Worst enemy – when we accept without question rhetoric Which will impact our future and our children’s future We are the enemy, we fall on our own sword willingly And then when the worst happens, we will shamefully proclaim ‘Well, we didn’t know – no, we knew, we failed to recognize We chose the easy way, we drank the Kool Aid We followed our emotions, and not our intellect Add balance to your life, a meditative/contemplative practice A few minutes each day for silence, stillness, and Isness Focus on your breath or a brief mantra, breathe in, breathe out Your ego will resist, your mind will wander, rinse and repeat Listen for the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
