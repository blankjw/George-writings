GIGO

By George Gondron | Source: GIGO.docx

GIGO GIGO, an anacronym that became popular As the advent of computers became so prevalent Meaning, ‘garbage in, garbage out’ The human mind works the same way If we are to trust what we take in, or put out Certain measures are necessary for us to follow Without a great deal of detailed information On their surface, they are quite evident Is the input accurate, is it comparable to other data? Is the input consistent, is it true in every instance? Is the input valid, does it have a cogent or sound basis? Is the input complete, is there only one reference? Is the input unique, it is being supported by several sources? Does the input have integrity, in other words, does it change When challenged? Garbage in, garbage out can be applicable in virtually Every area of our society In our personal lives, if we don’t take the time To apply the six previous steps to whatever we proclaim Then we fall into the trap of perpetuating ‘garbage out’ It is almost a guarantee, that whatever we share In whatever arena to which we have an emotional attachment There will be bias and prejudice, not accuracy or clarity We simply perpetuate what we take in without validity We fall into the trap of ‘alternative facts’ and ‘half-truths’ When we operate from a mindset that is dualistic and polarized Everything is black or white, right or wrong, good or bad That’s not reality not even close That’s the perfect environment for the ‘blame game’ I offer this suggestion for a balanced approach to your journey Incorporate a meditative/contemplative practice in your life Spend a few minutes of silence, with stillness, in the presence Of Presence each day – choose a short mantra for example Breathe in wellness to yourself breath out wellness to the universe Stay with it until the words fall away that you might hear The quiet whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit Awaken your ‘true self’, your inner core, your answers await

George G
