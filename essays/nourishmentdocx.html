NOURISHMENT

By George Gondron

NOURISHMENT That which sustains life, nourishment It is the how that matters The results being the quality of our lives On all levels Physical, intellectual, relational, spiritual The physical is obvious, food, liquids, air What we ingest often determines our bodily health How do we nourish our intellect? With meaningless advertising and propaganda? With the fallacy of our wants translated as our needs? What meaningful literature do I read? What meaningful media, of any nature, do I assimilate? Do I gain nourishment from only those like myself? Like me in thought, belief, and practice Like attracts like If you're not for me, you're against me Do I see myself in others or only see them as different? Ah, then there's spiritual nourishment Is it the rites, rules, and rituals, of long held tradition? Or is it the deeper yearning to be connected? Connected to That which gives me life My Center, more present to me than I am to myself I sense that is the Source of all nourishment The arising of the meaning of life A relational kinship lasting for eternity That becomes my undeniable knowing My undeniable Nourishment

George G
