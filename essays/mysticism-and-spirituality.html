MYSTICISM AND SPIRITUALITY

By George Gondron

MYSTICISM AND SPIRITUALITY The dilemma of most organized religion Of all faiths, seems to fall within the same parameters Their focus is on worship as opposed to practice Clinging to their dogma, doctrine, rites, and rituals Missing entirely the greater matter of a spiritual relationship An inner connection with the One they say they trust Or the One they say they believe in Ironically, Buddhism, which is not a religion Comes closer to explaining this conundrum than any other Supposed formal religion and practice It begins in the mind, Buddhism teaches one how they are Their own worst enemy as a result of their dysfunctional thinking It’s not the fault of thinking itself, but the lack of critical thinking Defending rather than analyzing, questioning, and doubting The great Swiss psychoanalyst Carl G Jung, once said Every problem we have past mid-life is a spiritual problem It doesn’t take an Einstein to recognize the truth of that statement If we simply listen and watch the events happening daily In our own country and worldwide The qualities of kindness, compassion, empathy, mercy, And real understanding are not present in our interactions What is present is the dominant ego, the dualistic mind The urgency of being ‘right’ whatever the cost Those other qualities are matters of the heart, soul, and spirit It’s almost as if as we have become more cognitive We proportionately have become less spiritual, if we ever were It takes both to be fully human, fully alive Probably the greatest motivator for personal change Is deep emotional pain which we avoid through distractions, Through prescribed medications, and through addictions As a result, do we not only prevent our own change We also will never experience our own spirituality As a beginning process do this for yourself, it will not fail Spend a few minutes each day in silence, in stillness With Presence – emptying your mind of its incessant and Repetitive thoughts – listen to the whispers of your own Heart, soul, and spirit – they patiently wait

George G
