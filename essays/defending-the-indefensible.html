DEFENDING THE INDEFENSIBLE

By George Gondron

DEFENDING THE INDEFENSIBLE As a result of being conscious If I can make that assumption The human mind is very capable of defending the indefensible And apparently doing so with little shame at all Especially those who claim to be followers of a faith system That teaches forgiveness, compassion, and love It is a mystery to me how one can defend destructive behavior Obvious lies, toxic words, and misrepresentations That even a child can see How do you explain that kind of behavior to a child? And better yet, how do we explain it to ourselves That's the real issue Does it not have consequences worthy enough To speak out, to make the obvious more obvious? Is this the kind of nation we have become? When even the so-called learned religious Remain mute, lest they lose the gratuities Dropped in the collection plate, each Sunday Ah, please don't tell me churches can't discuss politics From the pulpit It's not about politics, it's about morality We have become a nation where party is more important Than the dysfunctional nature of its members The emperor wears no clothes And no one dares speak up Do we really care what the younger generation is learning? What they are learning about integrity, ethics, and morals From the highest office in the land About its elected officials History is replete with many examples from other nations Of the consequences of remaining silent and mute In the face of what is so obviously wrong I admire those in positions of notoriety, regardless their position That have the courage to speak out at the risk of personal loss When we don't speak out against obvious wrong We risk as well, we risk our values, our self-worth We risk our souls

George G
