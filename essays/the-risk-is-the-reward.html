THE RISK IS THE REWARD

By George Gondron

THE RISK IS THE REWARD For those who fear to risk Who fear to be vulnerable Especially emotionally or psychologically The risk is the reward, are meaningless words It follows the analogy, nothing ventured, nothing gained Growth in many respects is about failure first, not success Therefore without risk, there is neither Our failures tell us more about ourselves Than our achievements, or our accomplishments How we deal with failure or falling short of the mark Says more about who we are than do our successes Our successes tend to inflate our egos 'Puff us up', as we 'pat ourselves on the back' But failure, failure has the wonderful capability of Keeping us humble, if we but allow And the importance of humility cannot be overstated For it acknowledges that I am no better than anyone else I am in need of 'grace' as much as anyone For the true reality of love is humility The humble person is a loving person As the humble God is a loving God Those who do not risk Who fight over who's God is the right God Are simply the believers, not the knower's They know neither humility, nor God Their God lives in their minds, not their hearts The risk of knowing one's God is not found through the mind For the mind is about belief, the heart is about knowing Knowing is always a result of personal experience Which is simply another way of saying We can't know God in mass, only as individual persons That alone reveals the important significance of each person That one's understanding of God is personal, relational It is a direct, compassionate, caring relationship Risking that relationship carry's an inestimable reward We give up nothing in the risk And gain everything as the reward

George G
