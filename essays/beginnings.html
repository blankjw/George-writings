BEGINNINGS

By George Gondron

BEGINNINGS We begin as a single cell A solitary cell that contains all that we will become The mystery of life, is still a mystery We are born, we begin to breath And ever so slowly we experience our senses The beginning of a whole new adventure Taste, touch, smell, hear, and see We begin to move To crawl, to walk, to run And at some moment we find we are other We are not an extension of mother, father We are separate, another new beginning We discover through our own efforts Our own risks and vulnerabilities, many new beginnings But then, at some point, it varies for each of us We no longer seek our own beginnings We relinquish that right to others For they, through fear, intimidation, or self-doubt Convince us that they are the truth sayers They carry the light of reason Only they can show us the way We no longer listen to our hearts, our souls, our spirits Those whispers that hold our personal truth After years of listening to others To other voices, to all the 'ism's' Following their directions, their rhetoric and hyperbole Of discovering they don't have all the answers That we are nothing more than a means to an end Their end Perhaps we may discover another new beginning One that bubbles up from inside ourselves One not necessarily concerned about all the externals About gathering, competing, desiring But more concerned about kindness, compassion, inclusion That beginning is our truer nature, our truer self That knows life is relationship, relationship is love Seek that beginning, seek love

George G
