COLLECTIVE IGNORANCE

By George Gondron

COLLECTIVE IGNORANCE First, what is ignorance? It's not stupidity, it's not the inability to learn It's not illiteracy or lack of culture The answer is really very simple It's the refusal to learn, to question, to doubt The willingness to accept the obvious lie as the truth Compounded further by defending the lie It's discovering your comfort zone and then Finding the narrative that supports your comfort It's being close-minded, closed-off, myopic It's laziness, too busy to learn something new It's the fault of perceiving that everything, everyone Not like me, is against me We are awash in 'collective ignorance' We breath it, we eat it, we swallow it, we all do And it thrives and permeates all venues of our culture From political, to educational, to religious, to economic There is nothing more dangerous than a closed Ignorant mind A mind that clings to the familiar Unwilling to explore the possible Unwilling to listen to what may be different, may be better We live in ruts, graves with both ends open We accept the illusion that the problems lie elsewhere Not within ourselves It's always the other, those others, all others Not us To live and not learn is resignation, is surrender, is cowardice It is to blame, finger point, and be complicit There is no valid excuse in the world in which we live To not learn, there is no age limit to learning Repetition, redundancy, duplication are the enemies In thought, in feeling, and in action Risk and vulnerability are the challenges to transform Learn something new, refuse to accept the lies Avoid being a part of the collective ignorance that exists Run ahead of the crowd, don't just follow it

George G
