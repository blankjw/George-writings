TO SEE

By George Gondron | Source: TO SEE.docx

TO SEE To be able to see, to comprehend what is really there Is to be objective, that's the dilemma For the ego is by and large, totally subjective The ego sees through 'a glass darkly' How we see, is much more important Than what we see Many words can be used to describe how we see Tunnel vision, myopia, rose colored glasses, blinders With prejudice and lack of clarity Which leads to this simple question How can I respond accurately If I do not see clearly, the answer, I can't So consequently, I simply continue to convince myself That what and how I see is reality, is the truth And that's the survival of chaos and disharmony I am right and all else is wrong Well, maybe not all else There are some that see what I see And those, like myself, are the right 'seers' Unless and until I see in myself What I see in others, especially the uncomfortable I will never perceive correctly My vision will always be 'blurred' The 'splinter' will always be in the other's eye As I miss the 'log' in my own To see with the eyes of a child, the eyes of innocence Before they are influenced with all the 'labels' With the prejudice, discrimination, and bias of their culture Indoctrinated with a small belief system intolerant of others Those are the eyes with which we need to see The 'before' eyes Eyes that see with compassion and loving kindness With inclusion, togetherness, not division Right 'seeing' is with the mind, heart, soul, and spirit The ego sees a distortion of the real, always Silence, stillness, and Presence teach us how to see With clarity, so that we may respond with accuracy

George G
