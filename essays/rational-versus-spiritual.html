RATIONAL VERSUS SPIRITUAL

By George Gondron

RATIONAL VERSUS SPIRITUAL There is no context in which ‘rational and spiritual’ merge Simply because a numinous spiritual experience Is not cognitive nor intellectual, of the mind or the ego It is a felt experience known by the heart, soul, and spirit An experience so self-evident it’s undeniable So deep and propound it’s unexplainable, as it should be Consequently, spirituality cannot be taught There’s not a step one, two, or three chosen by the mind That leads to spirituality or a spiritual experience There can be spontaneous breakthroughs or breakdowns Often when the experiences of the individual life Become so painful, so unmanageable, so undefended That the individual reaches the ‘end of their rope’ In our modern culture, it’s the exception, not the rule We manage through all our available addictions To remain distracted, preoccupied, and other focused Constructing a self-made prison to ward off any possibility For a spiritual breakthrough of any kind

Or one can choose a practice or a method That quietens the mind, that silences the incessant thoughts That occupies our consciousness every waking moment A meditative or contemplative practice Engaged by all the great mystics and spiritual teachers Throughout history, their writings are well documented Christianity itself has survived primarily through the various Religious orders, male and female, not through sermons and Homilies on a Sunday morning by parish priests and pastors The success of a contemplative practice is very simple Commitment – each day for just a few minutes Begin with just five minutes, focusing on the breath Or a very short mantra like ‘wellness to yourself’ on the in-breath ‘Wellness to the universe’ on the out-breath Slowly work your way up to a 20 to 30-minute sit, daily It may seem impossible at first, but stay with it Slowly the mind will clear, you will discover your true self Experience the whispers of heart, soul, and spirit – they wait

George G
