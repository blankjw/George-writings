cause and effect

By George Gondron

CAUSE AND EFFECT All that is detrimental to the human species, with the exception of natural disasters, is a direct result of the human mind, the ego Especially a mind that has no desire to learn, is not curious A mind that operates only as an input device without any filters whatsoever, you know, ‘garbage in, garbage out’ The nature of critical thinking includes the resistance to accept what is, as it is, without questions, doubt, research, genuine learning In other words, we don’t learn by ‘luck’, but through effort and unfortunately, the last place to learn, if you’re really wanting to educate yourself and truly seek reliable information is the internet With everything in life that pulls us toward further understanding there is risk and there is reward, social media today is very risky Every initial cause is tied personally to many processes Critical thinking or no thinking at all Questions, doubts, skepticism, uncertainty, or total acceptance Our tendency to have a ‘knee-jerk’ reaction or a reflective response Whichever of those paths we choose is very determinative of its final effect, the less educated, aware, conscious, and understanding we are the more drastic, damaging, and risky the effect When intelligence fails, when ignorance prevails, when ‘power’ loves the poorly educated, the effects will never be promising Unlike our five senses, taste, touch, smell hear, and see from which we receive an immediate experience, our thoughts and feelings are much more illusive They require reflection, discernment, critical analysis for us to foresee as best as possible the causes we choose and their ultimate effects, in order to avoid being manipulated and controlled I know this for certain, any cause that is not a decision which Includes mind and ego as well as heart, soul, and spirit is very questionable, it is fragmented to start To put it very succinctly, we are a nation morally and spiritually bankrupt, it is far too obvious to ignore, blind leading the blind For balance, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, stillness, with Presence Awaken your internal resources, connect inner and outer Focus on your breath or a short mantra, breathe in, breathe out Listen to the whispers of your inner guides, heart, soul, spirit

George G
