THE MOST UNFAVORABLE PLACE TO LIVE

By George Gondron

THE MOST UNFAVORABLE PLACE TO LIVE Without a doubt, it is our own mind, our ego Addicted to our own repetitive thoughts That are primarily focused consistently on the past Which can never be relived, or the future which is not yet Seldom are we present to now, which is the only reality The past of ‘shoulda’s, coulda’s, and woulda’s’ The future of ‘what ifs, maybe’s, and what-abouts’ Our ability to think, disconnected from our abilities Held within our inner core become a burden, not a blessing Doom and gloom fear and insecurity, versus freedom and joy A mindset of unceasing worry and concern To be proactive about areas of worry is the better choice Especially when we can admit worry never helps, at all The underlying reason we continue to worry Clearly, we don’t want to change, worry is a choice In fact, every thought, every feeling, every action is a choice And we defend against those choices or make excuses We prefer the familiar, our familiar, to the strange Even when that familiar is uncomfortable, like worry Much easier to resist necessary change, than take ownership Of our chosen conditions and let go of what doesn’t work Without balance, without connection to our internal resources We will always cling to what doesn’t work To be proactive is always the better choice No risk, no reward, vulnerability leads to possibility Join your mind to the intuitive knowing of your inner core Live there, live in the balance, simply existing is not life No one else is responsible for your choices, only you We live in a culture that focuses on the externals There is virtually no encouragement for a reflective life Do yourself a favor, adopt a meditative/contemplative practice Spend a few minutes each day in silence, be still, embrace The presence of Presence, your image of a creator God Empty your mind of all thoughts, choose a short mantra To breathe in and breathe out, perhaps three words Become aware of the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
