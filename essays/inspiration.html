INSPIRATION

By George Gondron

INSPIRATION Inspiration diminishes and soon passes away It is only an initiator, the precursor of something more But for most, that something more is never pursued And the cycle only repeats itself to no meaningful end Inspiration with no change, becomes lip service only To inspire, excite, invigorate, is to be open to change But, the chance is soon lost, the door closes quickly If action does not follow that which inspires The words on a page, the sharing with another The workshop experience, or weekend retreat The story on the screen, the lyrics of the song All can inspire, but they're only the lure They're not the catch, they are only the pointers True inspiration is more about becoming Becoming more of who we are meant to be Not merely having, acquiring, and doing Doing more is ego driven, a disguised selfishness With some form of payoff as the end result But the inspiration of becoming is so very different It is new awareness, about myself and life About connections and relationships Purpose and meaning If what inspires does not touch the heart If what inspires does not move the spirit If what inspires does not awaken the soul Then, there is only one question that's necessary For what benefit is the inspiration? Inspiration that leads to self-knowledge That heals relationships and nurtures life That increases awareness and deepens consciousness That leads to love; those are the true benefits

George G
