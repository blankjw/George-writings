DEFEND

By George Gondron

DEFEND In psychology what are termed defense mechanisms Measure in the double digits; deny, blame, justify, etc. They exist for one reason only When we, you and I, are not able to accept and own Our behavior, our thoughts, feelings, and actions The result, we defend, always Making excuses is defending The quickest way to elicit a defensive response In an open conversation, use the word ‘why’ Two actions will occur simultaneously The respondent will begin to defend And all meaningful conversation will cease For if we’re defending we’re no longer listening Sadly, the more important question is not why, but how Rather than ‘Why did this happen, how did this happen’? Wouldn’t you rather hear, ‘How did this happen’, rather than ‘Why did you do this’? Can you imagine how many negative outcomes result From why versus how Polls taken on the most important traits of healthy Primary and secondary relationships reveal that number one Is healthy communication, which is desperately missing In our culture, That requires a whole other discussion Aside from the topic of defensiveness, but they are related So, if you’re faced with a ‘why’ comment Do your best to imagine the ‘how’ and respond accordingly For in truth, there is no satisfactory answer to why So we defend, and please avoid using ‘why’ yourself Pay attention to all conversations, especially social media You will discover how often the word ‘why’ is used And often there is no positive or satisfactory outcome Embrace a meditative/contemplative daily practice For a few minutes be silent, be still, be in the presence of Your image of a creative Source, of your God Your focus will become better, what you hear and see will change Listen to the whispers of your heart, soul, and spirit They hold your true self, they have your answers, they wait

George G
