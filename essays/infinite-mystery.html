INFINITE MYSTERY

By George Gondron

INFINITE MYSTERY We take for granted the wonderful gift of consciousness Our ability to question, seek, doubt, and explore Abilities that are rarely or seldom used Living lives of seeming relevant importance or inert complacency Void of mystery, satisfied with what is What we know of this vast universe today Compared to what we knew just one hundred years ago Is almost beyond comprehension And what is known is more mysterious now than then God laughs and is encouraged and excited Encouraged about our discoveries that lead us Lead us deeper into the mysteries of creation Excited at the discoveries that increase our awareness Expand our consciousness, stretch our horizons And reveal there is so much more to know Our tiny planet is to the expanse of the universe As our conscious mind is to our own unconscious We are a mystery to ourselves, a very deep mystery As is this ever expanding cosmos And as science struggles to find answers to these mysteries They are beginning to speak of an intelligent design A conscious purpose of this magnificent creation Held together with an un-nameable energy And with each new discovery, each new speculation The mystery only deepens, becoming more intriguing Revealing that nothing is static, there are no empty spaces All is connected, relational, and dynamic Just as all sentient life is interdependent And we as humans have a yearning to connect A longing to know, consciously or unconsciously That which nature inherently knows We wish to know the One that creates And if we don't know, the fault is ours For God always waits in the silence and stillness Always near, always present, always a connection To ask where is God, is the wrong question The question is, where is God not, Infinite Mystery

George G
