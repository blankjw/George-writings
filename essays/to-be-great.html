TO BE GREAT

By George Gondron

TO BE GREAT What does it mean to be great What are the requirements, constructs, and qualities of greatness? For the athlete, it is commitment and dedication To their craft mostly through physical exertion But also, mental sharpness and acuity How about the scientist, the engineer, the historian Their greatness is the result of an expansion Of their mental abilities combined with an intuitive mind Long hours of persistent research and critical thinking Knowing there will be repeated failure before success Often looking in the wrong places for the right answer And then there are those areas of greatness For which there are no guides, no books, no how-to’s Those areas in which success is measured as the result of Personal change, transformation, and individuation To become fully human, fully alive A metamorphosis, a change from the inside out It is the evolving of kindness, compassion, and love Into everyday actions and not just verbal platitudes It is the awakening of gifts freely given with our life It changes our relationships into actions of caring and sharing Versus grasping and clinging, but be warned The discovery of our ‘true self’ and the abandonment Of our ‘false self’ will not be without pain, emotional pain The journey of wholeness requires a lot of ‘letting go’ Two steps forward, one step back – the ego resists change We live in an addictive culture, it is the emotional and intellectual Addictions that are the most difficult to release and heal Our emotional, intellectual, and spiritual path to greatness Is an ‘inside’ job – a few minutes spent each day in silence, With stillness, and Presence, our image of a creator God A time of emptying our ‘monkey minds’ of their incessant And repetitive thoughts that go nowhere A time to listen to the whispers of our own heart, soul, and spirit To acknowledge the inner yearnings possessed by each of us To be in relationship with the Consciousness of the universe To be able to say, ‘we are great’, with deep, humility

George G
