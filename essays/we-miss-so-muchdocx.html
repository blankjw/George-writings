WE MISS SO MUCH

By George Gondron

WE MISS SO MUCH To associate only with those like ourselves We miss so very much And consequently we miss the wonderful diversity Of what it means to be human We miss the fact that our feelings, our emotions Our hopes and dreams Our thoughts, our goals, our beliefs and fears Are more alike than unalike To not know what it's like to be blind or deaf To live with a physical handicap or long term illness To be of a different ethnicity, a different culture Even, and probably most importantly, a different gender Without a sense of wonder or curiosity, is our loss To at least partially experience through dialogue Through interaction what that must be like Is surely a loss to ourselves as well as the other But the really sad reality is this Not knowing, not experiencing in any fashion What's not like us We begin to make assumptions, suppositions, conjectures About what seems to be different And the difference, almost always, is external All on the outside The process of a mind that is ego driven is as follows What we don't understand, we learn to fear Fear, through suspicion, eventually turns to hatred Where there is hatred there is no love Where there is no love, there is no God No one, emphasis no one, controls our minds Only and if we give them permission to do so That is the danger of living through the mind alone Heart, soul, and spirit are always inclusive Always open and welcoming, always connected It is easy to control a person who operates Through the mind alone, read history Be a person of heart, listen to your soul Experience your inner spirit, it is your truth

George G Post Script - As I finished this reflection, this poem, the image that entered my mind was of Donald Trump making fun of a handicapped person on national television, talking about forcing himself on women, grabbing their genitals, shooting someone on Fifth Ave in New York and still being elected, encouraging violence at many of his campaign rallies. I thought for sure that these actions would have been more than enough for him to have no chance of being elected to the highest office of our nation. But, I was wrong. I had to ask myself, 'what does all this mean in retrospect?' Not only as individuals have we greatly compromised our morality, but collectively as a nation as well. We, it seems, have lost our moral compass. History, recent history, is witness and proof of what follows. And it's not promising, it is frightening.
