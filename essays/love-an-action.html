LOVE, AN ACTION

By George Gondron

LOVE, AN ACTION The word love has been used and abused For hundreds of years Especially in modern times It’s unfortunate that we have only one word In the English language to convey feelings and emotions Which seem to all fall under that one word, love I love that book, that movie, that meal, that dress That car, that song, that dance, etc., etc., etc. To ask a significant other ‘Do you believe I love you?’ Versus ‘do you know I love you?’ Carries the true meaning of love Genuine love is an action Not a belief We claim to believe many things That we actually don’t know For knowing is experiential It arises from personal experience Genuine love flows from a perceived need In the witnessed existence of the other or others What we do out of kindness and compassion Arises from sincere and genuine love Love freely given asks for nothing in return And sadly, it is a rare occurrence There more often than not seems to be a string attached To the gratuity of love But then again in those circumstances It’s not really love at all It’s a feigned selfless act Disguised in selfishness When genuine love is present The question, ‘do you love me?’ Need never be asked For in our interactions the answer is already given It seems I read this line somewhere ‘Love one another as I have loved you’ Would that that were true, universally

George G
