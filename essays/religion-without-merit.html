RELIGION WITHOUT MERIT

By George Gondron

RELIGION WITHOUT MERIT What is the purpose of a belief system that excludes compassion? Is not inclusive of all people, with no exceptions? That requires you to meet a certain standard to be included? Is there merit to a religion that puts other religions down? That always sees difference as something abject and unworthy Who's very principles and leaders are abusive More concerned about form than substance More focused on clinging than letting go Attached to their formulated doctrines and dogma Their rites and rituals exclusive of 'hands on kindness' What can be said about a religion that is selective in its teaching Teaching only what lends to keeping the membership content Keeping the 'plates' filled and the 'fire insurance' paid If from one man a belief system arose about being human About the transformation of self, and the connection to all About a God he called his 'daddy' One who was the epitome of love and inclusion How do we ignore that and focus on minutiae instead The bottom line is this If our religious leaders can convince us we are good people By simply going through the motions of form Without the risk of substance, then we convince ourselves We have an out to justify our incongruent behavior The prophets have all but died There are few left to 'hold our feet to the fire' If you have one in your life, cherish them They are truly images of the divine walking among us The above applies to all belief systems not just mine or yours It seems to be the nature of leading a life through the ego Exclusive of the heart, soul, and spirit Exclusive of a relational connection to one's image of God One can't become spiritual through the intellect, only religious It doesn't work and never will Kindness, compassion, peace, mercy, inclusion, and justice Are matters of the heart, not the head They are, what will in the end, help us survive Ego's blessing is as much a curse, follow your heart

George G
