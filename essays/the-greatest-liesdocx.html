THE GREATEST LIES

By George Gondron

THE GREATEST LIES The greatest lies are the ones we tell ourselves And to believe we don't lie to ourselves Is itself a lie Without a doubt, the most damaging Is the self-talk of not being 'good enough' A terrible lie We are, without a doubt, 'good enough' Or, there's the one where we 'puff' ourselves up Being better than everyone else Especially those ______________, you fill in the blank Every ego statement of compare and contrast Is nothing more than the ego's lie about itself Either judgmental or exaggerated The posture of, I may not be right But, I'm never wrong The greatest lies are never about humility They are always about being more than or less than Both of which are self constructed prisons To which only we hold the key Self-deception never ends until we embrace who we are Just as we are Until then, we simply continue to live the lie Living in an atmosphere of expectation Expecting everyone and everything to change Except ourselves The opposite of how real transformation happens What needs to change first is our self, not the other Whatever or whoever the other may be Criticism is for the system, the imagined problem Always outside of ourselves, always pointing elsewhere Wrapped in our own righteousness, our own false beliefs Followed blindly without any experience at all Just empty words that never seem to satisfy We say, 'how are you?', and the reply, 'I'm fine' Another lie, strangers to ourselves and to one another Be still, be silent, be in the Presence No need to lie, it's a dead end road, leading nowhere

George G
