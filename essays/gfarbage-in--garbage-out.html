GFARBAGE IN - GARBAGE OUT

By George Gondron | Source: GFARBAGE IN - GARBAGE OUT.docx

GARBAGE IN – GARBAGE OUT A decades-old computer analogy As relevant to our mind as it is to the PC But unlike the PC which can be corrected Our mind tends to resist changing that Which has become its familiar The problem is further strengthened If the ‘garbage in’ carries emotional energy For the emotions, particularly if they are strong Lessen objectivity and cloud perception Ergo, ‘there are none so blind as those who will not see’ Regardless the content The ego refuses to let go A dualistic mind is reluctant to change Even in the face of facts to the contrary For example, the term hermeneutics Is the interpretation of sacred literature There are four levels of interpretation The most basic or lowest is ‘literal’ The literalist is very fundamental and conservative Unable to consider any other alternative interpretation Even if the words or stories are scientifically proven To be wrong, the mind clings to the ‘literal’ Which ultimately builds walls, not bridges In addition, a literalist is almost always close-minded Unable to see the forest for the trees A literalist, a closed mind operates from an emotional Posture of fear, not love Even if their actions appear to be loving Internally is the thought, ‘what will happen if I don’t do this?’ In other words, it’s belief based on meritocracy I do good I get rewarded, I do bad I get punished That’s level one morality, that of a child With this gift we call life comes free will And the ability to think critically, learn, and evolve Take a few minutes each day for silence, stillness, With Presence – empty the mind that you might hear The whispers of the heart, soul, spirit, and the Truth

George G
